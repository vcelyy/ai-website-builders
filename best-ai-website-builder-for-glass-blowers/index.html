Glass Artist Website FAQ

Should glass artists include pricing on their website?

YES. Publish starting ranges by piece type: "Functional vessels $400-800, Sculptural pieces $1,200-5,000, Architectural installations $5,000+." Filters budget matches, saves time answering "how much" emails, positions you professionally. Serious collectors appreciate pricing transparency. Only exceptions: one-of-a-kind museum-quality pieces (these get "Contact for exhibition pricing").

What glass process videos should artists feature?

Show COMPLETE cycle: (1) Design sketch + color selection, (2) Furnace prep + gathering, (3) Shaping + blocking, (4) Annealing process explanation, (5) Cold work + finishing. 2-4 minute edited videos, narrated with technique insights. Behind-the-scenes footage = authenticity = value. Every master glassblower I know increased commissions after adding process videos.

Do glass artists need e-commerce on their portfolio site?

DEPENDS. If you sell functional pieces under $2,000? YES - Shopify or Squarespace Commerce. If you sell sculptural/commemorative work $5,000+? NO - focus on commission inquiries. High-end glass art = custom vision collaboration = phone call, not cart checkout. E-commerce distracts from your primary revenue: gallery exhibitions and custom commissions.

How do glass artists attract gallery representation through their website?

Three elements: (1) COMMISSION PORTFOLIO - separate gallery showing client request â†’ your design â†’ final piece documentation, (2) PROCESS VIDEOS - prove your technique mastery, (3) ARTIST STATEMENT - your vision, philosophy, influences. Gallery curators search for artists with VISION, not just technical skill. Make your vision impossible to miss. Include "For Gallery Inquiries" prominent contact.

What's the best website platform for emerging glass artists?

Squarespace. Why? Artisan templates designed for visual work, built-in video galleries, commission forms, launches in 1 day. $23/month is reasonable when starting. Webflow becomes worth investment once you're selling $5,000+ pieces and need custom collections. Wix for quick launch only - performance issues hurt art presentation. Start with Squarespace, upgrade when revenue justifies.

Should glass artists separate commission work from portfolio pieces?

ABSOLUTELY. Create TWO distinct portfolio sections: (1) "Personal Work" - your artistic vision, experimental pieces, gallery exhibitions, (2) "Commission Portfolio" - client collaboration examples with process documentation. Galleries want to see YOUR vision, corporations want to see collaboration capability. Separate sections = right audience sees right work.
