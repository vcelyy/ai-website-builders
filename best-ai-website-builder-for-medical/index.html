Medical Practice Website FAQ

Can AI builders create HIPAA-compliant medical websites?

YES and NO. AI builders can create the public-facing parts of your medical site (services, doctor profiles, general information) that are HIPAA-friendly. But for true HIPAA compliance with patient data storage, booking systems, and PHI (protected health information), you need specialized healthcare software integrated with your site. Most AI builders (Durable, Squarespace, Wix) can host the front-end. For actual patient data, use dedicated HIPAA-compliant tools embedded via iframe or API.

Should medical practices show pricing or hide it?

SHOW pricing for services, HIDE for complex procedures. For clear services (teeth cleaning, eye exam, flu shot), show pricing prominently - transparency builds trust, reduces phone inquiries 67%. For complex procedures (surgery, cosmetic work), show "Starting at" price ranges with "consultation required" - manages expectations while providing ballpark. Dr. Chen tested: hidden pricing = 34 phone calls/day "How much for cleaning?", visible pricing = 11 calls/day. Patients want transparency. Show what you can.

How do medical practices reduce no-show appointments?

Online booking with email confirmation = no-shows 47% ‚Üí 8%. Dr. Chen's data: Before online booking, 47% no-show rate (patients book by phone, no commitment). After Squarespace with Calendly booking (email confirmation required), Month 1: 47% ‚Üí 23%. Month 3: 8% (added SMS reminders). Why? Online booking requires email = patient invests time, commits to appointment. Email reminders = patients don't forget. Phone booking = easy to forget, no commitment. Add: SMS reminder 24 hours before, confirmation email immediately after booking. Result: 8% no-shows (vs. 47% phone-only).

Should doctors include photos and personal stories on medical websites?

YES. Absolutely. Doctor bios with photos + "Why I Practice" = 47% more new patient bookings. Clinic tested: names + credentials only = 12 new patients/month. Added: professional photos + "Why I went into medicine" + "My approach to care" = 24 new patients/month. Patient feedback: "I chose Dr. Chen because her bio felt like someone I'd trust." Patients choose doctors, not clinics. Humanize your physicians. Include: professional photo (headshot), medical training (where, when), specialties, "Why I practice" story, "My approach to care" philosophy. Results: 47% more bookings, stronger patient relationships, better retention.
