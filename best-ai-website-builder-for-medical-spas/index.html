Medical Spa Website FAQ

Do I really need to show before/after photos on my website?

Yes. This is non-negotiable for medical spas. Clients want proof of results before spending $500+. Stock photos of models don't build trust. Real before/after photos of your actual work do. One med spa saw 5x more bookings in 30 days after adding photo gallery. Use real clients (with signed release forms). Organize by treatment type (Botox, fillers, laser). Visible results = proof = trust = book treatment. No photos = they assume poor work = choose competitor.

Should I list pricing or use "contact for consultation"?

List starting prices. "Contact for consultation" kills conversions because clients worry about cost - they don't ask, they leave. List: "Botox: $12/unit starting," "Lip Fillers: $600/syringe starting." Add "pricing varies by amount - consultation provides exact quote." Results: 67% more qualified bookings after transparent pricing. Cost visible = can afford = confident booking = appointment scheduled.

How do I showcase practitioner credentials effectively?

Add credentials prominently on homepage, not buried "About Us" page. "Dr. Wong: MD, UCSF Medical School, 15 years experience." "Our Injectors: RN-certified, 10+ years experience." Include photos if possible (builds trust). Results: 53% more qualified bookings. Medical spa clients = risk-averse. They need credentials visible before booking. Credentials = safe = skilled = book.

Should I create separate pages for each treatment?

Yes. Create dedicated SEO-optimized page per treatment: "Botox San Francisco - $12/unit, Book Online" with before/after photos, FAQs, pricing, results timeline. These rank in Google for "[Treatment] [City]" searches. One med spa created 15 treatment pages, drove 150+ organic appointments/month from search. Treatment-specific pages = SEO dominance = organic revenue = free leads.

How important is online booking for medical spas?

Critical. Medical spa treatments = impulse decisions. Client wants Botox during lunch break = searches on phone = wants to book now. If you require phone call only, they'll "call later" (they forget). Online booking with real-time availability captures impulse. Results: 43% more appointments after adding online booking. Convenience = book now = capture revenue.

What content should I include in treatment photos?

Before/after photos from multiple angles. Real clients (with signed release forms showing treatment area). Consistent lighting (same before/after conditions). Treatment type labeled. Time since treatment noted (e.g., "2 weeks post-treatment"). Results visible but realistic (not over-promised). One med spa added "2-week results" timeline: 34% fewer "didn't work" complaints because clients had realistic expectations. Authentic photos = realistic expectations = happy clients.
