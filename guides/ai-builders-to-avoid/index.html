Already Stuck With a Bad Builder? Escape Plan.

How to migrate with minimal pain.

STEP 1: AUDIT YOUR ASSETS

Export everything possible: copy all text to Google Docs, download all images, screenshot every page layout. If platform blocks export, manually copy-paste content. Even if export is garbage, you need raw content. Document structure: page hierarchy, navigation menu, form fields. Better to have messy backup than nothing. This audit takes 2-4 hours depending on site size—worth it to prevent complete data loss.

ACTION: Copy all text/images to Google Drive before canceling

STEP 2: CHOOSE NEW PLATFORM WISELY

Don't repeat mistake. Test export feature on new platform before committing. Webflow and Framer both allow clean export. 10Web exports WordPress. Build migration site on new platform while keeping old site live—no downtime. Use subdomain like 'staging.yoursite.com' for testing. Only switch DNS when new site perfect. Avoid builder lock-in round two—verify exit strategy exists before investing time.

ACTION: Choose Webflow/Framer/10Web for guaranteed export

STEP 3: MIGRATE CONTENT SYSTEMATICALLY

Start with most important pages: homepage, about, services, pricing. Recreate layout from scratch using new platform's tools—don't try to import bad export. Copy text from your Google Docs backup. Re-upload images from Drive. Build forms from scratch using third-party tools (Typeform, Tally). Work systematically page by page. Quality rebuild beats migrating garbage code. Plan 30-60 minutes per page depending on complexity.

ACTION: Rebuild from scratch, don't import bad code

STEP 4: PRESERVE SEO DURING MIGRATION

Keep identical URL structure when possible. If URLs must change, set up 301 redirects from old URLs to new. Submit new sitemap to Google Search Console. Keep old site live for 2 weeks after launching new site—catch any broken links. Monitor Google Analytics for traffic drops post-migration. Most SEO recovers within 30 days if redirects implemented correctly. Don't panic if traffic dips temporarily—that's normal during site migration.

ACTION: Set up 301 redirects, submit new sitemap to Google

AI BUILDERS TO AVOID
RED FLAGS, PLATFORM RISKS, WARNINGS

Not all AI builders are created equal. Some have hidden fees, poor support, platform instability, limited features that lock you in. Others overpromise and underdeliver—AI generates generic content requiring extensive rewriting. This guide identifies red flags, specific platforms to approach with caution, warning signs of bad AI builders, and how to test platforms safely before committing time and money.

RED FLAGS TO WATCH FOR

HIDDEN FEES & SURPRISE CHARGES

Read pricing pages carefully

Some AI builders advertise low prices but charge extra for: custom domains, removing branding, basic features (forms, scheduling), bandwidth overages, email support. "Free tier" that's unusable without upgrades = bait-and-switch. Calculate true cost before committing.

Real example: Platform advertised "Free forever." After building site, discovered: custom domain $10/mo extra, branding removal $15/mo extra, forms $5/mo extra. Actual usable cost: $30/mo, not $0.

💸 HIDDEN FEES = Budget overruns

POOR OR NO CUSTOMER SUPPORT

Test support before committing

Some AI builders have: email-only support with 48+ hour response times, no phone/chat options, self-help forums only. When site breaks (and it will)—slow support = business downtime. Test support responsiveness before building: ask a question, gauge response time.

Real example: SaaS startup built site on platform with email-only support. Site crashed during launch. Support responded in 72 hours. Lost $3,000 in sales during downtime. Learned: test support before building.

🐌 SLOW SUPPORT = Business risk

PLATFORM INSTABILITY

Check uptime and reliability

New AI builders launch frequently—many shut down within 2 years. Platform shutdown = your site disappears. Check: company age, funding status, user count, recent updates. New platforms = exciting but risky. Established platforms = boring but safe.

Real example: Startup built site on new AI builder (6 months old). 18 months later, platform shut down unexpectedly. Site vanished, no export option. Lost entire website. Choose platforms with 3+ years track record.

⚠️ NEW PLATFORMS = Shutdown risk

NO EXPORT/BACKUP OPTIONS

Lock-in is dangerous

Some platforms don't allow exporting your content. If you leave—you lose everything. Look for: export to HTML/WordPress, backup options, data portability. If platform doesn't offer export—you're trapped. Test export before building serious site.

Real example: Business outgrew platform after 2 years. Discovered no export option. Had to manually copy-paste 200 pages of content (40 hours). Choose platforms with export from day one.

🔒 NO EXPORT = Locked in forever

💡 RED FLAG VERDICT: Test before committing

Before building serious website on AI builder: test customer support (ask question, measure response time), verify export options (try exporting test site), check platform age (3+ years preferred), read recent reviews (Trustpilot, G2), calculate true pricing (add up all features needed). 1 hour of testing prevents 100 hours of pain later.

PLATFORMS TO APPROACH WITH CAUTION
SPECIFIC WARNINGS

Certain AI builders have documented issues: frequent downtime, poor support, misleading pricing, limited features. This doesn't mean "never use"—but proceed with eyes open. For testing/idea validation, risky platforms are acceptable. For business-critical sites, choose established platforms. The cost of platform failure (downtime, lost data) exceeds the savings from cheaper/unproven options.

HOW TO TEST PLATFORMS SAFELY

STEP 1: TEST FREE TRIAL/TIER

Build real test site

Don't just explore demo—build actual test site with your content. Test: image upload, text editing, mobile preview, form creation. Platform looks good in demo but fails in practice. Real testing reveals issues before you're committed.

Time investment: 1-2 hours per platform tested. Saves 20+ hours of pain if platform sucks.

STEP 2: TEST CUSTOMER SUPPORT

Submit support ticket before paying

Ask: "How do I export my site?" or "Can I add custom code?" Measure: response time (should be <24 hours), quality of answer, helpfulness. Slow/unhelpful support on sales question = worse support when you have urgent problem.

Real example: Tested 3 platforms. Platform A: 48-hour response, vague answer. Platform B: 4-hour response, detailed answer. Platform C: no response. Chose Platform B.

STEP 3: VERIFY EXPORT/BACKUP

Try exporting test site

Build test page, attempt export. If export fails or doesn't exist—you're locked in. For business sites, export is non-negotiable. Platforms without export are fine for temporary tests, not for permanent business presence.

Real example: Tested platform, export function existed but produced broken HTML. Site wouldn't load after export. Useless export = same as no export.

STEP 4: CHECK RECENT REVIEWS

What do current users say?

Check: Trustpilot, G2, Capterra, Reddit (r/webdev, r/SaaS). Look for: recent complaints (last 3 months), patterns (same issue repeated), company responses (helpful or defensive?). Reviews from 2+ years ago = irrelevant. Platform quality changes.

Real example: Platform had 4.5-star rating... until checked reviews. Recent 3 months: 1.2 stars, frequent downtime, support ignoring tickets. Avoided disaster.

🎯 TESTING VERDICT: 2 hours testing prevents 100+ hours pain

Safe platform testing process: build real test site (1-2 hours), test support responsiveness (1 hour), verify export function (30 minutes), check recent reviews (30 minutes). Total: 3-4 hours per platform. Test 2-3 platforms before deciding. 10 hours testing vs 100+ hours migrating from bad platform later. Testing is insurance against platform failure.

🎯 AVOID VERDICT: Choose established over exciting

AI builders to avoid: new platforms (<2 years old), platforms without export options, platforms with poor support (48+ hour response times), platforms with hidden fees (bait-and-switch pricing), platforms with recent pattern of complaints. Red flags: "Free forever" that's unusable without upgrades, no export function, unresponsive support, frequent downtime, misleading marketing. Safe bets: Mixo (proven, fast), Wix (established, 500+ apps), Squarespace (design quality, reliable), Durable (innovator, solid backing). For idea validation—risky platforms acceptable (temporary use). For business sites—choose established platforms (3+ years track record).
