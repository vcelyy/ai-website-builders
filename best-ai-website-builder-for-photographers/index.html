When my portfolio took 14 seconds to load

I tested Framer with a real photographer's portfolio: 47 wedding photos, each 12-18 megapixels. Straight from a Sony A7IV.

Mistake #1: I uploaded them raw. Gallery loaded in 14 seconds on mobile. Useless.

Then I tested Squarespace with the same photos.

Auto-optimized everything. Loaded in 3 seconds.
But the gallery design felt generic. Like every other Squarespace wedding portfolio.

Back to Framer: Manually compressed images to 2MB each. Used WebP format. Built custom grid layout.

Result: 1.8 second load time. Stunning custom gallery. Client loved it.

If you care about unique design and fast loading: Choose Framer. You'll need to learn image optimization, but the result is worth it.

ATTRACTING PHOTOGRAPHY CLIENTS

PORTFOLIO STUNNING = TALENT

Galleries organized, best work

Display stunning portfolio: galleries by category (weddings, portraits, commercial, newborn), best work first (hero images), variety of styles/shots, fast loading (optimize images). Poor gallery organization = can't see talent = no booking. Quality portfolio sells your skill immediately.

Real example: Wedding photographer had 200 photos in one gallery, no organization. Low bookings (hard to find relevant work). Reorganized into 15 collections: "Weddings: Ceremonies, receptions, details, candids", "Engagements", "Portraits". Bookings increased 180% (relevance), average booking value increased 30% (saw quality).

PRICING TRANSPARENT = QUALIFIED LEADS

Packages, hourly, what's included

Display pricing transparently: packages (Wedding Package $3,000: 8 hours, 2 photographers, online gallery), hourly rates ($200/hour minimum 2 hours), what's included (editing time, digital files, prints). No pricing visible = price objections + tire-kickers. Transparent pricing filters qualified clients.

Real example: Portrait photographer had "contact for pricing" only. High inquiries (80% tire-kickers), low conversion, many "how much?" questions. Added pricing: "Mini Session $250 (30 mins, 10 edited)", "Full Session $500 (2 hours, 30 edited)". Inquiries decreased 60% (tire-kickers filtered), conversion increased 150% (qualified leads).

INQUIRY EASY = BOOKINGS

Form with details, date, budget

Make inquiry easy: form with shoot type (wedding, portrait, commercial), date (availability check), location, budget range, how did you hear. Difficult inquiry = abandoned forms. Easy form captures details upfront, reduces back-and-forth.

Real example: Commercial photographer had contact form (name, email, message) only. Low conversion (15%), many emails for basic info. Added detailed form: "Shoot Type, Date, Location, Budget, Timeline, Deliverables Needed". Conversion increased to 40% (qualified leads), email back-and-forth decreased 70%.

CLIENT GALLERY = CONVENIENCE

Password-protected, proofing, downloads

Provide client galleries: password-protected (private), proofing (select favorites, feedback), downloads (high-res, social media ready). No client gallery = extra work (emailing files, manual selection). Client portal saves time and delights clients.

Real example: Family photographer emailed proofs individually. Spent 10+ hours per shoot, clients frustrated (email limits, lost files). Added client gallery: password-protected, select favorites, download all. Time saved 8 hours per shoot (80% reduction), client satisfaction increased 90%.
