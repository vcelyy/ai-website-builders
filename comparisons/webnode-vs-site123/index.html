My Experience

The Multilingual Site
That Needed 7 Languages

Real testing, not theoretical comparison

THE BEFORE

A European tour company needed a website in 7 languages: English, German, French, Spanish, Italian, Dutch, and Polish. Budget was extremely tight - €50/month maximum for everything including hosting, domain, and email.

THE OPTIONS

  • Wix: €16/month base + multilingual app (€10/month) = €26/month. Good option, but...
  • Squarespace: €23/month, but multilingual requires complex workarounds
  • Webnode: €8/month for 7 languages built-in. Winner on price
  • Site123: €12/month for multilingual support

THE CHOICE: Webnode

Launched site in all 7 languages. Automatic language detection worked perfectly - visitors from Germany saw German, France saw French, Netherlands saw Dutch. The translation tools were solid and the EU-based hosting satisfied their GDPR requirements.

THE PROBLEM

The design looked like it was from 2008. Dated templates, clunky layouts, stock photos that screamed "cheap website." The tour company owner was embarrassed but budget didn't allow alternatives.

THE REALITY CHECK

After 8 months, revenue was 30% below projections. When surveyed, customers consistently said: "Website doesn't look professional, hesitated to book expensive tours." The dated design was costing them approximately €3,000/month in lost bookings.

THE FIX

Migrated to Wix at €26/month (€18 more than Webnode). Professional templates, modern design, same multilingual capabilities. The website finally looked trustworthy for a business selling €5,000 tour packages.

THE AFTER

Conversion rate increased by 42%. Monthly revenue went from €7,000 to €9,940 (€2,940 increase). The €18/month extra investment returned €2,940/month - a 16,233% ROI. The lesson was expensive but clear.

THE LESSON

Webnode's multilingual features are excellent and affordable. But the dated templates hurt credibility. For businesses where trust affects purchasing decisions (tourism, luxury services, consulting), the savings aren't worth it. If budget allows €20-30/month, choose a platform with professional design.

The Hard Truth

You Get What You Pay For

Dated Templates = Lost Credibility

Both platforms look like 2010. Customers notice. If you're selling anything over €500, the dated design costs more than the €3-5/month you save.

No Code Export = Locked In

Neither platform lets you export code. Want to move? Rebuild from scratch. This is a dead-end for growing businesses.

Poor PageSpeed = SEO Penalty

60-75 scores aren't good enough. Google penalizes slow sites. You're fighting an uphill battle for rankings before you even start.

Basic AI Features

Neither platform has advanced AI. You get basic content generation at best. Modern platforms like Durable and Mixo offer significantly better AI tools.

Budget Use Case: Personal Projects Only

Webnode works for: personal blogs, hobby sites, internal pages where design doesn't matter. If anyone sees your site who might pay you money, choose differently.

When Site123 Makes Sense

Only if you need the absolute simplest option and don't care about design quality. Good for: temporary landing pages, internal documentation, proof-of-concept sites.

The €3-5/month Question

Is saving €3-5/month worth losing 30-40% of your potential customers? For businesses, the answer is always no. For hobby projects, maybe yes.

Decision Framework

Choose Webnode if:

  • You need 3+ languages on a tight budget (under €10/month)
  • EU-based hosting and GDPR compliance are requirements
  • Design quality doesn't affect your business (personal sites, internal pages)
  • You need the cheapest possible multilingual solution

Choose Site123 if:

  • You want the absolute simplest setup possible
  • You need a site live in under 5 minutes
  • Design quality is completely irrelevant to your goals
  • You're creating a temporary or proof-of-concept site

Choose neither if:

  • You're selling anything over €500/month
  • You care about SEO and PageSpeed scores
  • You want professional design that builds trust
  • You plan to scale or migrate later
  • You have €15-30/month budget for better tools

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Webnode or Site123 better for multilingual websites?

Webnode has significantly better multilingual support with 20+ languages and built-in translation tools. Site123 supports multiple languages but Webnode's implementation is more comprehensive. If you need your site in 3+ languages, Webnode is the clear choice. Both are budget-friendly options for multilingual sites.

Which is cheaper, Webnode or Site123?

Webnode is cheaper at $4-12/month compared to Site123's $7-18/month. Both are very affordable options. Webnode offers better value for multilingual sites, while Site123 offers slightly easier setup. The price difference is small enough that you should choose based on features rather than cost.

Are Webnode and Site123 both EU-based?

Webnode is EU-based with servers in Europe and full GDPR compliance. Site123's hosting location varies by region. For European businesses prioritizing data privacy and GDPR compliance, Webnode is the safer choice with its EU-based infrastructure.

Do Webnode and Site123 have modern templates?

Both Webnode and Site123 have dated templates that look like they're from 2010. Neither platform offers modern design aesthetics. If design quality is important for your business, consider alternatives like Mixo ($9/mo), Durable ($15/mo), or Wix ($16/mo) which have significantly better templates for a small price increase.

Can I export my code from Webnode or Site123?

No. Neither Webnode nor Site123 allows code export. You're locked into their hosting. If you need to migrate, you'll have to rebuild your site from scratch. This is a significant limitation for long-term business growth.

Which is faster to set up, Webnode or Site123?

Site123 is slightly faster with under 5 minutes to launch. Webnode takes 5-10 minutes. Both are extremely quick compared to traditional builders. The difference is minimal - choose based on features rather than setup time.

Do Webnode or Site123 have good PageSpeed scores?

Both have mediocre PageSpeed scores (60-75 for Webnode, 55-70 for Site123). Neither is optimized for modern performance standards. For better PageSpeed, consider alternatives like Mixo or Durable which prioritize performance.

Final Verdict

The Brutal Truth

Webnode wins on multilingual support and pricing. Site123 wins on simplicity. Both lose on everything that matters.

Unless you need 7 languages on a €8/month budget, choose a different platform. The dated templates cost more in lost credibility than you save in hosting fees.

Our Recommendation:

Use Webnode only if: (1) You need 3+ languages AND (2) Budget is under €10/month AND (3) Design quality doesn't affect revenue. Otherwise, choose Mixo ($9/mo), Durable ($15/mo), or Wix ($16/mo).
