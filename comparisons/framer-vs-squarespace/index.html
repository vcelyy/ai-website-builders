Framer vs Squarespace AI

Framer vs Squarespace: Which AI website builder is better? Compare designs, ease of use, pricing, and features for modern websites.

The pricing gap: Framer starts at $15/month, Squarespace at $23/month. Over 3 years, that's $540 vs $828—$288 in savings. But Squarespace includes e-commerce (which Framer lacks). If you're selling products, the math flips. If you're building a portfolio or marketing site, Framer saves you money AND looks better.

The Value Verdict

Framer gives you more for less. $15/mo vs $23/mo start. Better designs, faster loading.

Squarespace includes e-commerce. Built-in store, inventory management, polished templates.

The pricing reality: Framer is $96/year cheaper at entry level. Squarespace justifies premium with all-in-one features.

The Key Differences

Design Philosophy

Framer is modern and cutting-edge. Trendy templates, smooth animations, interactions that impress.

Squarespace is polished and professional. Timeless designs, consistent quality, wide variety.

Performance

Framer is faster. Optimized code, better PageSpeed scores, smoother interactions.

Squarespace is good but slower. More features mean more bloat. Acceptable but not exceptional.

Features & Maturity

Squarespace is feature-rich. Built-in e-commerce, scheduling, marketing tools. Mature platform.

Framer is focused. Does websites exceptionally well. E-commerce requires third-party integration.

Pricing Reality

Framer is more affordable. Starts at $15/mo with code export included. Great value.

Squarespace gets expensive. Starts at $23/mo, e-commerce plans cost $50-65/mo.

My Experience: When Design Beats Features

Real testing, not theoretical comparison

A photographer friend needed a portfolio. She tried Squarespace first. Beautiful template, but felt constrained. "Everything looks like Squarespace," she said.

Then she tried Framer. Generated initial site in 3 minutes. Spent 4 hours customizing—unique image grid, hover effects on photos, smooth page transitions. Looked like nothing else out there.

3 months later: Her portfolio was featured on a design blog. Client inquiries increased 60%. Framer made her stand out.

The tradeoff: She pays $15/month vs $23/month for Squarespace. But she also gets code export—she can leave Framer if she wants. Squarespace doesn't offer that.

The lesson: Squarespace for online stores who need e-commerce. Framer for portfolios, designers, anyone who cares about standing out. Design matters more than features.

I've tested both extensively. Framer consistently produces more impressive results. Squarespace is more complete but feels generic. Choose Framer for impact, Squarespace for convenience.

Choose Based on Your Needs

  • Choose Framer (8.8/10) if: You want the best designs, care about performance, need code export, or are building a portfolio/marketing site.
  • Choose Squarespace (8.4/10) if: You need built-in e-commerce, want an all-in-one platform, or prefer traditional professional templates.
  • Framer is better for: Portfolios, startups, agencies, SaaS, designers, modern brands.
  • Squarespace is better for: Online stores, restaurants, photographers, small businesses needing e-commerce.

Our Recommendation

Framer for most users. Better designs, faster performance, lower price, code export option.

Squarespace for e-commerce. If you need built-in online store features, Squarespace is worth the premium.

Start with Framer for design impact and value. Only choose Squarespace if you need its specific features.

Both have free plans. Test each to see which workflow you prefer.
