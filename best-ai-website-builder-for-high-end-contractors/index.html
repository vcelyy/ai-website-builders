High-End Contractor Website FAQ

What should high-end contractor website include?

Project gallery (40+ high-res photos), before/after showcases, "Our Process" page, visible credentials (licenses, insurance, bond), project budget range ($250K-2M+), client testimonials, quote request form, contact info, service areas. Premium contractors need premium presentation to justify premium pricing.

How many project photos should I display?

Minimum 40. 5-10 photos signals weak portfolio. 40+ quality photos with before/after showcases communicates experience and quality. Organize by project type (kitchens, bathrooms, whole-home). More photos = more credibility = more contracts.

Should I display project pricing?

Display budget range, not exact pricing. "Projects $250K-2M+" signals premium focus while maintaining flexibility. Exact pricing limits you. Budget range attracts right-fit clients, eliminates unqualified leads. Right fit = serious = contract.

Do I need before/after galleries?

For renovations, absolutely. Before/after showcases provide visual proof of transformation capabilities. Homeowners can't imagine from after photos alone. Before/after = visual proof = quality = hire you. 3x more quote requests with before/after galleries.

How important is PageSpeed for contractors?

Critical. PageSpeed under 70 signals outdated, unprofessional. Premium clients notice slow loading. Fast site (90+) = attention to detail = trust = hire you. Plus, Google ranks fast sites higher. PageSpeed 95+ = more leads = more contracts.

Should high-end contractors use blog?

Yes, blog builds authority and improves SEO. Write about renovation process, planning guides, material selection. "How to Plan $500K Kitchen Renovation" attracts homeowners searching for guidance. Educate = authority = trust = contract. 2-3 posts/month sufficient.
