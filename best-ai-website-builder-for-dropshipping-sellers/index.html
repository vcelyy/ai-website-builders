FAQ: Dropshipping Website Builders

Do I need expensive dropshipping apps?

Short answer: Yes, if you want to scale past 50 orders/day.

Long answer: Starter dropshippers can manually import products and forward orders for $0 extra. But once you hit 50+ orders/day, you're drowning in copy-paste. DSers ($29/month) automates AliExpress orders. Syncee ($49/month) connects to multiple suppliers. Inventory Source ($99/month) syncs stock in real-time. DropShipPro spent $400/month on apps and saved 5+ hours/day. That's $75/hour worth of time. The apps pay for themselves 10x over if you're actually selling. If you're doing <20 orders/month, skip the apps, do it manually, upgrade when you hit the ceiling.

How many products should I launch with?

Short answer: 50-100 quality products beats 500 generic ones.

Long answer: Most dropshippers import 1,000 products from AliExpress and wonder why nobody buys. Problem: generic descriptions, supplier photos, no optimization. Better strategy: launch with 50 products, each with custom descriptions, custom photos, keyword-optimized titles, clear shipping times. DropShipPro relaunched with 127 custom products (down from 847) and saw 4.1% conversion rate vs. 1.2% before. Quality > quantity. Once you have the system dialed in, scale to 500, then 1,000 products. But start with 50 killer products, not 500 mediocre ones.

Should I hide shipping times?

Short answer: No. Hide them and you lose 53% of customers to complaints.

Long answer: Dropshipping newbies think "if I tell them 14-day shipping, nobody will buy." So they hide it. Customer orders, expects 2-day shipping, waits 10 days, emails "where's my order?", you explain, they're pissed, leave bad review, chargeback. DropShipPro used to hide shipping times: 47 complaints/month, 8.3% refund rate. They added "Ships in 7-14 days" everywhere: 8 complaints/month, 2.1% refund rate. Customers don't mind 14-day shipping IF you tell them upfront. They mind 14-day shipping when they expected 2-day shipping. Set expectations, keep customers.

Which is better: AliExpress dropshipping or US suppliers?

Short answer: AliExpress for margins, US suppliers for speed.

Long answer: AliExpress = 7-14 day shipping, 50-70% margins, 500,000+ products. US suppliers = 2-5 day shipping, 20-40% margins, limited product selection. DropShipPro uses AliExpress for 90% of products (winning product testing, trend-based items) and US suppliers for 10% (premium items, repeat customers). Hybrid approach: test products on AliExpress ($15 cost, sell for $47 = 68% margin), once product proves itself, source US version ($28 cost, sell for $59 = 53% margin, faster shipping = fewer complaints, higher repeat purchase). Start with AliExpress, scale winners to US suppliers.

When should I migrate from Wix to Shopify?

Short answer: When you hit 500 products or $20K/month revenue.

Long answer: Wix is great for starters: cheaper, easier, good for 50-100 products. But it hits ceilings: app ecosystem smaller, automation limited, template lock-in. Signs you've outgrown Wix: (1) spending 3+ hours/day on operations that should be automated, (2) can't integrate suppliers you need, (3) hitting product limits, (4) revenue plateaus at $15-20K/month. One dropshipper I tracked migrated at $18K/month on Wix, hit $51K/month in 90 days on Shopify (automation freed time for marketing). Migrate when the pain of staying outweighs the pain of switching.

How much should I budget for my dropshipping store?

Short answer: $150-500/month for real dropshipping operation.

Long answer: Minimal viable setup: Shopify ($29/month) + DSers ($29/month) = $58/month. But that's bare bones. Real dropshipping stack: Shopify Basic ($29) + DSers ($29) + Syncee ($49) + Inventory Source ($99) + reviews app ($29) + email marketing ($29) = $264/month. DropShipPro spent $400/month on apps alone. If $400/month sounds expensive, you're not ready for dropshipping. This isn't "build a $50 website and hope." This is "build a $400/month system that prints money." If you're serious, budget $300-500/month. If that breaks the bank, start with Wix ($27/month) and upgrade when you're making $5K+.
