Common Small Business Website Mistakes

No contact information visible

Put phone, email, address in header. Make it effortless to reach you.

Outdated information

Update hours, prices, services regularly. Remove outdated pages entirely.

Poor mobile experience

Design mobile-first. Test on real phones. Fix any friction immediately.

Slow loading

Optimize images, use fast hosting. Every second loses customers.

No clear call-to-action

Tell customers exactly what to do: call, visit, email, book now.

No social proof

Add testimonials, reviews, ratings. Build trust with real customer feedback.

Complicated navigation

Simple menu, clear pages. Don't make customers think.

Post current hours prominently. Update when they change.

No location information

Add address, map, directions. Make it easy to find you.

Stock photos only

Show your actual business, team, products. Authenticity builds trust.

Small Business Website Tips

Keep it simple and focused

Small business websites don't need complexity. Focus on clear information: who you are, what you do, how to contact you. Everything else is secondary.

Mobile-first is mandatory

Most customers will find you on mobile. Design for phones first, ensure everything works perfectly on small screens.

Contact info on every page

Phone number, email, address in header or footer. Make it effortless to reach you. Every click required is a lost customer.

Real photos build trust

Show your actual business, team, products. Stock photos feel fake. Real photos build authentic connections.

Focus on benefits, not features

Customers care about outcomes. "Save 2 hours daily" beats "Enterprise-grade productivity system."

Test everything on mobile

Open your site on your phone. Try to call, find address, contact. Fix any friction immediately.

Keep information current

Outdated hours, old prices, wrong phone numbers lose customers and credibility. Update regularly or remove outdated info.

Local SEO matters enormously

Google Business Profile, local keywords, NAP consistency (name, address, phone). Local search drives real customers.

Fast loading = more customers

Optimize images, minimize scripts. Every second of load time loses visitors and potential customers.

Measure what matters

Track calls, form submissions, direction requests. These are the conversions that actually matter for local business.

Small business websites should be simple, mobile-optimized, and make contact effortless. Focus on what drives real customers.

Small Business SEO: What Actually Matters

What Works for Local SEO

  • ✓ Google Business Profile: Free, key, shows in Maps results
