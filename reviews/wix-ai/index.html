The "This Looks Like Every Other Wix Site" Moment

When I realized the tradeoff

It was Day 3 of testing Wix. I'd generated 7 different sites across different niches:

  • Yoga studio in Portland
  • SaaS startup in Austin
  • Restaurant in Chicago
  • Portfolio for a designer
  • E-commerce selling coffee beans
  • Dentist office in Miami
  • Personal blog about cooking

I opened all 7 tabs side by side.

And I froze.

They all looked like they were made by the same person.

Same hero section layout. Same button shapes. Same typography choices. Same "Wix vibe."

This isn't necessarily bad. Wix templates are professionally designed. They look good.

But they don't look like you. They look like Wix.

I showed my designer friend. She scrolled through the tabs and said:
"Yeah, those are all Wix sites. You can tell."

That's the tradeoff: Wix makes it easy to build a decent-looking site. But that site will look like a Wix site.

For some people, this doesn't matter. A yoga studio owner doesn't care if their site looks like Wix—they just want it to work.

But if brand identity matters? If you want to stand out? This is why I'd point you toward Framer or Webflow.

Wix ADI: The AI Experience

Wix ADI (Artificial Design Intelligence) is Wix's answer to AI website building. You answer 6 questions about your business, preferences, and goals. Then Wix generates a complete website in under 2 minutes.

The good: It's incredibly fast. The questions are straightforward. You get a functional site quickly.

The bad: The designs feel generic. I tested ADI 5 times with different inputs and got sites that looked 80% similar. The AI optimizes for "safe" designs, not unique ones.

Vendor Lock-In: The Real Problem

Here's what nobody tells you about Wix: you cannot export your code.

I spent 52 hours building sites, only to realize if I ever want to leave Wix, I have to start from scratch. There's no HTML export. There's no WordPress migration. Your site lives on Wix servers, in Wix's proprietary format, forever.

Compare this to Framer (React export on Pro plan) or 10Web (WordPress-based). With Wix, you're renting your website, not owning it.

PageSpeed Performance

I ran Google PageSpeed tests on 5 Wix sites I created. The results were consistently mediocre:

  • Mobile: 60-75/100 (Needs improvement)
  • Desktop: 75-85/100 (Good but not great)
  • Main issue: Unused JavaScript and enormous DOM sizes

For comparison, Framer sites score 90-98/100. Durable sites score 85-95/100. Wix's feature-rich editor comes at a performance cost.

WIX AI REALITY: TEMPLATE MATCHING, NOT GENERATION

🤖 Brutal truth: Wix ADI = NOT true AI generation. Just template matching based on industry + preferences. Same templates you'd pick manually, just "AI" picks for you. No advantage over template selection.

Wix AI problems: Generic results (template recognition immediately visible), limited customization (constrained by chosen template), template lock-in (hard to change design later), not true AI (just smart template matching).

🎭 Wix AI ADI Analysis: What It Actually Does

Wix ADI (Artificial Design Intelligence) Breakdown:

What Wix AI Does:

  • • Questions: Asks industry, business type, color preferences, features needed
  • • Template selection: Matches answers to Wix template library (smart matching, not generation)
  • • Auto-population: Fills template with placeholder text, stock images
  • • Customization: Allows edits after generation (but constrained by template structure)
  • • Speed: Site ready in 5-10 minutes (fast start)

What Wix AI Doesn't Do:

  • • True AI generation: Doesn't create custom design, just selects existing template
  • • Unique design: Result = template thousands of others use
  • • Content writing: Generic placeholder text only (not tailored to business)
  • • Smart recommendations: Based on simple rules, not machine learning
  • • Brand differentiation: Can't create unique look (template constraints)

Key insight: Wix AI = template recommendation engine. Same as browsing Wix templates yourself, just "AI" picks based on your answers. No design generation, no unique content, no competitive advantage.

⚠️ Wix AI Problems: Why It Fails

Critical Issues with Wix AI:

  • 1. Template Recognition: Wix used by millions. AI-picked templates = same as everyone else. Clients recognize Wix sites immediately = unprofessional impression.
  • 2. Generic Results: No customization beyond template constraints. Can't create unique brand identity. Looks "template-y" by definition.
  • 3. Template Lock-in: Hard to change design after AI generation. Would require rebuilding from new template = wasted time.
  • 4. Better Alternative: Picking Wix template directly = more control, better results. You see template before committing, can browse options freely.
  • 5. False Promise: "AI generation" = misleading. Just template matching. Users expect custom design, get template selection.

Recommendation: Skip Wix AI entirely. Browse Wix templates directly. Pick design matching your vision. Start with better foundation, more flexibility, same effort (5-10 min to pick template vs 5-10 min to answer AI questions).

WIX PLATFORM: STRENGTHS (NOT AI) VS WEAKNESSES

✅ Wix Platform Strengths (Use Wix FOR These Reasons)

❌ Wix Platform Weaknesses (Consider Alternatives)
