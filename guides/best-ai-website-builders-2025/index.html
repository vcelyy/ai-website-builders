Which AI Builder Should You Use?

Choose FRAMER if...

  • • You care about design quality above all else
  • • You're building a portfolio or creative site
  • • You want smooth animations and micro-interactions
  • • You're okay paying $15-25/month for premium results

Choose 10WEB if...

  • • You need real e-commerce (WooCommerce)
  • • You want WordPress flexibility with AI generation
  • • You care about owning your data (full export)
  • • You're okay with a steeper learning curve

Choose DURABLE if...

  • • You need a simple site live TODAY
  • • You're a local business (plumber, bakery, etc.)
  • • You don't care about fancy design
  • • You want the cheapest option ($12/month)

Choose RELUME if...

  • • You're an agency building sitemaps for clients
  • • You use Figma and want seamless workflow
  • • You plan to build in Webflow or Framer
  • • You want incredible wireframes in minutes

Unexpected Findings After 127 Hours

Things that surprised me

!

AI-generated content sounds robotic in EVERY tool

Not just one builder—all of them. The text AI generates is generic, repetitive, and obviously AI-written. Expect to rewrite 80% of what it produces.

!

PageSpeed scores vary wildly (and matter more than you think)

Framer hits 96-98/100. Some builders struggle to break 60. I tested a Durable site that loaded in 8 seconds on mobile. Performance directly affects your Google rankings.

!

"Free forever" plans are basically marketing bait

Most free tiers limit you to 3 pages, remove your branding, or hit you with upgrade prompts after 2 days. There's no such thing as a free lunch.

!

E-commerce is an afterthought for most builders

Only 10Web (with WooCommerce) and Shopify AI have functional online stores. Everyone else just adds a "buy button" that links to Gumroad or Stripe. If you need real e-commerce, most AI builders can't help.

!

Export is almost never production-ready code

Even tools that promise "code export" generate bloated HTML with inline styles, unused classes, and broken responsiveness. You'll need a developer to clean it up.

!

The fastest builder isn't always the best choice

Durable generates sites in 30 seconds. But customization is so limited that you'll hit a wall in an hour. Speed ≠ quality. Sometimes slower is better.

The truth about AI website builders

None of these tools are perfect. All have:

  • Vendor lock-in: You can't easily leave (except 10Web/WordPress)
  • Limited customization: You're working within their templates
  • Recurring costs: You pay monthly forever or lose your site
  • AI limitations: The AI gets you 80% there, you do the last 20%

But they're also incredibly useful if you match the right tool to the right use case. A designer using Framer builds a stunning portfolio in hours, not weeks. A plumber using Durable has a site live in under a minute. An e-commerce brand using 10Web has a functional store without hiring developers.

The real question: What are you actually building? Answer that first, then choose the tool that matches. Don't start with the tool and figure out what to build with it.
