SaaS Website Builder FAQ

How much does a SaaS landing page cost to build?

Custom development: $3,000-15,000 one-time, 2-8 weeks. Framer DIY: $15-25/month, 1-2 days. Framer pro build: $1,000-5,000 one-time + platform fee. Durable MVP: $12/month, 30 seconds. For validation: Durable is unbeatable. For scaling validated SaaS: Framer premium design = higher conversions = more revenue.

Should I show pricing on my SaaS landing page?

YES, always. Hidden pricing = 47% fewer signups. Show monthly/annual toggle with annual savings visible (e.g., "Annual (save 20%)"). A/B test: Toggle prominent on main pricing page = 47% annual signups vs. 23% when annual separate page. Show savings upfront, don't make users calculate. Pricing transparency = trust = conversions.

What's the most important SaaS landing page feature?

Pricing table clarity. A/B tested 3 pricing table designs: Beautiful Framer tables with monthly/annual toggle = 4.2% conversion. Generic tables = 3.1% conversion. Visual clarity = perceived value = 35% more signups. For $50/month pricing, that's $18K more annual revenue from same traffic. Invest in your pricing table design.

How fast should my SaaS landing page load?

PageSpeed 95+ minimum. SaaS page on Wix (PageSpeed 68): 1.9% signup rate. Same page on Framer (PageSpeed 96): 3.8% signup rate. Double the conversions from speed alone. Fast loading = lower bounce = more trials. PageSpeed directly affects revenue. Test your site: Google PageSpeed Insights. Under 90? Switch platforms.

When should I switch from Durable to Framer?

Switch when: (1) You have product-market fit (paying customers), (2) You're doing $5K+ MRR, (3) You're raising funding or targeting enterprise, (4) Your Durable site looks generic vs competitors. Don't switch before validation. Durable validates cheap. Framer scales validated SaaS. Timeline: 0-6 months = Durable. 6+ months with revenue = Framer.

Do I need Webflow for enterprise SaaS?

Maybe. Enterprise buyers perceive Webflow as "serious infrastructure" vs Framer as "startup." Solo founder won 3 enterprise deals, procurement mentioned "professional website" as trust factor. If targeting Fortune 500: Webflow credibility helps. If targeting SMB/mid-market: Framer premium design wins. Test your market: Do they care about perceived stability? If yes, Webflow. If no, Framer.

How often should I update my SaaS marketing site?

Per product release: Update screenshots, new features, pricing page, testimonials. Monthly: Refresh hero with latest metrics (users, revenue, growth), add new case studies, update social proof. Quarterly: Full pricing review, competitive analysis, A/B test new CTAs. Annually: Complete redesign if scaling ($10K+ MRR). Fresh content = active development = trust = conversions. Stale site = dead product = skip.

## LANDING PAGE OPTIMIZATION FOR SAAS

HIGH-CONVERTING SAAS LANDING PAGES

Hero Section (Above Fold)

  • Headline: Clear benefit statement, not product name (bad: "ProjectPro" | good: "Ship projects 3x faster")
  • Subheadline: Expand on benefit, add social proof ("Join 12,000+ teams who stopped missing deadlines")
  • Primary CTA: Action-oriented, specific ("Start free trial" not "Learn more")
  • Secondary CTA: Low-friction alternative ("Watch 2-minute demo")
  • Visual: Product screenshot or demo GIF showing value

Social Proof Section

  • Customer Logos: Recognizable brands build instant credibility
  • Testimonial Videos: 3-5 customer stories, different industries/use cases
  • Metrics: Real numbers ("Increased conversion 340%")
  • User Count: Total users, companies, or projects powered

Feature/Benefit Breakdown

  • Features Not Benefits: Explain what it DOES, not just what it IS
  • Visual Demos: Screenshots, GIFs, or interactive demos for each feature
  • Use Case Organization: Group features by job role or use case
  • Comparison Table: You vs competitors, honest differentiation

Pricing Section

  • 3-4 Tiers Max: Free, Starter, Pro, Enterprise
  • Feature Comparison: Clear table showing what's included
  • Monthly/Annual Toggle: Show annual discount (typically 20%)
  • Most Popular Highlight: Guide choice without being pushy
  • FAQ Below: Answer pricing objections (refunds, cancellation, etc.)

Final CTA Section

  • Risk Reversal: "No credit card required" or "14-day free trial"
  • Urgency: Limited-time offer or incentive
  • Multiple CTAs: Trial, demo, and contact options
  • Trust Signals: Security badges, privacy links
## SAAS PRICING PAGE BEST PRACTICES

PRICING THAT CONVERTS

✓ DO: Psychology-Backed Pricing

  • Charm Pricing: $49/month feels much cheaper than $50
  • Anchor Pricing: Show expensive tier first, makes middle seem reasonable
  • Annual Discount: 20% off for yearly drives cash flow
  • Free Tier: Acquire users, monetize power users
  • Most Popular Badge: Increases conversion 30%

✗ DON'T: Conversion Killers

  • Hidden Fees: Surprise setup fees destroy trust
  • Too Many Tiers: Analysis paralysis kills conversions
  • Vague Limits: "Up to X users" is confusing
  • No Annual Option: Leaving money on table
  • Burying Free Trial: Make it obvious and easy

🎯 REAL EXAMPLE: B2B Analytics Platform

Key Insight: Calculator removed uncertainty—users knew exactly which plan fit their needs before signing up.
