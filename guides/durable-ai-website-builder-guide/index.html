Frequently Asked Questions

How does Durable AI website builder work?

Durable generates complete business websites in under 30 seconds. Enter your business name, describe what you do, answer questions about services and location. AI creates complete website with homepage, services, about, contact. Unique features: CRM tools, invoice management.

Is Durable really free?

30-day free trial with full access. After trial, paid plans start at $15/month including custom domain, no branding, CRM features, invoice tools. Longer trial than most competitors (14 days typical).

What makes Durable different from Mixo?

Both offer ultra-fast generation (under 60 seconds). Difference is focus: Durable for service businesses with CRM tools, invoicing, business templates. Mixo is general-purpose with broader templates and free tier.

Can I use Durable for ecommerce?

Durable has basic checkout but isn't a full ecommerce platform. No shopping cart, inventory, product catalog. For >5 products, use Shopify or 10Web with WooCommerce. Durable is ideal for service-based businesses, not product stores.

How long does it take to build a site?

AI generation: 30 seconds. Real customization: 2-4 hours. Marketing's 'live in 30 seconds' is misleading—real businesses need time for custom content, photos, branding. Still faster than traditional builders (4-8 hours), but not instant.

What are Durable's CRM features?

Contact form submissions, lead pipeline, basic customer tracking, email reminders. Sufficient for solo operators and small service businesses. Compared to HubSpot/Pipedrive: basic contact list with labels, not automation. Good for starting, insufficient for scaling.

Can I export my Durable site?

No code export on any plan. Leave Durable, lose everything, rebuild from scratch. Intentional vendor lock-in—common among AI builders. If you care about owning code, choose Framer or CodeDesign.ai. Trade-off: convenience vs ownership.

DURABLE'S LIMITATIONS
Brutal Honesty

🎨 Design Repetition

All Durable sites look similar. Same layouts, same structure, just different content and colors.

You can change content, images, and basic styling. But the underlying structure is fixed. Your plumber site will look like 80% of other plumber sites on Durable.

Reality: If you need a unique design, Durable isn't the right choice.

🚫 No Code Export

Your site lives on Durable's servers forever. No HTML/CSS export, ever.

Migration means rebuilding from scratch on another platform. You're locked in to Durable's hosting and ecosystem.

Reality: This is proprietary lock-in. Plan to stay on Durable long-term or migrate early.

📱 Limited Mobile Control

Mobile layout is auto-generated from desktop. You can't customize it independently.

If something looks wrong on mobile, your only fix is changing the desktop layout and hoping it translates better. No mobile-specific editing.

Reality: Test mobile preview early. If it doesn't work, you're limited in fixes.

🛒 E-commerce Limitations

Durable has basic e-commerce at best. Product catalog, checkout, that's about it.

No advanced inventory, no multi-channel, no dropshipping, no subscriptions. If online sales are core to your business, choose Shopify or Wix instead.

Reality: Durable for e-commerce is like using a sedan to haul lumber. It works, but it's not built for it.

🔧 Template Constraints

Durable has ~15 base templates. AI customizes them, but you're still working within those constraints.

Can't add new sections easily. Can't change layout structure. Can't add custom functionality. What you generate is what you get, plus minor tweaks.

Reality: If the AI doesn't generate a section you need, you might be out of luck.

📈 Scaling Challenges

Durable is great for MVP and simple sites. It doesn't scale well with business growth.

As you add more pages, features, and complexity, Durable's limitations become obvious. No advanced marketing tools, no integrations, no automation.

Reality: Plan to migrate to a more powerful platform when you hit ~$10K/month revenue.
