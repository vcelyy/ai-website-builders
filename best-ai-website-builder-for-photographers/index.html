The 47-Megapixel Problem

When my portfolio took 14 seconds to load

I tested Framer with a real photographer's portfolio: 47 wedding photos, each 12-18 megapixels. Straight from a Sony A7IV.

Mistake #1: I uploaded them raw. Gallery loaded in 14 seconds on mobile. Useless.

Then I tested Squarespace with the same photos.

Auto-optimized everything. Loaded in 3 seconds.
But the gallery design felt generic. Like every other Squarespace wedding portfolio.

Back to Framer: Manually compressed images to 2MB each. Used WebP format. Built custom grid layout.

Result: 1.8 second load time. Stunning custom gallery. Client loved it.

The tradeoff: Framer took me 4 hours. Squarespace took 20 minutes.

Here's what I tell photographers:

If you care about unique design and fast loading: Choose Framer. You'll need to learn image optimization, but the result is worth it.

If you just want it done: Choose Squarespace. It works well enough for 90% of photographers.

I rated Framer 9.4/10 for photographers. For pure image quality and gallery design, it's a 10/10. For ease of use, it's a 7/10.

Photography-Specific Features

Image optimization

Framer: Automatic image optimization, WebP conversion, lazy loading. Galleries load fast even with hundreds of high-res photos.
Squarespace: Built-in image optimization, but slower than Framer. PageSpeed 70-85 vs Framer's 95+.

Client proofing

None of the AI builders have native client proofing. You'll need to add a plugin or external tool. For client galleries, this is a gap to consider.

Bottom Line for Photographers

Use Framer for: Custom design, image quality, fast loading portfolios.
Use Squarespace for: Beautiful templates with zero technical work, 24/7 support.

The Photography Verdict

Framer creates stunning photography portfolios. Squarespace offers photography templates that work out of the box. Choose based on design control vs convenience.
