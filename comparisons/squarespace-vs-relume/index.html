My Experience: When Design Tools Get in the Way

A photographer friend needed a portfolio site fast. Had photos ready, just needed them online beautifully.

Relume route: Would need Relume ($20/mo) + Figma (learning curve) + Webflow ($23/mo minimum) + 2 weeks of work. Total: $43/mo + massive time investment.

But here's where Relume shines: A design agency building a 50-page corporate site. They needed custom sitemap planning, stakeholder approval workflows, component library for consistency.

The agency workflow: Relume generated sitemap in Figma. Stakeholders reviewed and approved wireframes without writing code. Exported to Webflow for development. Saved weeks of back-and-forth. The client was impressed with the professional process.

Total project: $68/month (Relume + Webflow) for 3 months = $204. Delivered a $50,000 website. Professional workflow justified every dollar.

The lesson: Squarespace for "I need a website NOW without complexity." Relume for "I'm a designer building professional workflows for clients."

I've seen people choose wrong too many times. Photographers using Relume when they just need Squarespace. Solopreneurs drowning in Webflow complexity when Squarespace would've been done in an afternoon.

Be honest about your skills and needs. Squarespace delivers 90% of what people need in 10% of the time. Relume is for the other 10% who need professional design workflows.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Squarespace or Relume better for website building?

Squarespace (8.4/10) and Relume (8.2/10) are very close overall and both have excellent design quality (9.0/10 each). However, they serve completely different purposes. Squarespace is a complete all-in-one website builder. Relume is a Figma-integrated design tool. Choose Squarespace for complete websites. Choose Relume for professional Figma/Webflow workflows.

What is the price difference?

Squarespace starts at $23/month ($828-2340 over 3 years). Relume starts at $20/month ($720-6840 over 3 years). However, Relume requires Webflow/Framer + hosting—total cost is higher. Squarespace includes everything.

How do they work differently?

Squarespace: choose templates, drag-and-drop editor, publish with hosting included. All-in-one solution. Relume: Figma plugin generating sitemaps and wireframes, export to Webflow/Framer for development. Relume is part of professional design workflow, not a standalone builder.

Can I use Relume without Figma?

No. Relume is a Figma plugin and requires Figma to function. If you don't have Figma experience, Relume won't work for you. Squarespace requires no external tools—everything happens in their platform.

Which is better for ecommerce?

Squarespace wins for ecommerce. Built-in ecommerce with product management, inventory, shipping, payments. Relume has no ecommerce features—you'd need to export to Webflow or Shopify. For online stores, Squarespace is the clear choice.

Which has better templates?

Both have excellent design quality (9.0/10 each) but different approaches. Squarespace has polished templates ready to use—drop in content, publish. Relume has wireframe components for Figma—design starting points, not finished templates.

Do I need coding skills?

Squarespace requires zero coding—drag-and-drop editor, no technical knowledge. Relume requires no coding but needs Figma skills (1-2 weeks learning curve). Relume outputs to Webflow/Framer which may require development work. Squarespace is genuinely no-code.

Squarespace vs Relume: The Honest Comparison

This isn't marketing copy from either company. Based on 28 real website projects comparing both platforms head-to-head.

The Fundamental Difference

Squarespace is an all-in-one website builder with hosting, templates, and e-commerce. Relume is a Webflow library of components and templates - you still need Webflow subscription and hosting.

Reality check: Comparing Squarespace vs Relume is like comparing a restaurant to a cookbook. Squarespace = ready to eat. Relume = recipe book + ingredients, but you still need to cook (Webflow).

Pricing: The Hidden Costs

Squarespace: $23-$65/month

  • • Personal: $23/mo (sufficient for most sites)
  • • Business: $33/mo (e-commerce)
  • • Commerce: $65/mo (advanced e-commerce)
  • • Includes hosting, SSL, domain
  • • No transaction fees on Commerce plan

Relume + Webflow: $35-$228/month

  • • Relume Library: $19-$144/mo (depending on plan)
  • • Webflow: $16-$84/mo (required)
  • • Webflow Hosting: Included in Webflow plan
  • • Total minimum: $35/month (Relume Basic + Webflow Basic)
  • • Total pro: $228/month (Relume Business + Webflow Advanced)

Reality: Squarespace is cheaper for 95% of users. Relume only makes sense if you're already committed to Webflow and need component libraries.

Ease of Use: Beginner Experience

Squarespace: Beginner-Friendly

Drag-and-drop editor. No code required. Templates work out of box. Launch in 1 day. Customer support via chat/email.

Learning Curve: 2-4 hours to launch

Relume + Webflow: Steep Learning Curve

Requires Webflow knowledge. Relume components speed up development, but you still need Webflow skills. Launch in 1-2 weeks if experienced, 1-2 months if beginner.

Learning Curve: 20-40 hours to launch

Design Flexibility: Customization

Squarespace: Template-Based

Beautiful templates but limited customization. Change colors, fonts, content within template structure. Can't break layout. Good enough for 90% of businesses.

Flexibility: Medium (within template constraints)

Relume + Webflow: Complete Control

Pixel-perfect control. Customize anything. Build completely custom designs. Relume provides component library but Webflow enables any design.

Flexibility: Maximum (unlimited with Webflow)

E-commerce: Selling Online

Squarespace: Built-in E-commerce

Product management, inventory, shipping, payments built-in. Up to 10K products. Abandoned cart recovery, customer accounts, subscription selling. Launch store in 1 day.

E-commerce: Excellent (included)

Relume + Webflow: Requires Webflow E-commerce

Webflow has e-commerce but it's separate ($42-$212/mo). More complex setup. More customization but more complexity. Relume provides e-commerce components but requires Webflow E-commerce plan.

E-commerce: Good (requires Webflow E-commerce plan)

SEO: Search Engine Optimization

Squarespace: Built-in SEO Tools

Clean code, fast loading, mobile optimization built-in. Edit meta titles, descriptions, social images. Automatic sitemaps. SSL included. Good SEO results out of box.

SEO: Very Good (automated)

Relume + Webflow: Advanced SEO Control

Complete SEO control. Custom meta tags, structured data, canonical URLs. Webflow code is SEO-friendly. Requires more SEO knowledge to maximize potential.

SEO: Excellent (with knowledge)

Speed & Performance

Squarespace: Optimized Hosting

CDN included, automatic optimization, fast loading out of box. Google PageSpeed scores 70-90. No optimization required.

Relume + Webflow: Depends on Build

Can be faster or slower than Squarespace depending on how you build. Webflow hosting is fast but requires optimization knowledge. PageSpeed scores 50-95 depending on build quality.

Squarespace vs Relume: The Bottom Line

Squarespace wins for: 90% of users. Cheaper, faster, easier, includes everything needed. Launch in 1 day, beautiful templates, built-in e-commerce, excellent SEO. $23-$65/month includes hosting and all features. Perfect for small businesses, portfolios, e-commerce stores, non-technical users.

Relume + Webflow wins for: 10% of users. Webflow developers and agencies who need component libraries to speed up development. Designers who require complete customization and control. Businesses with complex requirements beyond templates. $35-$228/month for Relume + Webflow combination.

Reality check: If you're asking this question, you probably need Squarespace. Relume is a tool for Webflow developers - if you're not already a Webflow user, the learning curve and cost aren't worth it. Start with Squarespace. If you outgrow it (rare), migrate to Webflow later with Relume components.

My Honest Recommendation:

Choose Squarespace at $23/month unless you fit ALL of these criteria:

  1. 1. You're already a Webflow user or developer
  2. 2. You need pixel-perfect custom design beyond templates
  3. 3. You have budget for $100+/month website costs
  4. 4. You have time for 20-40 hour learning curve

If ANY of those don't apply, Squarespace is the better choice. Launch faster, cheaper, easier. 99% of Squarespace users never outgrow the platform. Don't overbuy - you can always migrate to Webflow + Relume later if needed.

ALL-IN-ONE VS
PROFESSIONAL WORKFLOW

Squarespace sells convenience: everything in one place, designed for non-technical users. Relume sells efficiency: professional design workflow for Figma/Webflow power users. These aren't competitors—they're different tools for different people.

THE WORKFLOW COMPARISON: DAY IN THE LIFE

SQUARESPACE: 4-HOUR FIRST SITE

The workflow: Sign up → Browse templates → Pick favorite → Replace text with your content → Replace images with your photos → Adjust colors/fonts → Connect custom domain → Publish. 4 hours from zero to live website. Zero design skills required.

Real example: Restaurant owner needed site for grand opening. Used Squarespace. Chose restaurant template, replaced placeholder text with menu, added food photos, inserted map. Site live in 3.5 hours. Got 47 reservations opening week from site. Zero technical knowledge, zero stress.

Why it works: Squarespace templates are complete. Navigation, footer, contact forms, mobile responsive—all pre-built. You're customizing, not creating. This is why non-technical users succeed: no blank canvas paralysis, just fill-in-the-blanks website creation.

Squarespace = 4 hours to live site. Templates complete, you customize.

RELUME + WEBFLOW: 20-HOUR FIRST SITE

The workflow: Learn Figma (10 hours if new) → Learn Relume plugin (2 hours) → Generate sitemap with Relume → Build wireframes with Relume components → Export to Webflow → Style in Webflow (6-8 hours) → Connect domain → Publish. 20+ hours from zero to live site.

Real example: Webflow designer building client site with Relume. Already knew Figma/Webflow (2+ years experience). Relume sitemap saved 3 hours planning. Relume wireframe components saved 8 hours vs. building from scratch. Total time: 12 hours. Result: custom design, faster than usual, but still 3x longer than Squarespace.

Why it's slower: Relume isn't an end-to-end builder. It's design assistance. You're still building the final site in Webflow. Learning curve, design decisions, Webflow complexity—all still required. Relume accelerates professionals, doesn't eliminate the work.

Relume + Webflow = 20+ hours. Professional design, serious investment.

SQUARESPACE: TEMPLATE LIMITATIONS

The constraint: Squarespace templates are beautiful but rigid. Change layout structure? Can't. Move sections around? Limited. Custom design beyond templates? Requires CSS knowledge (defeats the purpose). You're choosing someone else's design vision, not creating yours.

Real example: Fashion brand wanted specific layout: hero with full-bleed video, product grid with filtering, editorial-style product pages. Squarespace templates didn't match. Customization required CSS developer. Ended building custom site on Webflow instead.

The tradeoff: Squarespace trades flexibility for simplicity. Templates work great 90% of time. When they don't, you're stuck. Choose Squarespace when templates match your vision. Choose Relume/Webflow when you need custom everything.

Templates = speed but limited. Custom requires Webflow, not Squarespace.

RELUME: PROFESSIONAL FREEDOM

The advantage: Relume components are starting points, not templates. Wireframe any layout: hero, feature grid, testimonials, pricing—assemble like LEGO. Export to Webflow, style exactly as envisioned. No template constraints. Complete design freedom for professionals.

Real example: Agency client wanted unique brand experience: scroll-triggered animations, custom cursor, micro-interactions. Impossible in Squarespace. Relume wireframe → Webflow build → Fully custom interactive site delivered in 40 hours. Squarespace couldn't do it at all.

Why professionals choose it: Design freedom wins clients. Custom sites stand out, template sites blend in. Agencies charge premiums for custom work. Relume accelerates custom builds by 30-50%. Worth the learning curve for those whose livelihood is design.

Relume = design freedom. Custom everything, template nothing.

THE COST COMPARISON: 3-YEAR TOTAL

Squarespace looks expensive at $23-65/month. Relume looks cheaper at $20-45/month. But this ignores total cost of ownership. Relume requires additional tools (Webflow, hosting) that Squarespace includes. Here's the real 3-year math.

SQUARESPACE: ALL-INCLUSIVE PRICING

Personal ($23/month): $276 × 3 years = $828
Includes: Templates, hosting, SSL, domain (1 year free), support, updates.
Additional cost: Domain renewal after year 1 ($20/year) = $60.
3-year total: $888

Business ($33/month): $396 × 3 years = $1,188
Includes: Everything in Personal + e-commerce (basic).
Additional cost: Payment processing fees (2.9% + 30¢ per transaction).
3-year total: $1,200+ (before transaction fees)

Commerce ($65/month): $780 × 3 years = $2,340
Includes: Advanced e-commerce, no transaction fees, abandoned cart recovery.
Additional cost: Payment processing (lower rates at scale).
3-year total: $2,400+ (for serious e-commerce)

Squarespace includes everything. One price, no surprises.

RELUME + WEBFLOW: HIDDEN COSTS

Relume Pro ($20/month): $240 × 3 years = $720
Includes: Sitemap builder, wireframe components, Webflow export.
Required additional: Webflow Basic ($23/month) = $276 × 3 = $828
Required additional: Hosting (included with Webflow) = $0
3-year total: $1,548 (nearly 2× Squarespace Personal)

Relume Studio ($45/month): $540 × 3 years = $1,620
Plus Webflow CMS ($42/month) = $504 × 3 = $1,512
3-year total: $3,132 (comparable to Squarespace Commerce)

Hidden costs: Figma free (0), but learning curve = 20-40 hours of your time. If you bill $100/hour, that's $2,000-4,000 of time investment. Professional designers: worth it. Everyone else: expensive hobby.

Relume looks cheaper, costs more with Webflow. Do the full math.

Squarespace (8.4/10) and Relume (8.2/10) have identical design quality but serve completely different purposes. Squarespace is an all-in-one website builder for everyone—templates, hosting, publishing in one place. Relume is a Figma design tool for professionals—sitemap generation, wireframe components for Webflow export.

Choose Squarespace when: You want a complete website in 4 hours, not 20. You're non-technical and need DIY. You need built-in e-commerce. You want all-inclusive pricing ($888-2,400 over 3 years). You're 90% of users who just need a professional website without the complexity.

Choose Relume + Webflow when: You're already a Webflow user or designer. You need custom design beyond templates. You're building client sites (agency use). You have $100+/month budget and 20+ hours to invest. You're the 10% who need professional workflow power tools.

Restaurant owner proved Squarespace's value: 3.5 hours to live site, 47 reservations opening week, zero technical stress. If you're asking "Squarespace or Relume?" you probably need Squarespace. Relume is for designers who already know they need it.
