FAQ: Hostinger vs Durable

Is Durable worth 5x the price of Hostinger?

If you're running a service business: absolutely. Durable's CRM, automation, and invoicing can generate $6,000+ monthly in additional revenue. The $144/year difference is irrelevant. If you're a hobby blogger: no, you'll never use those features. Hostinger at $36/year is perfect.

Can I switch from Hostinger to Durable later?

Yes, but it's painful. You'll rebuild your site in Durable's AI, migrate content manually, lose any SEO progress during transition. Migration costs $500-2,000 if you hire help. If you know you'll need business features, start with Durable.

Does Hostinger's AI actually work?

Yes, it works. Generates functional sites in 90 seconds. But results are generic and dated. You'll get online fast, but your site will look like thousands of others. Fine for hobby sites, problematic for businesses that need to stand out.

What's Durable's 30-day trial actually like?

Full access to all features. Build your site, test CRM, set up email sequences, create invoices. Only pay when you're ready to publish or after 30 days. Hostinger has no trial - you pay upfront before testing anything.

Do I really need CRM as a solo consultant?

You will. Month 1: you'll remember everyone. Month 3: you'll have spreadsheets everywhere. Month 6: you'll miss follow-ups, lose lead details. CRM becomes key. Durable includes it from day one. Start with it, don't add it later.

Which is better for SEO?

Both are fine for basic SEO. Durable's better content creation (AI writing assistant) helps with blog posts and pages. Hostinger's dated designs can hurt credibility (users perceive amateur sites as less trustworthy). Neither is an SEO powerhouse - both are adequate.
