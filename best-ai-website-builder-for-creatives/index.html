Design Skills ≠ Website Skills

Why talented creatives struggle with portfolios

Here's what I've noticed after reviewing hundreds of creative portfolios:

Graphic designers often have the worst portfolios. They tweak endlessly. Never launch.

Illustrators and artists tend to have the best. They focus on artwork, forget the "design" part.

The perfectionism trap:

Creatives judge their portfolio against the best work they see on Dribbble or Behance.

Then they feel inadequate and never launch.

Meanwhile, clients don't care about those details. They just want to see your work.

My recommendation for creatives:

Squarespace for artists/photographers: The templates are better than what you'll build yourself unless you're a web designer.

Framer for designers: If you're going to obsess over design, at least use a tool built for it.

Webflow for when your portfolio IS your portfolio: If you're a UI/UX designer, your website should demonstrate your skills.

The brutal truth:

A "good enough" portfolio that exists is infinitely better than a "perfect" portfolio that doesn't.

Launch in a day using Durable or Squarespace. Iterate later.

Clients hire you for your creative work, not your website code.
