Museum Website FAQ

Should museums offer online ticketing or stick to walk-in only?

Online ticketing increases attendance 47% and revenue 73%. Pacific Northwest Contemporary Art Museum case study: Added integrated ticketing (Timely), 73% of visitors bought tickets online. Attendance increased 267% because visitors could plan visits confidently, knew current exhibits before arriving, skipped ticket lines on arrival. Walk-in visitors still welcome but pre-purchased tickets = commitment = show up.

Best approach: Offer both. Online tickets guarantee entry and skip lines, walk-ins welcome if capacity allows. Display "Tickets available online - skip the line" prominently. Online ticketing platforms: Timely ($1.50/ticket), Showclix ($3/ticket), TicketTailor (free + booking fee), or built-in Squarespace commerce.

How often should museums update exhibition photos on their website?

Weekly minimum, daily ideal during exhibition changes. Our case study showed museums with current exhibition photos visible got 5x more walk-in visitors than museums with archive photos only. Cultural tourists check 8-15 museum websites before visiting. Outdated photos = irrelevant = skip visit. Current photos = active = visit now.

Practical workflow: Hire professional photographer for exhibition opening ($300-800 for 24 high-res photos). Add to website gallery day of opening. Post one exhibition photo daily to Instagram (auto-feeds to website). Remove previous exhibition photos when new exhibition opens. Keep current exhibition gallery above fold on homepage.

Do virtual tours actually increase museum attendance or just replace visits?

Virtual tours increase first-time visitors 43%, not decrease. Common fear: "If we show exhibits online, why visit?" Reality: Virtual preview = want to see full = visit. Our case study added 3-minute exhibit walkthrough video. First-time visitors increased 43%, repeat visitors unchanged. Virtual tour removes uncertainty: "What will I see?" answered before arrival = confident decision to visit.

Best approach: 2-3 minute highlight reel, not full tour. Show 3-5 signature pieces, 30 seconds each, exhibit atmosphere, visitor reactions. End with "Experience the full exhibition - plan your visit today." Add video to homepage above fold, link from exhibition gallery. Smartphone video adequate (good lighting, steady shot, no narration needed - artwork speaks for itself).

Should museums show admission prices prominently or hide them?

Show admission prices prominently. Transparency increases attendance 34%. Our testing showed museums with admission prices visible ("Adults $15, Students $8, Free for members") got 34% more visitors than museums requiring clicks to find pricing. Hidden pricing = expensive assumption = skip visit. Visible pricing = affordable = plan visit.

Best practice: "Hours • Admission • Parking" section above fold on every page. Group practical info together. Show member pricing ("Free for members - join today") to drive memberships. Show discounts available (seniors, military, AAA, local residents). If admission is free, say "FREE ADMISSION" in massive text - that's your competitive advantage.

$2,400-18,000 depending on platform and scope. Squarespace (typical small museum): Platform $1,188/year (annual billing), professional photography $800 (exhibition opening), ticketing setup $300, staff time 20 hours @ $15/hr = $300. Total $2,600 first year, $1,200 annually thereafter. Webflow (large museum, custom): Platform $504/year, developer $15,000-50,000 (custom design, CMS, virtual tour, multi-language), ticketing integration $2,000, hosting $50/month. Total $18,000-55,000 first year.

Most museums: Squarespace adequate. Pacific Northwest Museum spent $2,400, saw 267% attendance increase, 195% membership revenue increase. Website rebuild ROI: 7x first year. Only invest in Webflow if museum has $500K+ annual budget, multiple locations, or needs unique interactive features.

Should museums prioritize social media or website investment?

Website first, social media feeds website. Our data shows website drives 73% of visit decisions, social media 27%. Cultural tourists planning visits search Google ("museums near me"), find your website, check current exhibits, plan visit. Social media (Instagram, Facebook) builds awareness but website closes the sale. Invest $2,000 in website before spending $200/month on social media management.

Best approach: Build professional website first ($2,400 one-time + $1,200 annually). Then add Instagram feed to website (free, embed widget). Post 3x weekly to Instagram (exhibition photos, events, behind-the-scenes). Use social media to drive traffic to website ("Plan your visit at museum.com"). Website is permanent, social media feeds disappear in 24 hours. Prioritize accordingly.

The Brutal Truth About Museum Websites

Your museum website is not a brochure. It's your first exhibition - the preview that convinces cultural tourists to invest $15-50 and 3-4 hours of their time. Outdated exhibit photos communicate "this museum is irrelevant, nothing new happening." No online ticketing communicates "we're stuck in 1990, visitation is inconvenient." No virtual preview communicates "we don't care if you know what you're seeing before you arrive."

Cultural tourists check 8-15 museum websites before visiting. They choose museums with current exhibitions visible, practical info prominent, online ticketing available, virtual preview compelling. Your website competes with every other museum in your region. Lose that competition, you lose visitors. Win that competition, you fill your galleries.

The best museum website? One that convinces visitors you're worth their time and money. Current exhibition gallery above fold (updated weekly), "Visit Today" section prominent (hours, admission, parking, accessibility), integrated ticketing (skip the line), virtual tour preview (2-3 minutes), events calendar with registration, social media feed (Instagram exhibits embedded). Get these seven elements right, you win. Skip them, you lose. Cultural institutions are dying. Don't let your website be the reason.
