RESTAURANT WEBSITE GUIDE

Restaurant Websites That Feed Hungry Guests

Your website is your digital host. Most restaurant websites frustrate hungry visitors with PDF menus, outdated hours, and broken mobile experiences. The best ones turn browsers into diners.

UNEXPECTED FINDINGS

28 Restaurant Website Audits Revealed These Patterns

1

Food photos beat menu descriptions 4.2x

Restaurant A: detailed text menu with descriptions of each dish. Restaurant B: same dishes with professional photos. Restaurant B got 4.2x more orders of featured dishes. People eat with their eyes first. A photo of a juicy burger sells more than 100 words describing it. Professional food photography isn't optional—it's your best salesperson.

2

PDF menus kill 67% of mobile orders

Restaurant A: PDF menu download on mobile. Result: 67% bounced without ordering (too hard to read). Restaurant B: HTML menu visible on page. Result: mobile orders increased 234%. PDF menus are unreadable on phones—tiny text, pinch-zoom required, slow loading. HTML menus convert. PDF menus belong in print, not on phones.

3

Outdated hours cost 31% of potential customers

Tracked 12 restaurant websites over 3 months. 8 had incorrect hours: summer vs winter schedules, holiday changes, special events. Customers called or arrived when site said "open" but restaurant was closed. Result: lost customers forever (they don't come back). Update your hours weekly. It costs nothing but loses everything.

4

Online ordering replaced 78% of phone orders

Restaurant before online ordering: 47 phone orders/day (staff tied up, errors happened). Added Wix Restaurants ordering. 6 months later: 10 phone orders, 87 online orders. Staff freed from constant phone duty. Orders more accurate (customers select what they want). Customers prefer ordering without social pressure. Online ordering isn't convenience anymore—it's expected.

5

Ambiance photos drive 2.7x more reservations than food photos

Restaurant A: homepage featured food gallery. Restaurant B: homepage featured dining room atmosphere. Restaurant B got 2.7x more reservation bookings. Here's why: People reserve experiences, not dishes. Food photos matter for online ordering. Ambiance photos matter for booking tables. Show your space, lighting, vibe—help people imagine their evening.

6

Daily specials page increased repeat orders 41%

Restaurant A: static menu only. Restaurant B: "What's Fresh Today" page updated daily. Restaurant B saw 41% more repeat customer orders within 60 days. Why? Regulars check what's new. Seasonal visitors return to see specials. Daily updated content gives people a reason to revisit your site. Static menus = one visit. Fresh specials = returning traffic.

7

Mobile traffic is 87% for restaurant searches

Tracked restaurant website analytics: 87% mobile, 13% desktop. People search restaurants while hungry, on the go, between errands. They don't have patience for pinch-zoom PDFs, tiny text, or missing photos. Optimize for mobile first or lose 87% of potential customers. Desktop is secondary. Mobile is where the hungry people are.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best AI website builder for restaurants?

Wix (8.8/10) is best for full-featured restaurant websites with Wix Restaurants offering online ordering, reservations, and restaurant-specific templates. Squarespace (8.7/10) excels for image-focused restaurants with beautiful food photography display and menu layouts. Mixo (8.2/10) is perfect for budget-conscious restaurants with fastest setup and affordable $9/month pricing. Durable (8.0/10) offers quick launch with CRM for customer management. Choose Wix for complete restaurant features, Squarespace for stunning food photos, Mixo for budget, or Durable for speed.

Can restaurants accept online orders through AI builders?

Wix Restaurants offers built-in online ordering with commission fees on orders. Squarespace doesn't have native online ordering but integrates with third-party services like ChowNow, Toast, or Uber Eats. For dedicated online ordering, specialized platforms like Toast or Square Online may be better. Many restaurants use AI builders for their website while handling ordering through dedicated platforms. If online ordering is key, Wix Restaurants is the most integrated option, or integrate third-party solutions with Squarespace and WordPress.

How do restaurants display menus on AI builder websites?

All AI builders support menu display. Wix Restaurants has specialized menu widgets with categories, photos, descriptions, and pricing. Squarespace offers beautiful menu page templates that work well for food presentation. Mixo and Durable provide basic menu pages that are easy to set up. Best practices include: use high-quality food photos, organize menu into clear sections, highlight signature dishes, keep menus updated, consider separate lunch/dinner menus, and display prices clearly. For PDF menus, all builders allow file downloads. For dynamic pricing that you update frequently, Wix and WordPress offer the most flexibility.

How much does a restaurant website cost?

AI website builders for restaurants cost $9-65/month. Mixo starts at $9/month (free tier available), Durable at $15/month, Wix at $16/month, Squarespace at $23/month. Professional restaurant websites from agencies cost $3,000-15,000 upfront plus $100-300/month for maintenance. AI builders include hosting, SSL, and built-in features. Custom sites require separate hosting ($20-100/month) and ongoing costs. Most restaurants can launch professional sites with AI builders for under $500 total (first year). The key difference: AI builders are DIY with everything included. Custom sites require agencies but offer full customization.

Should restaurants use PDF menus or HTML menus?

HTML menus beat PDF menus by 234% for mobile orders. Here's the data: Restaurant A used downloadable PDF menu on mobile—67% of visitors bounced without ordering. Restaurant B used HTML menu visible on page—mobile orders increased 234%. PDF menus are unreadable on phones (tiny text, pinch-zoom required, slow loading). HTML menus are instantly readable, tappable for phone orders, and load faster. Use PDF for print-only purposes: catering menus, wine lists, downloadable takeout. Use HTML for your main menu: appetizers, entrees, drinks, desserts. The best approach: HTML for mobile ordering, PDF optional for desktop users who want to print.

What restaurant website features drive the most orders?

High-converting restaurant websites include: (1) Professional food photos (4.2x more orders), (2) HTML mobile menu (234% increase), (3) Online ordering button visible above fold, (4) Clear location with map (73% check location before visiting), (5) Accurate hours updated weekly (31% lost customers to outdated hours), (6) Phone number tappable for mobile, (7) Reservation system for sit-down restaurants, (8) Featured dishes/highlights, (9) Daily specials section, (10) Customer reviews with photos. Most overlooked: accurate hours. 67% of restaurant websites have incorrect hours (seasonal changes, holiday updates). Customers arrive when site says 'open' but restaurant is 'closed' = lost customer forever.

How do I make my restaurant website mobile-friendly?

Restaurant websites get 73-89% mobile traffic (people search while hungry, on the go). Mobile optimization isn't optional. Essentials: (1) Tappable phone number (click-to-call), (2) GPS directions button, (3) HTML menu (no PDF), (4) Large buttons for 'Order Now' or 'Reserve', (5) Fast load time (under 3 seconds), (6) Readable text without zoom, (7) Horizontal scrolling photo galleries, (8) Mobile payments/ordering integration. Test your site: Visit on your phone while hungry. Can you find the menu in 3 seconds? Can you see food photos clearly? Can you order without pinch-zoom? If answer is no to any, you're losing hungry customers to competitors with better mobile experience.

Restaurants have excellent options. Wix for features, Squarespace for photos, Mixo for budget.
