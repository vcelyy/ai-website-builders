Notice what's included even on Personal:
- Unlimited storage

Most competitors charge extra for storage overages, limit bandwidth, or gate AI features behind higher tiers. Dorik gives you the whole package at the entry level.

### 2. The AI Generation is Fun to Watch

Dorik's AI generates your site in real-time. You type a prompt, hit enter, and watch it scroll through creating sections like ChatGPT writing a response.

My first site (a photography portfolio) generated in 47 seconds. The AI:
- Created a hero section with compelling headline
- Built an about section with placeholder bio
- Added a gallery grid layout
- Generated a contact form
- Wrote basic SEO meta descriptions

Was it perfect? No. But it was 80% ready in under a minute. The remaining 20%—custom photos, actual copy, color tweaks—took another 45 minutes.

Compare that to starting from scratch. Dorik saved me 3+ hours on my first site alone.

### 3. Unlimited Storage is Not a Gimmick

I tested this. My photography portfolio site now has 2.3 GB of high-resolution images. My blog has 47 posts with featured images. My business site has PDF downloads and video backgrounds.

Total storage used: 4.7 GB across 4 sites.

My monthly fee: Still $9.

Every other builder I've used starts sending warning emails at 2 GB. Dorik hasn't said a word. This alone makes it worth considering for image-heavy sites.

### 4. Membership Features Built-In

Dorik includes membership functionality on all plans. You can:
- Password-protect pages
- Create member-only content
- Build paid membership portals
- Integrate with payment processors

I built a simple client portal for a freelance designer in 2 hours. Private project galleries, download areas, and a contact form. Zero additional cost.

On WordPress, this would require membership plugins ($100-300/year). On Squarespace, you'd need a Commerce plan ($33/month). Dorik includes it at $9.

### 5. White-Label Options for Agencies

If you build sites for clients, Dorik's Business and Agency plans offer white-label dashboards. Your clients see your branding, not Dorik's.

The Agency plan ($39/month) includes:

- Agency branding throughout
- White-label documentation

For agencies building 5+ client sites, this is genuinely valuable. The math works out to under $8/site/month for hosting, CMS, and client management.

## The Bad (Where Dorik Falls Short)

### 1. E-commerce is Basically Non-Existent

Dorik is not an e-commerce platform. You can:
- Accept payments for memberships
- Use Stripe/PayPal buttons for simple transactions
- Sell digital downloads

You cannot:
- Build a full online store
- Manage inventory
- Handle complex shipping
- Process marketplace-style transactions

If you need to sell products, Dorik is the wrong choice. Period. Use Shopify, WooCommerce, or even Squarespace Commerce.

### 2. Design Flexibility is Limited

Dorik gives you 110+ templates. They're clean, modern, and professional.

But customizing beyond the template structure is frustrating. Want to move the navigation to the right side? You're fighting the system. Need a custom layout that doesn't fit their section types? Good luck.

Compared to Webflow's pixel-perfect control or Framer's design freedom, Dorik feels like working with training wheels.

For simple sites, this is fine. For unique brand expressions, you'll hit walls fast.

### 3. No True Free Plan

Dorik offers a 14-day free trial. That's not a free plan—it's an evaluation period.

After 14 days, you pay or lose access. There's no "free forever" tier like Wix or WordPress.com offer.

This isn't necessarily wrong (Dorik needs to make money), but it's worth knowing before you invest time building on the platform.

### 4. Fewer Templates Than Competitors

110 templates sounds like a lot until you compare:
- Wix: 800+ templates
- Squarespace: 200+ templates
- Webflow: 500+ templates

Dorik's templates are good quality. But if you're looking for hyper-specific starting points ("dentist office in Tucson"), you might not find the perfect match.

### 5. The Editor Can Feel Overwhelming

Dorik packs a lot into its interface. For power users, this is great. For first-time builders, the options can feel endless.

I've had clients send me screenshots asking "which button do I click?" because there are so many panels, settings, and edit options visible at once.

The learning curve isn't steep, but it's real. Budget 2-3 hours to get comfortable with the editor.

## Who Should Use Dorik

Use Dorik if:

- **Budget is your primary concern.** $9/month for unlimited everything is unbeatable.
- **You need membership features.** Built-in at no extra cost.
- **You're building a portfolio or blog.** Dorik excels at content-heavy sites.
- **You're an agency building simple client sites.** White-label at $19/month is valuable.
- **You have lots of images/media.** Unlimited storage actually means unlimited.

## Who Should Avoid Dorik

Skip Dorik if:

- **You need e-commerce.** It's not built for online stores.
- **You want pixel-perfect design control.** Use Webflow or Framer instead.
- **You expect a forever-free option.** Dorik requires payment after 14 days.
- **You need complex integrations.** Limited compared to WordPress/Wix.
- **You're building a large-scale business site.** The features aren't powerful enough.
