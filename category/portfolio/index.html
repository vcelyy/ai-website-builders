Common Questions About AI Portfolios

Can I use an AI builder if I'm a designer?

Yes, but choose wisely. Framer and Webflow give you full design control. Most other AI builders limit you to templates—which defeats the purpose of having a portfolio. As a designer, your portfolio should reflect your design sensibility.

Do I need code skills for a good portfolio?

Not with Framer. It's visual-first but allows code-level customization if you want it. Webflow has a steeper learning curve but more power. 10Web requires no code but depends on themes. Durable requires no code but has limited customization.

What about video in my portfolio?

Video backgrounds hurt performance significantly. Use them sparingly if at all. Better approach: video thumbnails that open in a lightbox or link to a separate video page. Framer handles video embedding well. Most other builders struggle with video optimization.

Should I include case studies?

Absolutely. Portfolios with case studies get 3x more client inquiries than image-only portfolios. Clients want to see your process: problem, approach, result. Framer makes it easy to create beautiful case study pages. Most AI builders make this difficult.

Can I migrate my portfolio later?

Framer and Webflow export clean code—easy to migrate. 10Web is WordPress (harder to migrate). Durable: no export option. Think about exit strategy before committing. Your portfolio should be portable.

What matters: Show 3-5 strong projects. Demonstrate process, not just final result. Include case studies: problem, approach, solution, outcome. Contact form prominent. Platform matters less than content quality.

Juniors: Launch fast on Mixo/Durable. Focus on case studies, not custom design. Iterate content, not platform.

PROJECT SELECTION STRATEGY

Quality over quantity: Juniors show everything. Senior portfolios show 3-5 exceptional projects. Quality trumps quantity. Employers/clients remember standout work, not volume.

Project criteria: (1) Real problem, real solution (no fake projects). (2) Demonstrates your process (not just pretty UI). (3) Shows diverse skills (research + design + prototyping). (4) Has measurable outcome (conversion increased, user satisfaction improved).

Real example: Junior graphic designer showed 15 projects: logos, posters, random designs. Applied to 50 agencies, zero callbacks. Mentor feedback: "can't see your thinking." Designer rebuilt portfolio with 4 detailed case studies: brand identity project (research → concepts → final), packaging design (problem → iterations → solution), editorial design (brief → process → final), web design (user journey → wireframes → UI). Applied to 20 agencies: 7 callbacks, 3 job offers. Fewer, deeper projects beat many shallow ones.

Project selection: 3-5 exceptional case studies over 15 mediocre projects. Depth beats breadth.

CASE STUDY STRUCTURE

The framework: Effective case studies follow narrative structure. Challenge: what problem did you solve? Role: what was your contribution? Process: how did you approach it? Solution: what did you create? Impact: what was the result?

Juniors skip: They show final designs only. Skip process. Skip challenges. Skip impact. Employers can't evaluate their thinking. Process demonstrates problem-solving ability. Final designs just show visual skills.

Real example: Junior designer showed 10 "finished" logos. Recruiters passed—couldn't evaluate thinking. Redesigned portfolio with 3 detailed case studies. Logo project: client brief (local bakery needing rebrand), research (competitor analysis, target audience), concepts (12 initial directions), iteration (client feedback, refinements), final (4 logo variations), impact (client sales increased 23% after rebrand). Showed entire process, not just pretty outcome. Recruiters could evaluate thinking, problem-solving. Landed junior designer role at agency.

Case studies: show problem → process → solution → impact. Process proves thinking, final design proves skill.

Update cadence: Every 3 months, add 1-2 new projects, remove 1-2 weak projects. Portfolio stays current (shows recent work), maintains size (5-7 projects optimal). Overhauls unnecessary.

PERFORMANCE ANALYTICS

Measure what matters: Portfolio analytics guide optimization. Track visitors, time on site, bounce rate, contact form submissions. Identify what works, double down.

Key metrics: Visitors (traffic source insights). Time on case study pages (engagement). Contact form submissions (conversion). Most-viewed projects (what resonates). These guide updates.

Real example: Designer tracked portfolio analytics. Discovered: 3 case studies drove 67% of form submissions. 2 projects had zero views in 6 months. Analytics revealed what worked, guided strategic updates.

Designer Portfolio: Minimal Gallery Approach

Platform: Framer | Approach: Full-screen images with minimal navigation

Why it works: Lets the work speak for itself. Users focus on visuals, not UI. High-quality images paired with brief project descriptions. Simple contact form at the end.

Consultant Portfolio: Results-Driven Case Studies

Platform: Durable | Approach: AI-generated case studies with metrics

Why it works: Each project shows problem, solution, and measurable results. Testimonials from clients. Clear service offerings. Easy inquiry form with specific project type selection.

Agency Portfolio: Multi-Media Showcases

Platform: Webflow | Approach: Detailed case studies with video, images, process

Why it works: Long-form storytelling about each project. Behind-the-scenes content. Team bios. Client logos. Award badges. Demonstrates expertise through depth, not just pretty pictures.

PORTFOLIO MISTAKES TO AVOID

Don't Do This
  • • Upload low-resolution images
  • • Use inconsistent styling across projects
  • • Hide contact info (make it easy!)
  • • Overwhelm with too many projects
  • • Use auto-playing video/audio
  • • Make navigation cryptic or "creative"
  • • Forget mobile optimization
Do This Instead
  • • Use high-quality, optimized images
  • • Maintain consistent design language
  • • Put contact form on every page
  • • Showcase 8-12 best projects only
  • • Let users control media playback
  • • Keep navigation simple and obvious
  • • Test thoroughly on mobile devices
