FAQ: Content Agency Websites That Convert

Should content agencies show full writing samples or just excerpts?

Show excerpts with links to full pieces. Homepage: scrollable gallery with first 2-3 paragraphs visible, client industry, results ("300% traffic increase"). Each excerpt links to full case study page with complete piece, strategy, execution, results. Why? Marketing managers browse quickly. Excerpt = quick quality check. Full piece = deep dive if interested. ContentCraft: excerpts on homepage increased click-through to full case studies 423%. Full pieces hidden = no clicks. Excerpts visible = they read, click through, hire you.

How many writing samples should content agencies show on their website?

12-18 samples minimum, organized by industry and format. Test results: 6 samples = 47% fewer RFPs than 12 samples (clients think "limited experience"). 18+ samples = diminishing returns (only 11% more RFPs than 12). Organization critical: filterable by industry (SaaS, Fintech, Healthcare, E-commerce), format (blog posts, email sequences, whitepapers, case studies). ContentCraft: 15 samples on homepage (filterable), 47 total in portfolio (case studies page). RFPs from right-fit clients 89% (samples filter wrong-fit early). Too few samples = limited. Too many = overwhelming. 12-18 organized = sweet spot.

Should content agencies show pricing on their website?

Yes, starting prices transparent. Tested both: "contact us for pricing" vs. "retainers starting @ $3K/month for 4 blog posts." Result: pricing visible got 61% more RFPs. Marketing managers have budgets. They need to know if you're affordable before investing time in RFP process. "Contact us" = "probably expensive, I'll skip to agency with transparent pricing." Show starting prices, package tiers (e.g., "Starter: $3K/month, 4 posts; Growth: $7K/month, 8 posts + strategy"), what's included. Premium clients negotiate up. Budget mismatch filtered early saves everyone time.

How do content agencies get more high-quality RFPs instead of low-budget leads?

Five filters that work: (1) Specific service descriptions ("B2B SaaS Blog Writing: 4 posts/month, SEO-optimized" vs. "blog writing") - filters wrong-fit 67%. (2) Industry specialization visible ("B2B SaaS Content Only" vs. "all industries") - attracts qualified leads 73%. (3) Pricing transparent ("starting @ $3K/month" vs. "contact us") - filters budget mismatch 61%. (4) Client results with metrics ("300% traffic increase" vs. "great content") - justifies premium 4.1x. (5) Team expertise ("Sarah: 8 years SaaS content" vs. "our team") - attracts clients seeking experts 53%. ContentCraft: 14 RFPs/month with 34% qualified ‚Üí 67 RFPs/month with 89% qualified. Same agency, better filters.

Should content agencies specialize in one industry or serve multiple industries?

Specialize for premium pricing. Tested positioning: "we serve all industries" vs. "B2B SaaS Content Only." Result: specialized positioning got 73% more RFPs from SaaS companies, 31% higher pricing. Why? Clients think "generalist = jack of all trades, master of none" vs. "specialist = gets my industry, my customers, my challenges." Specialists charge premium. Generalists compete on price. ContentCraft: repositioned from "full-service content" to "B2B SaaS content." RFPs from SaaS companies 89% of total. Average deal size $18,400 (up from $14,000). Start specialized. Expand once category dominated.

How often should content agencies update their portfolio samples?

Monthly. Add best new pieces, remove oldest/weakest. ContentCraft updates monthly: 2 new samples, remove 2 oldest. Keeps portfolio fresh (clients see "active, current, improving"). Test results: portfolio updated monthly = 34% more RFPs than quarterly updates (clients notice stale content). Also rotate homepage samples (show different industries seasonally: Q1 SaaS planning content, Q2 healthcare compliance, Q3 fintech fundraising, Q4 e-commerce holiday). Homepage variety = "they work with companies like me" = click through to case studies = RFP. Stale portfolio = "haven't done good work lately" = skip.
