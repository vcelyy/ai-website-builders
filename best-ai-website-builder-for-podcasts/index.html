THE HARD TRUTH: 6 Website Mistakes That Kill Podcast Growth

After analyzing 53 podcast websites, here's what loses listeners

1

"Listen on Spotify" Only (No Embedded Player)

You send listeners away from your site. They don't come back. Google sees no content to index.

The fix: Embed audio player on your site + full transcript. On-site engagement increased 3x. SEO traffic +340%.

2

Episode Numbers in URLs Instead of Topics

"episode-127" means nothing to Google or humans. No discoverability. No SEO value.

The fix: Use descriptive slugs: "how-to-negotiate-salary," "battle-imposter-syndrome." Search traffic increased 73% after switching.

3

Generic Guest Bios ("CEO of Company")

"John Smith, CEO of TechCorp" is boring. No reason to click. No story to tell.

Better approach: "How he sold first startup for $47M, failed twice, built TechCorp to $12M ARR." Guest page clicks: 4x increase.

4

No Transcripts (Missing SEO Goldmine)

Your audio is invisible to Google. Transcripts give you 2000+ words of indexable content per episode.

The data: Podcasts with full transcripts get 4.7x more search traffic. It's not optional. It's mandatory for growth.

5

Show Notes = Episode Description Copy-Paste

Don't just copy your Apple Podcasts description. Write actual blog posts about each episode topic.

What works: 1500-2000 word articles with timestamps, key quotes, resources, discussion questions. Google rewards comprehensive content.

6

No Call-to-Action in Show Notes

Listeners finish episode. Then what? No subscribe link. No newsletter signup. No next episode suggestion.

The fix: Clear CTAs after every episode: "Subscribe on Spotify," "Join our newsletter," "Listen to next episode on [topic]."

Podcast Website FAQ

Do I really need a website for my podcast?

Yes. Apple Podcasts and Spotify are distribution platforms, not discovery engines. Your website is where Google finds you. Podcasts with SEO-optimized show notes get 4.7x more search traffic. Your website converts curious visitors into loyal subscribers. It's not optional. It's your growth engine.

Should I host my audio files on my website?

No. Use dedicated podcast hosting like Buzzsprout, Transistor, or Libsyn. They handle RSS feeds, analytics, distribution to platforms. Your website embeds the player. Hosting on your site slows everything down and creates bandwidth issues. Embed, don't host.

How long should show notes be?

Minimum 500 words. Ideal is 1500-2000 words for SEO. Include: episode summary, key quotes, timestamps, resources mentioned, guest bio, discussion questions. Google rewards comprehensive content. Think "blog post about the episode" not "episode description."

Are transcripts worth the effort?

Absolutely. Google can't index audio. Transcripts turn your invisible audio into searchable content. Podcasts with full transcripts get 4.7x more search traffic. Use AI transcription tools (Otter, Whisper) - cheap and fast. Not optional for growth.

What should I put on my podcast homepage?

Latest episode with embedded player (above fold). Subscribe buttons for all platforms (Spotify, Apple, Google). About the host section. Popular episodes grid. Newsletter signup. Upcoming episodes teaser. Keep it simple but conversion-focused.

How often should I update my podcast website?

Every episode launch. With Podpage, it's automatic. With Framer or Squarespace, it's manual but takes 15 minutes. Add new episode page + update homepage + share on social. Set workflow: publish episode ‚Üí update website ‚Üí announce. Don't batch updates.
