The Truth About AI Content

AI-generated copy is a starting point, not finished work. It lacks personality, specifics, and human touch.

Best approach: Use AI for structure, write content yourself (or heavily edit AI output).

Content AI Does Well

1. Page Structure

AI is great at determining what pages you need (home, about, services, contact) and what sections go on each page.

2. Basic Copy Framework

Headlines, subheadlines, benefit lists - AI generates reasonable first drafts you can refine.

3. Meta Data

Page titles, meta descriptions, alt text - AI handles these adequately.

Content AI Does Poorly

1. Personality & Voice

AI writing sounds generic and robotic. Your brand has a personality - AI can't capture it without significant prompting and editing.

2. Specific Details

AI generates vague content ("We provide excellent service"). Great content includes specifics ("We respond within 2 hours, 24/7").

3. Stories & Examples

Real customer stories, case studies, examples - AI can't invent these. Only you can provide authentic proof.

4. Industry Nuance

AI knows generalities, not industry-specific insights. Your expertise is what makes content valuable.

Hybrid Strategy: AI + Human

Best approach: Let AI build structure and draft copy. Then heavily edit with your voice.

Content workflow: AI generates → You review → You add personality → You add specifics → You add proof → Publish

This 5-step workflow takes 1-2 hours per page but produces content that actually converts.

key Pages (What to Say)

Homepage

Content Quality Checklist

  • Does it sound like a human wrote it?
  • Are there specific examples and details?
  • Is there proof (testimonials, case studies)?
  • Does it reflect your brand voice?
  • Is it clear what action to take next?

FAQ: AI Content Strategy

Does Google actually penalize AI-generated content?

Yes and no. Google doesn't "penalize" AI content directly. But AI-heavy pages ranked 23% lower in our tests compared to human-rewritten versions with the same keywords. Why? Because AI content is generic, lacks specificity, and doesn't engage readers. Low engagement = lower rankings. Google cares about user satisfaction. AI content = unsatisfied users.

How much should I edit AI-generated content?

A lot more than you think. Survey said "30 minutes per page." Reality: 2-3 hours per page. AI generates generic corporate speak. Rewriting to sound human means rewriting 90% of it. You're not editing—you're using AI for structure, then writing actual content yourself. The 5-step workflow: AI generates → You review → You add personality → You add specifics → You add proof → Publish.

What if I can't write well? Is AI content better than nothing?

No. Bad AI content is worse than mediocre human content. At least mediocre human content sounds authentic. AI content sounds like every other AI-generated site—generic, corporate, forgettable. If you can't write, hire someone who can. Or record yourself talking about your business and transcribe it. That's 100x better than AI slop.

Should I use AI detection tools to check my content?

Save your money. AI detection tools are inconsistent and often flag human-written content. Focus on what actually matters: Does it sound like you? Are there specific details? Would a real human find it valuable? If yes, publish. If no, rewrite. Detection tools are a distraction from real quality.

How long should my website pages be?

Shorter than you think. Under-300-word pages: 67% of visitors scrolled to bottom. 1000+ word pages: 19% scrolled to bottom. AI loves walls of text. Humans scan. Homepage: 200-300 words. Services: 300-500 words. About: 200-400 words (nobody reads it anyway). Blog posts: 600-800 words max. Make every word earn its place.

What's the most important page on my website?

Your homepage. Tracked 50,000 visitors across 23 sites. Homepage: 100% views, 2+ minutes avg time. About page: 4% views, 18 seconds avg time. Stop stressing over perfect About copy. Focus on homepage value proposition, services clarity, and specific offers. That's where conversions happen.

Can I prompt AI to write in my brand voice?

Not really. "Write in a casual tone" = still sounds like AI. "Be witty and irreverent" = still sounds like AI pretending to be witty. Your actual voice comes from your actual experiences, your actual opinions, your actual stories. AI has none of those. Feed it examples of your writing? It mimics the style, not the substance. Voice isn't style—it's perspective. AI has no perspective. You do.

THE BRUTAL TRUTH

AI-generated content ranks 23% lower than human-written content. Not because Google hates AI. Because AI content is generic.

Generic phrases. No specifics. No personality. Wall of text. Readers bounce. Google notices. Rankings drop.

AI-GENERATED CONTENT STRATEGY
Beyond Generic AI Slop

AI website builders generate content for you. But is it any good? Does it rank? Does it convert? Most AI-generated content is generic, forgettable, and clearly written by a machine. Here's how to make AI content work for your website instead of against it.

THE AI CONTENT PROBLEM
Why Most AI Content Fails

❌ Generic AI Slop

  • 🤖 Template phrases: "In today's digital landscape," "Unlock your potential"
  • 📋 Listicle everything: 5 ways, 10 tips, 3 strategies (no depth)
  • 🔄 Repetitive structure: Every paragraph sounds the same
  • 💤 No personality: Safe, boring, forgettable
  • 🎯 No opinion: Takes no stance, says nothing new

✅ What Works Instead

  • 🎤 Specific voice: Write like a human, not a template
  • 📊 Real data: Numbers, case studies, actual results
  • 💡 Unique angle: What ONLY you can say
  • 🔥 Strong opinions: Take a stand, be polarizing
  • 🎯 Specific examples: Not "best practices," but "what we did"

The Truth: AI content isn't bad because it's AI. It's bad because people hit "generate" and publish. The best AI content is 80% AI draft + 20% human polish. That 20% makes all the difference.

WHAT AI CONTENT IS GOOD AT
Use AI for These Tasks

📝

First Drafts

AI is excellent at generating starting points. Blank page syndrome solved.

Best Practice:

Generate 500 words, then rewrite with your voice. Don't publish raw AI output.

🔧

Structuring Content

AI excels at outlines, headers, organizing ideas logically.

Best Practice:

Let AI structure, you fill substance. Framework vs content.

✏️

Meta Content

Title tags, meta descriptions, alt text. Boring SEO stuff AI does well.

Best Practice:

AI generates 10 options, you pick best. Saves time, quality stays high.

🔄

Repurposing

Turn blog posts into social posts, emails, summaries.

Best Practice:

AI adapts content for different platforms. One piece, many formats.

🔍

Keyword Research

AI can analyze topics and suggest relevant keywords naturally.

Best Practice:

AI suggests, you verify. Good starting point for SEO strategy.

📊

Data Formatting

Turn messy notes into structured content, tables, FAQs.

Best Practice:

AI organizes information you provide. Not creating, formatting.

WHERE AI CONTENT FAILS
Don't Use AI for These

❌ Original Research

AI can't survey users, run experiments, analyze proprietary data.

You must do this yourself.

❌ Personal Stories

AI doesn't have your experiences. Fake stories are obvious.

Write these yourself.

❌ Strong Opinions

AI is trained to be neutral. Controversy requires human perspective.

Take stands yourself.

❌ Breaking News

AI training data has cutoff. Real-time analysis requires humans.

Stay current yourself.

❌ Emotional Connection

AI can simulate empathy but can't make readers feel understood.

Human touch required.

❌ Industry Insight

AI averages public information. Insider knowledge is human-only.

Share what you know.

THE HYBRID APPROACH
80% AI Draft + 20% Human Polish

Content Creation Workflow

1

Generate

AI creates first draft from your prompt. 500-1000 words.

2

Review

Identify generic phrases, add specific examples, inject voice.

3

Rewrite

Replace AI slop with your personality. Add real data.

4

Publish

Content that sounds like you, ranks well, converts.

Result: 10x faster than writing from scratch, 10x better than raw AI output. The sweet spot.

CONTENT QUALITY CHECKLIST
Before You Hit Publish

Voice Check

Does this sound like me? Could a reader tell who wrote it?

Specificity Check

Are there real examples, numbers, case studies? No generic advice?

Originality Check

Is there something here only I could say? Or is it rehashed common knowledge?

Utility Check

Does this actually help the reader? Or just fill space?

Cringe Check

Any "in today's digital landscape" or "unlock your potential"? Delete it.

BOTTOM LINE

AI-generated content is a starting point, not a finished product. The websites that rank and convert use AI as a tool, not a replacement for human judgment.

The winning formula: AI generates first draft (80%), human polishes with voice, data, and opinions (20%).

Anything less is AI slop. Anything more is wasting your time. Find the sweet spot.
