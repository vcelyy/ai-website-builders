Include diverse patients: different skin types, ages, genders. Visible results = effective = trust = schedule appointment.

Buried Conditions Treated (67% make this)

Conditions list hidden on services page = patients don't know you help them. 67% search by specific condition. Visible = you treat this = call you. Add "We Treat: Acne, Eczema, Psoriasis, Rosacea, Melasma, Anti-Aging" prominently on hero and homepage. Patients need to know you treat their condition immediately. Buried conditions = patients don't know you help them = skip to dermatologist who clearly treats their concern.

No skin care blog = invisible in search = no organic patients. Educational content drives 7x more patients over 12 months. Content = found in search = trust = appointment. Add skin care blog with treatment tips, condition guides, prevention advice. "Call to Schedule" Only (71% make this)

Phone-only booking = friction = abandonment. 73% prefer online scheduling. Busy patients want convenience = book online = show up. Add online appointment scheduling with real-time availability. Not Mobile-Optimized (67% make this)

Dermatology Website FAQ

How many treatment photos should I display?

100+ minimum, organized by condition. Patients want proof treatments work. 79% want to see results for their specific concern. Organize by: acne, eczema, psoriasis, rosacea, melasma, cosmetic treatments. Show consistent before/afters: same lighting, same angles, realistic results. Include diverse patients: different skin types, ages, genders. Gallery = proven effectiveness = trust = schedule appointment. Investment in professional photography returns 10x through increased appointments. More photos = more proof = more trust = more bookings.

Should I list conditions treated prominently?

Yes, everywhere. 67% of patients search by specific condition. Add "We Treat: Acne, Eczema, Psoriasis, Rosacea, Melasma, Anti-Aging" to hero section, homepage, services page. Patients need to know you treat their specific concern immediately. Buried conditions list = patients don't know you help them = skip. Visible conditions = you treat this = call you = schedule. Primary care referrals go online first. If they don't see their condition on your site, they find another dermatologist. Make it obvious.

Do I need a skin care blog?

Yes, significantly. Educational content drives 7x more organic patients over 12 months (342 vs 48 without blog). Write about: acne treatment tips, eczema management, sun protection, anti-aging advice, rosacea triggers. Answer common patient questions. 300+ words per post, publish weekly. People searching for help find your content, then book appointments. Blog = found in search = trust = organic patients = revenue. Even 1 post/month = 12 articles/year = 12 new search entry points. Content compounds over time.

Should I offer online appointment scheduling?

Yes, absolutely. 73% of patients prefer online scheduling over phone. "Call to schedule" = friction = abandonment. Online booking with real-time availability = book 24/7 = show up. Reduces staff time 67% (no phone tag). Auto-confirm appointments immediately. Consider offering same-day spots online (captures urgent patients). Easy = they schedule = reduced admin work = more appointments = more revenue. Patients research at 10 PM. If they can't book, they forget. If they can book online, you have appointment tomorrow morning.

Should I offer telehealth consultations?

Yes, prominently. Telehealth option increases inquiries 43%. Busy professionals, parents with kids, rural patients want convenience. Offer virtual consultations for initial visits, follow-ups, treatment check-ins. Converts telehealth patients to in-person appointments (they need procedures, prescriptions, in-office treatments). "Virtual Consultations Available" on hero and services page. Telehealth = convenience = converts busy professionals to in-person. Even if only 20% continue virtually, 80% convert to in-person revenue. No telehealth option = those patients choose competitor with virtual option.

Is mobile optimization important for dermatology websites?

Critical. 73% of patients search on phones. Skin concerns = private mobile search (people don't want coworkers knowing, teenagers don't want parents knowing). Non-responsive site = broken experience = find another dermatologist. 67% leave immediately if broken on mobile. Ensure: treatment gallery swipeable, scheduling works on phone, click-to-call buttons. Mobile = they search privately = schedule appointment = more patients. For acne especially: private mobile research = no one knows = they book. Your mobile site IS your practice.

Which platform for high-volume practices (1000+ photos)?

Squarespace for treatment gallery organization. Beautiful galleries handle 1000+ photos efficiently. Organize by condition, treatment type, patient age. Consistent presentation converts viewers to patients. Webflow for custom condition guides if you need complex content organization. WordPress for content-heavy practices needing blog + plugins. For 1000+ treatment photos: Squarespace delivers beautiful organization that converts. Webflow scales too but requires more setup. Choose based on priority: gallery focus (Squarespace) or content focus (Webflow/WordPress).
