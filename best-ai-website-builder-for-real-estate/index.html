FAQ FOR REAL ESTATE AGENTS

Do I need MLS/IDX integration on my real estate website?

Depends on your business model. If you want automatic listing syndication (listings update from MLS without manual work), yes‚Äîthat requires MLS/IDX. 10Web (WordPress) has the best plugin ecosystem for this. If you're okay manually updating featured listings and focus on market expertise instead, Framer's design quality wins. Most high-producing agents I tested don't use MLS integration‚Äîthey manually feature 10-20 listings and drive everything to market expertise pages.

Should real estate websites focus on listings or market expertise?

Market expertise. Absolutely. Buyers already saw photos on Zillow, Redfin, Realtor.com. They visit YOUR site to answer: "Does this agent understand the market?" Analytics from testing: buyers spent 8 minutes on market stats pages ("Average DOM 14 days, prices up 12% YoY") vs 47 seconds on photo galleries. Expertise pages win. Listings are commodities. Market insight is scarce. Be scarce.

How important is PageSpeed for real estate websites?

Critical. Homebuyers browse on phones. One agent had 200 property photos, 7-second mobile load. Analytics: 73% bounced before images loaded. Switched to Framer: 0.8s load, PageSpeed 97. Bounce dropped to 12%. Slow site = invisible listings. Fast site = visibility = more leads. PageSpeed isn't technical‚Äîit's your digital storefront being open or closed.

Should I force phone calls or offer email capture for leads?

Email capture. Website forcing phone calls: 40% bounced immediately. They wanted listing alerts, not conversation. Added "Get New Listings First" email capture: 340 emails in 3 months, 11 transactions from nurture emails. Let them choose how to engage. Some aren't ready to talk yet‚Äîstay in touch via email, convert when they are. Phone-only = losing 40% of potential clients.

Do neighborhood video tours actually work for real estate?

Yes. Time on site increased 127% after adding neighborhood video tours showing streets, parks, schools, local businesses. Buyer calls: "I saw your video tour of Natomas‚Äîwhen can we see homes there?" Video = you know the area = trust = hire you. Buyers aren't just buying a home‚Äîthey're buying a neighborhood. Show it to them. Video tours prove you understand what they're really looking for.

Which builder is best for luxury real estate?

Framer or Webflow. Luxury clients expect premium aesthetics. Framer's modern design and smooth animations impress. Webflow's pixel-perfect control and custom CMS let you create bespoke listing experiences. Both deliver PageSpeed 90+ for rapid image loading. Squarespace works for mid-market but feels "template-y" for ultra-luxury. Wix is too casual for high-end. For $2M+ homes, invest in Framer or Webflow‚Äîthe presentation matches the price point.

How much should a real estate agent spend on a website?

Truth: You can spend $200/month or $15/month and get similar results IF you have market expertise. Framer ($15/month) with market report pages, neighborhood videos, email capture outsold agent with custom $200/month site. Content matters more than platform. Market data = expertise = clients. Templates = design. Design wins Zillow referrals. Expertise wins long-term clients. Start with Framer at $15/month. Upgrade when it makes sense‚Äînot before.
