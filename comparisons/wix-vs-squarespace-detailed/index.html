The Key Differences

Features vs. Design

Wix maximizes features. App marketplace lets you add anything.

Squarespace maximizes design. Consistently polished templates.

Flexibility vs. Consistency

Wix is more flexible. More freedom to customize and add features.

Squarespace is consistent. Rigid but harder to break, always looks good.

Price Difference

Wix is much cheaper. $10/mo vs $23/mo at entry level.

Squarespace costs more. But you get consistently polished design.

Both Have Same Trade-off

Neither offers code export. Both lock you into their platforms.

Similar ease of use. Both are designed for beginners and non-technical users.

Choose Based on Your Priorities

  • Choose Squarespace (8.3/10) if: You want consistently polished design, you prioritize aesthetics, you're in creative fields, you want faster loading.
  • Choose Wix (8.2/10) if: You need specific features, you want app marketplace, you're on a budget, you need more flexibility.
  • Squarespace is better for: Portfolios, restaurants, designers, photographers, visual brands.
  • Wix is better for: E-commerce, feature-heavy sites, budget-conscious users, specific needs.

My Experience: The $16/Month Wix vs. $23/Month Squarespace for a Restaurant

Real testing, not theoretical comparison

A restaurant owner needed a website. Menu, reservations, location, photos. Simple but professional. Budget: $30/month maximum.

The Squarespace route: Started 14-day trial. Built beautiful site in 4 hours. Restaurant templates were stunning - food photography looked amazing. But at $23/month, that left only $7/month for reservations system integration. Needed OpenTable integration - cost extra. Total: $23 + $29 = $52/month. Over budget.

The Wix route: Built site in 3 hours using Wix Restaurants template. Good but not Squarespace-level beautiful. But Wix Restaurants app included: online ordering, table reservations, delivery integration. All-in-one for $16/month (basic plan). Total: $16/month with everything included.

The brutal truth: Owner chose Wix. Why? "Squarespace site looked 20% better. But Wix has everything I need at $16/month vs. Squarespace's $52/month with add-ons. The visual difference isn't worth $36/month ($432/year)."

The lesson: Squarespace designs are undeniably more polished. But for small businesses with tight budgets, Wix's included features and lower pricing often win. Design is important, but $432/year in savings is real money for most businesses.

The framework: Choose Squarespace if design is your competitive advantage (designers, photographers, luxury brands). Choose Wix if you need specific features and have budget constraints. For restaurants specifically, Wix Restaurants is often the better value.
