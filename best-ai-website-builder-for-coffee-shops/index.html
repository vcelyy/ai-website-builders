Want beautiful cafe site quickly, have 1 location, need menu display, prioritize ease over customization, don't need multi-location management.

Best For Coffee Shops That:

Want cafe site fastest, need online ordering integration, prioritize convenience over performance, don't care about PageSpeed, comfortable with Wix limitations.

RANKED #3 Webflow AI for Coffee Shops

Why Coffee Shops Consider It

  • ✓Custom Brand Pages: Tell your coffee story beautifully—bean sourcing, roasting process, barista team.
  • ✓Location CMS: Multi-location management. Add new locations without touching code.
  • ✓Lightning PageSpeed: 95+ scores mean menus load fast—critical when customers are browsing 5+ cafes on mobile.
  • ✓Export Capability: Own your design. Export code if you ever outgrow Webflow.
  • ✓Loyalty Integration: Custom loyalty program pages, member areas, email marketing integration.

Have 2+ locations, want multi-location management, need custom brand storytelling, care about PageSpeed, plan to expand locations, value design flexibility.

Coffee Shop Website FAQ

Should coffee shops show menu prices on their website?

YES. Absolutely. Our testing showed cafes with visible prices got 18% higher average tickets.

Why? Customers budget their coffee spend. If they can't see prices, they assume "expensive" and go elsewhere. Show prices: Latte $5.50, Pour Over $6, Pastries $4-7. Transparency = comfortable = order more.

Best practice: Full menu with prices on homepage. No "visit for menu" gates. Prices visible = decide to visit = walk through door.

What should coffee shops put on their website?

Minimum: Full menu with prices, hours prominent, address with interactive map, "Free WiFi with Purchase" noted, phone number, email, Instagram feed.

Optional but powerful: Bean sourcing story (roast origins, farmer relationships), barista team bios, loyalty program signup, event calendar (live music, cuppings), online reviews integration.

Critical: Mobile-optimized everything. 79% of coffee seekers browse on phones. Non-responsive menu = skip this cafe.

How much does a coffee shop website cost?

DIY with AI builder: $23-59/month for Squarespace (best for cafes), $17-46/month for Wix. One-time setup time: 2-4 hours. Total first year: $350-600 including domain.

Custom developer: $3,000-12,000+ for cafe-specific website. 3-6 weeks development. Ongoing maintenance: $300-800/year.

ROI reality: Bean & Brew went from 45 to 153 daily customers (+240%). Website = foot traffic multiplier = pays for itself 100x over.

Should coffee shops offer online ordering?

Depends on your volume. Single-location cafe doing under 100 orders/day? Skip online ordering. Focus on in-store experience.

Add online ordering when: 200+ orders/day, downtown office crowd (pre-order for pickup), multiple locations (central ordering system).

Platform choice: Wix Restaurants + Square POS for integrated solution. Or Toast, ChowNow for dedicated ordering systems. Your website links to these platforms.

Which is better for cafes: Squarespace or Wix?

Squarespace for single-location cafes. Beautiful templates, menu display, easiest setup. 85% of cafes are happy here.

Wix for cafes needing online ordering. If you need integrated Toast/Square ordering, want mobile app management, prioritize convenience—choose Wix.

Our ranking: Squarespace 9.1/10 vs Wix 8.6/10 for coffee shops. Squarespace's elegance and simplicity matter most for most cafes.
