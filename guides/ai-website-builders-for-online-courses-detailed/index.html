Course Creation Tips

Start with presell

Don't build the full course first. Create landing page, presell to validate demand, build based on feedback.

Free value builds trust

Offer free mini-course or valuable content. Demonstrates teaching style, builds email list, converts better.

Clear outcomes over content volume

Sell transformation, not hours of video. What will students achieve? Focus on results.

Social proof is critical

Testimonials, case studies, before/after results. If you're new, offer free beta in exchange for testimonials.

Scarcity drives enrollments

Limited spots, enrollment deadline, bonus for early action. Create urgency without being manipulative.

Money-back guarantee reduces friction

30-day guarantee or first module risk-free. Reverses risk from buyer to seller, increases conversions.

Mobile optimization matters

Many students access on phones. Course video, materials, and navigation must work perfectly on mobile.

Community increases completion

Private community (Discord, Slack, Circle) boosts engagement and completion rates. Adds value.

Platform lock-in is real

AI builders are great for starting. Consider migration path for course delivery (Teachable, Thinkific) as you scale.

Analytics from day one

Track landing page visits, conversion rate, video completion, drop-off points. Data drives optimization.

Course websites should communicate clear outcomes, build trust through social proof, and make enrollment simple. Start with presell, build based on demand.

Most course creators make mistake: build full course, then launch. 6 months work, $0 sales. Preselling reverses this: sell course first, then build. If nobody buys, you saved 6 months work. If people buy, build with confidence. AI builders enable fast presell landing pages.

PRICING STRATEGIES: MAXIMIZE REVENUE

Course pricing isn't guesswork. Strategy drives revenue. Underprice = perception of low value, lost revenue. Overprice = no sales. Find sweet spot: high perceived value, competitive pricing, payment options that increase conversion.

VALUE-BASED PRICING

Price based on transformation, not hours

"This course will help you earn $10K more/year." Price: $497 (5% of outcome). "Save 20 hours/week with this system." Price: $997 (time value = $2,400/year at $50/hour). Price based on outcome value, not production cost. Students pay for transformation, not your time investment.

Real example: Career coach priced course based on salary increase outcome. "This course helped students average $15K salary increase." Price: $997 (6.6% of outcome). Perceived as bargain. Sold 237 copies in first year. Value-based pricing = higher prices without resistance.

PAYMENT PLANS: INCREASE CONVERSION

Offer payment plans: 3 monthly payments, 6 monthly payments. Higher total cost (3Ã— $197 = $591 vs $497 upfront), but lower barrier to entry. Conversion typically increases 30-50% with payment plans. Students can afford monthly better than lump sum. You get more revenue per student.

Real example: Course priced at $497. Added payment plan: 3 Ã— $197. Conversion increased from 12% to 18% (50% increase). Average revenue per student: $456 (mix of upfront and payment plans). More sales at slightly lower average price = higher total revenue.

EARLY BIRD PRICING: URGENCY + SCARCITY

Launch discounts drive first-day sales

"Launch week: $297 (40% off $497 regular price)." Early bird pricing creates urgency: buy now or pay more later. Launch day sales spike from early bird buyers. After launch week, price increases to regular. Scarcity (limited time) drives action.

Real example: Creator launched with early bird pricing ($297 vs $497 regular). 47% of buyers purchased during launch week at discounted price. Revenue spike: $12K in first week vs $3K average month. Early bird = urgency = sales.
