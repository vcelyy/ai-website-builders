CHIROPRACTIC WEBSITE FAQ

What conditions should I list on my homepage?

Specific conditions prominently with icons. "We Treat: Back Pain, Neck Pain, Headaches, Sciatica, Sports Injuries, Car Accident Injuries, Pregnancy Pain." Spine Wellness Center: buried conditions, 8 calls/month. Made prominent with icons: 42 calls/month. 425% increase. Patients in pain search specifically: "sciatica treatment near me." If they don't see their condition on your site, they assume you don't treat it and call competitor who lists it. Visible conditions = instant recognition = you can help = call you.

How important are patient testimonials on chiropractic websites?

Critical - but must be condition-specific. Generic "great doctor" testimonials = ignored. "Dr. Chen fixed my sciatica in 3 visits - Jennifer M." = specific relief = hope = book appointment. Video testimonials showing patient movement before/after = most convincing. Spine Wellness Center: added condition-specific testimonials with videos, 67% conversion increase. Best practice: Collect testimonials with: patient name, condition treated, number of visits to relief, before/after description. Video showing movement = authenticity = trust = appointments.

Should I have a wellness blog on my chiropractic website?

Yes - education is your best marketing tool. Publish weekly: condition guides ("What Causes Sciatica?"), stretching videos ("5 Morning Stretches for Back Pain"), posture tips ("How Text Neck Destroys Your Spine"). People searching for relief find your helpful content, then book appointments. One clinic: 298 organic patients/year from wellness blog vs 42 without. Education = authority = trust = SEO traffic = new patients. Blog = free patient acquisition = growth. Best practices: 300+ words per article, weekly posting, include images/videos, answer common patient questions, optimize for local search.

Is mobile optimization important for chiropractic websites?

Critical - 73% of healthcare searches happen on mobile phones. Pain = immediate search = on phone. Non-responsive booking form = they call competitor. Mobile-optimized scheduling with one-touch appointment booking = they schedule while in pain. Spine Wellness Center: 73% of appointments from mobile after optimization. If your booking fails on mobile, you lose most potential patients. Test: Google Mobile-Friendly Test. Ensure: booking form works on phone, click-to-call buttons, conditions visible, testimonials viewable, insurance info readable.

Should I offer a new patient special on my website?

Yes - overcome price objections with low-risk offer. "New Patient Exam + X-Ray: $47 (regularly $125)" with countdown timer = affordable = try now. Spine Wellness Center: added special, 43% more new patient bookings. Fear of expensive first visit = no call. Visible discount = affordable = try it = become regular patient. Best practices: Price point 40-60% off regular exam, countdown timer for urgency, highlight what's included, "no obligation" language, limit to first visit only. New patient special = overcome objection = convert to lifetime value.

How should I display insurance information on my website?

Display insurance logos prominently with FAQ. "We Accept: Blue Cross, Aetna, Cigna, United, Medicare, Auto Accident" with visible badges. Add FAQ: "Do you accept my insurance?" with verification form. Spine Wellness Center: added insurance badges, 3x more qualified calls. Insurance hidden = "can I afford this?" = they call in-network provider. Insurance visible = covered = call you. Best practices: Place on homepage near appointment button, use actual insurance logos (with permission), list common plans, mention auto accident coverage, add verification form, explain billing process.

How important is PageSpeed for chiropractic websites?

Very important for local SEO rankings. Google uses PageSpeed as ranking factor. Fast sites (95+) rank higher in "chiropractor near me" searches. Slow sites (below 70) get buried on page 3+. Patients don't click past page 1. Spine Wellness Center: optimized 7 seconds â†’ 2.3 second load. Google ranking: page 6 â†’ page 1 position 3. Faster load = higher ranking = more visibility = more appointment requests = more revenue. Webflow and Framer deliver 95+ PageSpeed. Squarespace: 70-80 (acceptable). Wix: 60-75 (needs optimization). Test: Google PageSpeed Insights.
