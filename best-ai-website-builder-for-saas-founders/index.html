SaaS Website FAQ

What should I include on my SaaS landing page?

Brutal value prop (specific problem, specific solution), 60-second demo video, transparent pricing, social proof (company logos, testimonials), integration showcase, FAQ, one-click trial signup. Every element must answer: "Why should I sign up?" Remove anything that doesn't contribute to conversion. Focus = converts.

How long should my demo video be?

60 seconds maximum. 30-60 seconds = sweet spot. Show: problem (3 sec), solution (10 sec), how it works (30 sec), results (10 sec), CTA (7 sec). Any longer = they drop off. Short = they watch to end = sign up. Test lengths, optimize for completion rate.

Should I show pricing on my SaaS site?

Yes. Transparent pricing = can afford = sign up. "Contact Sales" = expensive = skip. Even if pricing is complex, show starting price with "Custom pricing for enterprises" below. Pricing visible = 67% higher conversion. Hide pricing = hide trust = lose conversions.

What social proof works best for SaaS?

"Used by 500+ companies" with logos = others trust it = safe = sign up. Customer testimonials with results: "Saved 10 hours weekly" = specific benefit = want this. Case studies: "How Company X increased revenue 47%" = proof it works = sign up. Logos + testimonials + case studies = maximum trust.

How do I reduce signup friction?

One-click trial: "Start Free Trial - No Credit Card Required." Email only to start. Ask for more info after they're hooked in product. Every extra field = 20-30% drop-off. Name + email = maximum friction tolerated. Company size, role = ask in-product. Frictionless = more signups = more paid conversions.

Should I show integrations on my landing page?

Absolutely. "Works with Slack, Notion, Google Sheets, Zapier" visible = fits my stack = sign up. Integration logos with hover "How it works" tooltips = see integration = understand value = reduce risk. Integrations = reduce evaluation friction = sign up now.
