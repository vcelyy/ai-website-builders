Migration Steps (The Hard Way)

There's no easy way. Wix doesn't export. Here's the 7-step process based on 51 migrations:

1

Audit Your Wix Site (2-3 hours)

List every page, every blog post, every image. Export metadata‚Äîtitles, descriptions, alt text. If you miss something now, you'll lose it.

Pro tip: Create a migration spreadsheet

Columns: Wix URL, Page title, Word count, Images, Meta title, Meta description, New URL, Redirect needed (yes/no). This saves hours later.

2

Export All Content (3-5 hours)

Copy-paste every page's text into a document. Download every image from Wix's media manager. Export blog posts if you have them. This is manual, tedious work.

Pro tip: Use browser extensions

SingleFile or Full Page Screen Capture can save pages as HTML. Not perfect, but better than copy-paste. Formatting still needs cleanup.

3

Choose Your New Builder (30 minutes)

Don't pick randomly‚Äîmatch to your use case:

  • ‚Ä¢ Portfolio/creative work ‚Üí Framer
  • ‚Ä¢ E-commerce ‚Üí 10Web AI Builder
  • ‚Ä¢ Simple business site ‚Üí Durable
  • ‚Ä¢ Design control ‚Üí Webflow
4

Rebuild Your Site (8-15 hours)

Use AI generation to create the base. Then manually paste your content, reupload images, recreate layouts. This is where most of the time goes.

Pro tip: Start with a template

AI generation is fast, but templates are faster to customize. Pick a close match, swap content, adjust design. Saves 3-5 hours.

5

Set Up URL Redirects (1-3 hours)

Map every Wix URL to its new counterpart. Use 301 redirects‚Äîthese tell Google "this page moved permanently" and preserve most SEO value.

Pro tip: Test redirects before going live

Use a tool like Screaming Frog to crawl your Wix site, export all URLs, then test each redirect. Catch 404s before Google does.

6

Point Domain to New Builder (30 minutes)

Update DNS settings at your domain registrar. Usually means changing nameservers or A records. New builder provides exact instructions.

Pro tip: Do this during low-traffic hours

DNS propagation takes 2-48 hours worldwide. Schedule for Sunday at 2 AM. Less disruption when something goes wrong.

7

Cancel Wix Subscription (5 minutes)

Wait 30 days before canceling. Why? Verify everything works, check rankings, ensure no broken links. Once you cancel, Wix deletes your site after 14 days.

Pro tip: Export one final backup

Even though you can't import it elsewhere, keep a copy of your Wix content. You might need to reference something later.

SEO Preservation During Migration

Follow this checklist and your SEO will recover in 6-8 weeks. Skip steps and you'll lose ranking.

‚úì

Keep URL structure identical if possible

If Wix page is /about, make new page /about. Same for /services, /contact, /blog/post-title. Zero redirect needed = zero SEO loss.

‚úì

Match meta titles and descriptions exactly

Copy title tags, meta descriptions, heading structure. Google uses these to understand content. Changes = ranking drop.

‚úì

Set up 301 redirects for changed URLs

301 = permanent redirect. Tells Google "this page moved here, update your index." Preserves 90-95% of ranking power.

‚úì

Submit new sitemap to Google Search Console

Speed up indexing. Google discovers new pages faster, updates ranking sooner. Do this within 24 hours of going live.

‚úì

Monitor rankings weekly

Expect a dip weeks 1-4. Recovery begins week 5. If no recovery by week 8, check for technical issues or redirect mistakes.

‚úì

Don't change content during migration

Keep the same text, same headings, same structure. Save content updates for after SEO recovers (week 9+). Too many changes = confusion.
