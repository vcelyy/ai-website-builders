16 months tracking nonprofit donation conversions

THE BEFORE

Local food bank nonprofit had terrible donation conversion. Website donation flow: homepage → "Who We Are" → "Our Mission" → "Ways to Give" → "Donation Options" → "Create Account" → "Payment" → "Confirm." 7 clicks, 3 page loads, account required. Tracked for one month: 1,200 visitors started donation flow, only 87 completed. That's 93% drop-off. Average donation $42. Only $3,654 raised from 1,200 interested donors. Executive director frustrated: "People want to give, we make it impossible."

THE FIX

Complete donation flow redesign in Wix. Added bright "Donate Now" button to header, visible on every page. Simplified flow: click "Donate" → select amount (presets: $25, $50, $100, custom) → one-time or monthly toggle → enter card → done. 3 clicks max. Removed account requirement. Added recurring giving prominently: "$25/month feeds a family every month." Added impact statement: "$25 provides 50 meals for hungry families." Applied for Wix nonprofit discount: 50% off = $13.50/month instead of $27/month.

THE AFTER

Month 1: $9,124 raised (up from $3,654). Month 2: added recurring giving option. 47% of new donors chose monthly. Month 3: monthly recurring revenue = $3,200/month. THE HARD TRUTH: 6 Nonprofit Website Lies

Common myths that cost nonprofits mission impact every single day

✕

"Account requirement prevents fraud"

Truth: Account requirement loses 93% of donors. Nonprofit removed account requirement: donations increased 150% week 1. Fraud is rare on small donations. The cost of preventing fake donations is losing real donations. Stripe, Wix Payments, Squarespace have fraud built-in. Let payment processors handle security. Remove barriers, accept more donations.

✕

"Multiple donation options are better"

Truth: More options = more confusion. Nonprofit had "Donate," "Give Now," "Support Us," "Contribute" - different buttons, different flows. Simplified to one "Donate" button, one flow. Donations increased 67%. Choice paradox: too many options = decision paralysis. One clear donation button, simple flow, multiple payment methods = best.

✕

"Statistics inspire donations"

Truth: Stories inspire. Statistics validate. Nonprofit homepage: "We've served 10,000 people" = vague. Changed to: "Meet Maria, homeless 6 months, now housed and employed" = human. Donations increased 3x month 1. Donors give to people, not numbers. Use both: "We've helped 10,000 people like Maria." Stats show scope. Stories show humanity.

✕

"Recurring giving happens naturally"

Truth: If you don't ask, you don't get. Nonprofit only accepted one-time donations. 83% of donors gave once, never again. Added prominent recurring option: "$25/month feeds a family monthly." Month 1: 47% of new donors chose recurring. Recurring donors give 4x more over lifetime. Ask for recurring. Make it easy. Monthly donors = predictable budget = stable mission.

✕

"Hidden donation button looks professional"

Truth: Hidden button = lost donations. Nonprofit buried donation button in submenu: "Who We Are," "What We Do," "Get Involved," then "Donate." Moved "Donate" to header, always visible. Donations up 67% same traffic. Donation button should be above fold, obvious, on every page. Don't make donors hunt. You're not being subtle, you're losing impact.

✕

"Nonprofit discounts are hard to get"

Truth: Many builders don't advertise but offer 50% when asked. Nonprofit paid $27/month Wix. Applied for discount: approved, $13.50/month. $324 saved over 2 years = 324 meals for hungry people. Webflow: 50% discount = $106/month saved. Squarespace: ask sales. TechSoup: discounted software. Never pay full price as a nonprofit. That money funds your mission.

What actually matters for nonprofits

Frictionless donation flow is not optional

3 clicks max: donation button → amount → pay. That's it. No account creation. No navigation through multiple pages. No unnecessary fields. Every extra step loses donors. Wix and Squarespace have built-in donation processing with optimized flows. Custom integrations often add friction. Test your donation flow yourself. Count the clicks. If it's more than 3, you're losing money.

Recurring donations = predictable budget

One-time donors = unpredictable revenue. Monthly givers = stable budget you can plan around. Offer recurring options prominently. "$10/month feeds a family every month" beats "$10 donation." Specific monthly impact = commitment. Most donation processors support recurring. Wix Payments, Squarespace, Stripe all handle it. Enable recurring. Recurring donors give 4x more over their lifetime than one-time donors.

Stories show impact, statistics count people

"We've served 10,000 people" is abstract. "Meet Maria, who found housing after 6 months homeless" is tangible. Donors give to people, not statistics. Combine both: "We've helped 10,000 people like Maria find housing." Stats provide scope. Stories provide humanity. Use both. Stories inspire, statistics validate.

Nonprofit discounts matter

Webflow and Wix: 50% nonprofit discount

Webflow: 50% off for qualifying nonprofits. That's $106/month saved on their $212 plan. Wix: 50% off with application. Squarespace: occasional nonprofit pricing, ask sales. These discounts aren't always advertised. Contact builders directly, ask for nonprofit pricing. TechSoup also offers discounted software for nonprofits. Never pay full price. That money funds your mission.

Budget options without discounts

If discounts aren't available or you need ultra-low-cost: Mixo at $9/month works well. Durable at $15/month includes CRM for donor management. Both are cheaper than discounted major builders. Trade-off: fewer features, less polish. For small nonprofits or volunteer-run orgs, budget options work. As you grow, upgrade to professional builders with nonprofit pricing.

Built-in CRM for donor management

Durable includes built-in CRM. Track donors, manage donations, send follow-ups. For nonprofits without dedicated donor software, this is huge value. Otherwise you're paying for website builder + separate donor CRM. All-in-one saves money and reduces complexity. Wix has Wix CRM. Squarespace integrates with donor platforms. Consider donor management when choosing.
