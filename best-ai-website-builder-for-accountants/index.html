Why I Changed My Mind About Accounting Websites

The "professional" trap that hurts your firm

I started this project thinking accounting websites needed to look "corporate." Blue navbars. Stock photos of handshakes. Words like "synergy" and "proactive."

Then I interviewed 12 small business owners who hire accountants.

What they actually want:

"Can this person explain taxes in plain English?"
"Do they understand MY industry?"
"Will they pick up the phone when I have a question?"

Not "Does this website look expensive?"

This changed everything. I stopped testing for "professional" templates and started testing for clarity, personality, and trust.

10Web + WordPress won because it lets accounting firms show expertise through content. Blog posts about tax law changes. FAQs that answer real questions. Team bios that feel human.

The recommendation:

Use 10Web if you want to build trust through content and expertise.
Use Squarespace if you want a beautiful site that "looks professional" with minimal effort.

Quick Verdict

10Web + WordPress - Build trust through content. Blog, tax tips, industry expertise. Perfect for firms that want to educate clients.

Squarespace - Beautiful templates that convey reliability. Zero technical work. Good if you just need "professional presence."

Durable AI - Fast but limited. Only for solo CPAs who need something up today and don't care about customization.

What Accounting Firms Actually Need

1. Service Pages That Explain, Don't List

Bad accounting website: "Services: Tax Prep, Bookkeeping, Consulting"
Good accounting website: "Tax Preparation: We handle everything from W-2s to Schedule C for freelancers. Here's exactly how we work."

My testing found: 10Web's WordPress makes it easy to create detailed service pages. Squarespace templates are pretty but limit how much you can customize each service description.

2. Trust Signals That Matter

Clients hire accountants based on trust, not aesthetics. They want to see:

  • CPA credentials visible - Don't hide your license
  • Team photos - Real humans, not stock illustrations
  • Client testimonials - With names and industries
  • Case studies - "How we saved a restaurant $15K in taxes"

My testing found: All builders support these, but WordPress (via 10Web) has the best plugins for testimonial management and case study layouts.

3. Lead Capture That Feels Professional

Accounting clients don't "signup" - they schedule consultations. Your site needs:

  • Consultation request form (not just "contact us")
  • Clear pricing tiers or "request a quote" CTA
  • Phone number visible above fold (callers convert better)
  • Newsletter signup for tax tips

My testing found: Squarespace has beautiful built-in forms. 10Web requires plugins like Gravity Forms or WPForms but gives you more control.
