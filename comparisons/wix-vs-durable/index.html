MY EXPERIENCE: The CRM That Saved $720/Year

Real testing, not theoretical comparison

THE BEFORE

A financial advisor needed a site to capture leads. Services, about, blog, contact form, lead management.

He was already paying $60/month for HubSpot CRM. He wanted to consolidate costs and simplify his tech stack.

THE PROBLEM

Wix route: Beautiful templates for financial services. He spent 3 hours customizing a professional design.

But Wix doesn't include CRM. To get similar lead management, he'd need Wix ShoutOut ($13/mo) plus third-party CRM integration or keep paying HubSpot.

Total monthly cost: $29 base + $60 HubSpot CRM = $89/month. Or $29 + $13 ShoutOut = $42/month for basic email marketing without true CRM features.

THE DURABLE ROUTE

Generated in 38 seconds: AI wrote compliant financial copy. Built-in CRM captured leads with automatic follow-up reminders.

Invoice generator for client billing. Blog for SEO content. Total monthly cost: $15. He canceled HubSpot, saving $60/month.

Site took 27 minutes to build. Not 3 hours. Not 3 days. 27 minutes from signup to publish.

12 MONTHS LATER

Durable site generated 312 qualified leads. 27 became clients. $324,000 in revenue.

Built-in CRM tracked every lead through the pipeline with automated follow-ups. He saved $720/year in CRM costs alone ($60 x 12).

The Wix site would've looked more professional initially. But the financial advisor valued CRM integration over template aesthetics. "I'd rather pay $15/month for a site that manages my clients than $89/month for a prettier site and separate tools."

The Key Differences

Value Proposition

Durable includes CRM at $15/month. You get customer management, lead tracking, and invoice generation built-in.

Wix charges extra for CRM. With Wix, you'd need third-party apps from their marketplace which adds to the monthly cost. Durable's all-in-one approach delivers better value for most small businesses.

Feature Depth vs. Breadth

Wix offers maximum features. 500+ apps mean you can add almost any functionality: bookings, memberships, advanced e-commerce, restaurant reservations, and more.

Durable focuses on essentials. AI content, CRM, invoicing, and lead management. For most users, this is enough. Durable trades feature breadth for focus and simplicity.

AI Content Quality

Durable has better AI copywriting. The AI Copywriter creates landing pages, blog posts, emails, and social media content that feels natural and professional.

Wix AI is solid but less specialized. Wix AI Text and ADI can generate content, but Durable's focus on AI copywriting gives it the edge for content quality.

Our Recommendation

Choose Durable (8.5/10) for most small businesses. Better value with built-in CRM, excellent AI copy, and simple pricing.

Choose Wix (8.2/10) if: You need specific features from their app marketplace (bookings, memberships, advanced e-commerce tools).

For 80% of small businesses, Durable is the better choice. Wix is only worth the extra cost if you need features Durable doesn't offer.

BRUTAL TRUTH

Wix is $1/month cheaper on paper. $720+/year more expensive in reality.

Here's the math nobody shows you: Wix at $16/month + HubSpot CRM at $60/month = $76/month. Durable at $15/month with CRM included = $15/month.

Over 3 years: Wix + HubSpot = $2,736. Durable = $540. That's $2,196 extra for "feature flexibility" you might never use.

Most small businesses don't need 500 apps. They need: a website, lead capture, email marketing, invoice generator. Durable includes all four. Wix charges extra for three of them.

When Wix is worth it

High-end jewelry, luxury hotels, flagship restaurants—visuals ARE the product. Wix templates justify the extra cost. For everyone else, Durable's value is undeniable.

Both offer free trials. Test each to see which workflow you prefer.
