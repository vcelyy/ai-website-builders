Do FBA sellers even need a website?

What should FBA sellers put on their website?

Minimum: Brand story/mission, product pages with comparison guides ("Our Product vs Competitors"), 30+ customer testimonials with photos, prominent "Buy on Amazon" buttons, contact email.

Should FBA sellers sell direct or only on Amazon?

How do I drive traffic from my brand site to Amazon?

--- ## The FBA Website Framework
### **Essential Pages for FBA Sellers**
**HOME PAGE:** - Hero: Best-selling product + email capture - Product catalog overview - Social proof (reviews, sales numbers) - Email signup with discount (10-15% off) - "Shop on Amazon" prominent buttons - Brand story teaser **PRODUCT PAGES:** - High-quality photos (multiple angles) - Product videos (demonstrations) - Detailed descriptions (more than Amazon allows) - Comparison charts (vs competitors) - Customer reviews (import from Amazon or new) - "Buy on Amazon" button (primary CTA) - Email capture for back-in-stock **BRAND STORY PAGE:** - Why you started - Behind-the-scenes (sourcing, manufacturing) - Your values and mission - Quality assurance process - Team (if applicable) - Sustainability efforts (if applicable) **EMAIL CAPTURE PAGES:** - Lead magnet: 10-15% discount code - Product registration (warranty requires email) - Newsletter signup (recipes, tips, use-cases) - VIP list (new product launches) - Back-in-stock notifications
--- ### **Email Capture Strategy**
**EXIT-INTENT POPUP:** - Triggered when user tries to leave - Offer: 10-15% discount code - Immediate value for email - High conversion (15-25% of visitors) **PRODUCT REGISTRATION:** - Warranty requires email signup - Increases customer lifetime value - Provides customer service contact - Builds email list passively **LEAD MAGNETS:** - Discount codes (10-15% off) - Educational content (recipes, tips, guides) - Exclusive access (new products, VIP pricing) - User-generated content (photo contests, reviews) **EMAIL MARKETING AUTOMATION:** - Welcome series (5 emails over 30 days) - Abandoned cart recovery (if selling direct) - Product launch sequences - Replenishment reminders (30-60-90 days) - VIP exclusives and offers
--- ### **Linking to Amazon Strategically**
**PRIMARY AMAZON BUTTONS:** - "Buy on Amazon" prominent on product pages - Above fold on mobile - Multiple buttons on long pages - Opens in new tab (keeps your site open) - Amazon attribution links (track performance) **SECONDARY CONVERSIONS:** - Email capture (primary goal) - Social media follows - SMS signup (for VIPs) - Direct purchase (if inventory allows) **THE STRATEGY:** - Amazon = Primary purchase destination - Your site = Email capture + brand building - Email = Repeat purchases at higher margin - Result: Higher customer lifetime value

--- ## Common FBA Website Mistakes
### **Mistake #1: Competing With Amazon** **THE PROBLEM:** Trying to sell direct instead of linking to Amazon. **THE FIX:** - Amazon is your primary sales channel - Your site complements, doesn't compete - "Buy on Amazon" as primary CTA - Focus on email capture, not direct sales ### **Mistake #2: No Email Capture** **THE PROBLEM:** Missing primary purpose of FBA website. **THE FIX:** - Exit-intent popup with discount - Product registration for warranty - Lead magnets (discounts, content) - Email signup above fold - Multiple capture points ### **Mistake #3: Poor Product Photos** **THE PROBLEM:** Using same photos as Amazon listing. **THE FIX:** - Additional angles not on Amazon - Lifestyle photos (products in use) - Demonstration videos - Comparison photos (vs competitors) - High-resolution gallery ### **Mistake #4: Not Building Brand** **THE PROBLEM:** Site looks like generic catalog. **THE FIX:** - Tell your story (why you started) - Behind-the-scenes content - Your values and mission - Social proof and press - Customer stories ### **Mistake #5: Ignoring Mobile** **THE PROBLEM:** 60-70% of traffic is mobile, site not optimized. **THE FIX:** - Mobile-first design - Fast loading (<3s) - Large "Buy on Amazon" buttons - Simplified navigation - Test on real phones
