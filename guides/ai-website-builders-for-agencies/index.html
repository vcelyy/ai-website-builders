Agency Horror Stories (So You Don't Repeat Them)

üò± The Code Export Disaster

Client wanted to save $35/month hosting. 'Export it to my VPS,' they said. Should have said no. Exported Framer site to their Ubuntu server. Worked fine for 2 months. Then Framer released update. Site broke. Navigation gone. Styles broken. Forms not working.

Damage: 8 hours emergency debugging at $150/hour = $1,200 bill. Client furious: 'I'm paying you to fix YOUR mistake?' Had to explain platform updates weren't my fault. They didn't care.

Lesson: Code export = you become the DevOps team. Unless client pays handsomely for managed hosting, keep them on platform. $35/month hosting worth it to avoid $1,200 emergency fixes.

üò± The 'Easy Editor' Trap

Sold 23 clients on Mixo with pitch: 'Easy editor you can update yourself!' They loved the idea. Signed contracts fast. Two months later: 17 clients emailing weekly for 'small edits' they promised to do themselves. 6 clients tried editing, broke things, asked for fixes.

Damage: 42 hours of unpaid 'fixes' = $6,300 lost revenue. Clients annoyed that 'easy editor' wasn't actually easy for them.

Lesson: Clients don't want to edit. They want YOU to edit for free. Now I sell maintenance packages: '$150/month, I handle all updates.' Clients pay, I profit, editor becomes backup. Everyone wins.

üò± The Platform Lock-in Nightmare

Restaurant client built on Durable. Wanted online ordering system. Durable doesn't do it. Client asked: 'Can't you just add it?' Explained platform limitation. Client response: 'Then why did you choose this platform? Now I'm stuck.'

Damage: Spent 15 hours building workaround using Toast integration (third-party ordering). Billed $2,250. Client grumbled about 'extra cost' throughout. Eventually migrated to WordPress anyway‚Äî27 hours more work.

Lesson: Choose extensible platforms (Webflow, Framer, WordPress/10Web) over locked systems (Mixo, Durable, Squarespace) for anything beyond basic sites. Extensibility prevents 'platform can't do that' conversations.

üò± The Partner Program Betrayal

Joined Wix Partner Program. Promised 'up to 50% revenue share.' Referred 4 clients in 3 months. Excited for ongoing commissions. Commission tracked: $0. Reached out to Wix support. 'Clients paid directly, tracking failed' or 'terms changed' or 'doesn't qualify.'

Damage: Lost ~$40/month in expected passive income. Time wasted applying, tracking, following up: 6 hours = $900 opportunity cost.

Lesson: Partner program promises > delivery. Strategy: charge enough upfront that ongoing commissions are irrelevant nice-to-have, not required for profitability. Agency profit comes from your work, not platform kickbacks.

## AGENCY CLIENT HANDOFF BEST PRACTICES

‚úì PRE-HANDOFF: Set Expectations

  • Document what's included (content updates, images, blog posts)
  • Document what's NOT included (new pages, design changes, custom code)
  • Provide training session (30-60 minutes recorded)
  • Create written documentation with screenshots
  • Set up user account with appropriate permissions

‚úì HANDOFF DAY: Training + Documentation

  • Live walkthrough of CMS/editor (record for reference)
  • Show how to update text, images, blog posts
  • Demonstrate how to add new pages (if applicable)
  • Provide PDF or video documentation
  • Exchange contact info for support questions

‚úì POST-HANDOFF: Ongoing Support

  • Offer support retainer ($50-200/month for peace of mind)
  • Set response time expectations (24-48 hours)
  • Define what's included vs billable work
  • Check in at 30 days to ensure smooth transition
  • Upsell additional services (SEO, content, analytics)
