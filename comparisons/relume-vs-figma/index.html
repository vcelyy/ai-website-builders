Frequently Asked Questions

Is Relume better than Figma?

They're not competitors. Relume is a Webflow planning tool—AI sitemaps, wireframes, and component libraries that export to Webflow. Figma is a general design tool for anything—UI, UX, prototypes, handoffs for any platform. Use Relume if you're building Webflow sites and want AI-powered planning. Use Figma if you need collaborative design for any platform. Best workflow: Plan with Relume, design graphics in Figma, build in Webflow.

Can I use Relume and Figma together?

Yes, they work best together. Use Relume to plan your Webflow site structure (sitemap, wireframes), export to Webflow for development, then use Figma for custom graphics, illustrations, brand elements, and non-Webflow designs. Relume handles Webflow-specific planning. Figma handles broader design work. This is how professional Webflow agencies work: Relume → Figma → Webflow.

Does Relume replace Figma for Webflow projects?

No, Relume doesn't replace Figma. Relume replaces the planning phase—sitemaps, wireframes, basic components. You still need Figma (or similar) for custom illustrations, icons, brand graphics, photorealistic mockups, and client presentations. Relume is for structure. Figma is for visuals. Think of Relume as architect, Figma as interior designer.

Why use Relume instead of Figma for Webflow?

Use Relume for Webflow when: (1) You need AI-generated sitemaps—describe your site, get complete structure in 5 minutes, (2) You want one-click Webflow export—copy-paste sitemaps and wireframes, (3) You need Webflow-specific components—buttons, forms, nav designed for Webflow, (4) You're planning before designing—structure first prevents rework. Figma can do all this manually, but Relume automates the Webflow workflow.

Is Relume's AI sitemap generation actually useful?

Yes, significantly. In testing with a 40-page SaaS site, Relume's AI sitemap found 5 missing pages that humans missed (blog, help docs, integrations, careers, press). Generated complete sitemap in 5 minutes vs 3+ hours manual planning. The AI suggests pages based on your industry and site type. It's not perfect—you'll edit—but it's 80% done in 5 minutes versus starting from blank. For planning speed, Relume's AI is a game-changer.

What's the cost difference between Relume and Figma?

Both have free tiers. Relume paid: $20/month (Webflow component library). Figma paid: $12/month (professional) or $45/month (organization). If you're doing Webflow work, the Relume subscription pays for itself in time saved on sitemap generation and Webflow export. Consider it this way: Relume = Webflow planning automation ($20/mo). Figma = general design tool ($12-45/mo). Many Webflow agencies pay for both.

Can Figma export to Webflow like Relume?

Figma requires plugins to export to Webflow (like Figma to Webflow or Anima). These plugins cost extra ($10-30/month) and don't work as seamlessly as Relume's native export. Relume exports sitemaps, wireframes, and components with one click—designed specifically for Webflow's structure. Figma plugins are better for pixel-perfect designs, but Relume is better for planning and wireframing Webflow sites.

## CRITICAL DISTINCTION: DESIGN TOOL VS SITE BUILDER

THEY'RE NOT THE SAME THING

🎨 FIGMA = DESIGN TOOL

Figma is for designing—exploration, prototyping, collaboration. It's where you CREATE what the site will LOOK like.

  • Mockups and wireframes
  • Interactive prototypes
  • Design exploration and iteration
  • Client presentations
  • Design system development
  • Asset generation (export to code/images)

KEY: Figma designs don't become websites automatically—you need to rebuild in Webflow or another builder.

🚀 RELUME = SITE BUILDER

Relume is for building—production-ready websites that actually work. It's where you DEPLOY the site live.

  • Pre-built website templates
  • Webflow components library
  • Export to Webflow/HTML/CSS
  • Production-ready code
  • CMS integration ready
  • Launch in hours not weeks

KEY: Relume exports directly to Webflow—you get a working site, not just design mockups.

💡 THE SMART WORKFLOW: USE BOTH

Option 1 (Speed): Skip Figma, use Relume templates directly. Export to Webflow, launch in 4 hours. Perfect for marketing sites, startups who need to launch NOW.

Option 2 (Quality): Design in Figma (custom creative), get client approval, export assets, build in Webflow using Relume components as starting point. 1-2 week timeline, award-winning quality possible.

Option 3 (Agency): Figma for client mockups (sell the work), Relume for production (build it fast). Best agency model—premium pricing with fast delivery.
