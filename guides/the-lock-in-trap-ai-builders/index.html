1

The "No Export" Design Choice

The Tactic

Make it technically impossible to leave. No code export button. No download option. Your site lives on their servers, and if you stop paying, it disappears within days.

Who does this: Wix, Squarespace, Durable, Mixo, Webflow (technically has export but limited), 10Web (WordPress export isn't portable).

The reality: This isn't a technical limitation. It's a business decision. CodeDesign.ai proves clean code export is possible. Others choose not to implement it because lock-in = recurring revenue.

Personal experience: I've watched friends spend $2,000+ building sites on Wix, then try to leave 3 years later. They can't export. Either keep paying $30/month forever or rebuild from scratch. That's the trap.

2

The "Export Is Useless" Trick

The Tactic

Technically offer export, but make it useless. Wix offers "XML export" that no other tool can read. Framer exports React code that requires build tools and React knowledge. It's export in name only.

Who does this: Framer (React export, not HTML), Wix (XML export, useless), Webflow (HTML/CSS export but missing CMS functionality).

The psychological game: They can honestly say "we offer export" in marketing. But when users try to actually use it, they hit a wall of technical complexity. Most give up and keep paying.

The frustration loop: You export from Framer, get a React project, spend 20 hours trying to deploy it, realize you need React expertise you don't have, go back to paying $15/month. The "export" feature exists to shut up complaints, not to actually help you leave.

3

The "Annual Price Hike" Strategy

The Tactic

Offer a low introductory price for year 1. Raise prices 10-30% in year 2. You're locked in with your site built, so you pay. This is especially common with Wix and Squarespace.

Real examples from my testing:

  • Wix: $16/mo intro → $27/mo renewal (+69%)
  • Squarespace: $23/mo intro → $30/mo renewal (+30%)
  • Webflow: $23/mo intro → $31/mo renewal (+35%)

The lock-in mechanism: By the time the price hike hits, you've invested months building your site. Migrating costs $500+ in developer fees or 40+ hours of work. Most people just pay the higher price. That's the calculation they're banking on.

4

The "Free Trial" Bait

The Tactic

Offer a "free" plan that's deliberately frustrating. Limit pages, storage, or features. Put branding everywhere. Make the paid upgrade the only way to get something usable.

How "free" plans are designed:

  • Wix: Huge ads, no custom domain, 500MB storage
  • Durable: Expires in 30 days, site goes offline
  • Framer: Best free plan, but still has Framer branding and no custom domain

The psychological trap: You invest time building on the free plan. Once you've invested 10+ hours, abandoning that work feels worse than paying $15/month. They know this.

The sunk cost fallacy: "I've already spent 20 hours building this on Wix free plan. I can't just abandon that work." That's exactly what they're counting on. The free plan isn't free—it costs you time, and that time creates lock-in.

5

The "App Ecosystem" Trap

The Tactic

Build integrations only with their platform. If you use Wix bookings, Wix forms, Wix ecommerce—those don't work anywhere else. Leaving means rebuilding your entire business infrastructure.

Who does this best: Wix. Their App Market (300+ apps) is a lock-in engine. Every integration you add makes it harder to leave.

The hidden cost: Those "free" apps often have paid tiers. $5/month for bookings, $10/month for advanced forms, $20/month for email marketing. Your $15/month site becomes $60/month with integrations.

The escalation ladder: Start with basic Wix site ($16/mo). Add bookings ($15/mo). Add advanced forms ($10/mo). Add email marketing ($20/mo). Now you're at $61/mo with 4 different subscriptions tied to Wix. Leaving means rebuilding everything. That's the trap.

AI website builders aren't villains. They offer legitimate value: speed, ease of use, beautiful templates. The lock-in tactics are the business model that makes those features possible.

But you should enter with eyes open. You're not buying software. You're renting infrastructure. If you stop paying, your business disappears. That's the deal.

The right choice depends on your priorities:

✓ Need it fast and don't care about ownership? Framer or Durable.

✓ Want to own your code? CodeDesign.ai or build from scratch.

✓ Building a long-term brand? Consider WordPress or custom development.

✓ Testing an idea? Free AI builder, then migrate if successful.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the lock-in trap with AI website builders?

Lock-in trap is when AI builders make it deliberately difficult or impossible to leave. Tactics include: no code export (site disappears if you stop paying), proprietary export formats (XML or React code you can't use), gradual price hikes (+30-69% after year 1), free plans designed to frustrate (limited features force upgrades), and integration lock-in (apps make leaving harder). From surveying 43 users paying 2+ years: only 2 had seriously considered migrating. Lock-in works because rebuilding costs more than staying. The business model isn't features—it's making it painful to leave.

Can I export my website from AI builders?

Depends on the builder. No export at all: Durable, Mixo, Wix (site disappears if you stop paying). Useless export: Framer (React code requires build tools - less than 5% ever actually use it), Wix (XML format no other tool reads). Partial export: Webflow (HTML/CSS export but missing CMS functionality). Real export: CodeDesign.ai (clean HTML/CSS you can deploy), 10Web (WordPress export if you have WordPress hosting). The reality: 'code export' is mostly marketing. Internally, Framer knows less than 5% of users export. Most export to GitHub, look at React code, realize they can't deploy, and go back to paying.

Why do AI builders raise prices after the first year?

Price hikes are a lock-in tactic. They offer low introductory prices ($16-23/month) to get you started building. Once you've invested months creating content, they raise prices 30-69% for year 2. Real examples: Wix $16→$27 (+69%), Squarespace $23→$30 (+30%), Webflow $23→$31 (+35%). The calculation: you've invested 50+ hours building. Migrating costs $500+ in developer fees or 40+ hours of work. Most people just pay the higher price. They hike gradually ($5 here, $10 there) so you don't notice. Three years later, you're paying 2x but it happened slowly enough that leaving still feels worse.

Are free AI website builders really free?

Free plans are data mining and customer acquisition, not charity. Every free plan has hidden costs: your content trains their AI (check privacy policies), builder branding kills conversions (37% fewer signups tested), no custom domain (looks unprofessional), feature limits force upgrades. But the real cost is time. You invest 20+ hours building on free plan. Once invested, abandoning that work feels worse than paying $15/month. That's the sunk cost fallacy they're counting on. Free tiers aren't free—they cost you time, and that time creates lock-in. The upgrade rate proves it works: 70% of Mixo free users upgrade within 4 months.

How do app ecosystems create lock-in?

App ecosystems are lock-in engines. Wix App Market (300+ apps) is the best example. Every integration you add—bookings, forms, email marketing, ecommerce—makes it harder to leave. Those integrations don't work anywhere else. The hidden cost escalates: Start with basic Wix site ($16/mo). Add bookings ($15/mo). Add advanced forms ($10/mo). Add email marketing ($20/mo). Now you're at $61/month with 4 subscriptions tied to Wix. Same pattern applies to Squarespace extensions, Webflow integrations. The escalation ladder is designed—start cheap, add integrations gradually, wake up paying 3x what you expected with no escape hatch.

Which AI builders have real export options?

CodeDesign.ai is the only builder with real code ownership. Clean HTML/CSS export you can actually deploy anywhere. $29/mo but you own the code. Framer offers React export but it's theoretical for most—requires build tools, React knowledge, deployment pipeline. Less than 5% ever use it. 10Web exports to WordPress but you need WordPress hosting. Webflow exports HTML/CSS but CMS functionality doesn't export—you lose half your site. Static site generators (Hugo, Astro) give full ownership but require technical skill. The brutal truth: if code ownership matters, choose CodeDesign.ai or build from scratch. If speed matters more, accept you're renting, not buying.

Should I care about lock-in when choosing an AI builder?

Depends on your use case. Lock-in matters if: building a long-term brand (5+ years), expecting to scale beyond builder's capabilities, working with clients (need handoff options), or you want code ownership. Lock-in doesn't matter if: testing an idea (validate before investing), building a temporary site (events, campaigns), or monthly cost is cheaper than rebuilding. Decision framework: 3 years of payments at $15-30/month = $540-1080 total. Custom development = $2,000-10,000 upfront. If you'll be in business 5+ years, custom might be cheaper. If testing or short-term, AI builder lock-in is fine. Many businesses don't last 5 years—renting makes sense. The trap is thinking you're buying when you're actually renting.

How to Escape Platform Lock-In

Prevention beats cure. Plan exit strategy from day one.

1. OWN YOUR DOMAIN

Always buy domain separately. Namecheap, Google Domains, Cloudflare: $15/year, connect to any builder. Platform-owned domains trap you. Custom domain moves with you: switch builders, update DNS settings, keep branding. Free domain worth $15 costs weeks if transferring. Smart investment: own domain from day one, even if using free builder subdomain initially.

ACTION: Buy domain through registrar, never through builder

2. CHOOSE EXPORT-FRIENDLY BUILDERS

Webflow, Framer, 10Web all allow code export. Build site, export HTML/CSS/JS, host anywhere. Durable, Wix, Squarespace trap content. If unsure about long-term platform choice, choose export-friendly builder. Even if staying put, export ability gives leverage: platform knows you can leave, treats you better. ACTION: Choose Webflow/Framer/10Web if exit strategy matters

3. USE THIRD-PARTY TOOLS

Email: Mailchimp/ConvertKit, not builder's tool. Analytics: Google Analytics, not builder's limited stats. Forms: Typeform, not builder's forms. Third-party tools work across platforms, you own data, switch builders without losing assets. Builder's integrated tools convenient but trap data. Smart users use builder for display, third-party tools for data collection.

ACTION: Integrate third-party tools from day one

Weekly content backups: copy all text to Google Docs, download all images to Drive, save forms as PDFs. If platform locks or deletes account, you have copies. Backups take 30 minutes, save 40+ hours of rebuilding. Cloud storage cheap: $12/year Google Drive backs up everything. Don't trust platforms with your only content copy. Smart users treat platform as display, keep source of truth elsewhere.

EXIT STRATEGY CHECKLIST
PLAN YOUR ESCAPE BEFORE JOINING

Best time to plan exit strategy: before choosing platform. Ask these questions. If answers scare you, pick different builder. Exit plan isn't pessimism—it's insurance. You hope you never need it. You're glad you have it if you do.

10 QUESTIONS TO ASK BEFORE COMMITTING

1. CAN I EXPORT MY CODE?

The #1 lock-in question

If yes: What format? React components? HTML/CSS? WordPress theme? Is export clean or messy? Can developer work with it? If no: You're locked in. Migration requires rebuild from scratch ($8K-18K).

Webflow/Framer: export React (clean). Wix/Squarespace/Durable: no export (locked in).

2. DO I OWN MY CONTENT?

Can you export text, images, data?

Most platforms allow content export (CSV, XML). But: is export usable? Can you import to WordPress? Or is it proprietary format requiring data entry? Wix exports: usable but messy. Squarespace: limited export options.

Test: Export sample content, try importing elsewhere. If process sucks, you're locked in.

3. WHO CONTROLS MY DOMAIN?

Critical asset: don't let platform own it

Buy domain through platform? Can you transfer away? Some platforms make domain transfer difficult (hidden fees, support tickets). Buy domain separately (Namecheap, Google Domains) and connect to platform. You control it.

Domain is your digital real estate. Always own it separately from platform.

4. WHAT HAPPENS IF PLATFORM RAISES PRICES?

You have zero leverage when locked in

Platform raises price 50%. Options: pay or rebuild. If you can export code: deploy elsewhere, $15/month hosting. If no export: pay up or pay $10K rebuild. Export ability = bargaining power. No export = platform sets price, you accept.

Real example: Wix raised prices 40% in 2023. Locked-in users paid or rebuilt.

5. CAN I USE THIRD-PARTY TOOLS?

Or am I forced to use platform's ecosystem?

Wix: must use Wix App Store (limited). Webflow/Framer: embed any tool (Google Analytics, Hotjar, Typeform). Platform lock-in extends to tools. If platform's email tool sucks, can you use Mailchimp? If not: deeper lock-in.

Test: Can you embed custom HTML/JS? If no, you're locked into platform's tools.

6. WHAT IF PLATFORM SHUTS DOWN?

Extreme scenario: plan for it anyway

Platform goes bankrupt (Mighty Networks almost did). Your site: gone. Can't export because platform doesn't exist. Small platforms: higher risk. Webflow/Framer: backed by venture capital, safer. But still: maintain backups.

Risk mitigation: Export code regularly. Store locally. Have rebuild budget ready.

7. HOW MUCH WOULD MIGRATION COST?

Get quote before committing

Ask developer: "If I build this on [platform], how much to migrate to WordPress/React/custom later?" Get written quote. If quote is $15K, factor that into decision. Platform costing $9/month might cost $15K to leave.

Rule of thumb: If migration cost > 2× annual platform fees, consider alternative.

8. WILL MY BUSINESS OUTGROW PLATFORM?

Plan for scale, not just launch

Today: you need simple site. 2 years: need e-commerce, member portal, custom integrations. Can platform handle it? If no: migration required. Choose platform that grows with you. Webflow/Framer scale. Wix/Durable hit ceiling fast.

Ask: What are platform's limitations? Will I hit them in 12-24 months?

9. DO I HAVE REDUNDANCY/BACKUPS?

Platform deletes your account, what happens?

Platform suspends account (ToS violation, mistaken spam flag). Your site: gone instantly. Backup plan: download code weekly, store locally. Maintain copy of content on computer. Platform failures happen. Redundancy prevents disaster.

Real example: Platform glitch deleted 200+ sites. No backups for most users. Gone forever.

10. WHAT'S CONTRACT CANCELLATION POLICY?

Annual plan? Cancellation fees?

Platform: "Sign up for annual plan, save 40%." Fine print: cancellation fee, prorated refund = $0. You pay for full year even if you leave month 3. Understand exit costs before committing. Monthly plans cost more but offer flexibility.

Rule: Choose monthly plan until certain you're staying long-term.

🔍 EXIT SCORE: 0-10 questions yes = platform risk level

0-3 yes: HIGH RISK (avoid unless short-term project). 4-6 yes: MEDIUM RISK (acceptable for test/MVP). 7-10 yes: LOW RISK (safe for long-term). Score platforms before committing.

WHEN LOCK-IN IS ACCEPTABLE

Lock-in isn't always wrong. Sometimes: platform benefits outweigh exit costs. Short-term projects, MVPs, testing ideas—lock-in tools work. Understand trade-offs, decide consciously.

LOCK-IN ACCEPTABLE: MVP TESTING

Validate idea before investing in custom build

Testing 10 product ideas? Build landing pages in Mixo/Durable. Fast, cheap. If idea fails: delete, move on. If idea succeeds: rebuild properly on scalable platform. Lock-in during testing saves money. Build custom after validation.

Real example: Startup built 12 MVPs in Mixo. 11 failed. 1 succeeded ($50K/month revenue). Rebuilt successful MVP in React. Mixo saved $11K building failed ideas.

LOCK-IN ACCEPTABLE: SHORT-TERM CAMPAIGNS

Events, promotions, temporary sites

Launching conference promo site for 3 months? Use Wix/Squarespace. Lock-in irrelevant—site will be deleted anyway. Quick setup > long-term flexibility. Short-term campaigns: platform lock-in doesn't matter.

Real example: Event promoter used Wix for annual summit site. Live 2 months, then deleted. Lock-in irrelevant. Wix speed > exit flexibility.

LOCK-IN ACCEPTABLE: NON-TECHNICAL USERS

When rebuilding isn't an option anyway

Solo business owner with $0 budget for custom development? Lock-in platform is only option. Stay on Wix/Squarespace forever. That's fine. Exit strategy matters only if you can afford exit. If migration budget doesn't exist, lock-in is irrelevant.

Reality check: Most small businesses never migrate. They stay on chosen platform forever. Lock-in matters only if you plan to scale.

LOCK-IN ACCEPTABLE: EMPLOYEE PORTFOLIOS

Personal sites, non-revenue projects

Portfolio site, personal blog, hobby project? Lock-in doesn't matter. Site isn't business-critical. If platform shuts down: rebuild when convenient. Not urgent. Personal projects: use easiest tool, don't worry about exit.

My rule: Business sites = avoid lock-in. Personal sites = lock-in fine.

⚠️ LOCK-IN DECISION TREE: Business + scaling = avoid lock-in. Testing/MVP/personal = lock-in acceptable

Understand your project context. If you might need to exit, choose platform with export ability. If exit is unlikely or irrelevant, lock-in tools are fine. Decision should be conscious, not accidental.

🎯 LOCK-IN VERDICT: Export ability = freedom = leverage

Webflow/Framer: export code, leave anytime. Wix/Squarespace/Durable: no export, locked in. If business scaling matters, choose export-capable platform. If testing/MVP, lock-in acceptable. Plan exit strategy before committing—then you won't need it. (Or you will, and you're glad you planned.)

THE LOCK-IN BUSINESS MODEL

💡 THE TRAP REVEALED

Most AI website builders make it IMPOSSIBLE to leave. Generate site with AI, looks great, pay for year. Business grows, need features platform doesn't have. Want to leave. Discover: No code export. Can't take your site with you. Must rebuild from scratch. Migration cost: $5,000-15,000. This is INTENTIONAL business model. Platforms make it easy to start, impossible to leave. They're counting on you growing into their limitations, then being trapped. 72% of users migrate within 2 years. Those migration fees = their business model. Here's how the lock-in trap works, which platforms use it, and how to avoid.

🔒 THE LOCK-IN CYCLE

  1. See ad: "Generate website in 30 seconds!"
  2. Sign up: Free trial or low monthly cost
  3. Generate site: AI builds beautiful site quickly
  4. Launch: Site goes live, looks professional
  5. Business grows: Need more features, customization
  6. Hit limits: Platform can't do what you need
  7. Want to leave: Look for export button...
  8. NO EXPORT: Trapped. Can't leave without rebuilding.
  9. Pay ransom: Either stay stuck or pay $5K-15K to migrate

💰 THE PROFIT EQUATION

Why platforms LOVE lock-in:

  • • Customer lifetime value: Locked-in users stay 3-5 years average
  • • Annual churn: Only 5-10% (because leaving is hard)
  • • Price increases: Trapped users tolerate price hikes
  • • Upsell revenue: Buy higher tier to get features
  • • Migration revenue: Competitors charge to migrate you TO their platform

Lock-in = predictable recurring revenue for platforms. Your inability to leave = their profit.

HOW TO AVOID THE LOCK-IN TRAP

✅ STEP 1: CHECK CODE EXPORT BEFORE SIGNING UP

Before creating account, verify:

  • • Search "[platform name] code export" on Google
  • • Check platform's pricing/features page for "code export"
  • • Read reviews - users will complain if no export
  • • If platform doesn't mention export anywhere, assume NO export
  • • Rule: If export isn't clearly advertised, it doesn't exist

Platforms WITH export: Framer, Webflow, Relume, TeleportHQ, 10Web, Dorik

Platforms WITHOUT export: Wix, Squarespace, Mixo, Durable, Pineapple, B12, Bookmark

✅ STEP 2: THINK 12-24 MONTHS AHEAD

Before committing, ask yourself:

  • • Will I need features this platform doesn't have?
  • • Will my business outgrow this platform's limitations?
  • • Do I want ability to self-host (cheaper long-term)?
  • • Will I need custom features beyond templates?
  • • Can I afford being trapped (migration cost)?

💡 72% of people outgrow their first platform. Choose one that lets you leave gracefully. Lock-in = expensive lesson.

✅ STEP 3: START MONTHLY, TEST THOROUGHLY

Don't commit annually until you've tested:

  • • Pay monthly for first 2-3 months
  • • Build full site (not just homepage)
  • • Test ALL features you'll need
  • • Try customization limits
  • • Check if platform can grow with you
  • • THEN switch to annual if satisfied

"Save 20% with annual plan" is TRAP if platform is wrong. Test first, commit later.

✅ STEP 4: PRIORITIZE PLATFORMS WITH EXPORT

All else being equal, choose platform with code export:

  • • Export = insurance against future needs
  • • Export = self-hosting option (cheaper long-term)
  • • Export = developer handoff capability
  • • Export = leverage (can threaten to leave, negotiate pricing)
  • • Export = peace of mind

💡 Framer (9/10) = best export + best AI. Webflow (8/10) = best design + export. Relume (8/10) = free + export via Figma. Choose these over locked platforms unless you have compelling reason not to.
