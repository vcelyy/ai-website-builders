Consultant Website Essentials: What Actually Converts

Must-Have: Case Studies with Metrics

Why It Works: Clients don't hire consultants for potential - they hire for proven results. Case studies with specific numbers prove capability.

Real Example: Business consultant's case study: "Helped e-commerce client increase revenue from $120K to $340K in 6 months." Specific numbers = client inquiries.

Implementation: 3-5 detailed case studies minimum. Include: client problem, your solution, specific results with metrics, client testimonial.

Killer Mistake: Vague Service Descriptions

The Problem: "I help businesses grow" means nothing. "I help e-commerce stores increase revenue 40% in 6 months" means everything.

Why It Fails: Clients can't differentiate you from hundreds of other consultants. Vague = invisible = no inquiries.

The Fix: Specific outcomes for specific industries. Financial advisor switched from "wealth management" to "help tech professionals optimize equity compensation." Inquiries doubled.

Must-Have: Clear CTA

Why It Works: Most consultant websites bury contact info. Prominent "Book Discovery Call" button removes friction. Business consultant's case study CTAs increased clicks 35% with micro-interactions.

Implementation: CTA above fold, after every case study, in navigation. Use action-oriented text: "Book Free Consultation" not "Contact Us."

Nice-to-Have: Booking Calendar

Why It Helps: Removes email tag. Financial advisor's booking calendar captured 22 inquiries in 6 months. Clients book when they're ready, not when you reply.

Implementation: Calendly or Acuity embedded. Don't need fancy booking system - simple calendar works. Durable has built-in integration.

The Consultant Website Truth

Your results get clients, not your website design. But your website must communicate those results clearly. Case studies with specific metrics are mandatory.

Niche specialization wins. "I help e-commerce stores" beats "I help all businesses" every time. Financial advisor's focus on "tech professionals" doubled inquiries.

Platform choice matters less than content. Business consultant's 8 clients on Framer free tier. Marketing consultant's 12 clients on Webflow at $23/mo. Both worked because content was compelling.

Bottom line: Start with Framer free tier. If you generate real results, upgrade to Webflow when content marketing becomes priority. Don't overinvest in platform before proving your consulting model works.
