The "Am I Too Broke for Framer?" Decision Tree

Be honest with yourself

Here's the thing: Framer costs $15/month. Hostinger costs $2.99/month. That's a $12 difference.

Question 1: Will a bad-looking website cost you more than $144/year ($12/month)?

If you're a photographer, designer, consultant, or anyone selling based on credibility: Yes. A dated website loses you clients. Hostinger's 2015-era templates will cost you far more than $144/year in lost business.

Question 2: Are you testing an idea with zero revenue yet?

If you're validating a concept: Use Hostinger. Spend $3, test for 3 months, if it works then rebuild in Framer. You just saved $36+ on a might-fail idea.

Question 3: Do you have literally zero budget?

If $3 is stretching it: Hostinger. No judgment. Get something online, upgrade later when you can afford it.

The decision isn't about "which is better." Framer wins, obviously. It's about whether you're in the position to pay for quality.

I use Hostinger for throwaway tests. I use Framer for anything real. That's the honest distinction.
