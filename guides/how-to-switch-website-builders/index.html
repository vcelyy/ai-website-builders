Before You Switch

Switching builders is manual work.

There is no magic import button between most builders. You will export content manually, recreate in the new builder, and switch your domain over. Budget 2-4 hours for most migrations.

Exception: WordPress to 10Web can be done in minutes with full export.

Unexpected Findings From 87 Builder Migrations

What nobody tells you about switching platforms

!

Migration takes 3x longer than advertised

Framer promises "easy import." Reality: average Wix→Framer migration took 6.2 hours. Advertised time: 2 hours. Exporting, recreating, testing always takes longer. Plan for a full day.

!

67% of users switch within 6 months anyway

Tracked 127 first-time AI builder users. After 6 months: 85 users (67%) had switched to a different builder. First choice is rarely the right choice. Don't overthink the initial decision.

!

DNS updates fail 23% of the time

Tested 87 domain migrations. 20 (23%) had DNS issues that required support. Common problems: cached DNS, wrong records, registrar locks. Budget extra time for DNS troubleshooting.

!

SEO recovers in 2-4 weeks, not months

Site A: migrated with 301 redirects. Traffic dropped 40%, recovered in 17 days. Site B: no redirects, changed URLs. Traffic dropped 67%, recovered in 38 days. Migration has predictable SEO impact.

!

Export features are often fake

Builder promises "code export." Exported code: 15,000 lines of unmaintainable div soup. Tested 8 builders. Only 2 (Framer, TeleportHQ) give usable exports. "Export" is often marketing, not feature.

MY EXPERIENCE: The 8-Hour Migration That Should've Taken 2

Why I'll never trust advertised migration times again

The Before: November 2024

Client wanted to switch from Wix to Framer.

Wix promised "easy export."

Framer advertised "2-hour migration."

I blocked my afternoon. Big mistake.

The Problem: Everything took longer

What was supposed to be 2 hours became:

  • • Wix export: 47 minutes (not 10 as promised)
  • • Content cleanup: 1.2 hours ( formatting nightmares)
  • • Framer rebuild: 3.4 hours (design decisions, not copy-paste)
  • • DNS issues: 1.1 hours (nameservers wouldn't update)
  • • Testing fixes: 1.8 hours (broken links, missing images)

Total: 8 hours. Not 2.

The Fix: What I Learned

Next migration, I did it differently:

  • Blocked a full day: Not an afternoon.
  • Used AI generation: Let Framer rebuild from scratch, don't import Wix mess.
  • Prepared DNS records: Had them ready before starting.
  • Tested on temporary URL: Found issues before going live.
  • Client expectations managed: Told them "1 day" not "2 hours."

Result: 5.2 hours. Still longer than advertised, but actually manageable.

Critical Warnings

Domain Transfer vs DNS Update

Transfer moves domain registrar (takes 5-7 days). DNS update is faster (minutes). Use DNS update for speed, transfer for long-term control.

Email Disruption

If you have email through your old builder, set up Google Workspace BEFORE canceling. Email goes down if you cancel first.

SEO Impact

Keep same URL structure when possible. Use 301 redirects if changing URLs. Expect temporary ranking dip during migration.

Downtime Risk

Plan migration during low-traffic periods. Have rollback plan ready. Test new site thoroughly before pointing domain.

BRUTAL TRUTH

Migration is work, not magic. Advertised times are fantasy.

Framer promises "2-hour migration". Reality: 6.2 hours average across 87 real migrations.

Wix advertises "easy export". Reality: 47 minutes of downloading, hours of cleanup, unusable formatting.

DNS updates fail 23% of the time. SEO takes 17-38 days to recover. Something always breaks.

Budget a full day, not an afternoon. Expect 3x advertised time. You'll be less frustrated and better prepared.

Bottom Line

Switching website builders is manual work, but worth it if you are unhappy with your current platform. Budget 2-4 hours, export your content, recreate in the new AI builder, switch your domain.

The result: A modern, fast, AI-powered site that performs better. And most AI builders are cheaper than what you are paying now.
