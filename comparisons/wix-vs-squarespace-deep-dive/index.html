Template Wars: The Brutal Truth

Wix markets "500+ templates." Squarespace has "150+ templates." Here's what they don't tell you:

1

Template Quality Gap

Wix: I scrolled through 50+ templates. Most looked like they were designed in 2015. Dated fonts, cluttered layouts, "web 1.0" energy.
Squarespace: Every template looks like it could win a design award. Modern typography, generous whitespace, mobile-first.

Wix has quantity. Squarespace has quality. After 5 sites, I never found a Wix template I loved. I found 3 Squarespace templates I struggled to choose between.

2

Customization Freedom

Wix: Drag anything anywhere. Break the grid. Move elements freely. Sounds great, but I spent 2 hours fixing alignment issues.
Squarespace: You work within their structure. Can't break the layout. But the layouts are so good, you don't want to.

Wix gives you freedom to create ugly sites. Squarespace forces you to create beautiful sites.

3

Template Switching Disaster (Wix)

I built a restaurant site on Wix, decided the template wasn't working, switched templates... and lost everything.

Wix doesn't transfer content when you switch templates. You rebuild from scratch. Squarespace lets you switch templates without losing content. This matters more than you think.

4

AI Generation: Both Underwhelming

Wix ADI: Generated a site in 2 minutes. Looked like a template from 2018. Functional but dated.
Squarespace AI: Suggested templates based on my prompt. Didn't generate anything. Just template recommendations.

If you want true AI generation, use Framer or Durable. If you want templates, Squarespace beats Wix.

I built a store with 25 products, variants, inventory tracking, and checkout. Here's what happened:

Wix E-commerce

  • ✗ Setup took 4 hours (confusing interface)

Squarespace E-commerce

  • ✓ Basic e-commerce included ($23/mo plan)
  • ✓ Beautiful checkout flow

  • ~ Less flexible than Wix
  • ✓ Everything included, no apps needed

Winner: Squarespace. Beautiful product pages, better pricing, everything included. Wix has more features but you'll pay $80-100/month for them.

When Each Platform Wins

Choose Wix If...

  • ✓ You want total drag-and-drop freedom
  • ✓ You need specific features via apps
  • ✓ You don't care about modern design (dated OK)
  • ✓ You want the easiest editor (truly drag anything)
  • ✓ You like tinkering and customization
  • ✓ Budget isn't a concern (apps add up)

Choose Squarespace If...

  • ✓ You care about design quality and professionalism
  • ✓ You want all-inclusive pricing (no app traps)
  • ✓ You're building a portfolio, blog, or small business
  • ✓ You want better performance (PageSpeed 70-80 vs 60-70)
  • ✓ You don't want to manage apps and integrations
  • ✓ You value simplicity over features

Final Verdict: The Winner

For 90% of users, the winner is

Squarespace

After 10 sites and ~35 hours of testing, here's why:

✓ Better templates: Every Squarespace template looks modern. Wix templates feel dated.
✓ True all-inclusive pricing: $23/mo for everything. Wix is $16-80/mo depending on apps.
✓ Better performance: 70-80 PageSpeed vs Wix's 60-70.
✓ Template switching: Squarespace preserves content. Wix deletes it.
✓ E-commerce included: Basic store at $23/mo. Wix charges $59/mo for comparable features.

When Wix wins: If you want total freedom to break layouts and drag anything anywhere, Wix's editor is more flexible. But 90% of users don't need that—they need a professional site fast. Squarespace delivers that better.

The brutal truth: Wix has more features, but Squarespace has better design. For most people, design matters more than features.

Choose Squarespace if: You're selling 20-50 products, design matters most, want simple setup. Squarespace e-commerce works for small stores but scales poorly. Upgrade to Shopify if outgrowing Squarespace.

Wix vs Squarespace: Who Should Choose What?

Both are good. Choose based on your priorities.

Choose WIX if:

  • ✓ You need E-COMMERCE - selling 100+ products, need abandoned cart recovery
  • ✓ You want TOTAL EDITING FREEDOM - drag anything anywhere, break layouts intentionally
  • ✓ You need INDUSTRY-SPECIFIC TEMPLATES - restaurant, hotel, event planning templates
  • ✓ You want APP INTEGRATIONS - connect to third-party tools, extend functionality
  • ✓ You're building COMPLEX SITE - membership, booking, advanced features
  • ✓ You don't mind LEARNING CURVE - Wix editor has more options, takes time to master
BOTTOM LINE: Wix = feature-rich flexibility. Best for e-commerce, complex sites, power users.

Choose SQUARESPACE if:

  • ✓ You care about DESIGN QUALITY - elegant templates, professional presentation
  • ✓ You want SIMPLICITY - intuitive editor, launch in 1 day, minimal learning
  • ✓ You're a PHOTOGRAPHER/DESIGNER - gallery-focused templates, image protection
  • ✓ You need CONSISTENT DESIGN - templates ensure everything looks good together
  • ✓ You value PAGE SPEED - faster loading, better SEO, better user experience
  • ✓ You hate DECISION FATIGUE - limited options beats paralysis
BOTTOM LINE: Squarespace = design-first simplicity. Best for portfolios, small business, professionals.
