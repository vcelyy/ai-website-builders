6 Speed Myths That Waste Your Time

"Fastest generation = fastest to launch"

Wrong. Durable generates in 28 seconds but takes 2-3 hours to customize. Webflow generates in 4 minutes but takes 1 hour to customize. Total time: Durable 128 min, Webflow 64 min. Fastest generator = slowest total. Speed metric is backwards.

"I need it in 30 seconds or I can't use it"

You don't launch in 30 seconds. You edit for hours. The 3-minute difference between Durable and Webflow is 2% of total time. If you have 2-4 hours to build, 3 minutes doesn't matter. Final quality does. Choose based on output, not generation speed.

"Speed is what I'm paying for"

Speed is a marketing hook, not a product. Durable's "30-second website" is technically true but practically meaningless. 30 seconds gets you generic template with AI filler. Real humans don't launch that. They edit. And editing takes 10x longer than generation.

"I don't have time to wait 3 minutes"

You have time to edit for 2-4 hours though. The 3-minute wait is 2% of total project time. If you're choosing based on saving 3 minutes, you're optimizing for the wrong thing. Optimize for final quality, not first impression speed.

"Faster generation means more iterations"

Only if you're A/B testing landing pages. For building one site to launch, you generate once, edit once. Speed advantage only matters for volume generation. Most people aren't doing that. They're building one site.

"Clients care how fast it generates"

Clients care about the result, not the process. Show them Durable's 30-second demo: "Cool." Show them Webflow's 4-minute result: "I want that." Speed wins the moment. Quality wins the business. Clients pay for outcomes, not generation time.
