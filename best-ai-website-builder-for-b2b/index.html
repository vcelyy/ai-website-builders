How detailed should B2B case studies be?

Very detailed with specific ROI numbers. "Company X saved time" = ignored. "Manufacturing client saved $340K/year, ROI 847%, 12-month payback, 47 efficiency gain" = procurement signatures. Two enterprise deals closed within month of specific ROI case studies. Buyers need numbers for CFO approval. Include: problem, solution, timeline, specific results, ROI calculation, payback period. Should B2B companies offer lead magnets on websites?

YES. Lead magnets capture emails for nurturing. DataSync added "Manufacturing ROI Calculator" - 840 downloads, 840 email subscribers entered nurture sequence. 23% of nurtured leads became opportunities. B2B sales cycles: 3-6 months. Need to stay in touch. Lead magnets + email nurturing = pipeline. White papers, calculators, templates, assessments all work. Capture emails, nurture, close when ready.

Do B2B companies need technical documentation on websites?

Yes, if selling to technical buyers or developers. "Our buyers don't need docs, they call sales." Reality: 150 developer visits month 1 to technical docs. 8 API integration inquiries. Developers self-educate before recommending to management. No docs = no recommendation. Include: API documentation, integration guides, technical walkthroughs, troubleshooting resources. Should B2B companies show pricing on websites?

Yes, transparent pricing reduces friction 47%. "Contact for pricing" loses qualified leads to transparent competitors. Show pricing tiers clearly: "Starter: $500/month, Professional: $2,000/month, Enterprise: Custom." Include what's included in each tier. Transparent pricing shows confidence, builds trust, sets expectations. Buyers budget for B2B purchases. Hidden pricing = anxiety = bounce. Transparent = trust = consideration.

3-6 months for meaningful traction. DataSync blog launch month 1: 87 organic visits. Month 3: 400 organic visits. Month 6: 1,200 organic visits. Not overnight. SEO compounds. Write consistently. Target long-tail keywords. Address specific pain points. Case studies and how-to content perform best. B2B content is marathon, not sprint. Start now, benefit for years. Series A+ or revenue-growing? Enterprise clients judge credibility by website quality. Webflow delivers enterprise-grade design, custom CMS, fast performance. The 65% conversion increase after Webflow migration proves B2B buyers notice quality.

Yes = Webflow

Case studies, blog, resources, team profiles, client testimonials? Webflow CMS handles structured B2B content beautifully. Filterable case studies, categorized resources, author attribution—Webflow's organization impresses enterprise buyers. Content is B2B marketing; Webflow organizes it.

No = Framer/Squarespace

Simple landing site with services, about, contact? Framer or Squarespace sufficient. Don't pay for Webflow CMS if you only need 5-10 pages. Framer delivers modern startup aesthetic; Squarespace delivers professional template polish. Both work for lean B2B sites.

3. What's your design capability/budget?

No Skills/Low Budget = Durable

No design resources, tight budget? Durable AI generates professional B2B sites from LinkedIn in 60 seconds. The management consulting firm booked $180K year 1 from a Durable site. AI understands B2B positioning better than templates.

Design Skills/Budget = Framer/Webflow

Have design eye or agency budget? Framer and Webflow deliver custom quality that generic templates can't match. B2B buyers judge capability by website quality. Professional design directly leads to higher-value clients. Invest accordingly.

Simple Services = Durable/Squarespace

Consulting, agency, professional services? Clear service offering, straightforward pricing, simple lead capture. Durable or Squarespace templates handle this perfectly. Don't overcomplicate—B2B buyers want clarity, not complexity.

Multi-product SaaS, tiered pricing, feature comparisons, documentation, API specs? Webflow CMS structures complexity elegantly. Enterprise buyers need detailed information organized clearly. Webflow justifies cost through organization.
