Wine Importer Website FAQ

Should wine importers show full portfolio online?

YES. Complete portfolio = full sales opportunities. "Featured Wines" shows 12 selections. Restaurant wine director might need your $18 Gr√ºner for by-the-glass program, not your $72 Pinot featured. Importers with full portfolios get 5x more inquiries. Categorize by region, grape, price - make it browsable. Hidden wines = lost sales. Your entire portfolio should be visible, searchable, with current availability. Every wine has a buyer. Show them all.

How detailed should tasting notes be?

Very detailed with selling points included. "Crisp white wine" is useless. "Crisp acidity with notes of grapefruit and lemongrass, mineral finish from limestone soils, pairs perfectly with oysters, raw bar, goat cheese salads" sells wine. Include flavor profile, structure, food pairings, service suggestions. Wine directors need language to sell to guests. Detailed notes increase buyer confidence 67%. Your wines have character. Describe it.

Do wine importers need online trade portals?

Yes, trade portals increase conversions 43%. Restaurant buyers work weird hours - 11PM after service, 8AM before prep. They want to SELF-SERVICE: browse portfolio, check pricing, submit inquiry when inspiration strikes. Email chains waste time. Online trade inquiry forms with account applications capture details automatically. Portals with real-time availability = instant answers = faster orders. Your competition has online ordering. You lose accounts without it.

How do importers show winemaker stories?

Dedicated producer profile pages with specifics. "Family-owned winery" means nothing. "Third-generation vigneron Jean-Pierre Martin, vines planted 1952, biodynamic since 2008, hand-harvested, native yeast fermentation, aged in neutral French oak 18 months" justifies premium pricing. Include photos of vineyards, winemakers, production methods. Importers with winemaker stories sell 53% more premium placements. Your wines have history. Tell it prominently.

Should importers show pricing on websites?

YES, transparent trade pricing is essential. "Contact for pricing" loses 73% of buyers to transparent competitors. Show case pricing clearly: "$180/case, $15/bottle." Include tier discounts: "10+ cases: 5% off, 25+ cases: 10% off." Wholesale pricing visible = buyers know budget = can order. Your pricing isn't secret. Your competitor's is visible. Guess who gets the order? Transparent pricing = trust = trade accounts.

How important is age verification?

Legally required and non-negotiable. Wine shipping laws require age verification - 21+ in US, provincial requirements in Canada, varying rules internationally. Implement age gates on site entry AND checkout. Shopify, Squarespace, Webflow all have age verification apps/plugins (many free). Non-compliant = legal liability. Age verification protects your business. Every importer needs this. Period.
