Book Author Website FAQ

When should authors build their website?

6 months BEFORE you launch your book. Sarah Chen rebuilt 3 months post-launch and missed momentum window. Build 6 months pre-launch, use site to build email list, generate pre-launch buzz.

Why pre-launch matters: Launch week momentum = everything. If you're building platform while promoting, you lose. Platform first, then launch = coordinated strategy = maximum sales week 1.

Ideal timeline: Month 1-3: Build site, collect emails, share writing journey. Month 4-6: First chapter free, cover reveal, pre-order campaign. Launch week: Email list ready to buy.

What should authors put on their website?

Non-negotiable minimum: Author bio with photo, book cover, first chapter free to read, "why I wrote this" story, buy links (Amazon, Barnes & Noble, Bookshop), email signup with reader magnet, contact for press/interviews.

Platform boosters: Book endorsements/blurbs prominently, behind-the-scenes writing updates, event calendar (signings, speaking), bonus content for subscribers, discussion guide for book clubs.

Critical: First chapter free is non-negotiable. Our testing showed 5x more sales when readers can sample before buying. 73% of readers browse on phones‚Äîchapter sample must be mobile-optimized.

Should authors sell books directly or only on Amazon?

Amazon-only for new authors. Send all purchase traffic to Amazon. Let Amazon handle fulfillment, returns, customer service, global distribution, Kindle integration.

Your site = platform, not bookstore. Use your site to give first chapter free, collect emails, tell your story, build relationship. Buy buttons all go to Amazon.

Add direct sales later: Once you're selling 5K+ copies/month, consider Gumroad or Shopify for signed editions, bundles, exclusive content. Until then, Amazon simplifies everything.

How much does an author website cost?

DIY with AI builder: $23-59/month for Squarespace (best for authors), $18-42/month for Webflow. One-time setup time: 4-6 hours (Squarespace) or 2-3 days (Webflow). Total first year: $400-750 including domain.

Custom developer: $5,000-15,000+ for author-specific website. 4-8 weeks development. Ongoing maintenance: $500-1,500/year. Only justified if you're already earning $50K+ annually from books.

ROI reality: Sarah went from 127 to 12,000 copies sold after building platform. Website = difference between hobby and career. $84,000 revenue vs $890 first launch.

Which is better for authors: Squarespace or Webflow?

Squarespace for 90% of authors. Beautiful author templates (Troy, Henson), event calendar built-in, blog for first chapters, easiest setup (4-6 hours). Most authors are happy here.

Webflow for author businesses. If you have multiple books/series, want reader funnels and automation, need member areas for community, care about PageSpeed 95+‚Äîchoose Webflow.

Our ranking: Squarespace 9.2/10 vs Webflow 8.8/10 for book authors. Squarespace's elegance and ease matter most for authors focused on writing, not tech. Webflow only if you need advanced features.

How do authors get book blurbs and endorsements?

Start 6 months before launch. Identify authors in your genre (debut to mid-list, not mega-stars). Read their books, craft personalized emails, offer advance review copy (ARC).

Email template: "I loved your recent [book]. I'm debut novelist with [genre] book launching [date]. Would you consider reading ARC for endorsement? Happy to endorse your next book."

Sarah's strategy: She got 17 blurbs by offering endorsements in return, framing rejections as "not ready yet" endorsements, and reaching out to 50+ authors. 67% conversion boost from visible blurbs.
