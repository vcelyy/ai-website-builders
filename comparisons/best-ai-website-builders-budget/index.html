Frequently Asked Questions

Is Hostinger really worth $2.99/mo?

Yes, if budget is your #1 constraint. Hostinger works. The AI is basic, templates are dated, but you get a functional website with hosting included.

Tradeoffs: Design looks like 2015. AI generation is hit-or-miss. Performance is decent (75-80 PageScore), not great. But for $215 over 3 years? It's unbeatable value.

Best for: Personal sites, testing ideas, non-profits, hobby projects. Not for: Professional portfolios, agencies, design-focused brands.

Why is Framer considered "best value" at $15/mo?

Quality per dollar. Framer costs 5x more than Hostinger ($15 vs $2.99) but delivers 10x the quality:

  • Design: Modern, beautiful templates vs dated 2015 look
  • AI: Premium generation that understands context vs basic text replacement
  • Speed: 95-98 PageScore vs 75-80 (huge for SEO)
  • Flexibility: Full design control vs template constraints

Math: $540 over 3 years. That's $15/month. For a professional site that drives business? Worth every penny.

What's the catch with low prices?

Three catches:

  1. Annual billing required: All advertised prices demand upfront annual payment. Monthly billing costs 20-69% more.
  2. Renewal price hikes: First-year pricing is promotional. Year 2-3 costs 20-50% more.
  3. Feature limits: Budget plans limit pages, storage, bandwidth. You'll outgrow them fast.

Example: Hostinger's $2.99/mo is 1-year promo. Year 2-3: $5-7/mo. Still cheap, but not $2.99.

Can I switch from a budget builder to premium later?

Yes, but it's painful. Migration challenges:

  • No export: Most budget builders (Wix, Squarespace) don't let you export code
  • Manual rebuild: You'll recreate pages one-by-one on the new platform
  • SEO loss: URL structure changes = lost rankings
  • Downtime: Site offline during migration

Better approach: Start with the platform you'll use long-term. If budget is tight, use Hostinger short-term, then migrate to Framer once revenue allows. Plan for migration from day 1.

Are free plans worth it?

Only for testing, never for production. Free plan limitations:

  • Branding: Builder's logo/footer on your site (unprofessional)
  • Domain: yoursite.builder.com (not custom domain)
  • Features: Crippled functionality, storage limits
  • SEO: Poor rankings (subdomains rank lower)

Verdict: Use free plans to test the platform. Then upgrade to paid before launching. A $3-15/mo investment is worth professional presentation.
