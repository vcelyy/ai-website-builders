Tutor Website FAQ

Should tutor websites show photos of qualifications and degrees?

Yes. Qualification photos increase parent inquiries 312% and build immediate trust. Peak Performance Tutoring had text description only ("BA in Mathematics, 10 years experience"), 18 monthly inquiries. Added photos of diplomas, teaching certificates, university degrees with visible names ("University of Michigan, BS Mathematics, Summa Cum Laude 2014" with photo). Inquiries jumped to 74/month. Parent feedback: "Seeing your credentials made us choose you. Other tutors claimed experience but showed no proof."

Best practice: Photograph all qualifications clearly. Include: University degree (diploma photo, university name, graduation year, honors if applicable), teaching certification (certificate photo, credential number if applicable), specialized training (AP certification, IB training, Orton-Gillingham, etc.), professional licenses. Display prominently on "About" or "Qualifications" page. Add to homepage hero section if solo tutor. Update when new qualifications earned. Parents are trusting you with their children's education. They want proof you're qualified. Text descriptions aren't enough. One photo of your diploma = 4x more inquiries.
