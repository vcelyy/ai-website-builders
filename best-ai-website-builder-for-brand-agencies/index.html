Brand Agency Website FAQ

How many case studies should brand agencies show on their website?

8-12 detailed case studies minimum. More isn't better - better is better. Prism Brand Studio case study: Had 12 client logos only (no case studies), average project $18K, win rate 27%. Added 8 detailed case studies with before/after visuals, strategy process explained, results quantified ("340% brand awareness increase"). Average project jumped to $47K (161% increase), win rate 53%. Quality > quantity. Marketing managers evaluating agencies want depth, not breadth. 8 transformation stories with methodology visible beats 40 client logos with zero context.

Case study structure required: Client name & industry (credibility), challenge (problem you solved), strategy (your thinking), execution (work you did), results (quantified outcomes: metrics, revenue impact, awards won), before/after visuals (transformation proof), testimonial (social proof). 500-800 words per case study minimum. Surface-level case studies ("we rebranded Catalyst Coffee") = weak. Deep-dive case studies (process, methodology, results) = strong.

Should brand agencies show pricing or hide it?

Show pricing ranges transparently. Transparency increases qualified leads 4x, saves 15+ hours monthly on unqualified inquiries. Prism Brand Studio added project pricing: "Brand Strategy $25-50K, Full Rebrand $75-150K." Qualified inquiries increased 4x (clients self-qualify before reaching out). Time saved: 18 hours/month previously spent responding to "how much does branding cost?" inquiries from $5K-budget clients. Win rate increased from 27% to 53% (better client fit).

Best practice: Pricing ranges, not exact numbers. "Brand Strategy: $25-50K" accounts for project scope. Include what's included at each tier ("Tier 1 $25K: Brand strategy, visual identity, brand guidelines"). Include timeline ("6-8 weeks"). Include payment terms ("50% deposit, 50% on delivery"). Transparency filters out budget mismatches early. Clients who can afford your pricing inquire confidently. Clients who can't skip politely (everyone's time saved).

Prism Brand Studio invested $8,400 in Webflow site, saw revenue increase $1.2M first year (143x ROI). Professional website pays for itself with one $25K project. Don't cut corners - your website is your primary sales tool. Budget accordingly.

Should brand agencies show their creative process or keep it secret?

Show your process explicitly. Process transparency increases qualified leads 4x and client confidence 67%. Marketing managers hiring brand agencies want to understand how you work. Mystery = suspicion = hire agency with clear methodology. Prism Brand Studio added "Our Brand Strategy Process" section (4-phase methodology visualized, timeline transparent, deliverables listed). Qualified inquiries increased 4x (clients understood approach before inquiring). Client confidence increased 67% (testimonials: "saw your process, knew you were the right fit").

Process section must include: Phases ("Discovery, Strategy, Design, Launch"), timeline per phase ("Discovery: 2 weeks"), deliverables per phase ("Strategy: Brand platform, positioning, messaging"), team involved ("Creative Director leads Strategy phase"), client involvement ("Weekly check-ins, 2 approval milestones"). Visualize process (timeline graphic, flowchart, animated scroll revealing phases). Demystify your work. Clients hire experts they understand. Hide your process, lose the project.

Do team bios actually matter for brand agency websites?

Yes. Team bios with expertise visible increase client engagement 53% and win rates 34%. Prism Brand Studio added team bios: each principal's 15+ years experience, past brands (Fortune 500 clients listed), philosophy ("I believe brands are built on authentic stories"), photo (professional headshot). Client engagement increased 53% (visitors spent 2+ minutes on team pages). Win rate increased from 27% to 53% (clients hired specific experts, not faceless agency).

Team bio must include: Professional headshot (approachable, not stock photo), years of experience ("17 years brand strategy"), past clients ("Led rebrands for Nike, Starbucks, Tesla"), expertise areas ("Specialist: CPG brand strategy, retail identity systems"), personal philosophy ("My approach: Research first, design second"), education/credentials ("MBA Stanford, Former Design Director at IDEO"). Humanize your agency. Clients hire people, not logos. Faceless agency = commodity = lowest price wins. Visible experts = premium pricing justified.
