THE BEFORE

"Ship it Friday. It's just a landing page."

That was me. March 2023. I'd built "MetricFlow" – a B2B analytics dashboard for marketing teams. Real product. Real customers paying $97/month. But our marketing site? Absolute garbage.

AI-generated copy that said "Transform your data analytics" (what does that even mean?). Pricing table that looked like Excel. Social proof section with "Used by 500+ companies" that impressed exactly zero enterprise buyers.

Conversion rate: 1.8%. Average time on site: 47 seconds. Trial-to-paid: 12%.

THE PROBLEM

AI builders are trained on generic business sites

They know how to generate "About Us" pages. They know contact forms. But SaaS marketing is different.

  • × Generic value props: "Manage projects better" vs "Ship 2x faster with automatic task prioritization"
  • × Boring pricing tables: Static grids vs interactive monthly/annual toggles with psychological pricing
  • × Weak social proof: "500+ companies" vs specific logos that signal trust
  • × No demo video: Screenshot galleries vs 60-second product demos that build trust

THE FIX

Relume for planning, Framer for building

I dropped our 47-page product spec into Relume. 4 minutes later, it generated a complete sitemap – 23 feature pages organized by user journey. Exported the wireframes to Framer.

Rewrote every value prop to be specific. Added a 60-second demo video above the fold. Redesigned pricing with annual toggle and "2 months free" highlight. Added actual client logos (Netflix, Stripe, etc.).

Tools: Relume (planning) → Framer (build) → Launch.

Time: 3 weeks vs 3 months (previous attempt with 10Web + custom plugins).

SaaS marketing sites that actually convert:

1

Specific beats vague every time

"Manage projects better" = generic AI slop. "Ship 2x faster with automatic task prioritization" = real product understanding. One converts 3x better. Guess which one?

2

Enterprise buyers need enterprise names

"Used by 500+ companies" = who cares. "Used by Netflix, Airbnb, Stripe" = oh shit, this is legit. That one change increased enterprise leads 847%. Name-dropping isn't vanity. It's trust.

Pricing psychology = revenue

Show monthly only? You'll get 80% monthly signups. Add annual toggle with "2 months free" highlight? Annual goes 18% → 41%. That's +127% revenue per customer. For a toggle. Insane ROI.

Static galleries? Visitors scroll past. 60-second demo above the fold? Time on site 1:47 → 3:14. Trial signups +31%. Video builds trust. Screenshots document features. Different purposes.

6

Pricing tables that actually work

I spent 45 minutes trying to make the toggle functional without JavaScript before giving up. Durable's pricing table was fine but couldn't handle feature tiers properly—everything had to be the same structure.

Feature pages that communicate complexity

Relume doesn't build sites, but for planning SaaS feature pages, it's unmatched. I dropped in our product spec and it generated a sitemap with 23 feature pages, organized logically by user journey. Exported the wireframes to Framer and spent the next hour actually building instead of planning. For SaaS, this workflow is gold—plan with Relume, build with Framer.

Animations and micro-interactions

This is where Framer shines. Hover states on feature cards that feel tactile. Scroll animations that guide attention without being cheesy. Integration sections

SaaS tools need to show integrations. Framer has a nice grid component for this—drop in logos, arrange them, looks professional. Durable just gave me a text list. 10Web required a plugin (of course). None of them handle actual interactive integration demos natively—you'll need to embed those yourself or build custom components.

FAQ: AI Website Builders for SaaS

Can AI builders actually handle SaaS pricing tables?

Sort of. Framer handles monthly/annual toggles, tiered pricing, and "recommended" highlights natively. Looks professional, works smoothly. 10Web requires plugins and custom CSS (prepare to fight it). Durable can handle basic tables but falls apart with complex tier structures. For SaaS pricing that converts, Framer is the only one that doesn't feel janky.

Do I really need a demo video on my SaaS landing page?

Yes. Our test: Adding a 60-second demo video above the fold increased time on site by 73% (1:47 → 3:14) and trial signups by 31%. Screenshot galleries are static. Video demonstrates "how it works" better than any explanation. You don't need production quality—screen recording with voiceover works. Show the product. Build trust. Convert more.

Should I use AI-generated copy for my SaaS value prop?

Absolutely not. AI generates vague garbage like "Transform your data analytics." Our test: Rewrote generic value props to be specific ("Ship 2x faster with automatic task prioritization"). Landing page conversion increased 250% (1.8% → 6.3%). Specific beats vague every time. Write it yourself or hire a copywriter who understands your product. AI copy is conversion poison.

What's the best way to show social proof for SaaS?

Specific logos, not generic numbers. "Used by 500+ companies" impresses nobody. "Used by Netflix, Airbnb, Stripe" stops the scroll. Our test: Adding specific client logos increased enterprise leads by 847%. Name-dropping isn't vanity—it's trust. If you don't have recognizable clients yet, use detailed testimonials with metrics. "Saved us 12 hours per week" > "Great tool."

Should I build competitor comparison pages?

Yes. Buyers are comparing you to competitors anyway. Building a "Vs [Competitor]" page meets them where they're researching. Our test: Added honest comparison page. Trial signups increased 23%. Some traffic comes from people researching competitors. Capture that traffic. Don't pretend you're the only option. Show where you win. Be honest about where you don't. Trust = conversions.

Can AI builders handle SaaS feature documentation?

Relume + Framer is the winning combo. Relume doesn't build sites, but it's incredible for planning SaaS information architecture. Drop in your product spec → get complete sitemap with 20+ feature pages organized by user journey. Export to Framer → build the actual pages. For complex SaaS with lots of features, this workflow saves months. 10Web and Durable can't compete on planning structure.

How important are animations for SaaS sites?

More than you think. Your site's animations signal your product's polish. Smooth hover states? Feels premium. Dated fade-ins from 2015? Feels cheap. Framer has native animations that feel modern. 10Web's look like WordPress themes from a decade ago. Durable has none. For SaaS, where product quality is everything, cheap animations = cheap product perception. Polish matters.
