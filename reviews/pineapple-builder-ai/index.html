Why Pineapple's Obsession With Restaurants Works

When being too narrow is just right

I tested Pineapple Builder with skepticism. "A website builder ONLY for restaurants? That's too narrow. What if I want to expand?" Classic tech founder thinking - optimize for flexibility, not focus.

Then I talked to a restaurant owner using Pineapple. She showed me her old Squarespace site - generic template, manual menu updates, broken integration with OpenTable. 3 hours per week managing the website.

The switch: Moved to Pineapple. Menu updates sync with POS. Reservations feed directly to OpenTable. Delivery orders route to UberEats/DoorDash automatically. 15 minutes per week now.

The insight: Restaurant owners don't CARE about website flexibility. They care about not having to think about their website. Pineapple connects all the restaurant systems so they don't have to.

The tradeoff: Your site will look like other restaurant sites. The templates are basic. But your competitors are struggling with manual updates while yours just works.

Who is Pineapple actually for? Restaurants, cafes, food trucks, bakeries - anyone in food service who wants their website to just work.

Who should skip it? Anyone not in food service. This tool is USELESS if you're not selling food. And that's okay.

What I Wish I Knew Before Starting

Would have saved 5 hours

1. This is ONLY for restaurants. If you're a consultant, designer, agency, retail store - skip it. Pineapple has zero features for non-food businesses.

2. Design customization is limited. You're picking templates, not building custom designs. If you need a unique visual brand, look elsewhere.

3. Value comes from integrations, not design. The magic is POS sync, reservation systems, delivery platforms. If you don't need those, you're paying for features you won't use.

4. No code export means lock-in. But at $5/mo, switching costs are low. If you outgrow Pineapple, migrating to Squarespace or Wix is manageable.

Pineapple Builder's Focus: Restaurant Specialist, Generalist Failure

Great for restaurants, useless everywhere else

FAILURE #1: The "I'm Not a Restaurant" Problem

Retail boutique needed online store. "Try Pineapple Builder," I said. Built the site. Looked good. Then client asked: "How do I add product catalog?" "Where's the shopping cart?" "Can I track inventory?" Pineapple doesn't have e-commerce beyond basic ordering. It's for restaurants, not retail. Had to rebuild in Squarespace. Pineapple's niche is too narrow.

Restaurant-only = useless for 90% of businesses

FAILURE #2: The Menu Integration Lock-In

Restaurant loved Pineapple's menu features. OpenTable integration, Resy, UberEats - perfect. Six months later: "We're switching to Toast POS for our point-of-sale." "Can we migrate our website?" Nope. Pineapple's menu is tied to their system. Export would mean rebuilding everything manually. They stayed with Pineapple but are stuck with outdated POS integration. Niche tools have niche lock-ins.

Vendor lock-in even for specialized tools

FAILURE #3: The "All Restaurant Sites Look the Same" Issue

Built site for upscale bistro. Client: "This looks like every other restaurant website." Same menu layout, same reservation button placement, same photo gallery style. Pineapple's templates are optimized for restaurants but that means they all look similar. For a restaurant trying to stand out, this is ironic. We ended up customizing heavily to break the template mold.

Niche templates = cookie-cutter results

Bottom line: Pineapple Builder is perfect if you're a restaurant. If you're literally anything else, don't bother. The restaurant features are amazing but the narrow focus limits everything else.

Final Verdict

Pineapple Builder is purpose-built for restaurants. Menu display, reservations, delivery integrations - all the features a restaurant needs are built-in.

At $5-29/month, it's affordable. But if you're not in food service, this builder is useless.

Choose Pineapple if: You own a restaurant, cafe, or food service business.

Skip if: You're in any other industry, want flexible templates, need code export.
