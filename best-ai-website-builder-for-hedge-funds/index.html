Hedge Fund Website FAQ

What compliance features do hedge fund websites need?

SEC-reviewed disclaimers on every page ("Past performance is not indicative of future results"). Password-protected performance section (accredited investors only). Accredited investor verification (NET WORTH >$1M or INCOME >$200K). Risk disclosure visible. No guarantees of returns. PM bio accurate. No misleading performance claims. Legal review mandatory before launch.

Should hedge funds show performance on public website?

NO. Public performance = securities law violation. Performance goes in password-protected investor portal only. Public homepage should show: strategy framework, risk metrics (drawdown, Sharpe, volatility), PM pedigree, team bios, accreditation process. Login portal for LPs to access performance. Always consult securities lawyer before launching.

How detailed should strategy description be?

Very detailed but not proprietary. Specify: investment style (fundamental, quant, technical), position count (30-40 long, 20-30 short), holding period (3-5 years), sector focus (tech, healthcare, consumer), market-cap range (small, mid, large, all), long bias (120%/80% or market-neutral), currency hedging (yes/no). Don't reveal: current positions, entry/exit criteria, proprietary models. Specific enough to show expertise, general enough to protect edge.

What metrics build investor confidence?

Risk metrics visible on homepage: Max Drawdown (YTD, since inception), Sharpe Ratio, Sortino Ratio, Volatility (annualized), Beta to S&P 500, Correlation to benchmark. PM pedigree: previous firms, AUM managed, CAGR, education, certifications (CFA, CAIA). Team: analyst backgrounds, research process. Infrastructure: prime broker, auditor, administrator. Visible = professional = institutional-grade = allocate.

How much does hedge fund website development cost?

DIY (Wix/Squarespace): $17-59/month platform + $0-500 design = $204-708/year. Webflow: $18-42/month + $500-3,000 developer = $716-7,104/year. Custom development: $15,000-50,000 one-time + $2,000-5,000/year maintenance. ROI: Alexander Windsor invested $3,200 Webflow build ‚Üí $18M new capital in 90 days ‚Üí 11,200% ROI. Website = cheapest capital acquisition tool.

How often should hedge funds update website content?

Monthly: performance updated in investor portal (within 5 business days of month-end). Quarterly: market commentary blog (PM insights on positioning, outlook, sector views). Annually: PM bio updated, team bios (new hires), AUM updated, performance statistics refreshed. Ongoing: news/press mentions, conference appearances, media coverage. Fresh content = active fund = engaged PM = LP confidence.

Which builder is best for emerging managers vs established funds?

Emerging managers (<$50M AUM): Wix or Framer for budget-conscious credibility. Quick setup, professional templates, $15-17/month. Growing funds ($50M-500M): Squarespace or Framer for institutional presence with investor portal. Established funds (>$500M): Webflow for custom compliance, granular portal permissions, advanced CMS. Match platform to AUM, not aspirations. Overbuilding wastes capital. Underbuilding loses allocations.
