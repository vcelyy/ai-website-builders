Frequently Asked Questions

Is Webflow or Relume better for website building?

Webflow (8.5/10) and Relume (8.2/10) are very close overall and both have excellent design quality (9.0/10 each). However, they serve different purposes and can actually work together. Webflow is a complete visual CMS and website builder with hosting, powerful CMS features (9.5/10 flexibility), and code export. Relume is a Figma plugin that generates sitemaps and wireframes that can export directly to Webflow. These are complementary tools, not direct competitors - many designers use Relume for planning in Figma, then export to Webflow for development and hosting.

What is the price difference between Webflow and Relume?

Webflow and Relume have different pricing models reflecting their different purposes. Webflow starts at $15/month for basic sites, $29/month for CMS features, up to $59/month for advanced plans. Over 3 years, Webflow costs $540-2128 depending on plan level. Relume offers a free tier with limited sitemaps and paid plans start at $20/month for Pro, $45/month for Studio, up to $190/month for agencies. Over 3 years, Relume costs $720-6840 depending on plan level. However, many designers use both together - Relume for planning (often starting with free tier), then Webflow for hosting and development.

How do Webflow and Relume work together?

Webflow and Relume work together as complementary tools in a professional design workflow. Relume runs as a plugin within Figma, where you use AI to generate sitemaps and wireframe components. After designing in Figma with Relume, you export directly to Webflow using Relume's Webflow export feature. Webflow then becomes your development and hosting platform where you refine designs, add CMS functionality, and launch your website. This workflow combines Relume's AI-assisted planning with Webflow's powerful CMS and hosting.

Do I need Figma to use Relume?

Yes, Relume requires Figma to function. Relume is a Figma plugin - it doesn't work as a standalone tool. You must have a Figma account (free or paid) to use Relume. This is fundamentally different from Webflow, which is a complete standalone platform. If you don't use Figma or prefer not to work in Figma, Relume won't work for you. Webflow doesn't require Figma or any external tools - it's self-contained. For designers who live in Figma, Relume is natural and powerful. For those who don't use Figma, Webflow is the better choice.

Can Relume replace Webflow?

No, Relume cannot replace Webflow. Relume is a design and planning tool that outputs sitemaps and wireframes - it doesn't host websites, provide CMS functionality, or publish to the web. Relume exports to Webflow (and other platforms), but you still need Webflow or another platform to actually build and host your website. Think of Relume as planning/architecture phase and Webflow as construction/development phase. Relume helps you plan what to build. Webflow is where you actually build it. You can use Webflow without Relume. You cannot use Relume alone for a complete website.

Is Webflow harder to learn than Relume?

Webflow has a steeper learning curve (7.0/10 ease) compared to Relume (7.5/10 ease), but they teach different skills. Webflow requires learning: visual CSS concepts, the Webflow interface, CMS structure, interactions and animations, responsive design patterns, hosting and publishing. Relume requires learning: Figma basics (if you don't know them), Relume's sitemap builder, component library usage, export to Webflow workflow. Both take time to master. Webflow skills are universally valuable - any visual design or development work benefits from understanding Webflow's approach.

Which is better for client work?

For professional client work, using both Relume + Webflow is often the best approach. Relume lets you rapidly generate sitemaps with client in meetings - get instant feedback on structure, show visual hierarchy, iterate in real-time. Clients love seeing the sitemap planning process - it feels professional and thorough. After approval, export to Webflow for development. Real agency example: 2-hour Relume planning session + 12-day Webflow build won a $38,000 contract. Competitor bid 6 weeks at $45,000. The structured sitemap presentation was the clincher.

Decision Framework: Choose Your Workflow

1. What's your business model?

Premium/custom = Traditional Webflow

Selling unique design as differentiator? High-end clients, luxury brands, agencies with design reputation? Traditional Webflow justifies premium pricing. Custom work = portfolio builder.

Volume/speed = Relume + Webflow

Building 20+ sites/year? Clients want speed over uniqueness? Agency growth depends on throughput? Relume system delivers 3-5x faster builds. Scale through efficiency.

2. What do your clients actually care about?

Speed/cost = Relume

Small businesses, startups, budget-conscious clients? They want online fast, affordably. Don't care about "unique design." Relume delivers professional results in days, not weeks.

Brand/premium = Traditional

Luxury brands, funded startups, clients with strong brand guidelines? Design uniqueness matters. They'll pay premium and wait for custom. Webflow traditional justifies investment.

3. Are you building an agency or a studio?

Agency = Relume

Scaling to multiple designers? Need onboarding system? Consistency across team builds? Relume library means any designer builds correctly. Agency scalability.

Studio = Traditional

Solo or small team? Craftsperson approach? Reputation for unique work? Traditional Webflow builds portfolio. Higher prices per project compensate for slower delivery.

4. What's your team's skill level?

Mixed/junior = Relume

Hiring junior designers? Need consistent quality despite skill gaps? Relume components constrain bad decisions. Junior + Relume = senior output. Training time: days not months.

Senior/expert = Traditional

You're expert Webflow designer? Don't need components? Client pays for YOUR design vision? Traditional Webflow unlocks full creative potential. Charge premium for expertise.

The Hybrid Sweet Spot: Use Both Strategically

Relume for:

Internal pages (blog, help center, about), MVP builds, client speed priorities, junior designer work, consistent agency quality. 60% faster delivery on non-critical pages.

Traditional Webflow for:

Hero/landing pages, premium clients, brand-differentiating work, portfolio pieces, custom interactions. Charge 2-3x for custom sections that matter.
