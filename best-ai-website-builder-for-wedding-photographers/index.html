Wedding Photographer Website FAQ

How many wedding photos should I have on my website?

50-100+ complete wedding galleries. Each gallery = 80-150 photos showing full day coverage (getting ready, ceremony, reception, details, candids). Organize by wedding for easy browsing. Quality over quantity: professional editing, consistent style, emotional moments. Phone snapshots = amateur = lose bookings. Each gallery tells couple's love story = couples fall in love = book you.

Should I show wedding pricing on my photographer website?

YES, show package pricing clearly. "Elopement: $2,500 | 8-Hour Wedding: $3,500 | 10-Hour Wedding: $4,500 | All-Day: $5,500" with details (hours, photos, online gallery, album, engagement session, timeline). Add what's included so couples see value. Add starting price if packages vary. Transparent pricing = couples can afford = serious inquiry = higher booking conversion. Hidden pricing ("contact for quote") = assumes expensive = skip = lose bookings.

How do I describe my wedding photography style?

Be specific, not vague. Instead of "documentary style," use: "Candid & Documentary: Emotional moments, natural interactions, storytelling focus. I capture tears, laughter, glances, hugs - the love story unfolding naturally." OR "Fine Art & Timeless: Elegant, romantic, light-filled. Classic poses, beautiful compositions, magazine-worthy portraits." Add your approach (light, posing, editing). Specific = couples know if you match their vision = qualified bookings = happier clients.

What belongs in a wedding gallery section?

50-100 photos per complete wedding. Organize chronologically: getting ready (details, dress, prep), ceremony (processional, vows, rings, kiss), portraits (family, bridal party, couple), reception (first dance, cake, speeches, party). Include emotional candids + key formal shots. Show venue captured beautifully (attracts venue referrals). Each gallery = couple's complete love story = couples imagine their wedding = book you.

How often should wedding photographers update their website content?

Per wedding: Add complete wedding gallery (80-150 photos) within 2 weeks. Monthly: Real Weddings blog with latest couple story, update homepage with new featured wedding. Quarterly: refresh testimonials, update portfolio best-of, add new venues to "Venues I Love" section. Annually: professional portfolio review, remove older galleries, refresh pricing, update bio. Fresh content = active photographer = in demand = referrals = bookings.
