VC Firm Website FAQ

What should I include in my investment thesis?

Be brutally specific: "B2B SaaS Pre-Seed $500K-1.5M, 2 Founders Required, Technical CEO Preferred, US-Based." This lets founders self-select. Wrong fits skip (saves everyone time). Right fits apply (quality deal flow). Vague "we invest in great teams" = you'll review 10x more decks, most off-thesis.

Should I show my portfolio if we're a new fund?

Absolutely. Even 3 companies with "Deployed" status visible = you're active. Even 1 exit (any size) visible = you've done this. Empty portfolio or "Coming Soon" = unproven. Show what you have. Update quarterly. Growth visible = you're building.

How detailed should partner bios be?

Operator background is gold. "Former CEO, exited for $47M, 15 years climate tech" better than "20 years venture experience." Founders want VCs who've built. Show your exits. Show your founder empathy. Operator = been there = trust = your term sheet.

Should I have a pitch submission form or just email?

"Upload Deck" button + email field. 17 seconds. Founders are busy. 20-minute forms = they skip you. Email-only = unstructured = you'll chase for info. Middle ground: frictionless submission + structured data = best of both.

What's the most important section for attracting LPs?

Team. LPs invest in people, not theses. Partner bios with exit stories, fund size clearly stated, investment thesis showing focus, portfolio showing activity. Team visible = credible = LP commits capital.

Should I publish content (blog, podcasts, etc.)?

Yes, but only if you'll sustain it. One high-quality article monthly ("Why We Passed on 89 Deals") beats ghost blog. Content shows thinking, attracts like-minded founders, builds reputation. If you can't commit, skip it. Empty blog = abandoned fund.

How fast will I see founder inquiries after launching my VC website?

Depends on thesis clarity + portfolio visibility. If you have clear investment thesis + 10+ portfolio companies visible, expect first qualified inquiry in 3-6 weeks via Google search. Stealth mode/no portfolio? Expect 6-12 months while you build track record. The pattern: launch thesis page ‚Üí showcase portfolio ‚Üí publish team backgrounds ‚Üí SEO ranks for "VC investing in [your sector]" ‚Üí founders find you ‚Üí relevant deal flows. Speed = portfolio size + thesis clarity. 30 portfolio companies + specific thesis = inquiries in week 1. 3 portfolio companies + vague thesis = inquiries in month 8.

THESIS COMMUNICATION MASTERY
RELEVANT DEAL FLOW = BETTER DEALS

Founders apply to 50+ VCs. Most applications are wasted. Clear thesis attracts relevant founders. Vague thesis attracts everyone (and no one). Specificity wins. Your website is your thesis filter.

THESIS ELEMENTS THAT ATTRACT THE RIGHT FOUNDERS

STAGE & CHECK SIZE

"Seed stage, $500K-2M checks, 20% ownership target"

Founders need to know if you're their stage. Pre-seed founders don't waste time on Series A firms. Seed founders need to know check size fits their raise. Ownership target shows how you think about dilution. Specific numbers save everyone time.

Real example: VC firm had vague "early-stage" language. 200 applications/month, 4% relevant. Changed to "Seed stage, $750K-1.5M checks, 15-25% ownership." Applications dropped to 80/month, but 34% were relevant. Same quality deals, less noise.

üìà RELEVANCE INCREASE: 34% relevant vs 4% with specific thesis

SECTOR FOCUS

"B2B SaaS only. Fintech, devtools, vertical SaaS. No consumer."

Generalist VCs see everything, win nothing. Specialist VCs build expertise in specific sectors. Founders want investors who understand their space. "We invest in B2B SaaS" tells founders exactly who you are. Consumer founders don't apply. B2B founders prioritize you.

Real example: VC firm was generalist "tech investor." Repositioned as "B2B SaaS specialist." B2B deal flow increased 127%, consumer deal flow disappeared (good thing). Two years later, 4 portfolio companies exited, all B2B SaaS. Specialist focus = specialist returns.

üéØ SECTOR FOCUS: 127% more relevant deals with specialization

GEOGRAPHY & REMOTE

"US & Canada only. Remote-first companies preferred."

Founders outside your geography waste your time and theirs. Be clear: where do you invest? Are you remote-friendly? Do you require HQ proximity? Geography clarity filters deal flow before applications happen.

Real example: Silicon Valley VC had "global" language but only invested in Bay Area. 60% of applications were international (all rejected). Added "Bay Area only" to thesis. Applications dropped 40%, review time dropped 60%. Partners spent time on relevant deals, not geographic rejects.

"No consumer apps. No crypto. No pre-revenue. No ideas without traction."

Make it easy for founders to apply. Every friction point loses great companies. Clear process, simple form, fast response. The best founders have multiple term sheets. Your application process determines if you're in the process.

ONE-CLICK APPLICATION

Email + deck = submitted. Not 12 forms.

Best founders are busy. Don't make them fill forms. "Email your deck to [address]" is enough. Collect details after review. First touch: low friction, high volume. Qualify after initial review, not before.

Real example: VC firm had 12-field application form. 84 applications/month, 47% abandon rate. Changed to "email deck to [address]." Applications increased to 234/month. Quality unchanged (same thesis filter), but quantity and founder experience improved dramatically.

CLEAR PROCESS TIMELINE

"We respond within 48 hours. First meeting within 2 weeks."

Founders hate ghosting. Set expectations: when will they hear back? What's the process timeline? "Email deck ‚Üí 48-hour response ‚Üí partner review (1 week) ‚Üí first meeting (within 2 weeks)." Transparency builds trust. Fast responses signal interest.

Real example: VC firm promised 48-hour response, delivered 5-day average. Added automated response: "Received, we'll respond within 5 business days." Founder satisfaction increased (fewer follow-up emails). Managing expectations improved founder experience.

WARM INTRO VS. COLD APPLICATION

"We take warm intros AND cold applications"

Many VCs say "we only take warm intros." This filters out diverse founders without networks. Best firms say: "Warm intro preferred but not required. We review every cold application." Inclusivity attracts better deal flow from underrepresented founders.

Real example: VC firm was "warm intro only." 60% of deals came from founders who looked like existing partners (Ivy League, Silicon Valley network). Added "Cold applications welcome" to website. 23% of next fund's investments came from cold applications. Diverse founders = diverse returns.

DECLINE WITH DIGNITY

"We'll pass, but stay in touch. Here's why..."

Most VCs ghost founders who don't fit. Wrong approach. Every founder is a potential future investment, referral source, or acquirer. Decline gracefully: "Not a fit for us now, but impressive progress. Stay in touch, we'd love to see future updates." Goodwill pays long-term dividends.

Real example: VC firm sent personalized decline emails. 34% of declined founders responded with thanks. 8 referred other founders later. 2 reapplied 18 months later with more traction and received term sheets. Goodwill = future deal flow.

LP (LIMITED PARTNER) RELATIONS

Your website serves founders AND LPs. Family offices, endowments, fund-of-funds research you online. Make it easy for them to understand your fund, team, track record. LP section should be comprehensive yet gated for serious inquiries.

LP SECTION ELEMENTS

FUND OVERVIEW & STRATEGY

Fund size, stage focus, sector thesis, target check size, fund duration. LPs need to understand: what do you invest in, how much, for how long? Clear fund positioning helps LPs decide if you fit their portfolio allocation.

TEAM DEEP DIVES

Detailed partner bios with full backgrounds. LPs invest in teams. Show operating experience, prior funds, educational backgrounds, network. Team credibility drives LP commitment decisions.

TRACK RECORD & RETURNS

Historical fund performance (for established firms). IRR, MOIC, DPI, RVPI by fund vintage. Portfolio company progress. LPs need data to evaluate fund performance and make commitment decisions.

CASE STUDIES OF INVESTMENTS

Deep dives into best and worst investments. What did you see? Why did you invest? How did you help? What happened? LPs want to understand your decision-making process and value-add.

üîê LP GATING STRATEGY

Full LP details should be gated: "Request access to LP materials." Capture contact info, qualify interest. Basic fund info can be public (fund size, thesis). Detailed track record, case studies, financials require LP qualification. Gating filters tire-kickers, focuses on serious LPs.
