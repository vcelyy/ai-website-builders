The Subdomain Problem

Every free AI builder forces you onto a subdomain. Your site lives at their URL, not yours:

yoursite.wixsite.com
yoursite.framer.app
yoursite.durable.co

This matters more than you think:

  • Trust: Visitors notice subdomains. Looks amateur, temporary, or spammy. 47% of users say they'd trust a .com more than a builder subdomain.
  • SEO: Google ranks subdomains lower. All else equal, yoursite.com beats yoursite.wixsite.com every time.
  • Branding: You're building the builder's brand, not yours. Every visitor sees "wixsite.com" and thinks "template site."
  • Portability: If you leave, you can't take the subdomain with you. Your backlinks, your search rankings, your trafficâ€”stuck on their domain.

Custom domains cost $10-15/year from registrars like Namecheap. Most AI builders charge extra to connect one ($10-20/year). But it's worth it. For serious projects, don't skip this.

The "Freemium" Strategy Explained

Why do AI builders offer free tiers? It's not charity. It's a calculated business strategy called "freemium"â€”give away a limited version to hook users, then convert them to paying customers.

The math works like this: For every 100 free users, 3-5 convert to paid. Those paying customers subsidize the free ones. But to maximize conversions, free tiers must be limited enough to be frustrating, but functional enough to show potential.

The result: Free tiers are designed to push you toward upgrades. Page limits, storage limits, feature gatesâ€”these aren't technical constraints, they're business decisions. You're supposed to hit the wall and pay.

Is this bad? No, it's smart business. But understand what you're getting into. Free tiers are marketing tools, not products. Use them accordingly.

When to Upgrade from Free

Don't wait until free tiers break your workflow. Upgrade proactively when:

You're earning revenue

If your site makes $100+/month, the cheapest paid plan pays for itself. The ROI is immediate. Don't hamper revenue-generating features to save $15.

You need a custom domain

Free tiers lock you to subdomains. For serious projects, a custom domain is table stakes. Branding, trust, SEOâ€”all suffer on builder subdomains.

You're hitting feature limits

Need more than 2-3 pages? Want to remove builder branding? Running out of storage? These are signs you've outgrown free tiers.

You're doing client work

Builder branding on client sites makes you look amateur. Clients pay for professionalismâ€”show it with a paid plan.
