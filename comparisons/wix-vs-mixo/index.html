TEAM COLLABORATION: MULTI-USER WORKFLOWS

Solo sites are simple. Team collaboration adds complexity: writers need blog access, designers need layout control, clients need safe content editing. Each platform handles collaboration differently. Here is how to structure multi-user workflows on Wix and Mixo.

WIX TEAM ROLES & PERMISSIONS

Owner: Full access. Can manage billing, domains, team members. Typically business owner or agency lead. Keep this role minimal‚Äî1-2 people maximum.

Admin: Can edit site, manage blog, access dashboard settings. Cannot manage billing or team members. Good for content managers, marketing coordinators.

Editor: Can edit specific site pages (not entire site). Access limited to assigned pages only. Good for freelance writers, contributors who work on specific sections.

Contributor: Can create blog posts only. Cannot edit site pages. Posts require admin approval before publishing. Good for guest bloggers, freelance writers.

Wix team: Owner controls everything, admins manage content, editors limited to pages, contributors blog-only.

MIXO TEAM COLLABORATION

Simpler approach: Mixo doesn't have granular role permissions like Wix. Team members either have full edit access or read-only access. This is simpler but less flexible for large teams.

Full access: Can edit pages, blog posts, settings. Everything. Typically used for small teams where everyone trusted (freelancer + client, co-founders, small marketing teams).

Read-only access: Can view site analytics, content. Cannot edit. Good for stakeholders who need visibility but not editing capability.

Limitation: No granular permissions. Can't restrict specific pages or sections. If you need that level of control, Wix is better.

Mixo team: Full access or read-only. Simpler for small teams, limited for large organizations.
