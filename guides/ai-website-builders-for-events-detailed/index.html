Event Website FAQ

What is the best AI website builder for events?

For conferences and summits with complex scheduling needs, Webflow is the top choice with powerful CMS for speaker profiles, session scheduling, and ticket integration. Mixo provides the best budget option at $9/month for weddings, private events, and simpler gatherings needing RSVP forms and photo galleries. Framer excels for music festivals and visual events needing modern aesthetic (9.5/10), lineup showcases, and high visual impact. Squarespace works well for corporate events wanting professional presentation, registration forms, and reliable performance.

When should event websites launch?

Event website timeline: 3-6 months before - launch teaser page with date announcement, email capture for interest list. 2-3 months before - full event website launch, speaker announcements, early bird tickets, registration open. 1-2 months before - detailed agenda published, venue information, travel/accommodation details. 2-4 weeks before - final schedule confirmed, last-minute updates, comprehensive FAQ. Event week - live updates, check-in information, mobile-optimized schedule. For recurring events, keep website live year-round to build SEO and traffic over time.

What features should event websites include?

Event websites need hero section with event name, date, location, clear CTA, registration/RSVP form collecting attendee information, event schedule/agenda with session times, speaker/performer profiles with photos and bios, venue information with address/parking/transit, ticketing or registration payment integration, sponsor showcase section if applicable, FAQ section answering common questions, contact information, social media links, photo gallery from past events for recurring events, and mobile-optimized experience since 70%+ of attendees check on phones.

How do I handle ticketing and payments on event websites?

For ticketing, you have three main approaches. Integrated solutions like Eventbrite embeds work with all AI builders, provide professional ticketing and handle payments/check-ins, but charge fees (3.5% + $1.79/ticket). Direct payment through Stripe integration via Webflow, Squarespace Commerce has lower fees (2.9% + $0.30) but requires manual attendee management. Hybrid approach uses Eventbrite for initial ticketing then exports attendee list. For free events, use built-in forms. For paid events under 100 attendees, Stripe direct is most cost-effective. For events over 100 attendees, Eventbrite's management features justify the fees.

Should event websites include registration forms or link to external platforms?

Both approaches work depending on event complexity. Simple events like weddings, private parties, workshops should use built-in forms from Mixo, Squarespace, or Webflow - keeps visitors on your site, captures data directly, controls the experience. Complex events like multi-track conferences or festivals with multiple ticket types benefit from external platforms like Eventbrite for specialized features and attendee management. However, always embed the external registration on your site using iframe or button rather than redirecting entirely. Hybrid approach works best: collect basic info on your site, then link to Eventbrite for payment and detailed registration.

How do I optimize event websites for mobile attendees?

Mobile optimization is critical - 70%+ of attendees check event websites on phones. Start with mobile-first design: large touch targets (44x44px minimum), simplified navigation, single-column layouts. Critical mobile features: sticky header with register/ticket button always visible, one-click registration forms minimizing typing, clickable phone numbers for venue contact, Google Maps integration for directions, mobile-optimized schedule view, add to calendar buttons, QR code ticket display. Test heavily: load times under 3 seconds, forms work smoothly on mobile, schedule readable without zooming. Common mistakes: tiny text requiring zoom, complex navigation buried under menus, desktop-first layouts that break on mobile, slow loading from unoptimized images.

What information reduces support questions for events?

Comprehensive FAQ sections reduce support emails by 60-70%. key FAQ topics: registration/ticketing with deadline, refund policy, payment methods, group discounts; venue with parking details, public transit, ride share drop zones, accessibility; schedule with session timing, simultaneous tracks, meal breaks; accommodations with recommended hotels, room blocks, travel booking; what to bring including dress code, materials provided, devices needed; food/drink with dietary accommodations; contact info for different issue types. Include venue photos, embed Google Maps for directions, provide downloadable PDFs for offline access. Update FAQ weekly as new questions come in.

Event websites serve one purpose: get people to register. Your site must display: event details, date/time, venue location, speaker profiles, schedule, registration pricing, ticket purchasing. Potential attendees fear: wasting money on boring events, unclear logistics, commitment difficulty. Address fears, show value, simplify registration. CONVERSION ELEMENTS FOR EVENTS

EVENT COUNTDOWN TIMERS

Urgency drives action

Countdown timers create urgency: "Early bird ends in 3 days," "Only 50 spots left." FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out) triggers registration. Static dates ("March 15, 2026") feel distant. Ticking countdowns feel immediate. Urgency = registrations. Without countdowns, visitors procrastinate. With countdowns, visitors register now.

Real example: Conference added countdown timer to early bird pricing. Registration rate increased 89% during final 48 hours (last-minute surge). Same event without countdown: registrations trickled in evenly. Countdowns create urgency spikes = revenue spikes.

Star power sells tickets

Display speakers/performers prominently: photos, names, credentials, session titles. Headline speakers drive registrations. "Learn from industry experts" is weak. "Keynote by John Smith (Google CEO, 20M+ followers)" is strong. Name recognition = registration motivation. Profile quality = perceived event quality.

Real example: Tech conference listed speaker companies only ("Speakers from Google, Meta, Amazon"). Added speaker names, photos, credentials. Ticket sales increased 127% (attendees researched speakers, got excited). Specificity = credibility = registrations.
