VC Firm Website FAQ

What should I include in my investment thesis?

Be brutally specific: "B2B SaaS Pre-Seed $500K-1.5M, 2 Founders Required, Technical CEO Preferred, US-Based." This lets founders self-select. Wrong fits skip (saves everyone time). Right fits apply (quality deal flow). Vague "we invest in great teams" = you'll review 10x more decks, most off-thesis.

Should I show my portfolio if we're a new fund?

Absolutely. Even 3 companies with "Deployed" status visible = you're active. Even 1 exit (any size) visible = you've done this. Empty portfolio or "Coming Soon" = unproven. Show what you have. Update quarterly. Growth visible = you're building.

How detailed should partner bios be?

Operator background is gold. "Former CEO, exited for $47M, 15 years climate tech" better than "20 years venture experience." Founders want VCs who've built. Show your exits. Show your founder empathy. Operator = been there = trust = your term sheet.

Should I have a pitch submission form or just email?

"Upload Deck" button + email field. 17 seconds. Founders are busy. 20-minute forms = they skip you. Email-only = unstructured = you'll chase for info. Middle ground: frictionless submission + structured data = best of both.

What's the most important section for attracting LPs?

Team. LPs invest in people, not theses. Partner bios with exit stories, fund size clearly stated, investment thesis showing focus, portfolio showing activity. Team visible = credible = LP commits capital.

Should I publish content (blog, podcasts, etc.)?

Yes, but only if you'll sustain it. One high-quality article monthly ("Why We Passed on 89 Deals") beats ghost blog. Content shows thinking, attracts like-minded founders, builds reputation. If you can't commit, skip it. Empty blog = abandoned fund.
