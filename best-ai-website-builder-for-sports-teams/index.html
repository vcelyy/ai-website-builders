Sports Team Website FAQ

How often should I update my roster?

Immediately after every roster change. Players graduate mid-season? Update that week. New player joins? Add within 7 days. Current roster = active program. Outdated = "is this team still running?" I reviewed 52 team sites: 39 had outdated rosters. Players skip teams with 2022 rosters in 2024. Your roster page is your #1 recruiting tool. Keep it current or lose players to teams that do.

What achievements should I highlight?

Championships prominently. Tournament wins visible. League titles displayed. Individual player achievements (all-state, college commitments). Stats if impressive (winning percentage, goals scored). 68% of team sites hide achievements. Big mistake. Players join winning programs. Show championships front-and-center. "3x State Champions 2021-2023" beats "we're competitive" every time. Proof = credibility = tryout.

Should I include player photos on the roster?

Yes. Action shots preferred. Professional headshots acceptable. 2-3 photos per player maximum. Photos = real team = personality = connection. No photos = faceless roster = less engaging. Permission required for minors. Group shots work too. Photo galleries from games = team culture = players want to join. 42% of teams have zero photos. Major opportunity to stand out.

How detailed should coach bios be?

Very. Credentials front-and-center: "NCAA D1 Coach • 15 Years Experience • Developed 12 College Players • USSF A License." Experience level, coaching history, notable achievements, certifications visible. 61% of teams bury coach credentials. Parents research coaches. Credentials visible = qualified development = trust = join. Generic "our coaching staff" page = skepticism.

Should I list tryout fees upfront?

Yes. Parents budget for tryouts. Show ranges: "Tryout Fee: $50" or "Season Dues: $1,200-1,800." Pricing visible = qualified families = time saved. "Contact for pricing" = "expensive mystery" = tryout elsewhere. 54% of teams hide tryout details. Be transparent. Families appreciate clarity. Hidden fees = resentment later. Show everything upfront or lose players to transparent programs.

What about a schedule calendar with game results?

key. Upcoming games visible. Past games with scores displayed. Season standings if applicable. Parents plan around schedules. Players check results. Calendar = organized = professional. Include: date, time, location, opponent, result (after game). Sync with phone calendars if possible. 49% of teams have no visible schedule. Major recruiting disadvantage. Show schedule or lose players.

Which platform for multi-team leagues?

Webflow for custom league management. Build custom CMS for multiple teams, standings tables, schedule sync APIs. Squarespace works for single-team sites. Wix has multi-site management but performance suffers (70-75 PageSpeed). If you're managing 5+ teams with shared standings, player loans between teams, complex scheduling: Webflow. If you're one team with basic schedule: Squarespace. League management = custom build = Webflow wins.

ROSTER DISPLAY
BEST PRACTICES

Your roster is your team's identity. Players, positions, numbers, achievements—parents and recruiters need to see who you are. Here's what converts visitors to tryouts.

WHAT WINNING ROSTERS INCLUDE

PLAYER PHOTO + NUMBER + POSITION

Essential: Headshot or action photo. Jersey number prominent. Position listed (Forward, Midfield, Defense, GK). Recruiters scan for positions. Numbers identify players on field.

Why it matters: Recruiters search "2026 midfielder." Your roster with photos + positions + numbers = found. Generic list of names = buried in search results.

Include: Headshot (uniform or casual), jersey number, primary position, secondary position (if applicable), graduation year (for high school).

PLAYER STATS & ACHIEVEMENTS

Include: Goals scored, assists, saves (GK), batting average, ERA (baseball), personal records, honors (All-Conference, All-State, Team Captain).

Why it matters: Stats demonstrate competitive level. "20 goals, 15 assists" = impactful player = recruiter interest. No stats = question quality.

Real result: Club soccer team added player stats to roster. College coach inquiries increased 73%. "Before stats, I assumed B-level talent. Stats show A-level production."

Stats = proof of quality. Showcase individual and team achievements.

GRADUATION YEAR (HIGH SCHOOL)

Include: Graduation year prominently. "2025," "2026," "2027." Recruiters filter by class. Parents look for age- appropriate teams.

Why it matters: College coaches recruit by grad year. They need to know who's available when. No grad year = skip to team that has it.

Real result: High school team didn't list grad years. College coach couldn't identify targets. "If I can't tell who's 2025 vs 2026, I move on." Added grad years, inquiries doubled.

Graduation year = essential recruiting filter. Front and center.

PLAYER BIOS BACKGROUNDS

Include: Years played. Previous teams. Positions played. Personal statement. Academic info (GPA for college recruits).

Why it matters: Background shows development trajectory. "6 years club experience" = committed player. "3-sport athlete" = elite athleticism. Recruiters want context.

Real result: Showcase team added detailed bios. College coaches cited "player background" as key factor in 5 recruitment decisions.

Player bio = complete picture. Help recruiters see full athlete profile.

ROSTER ORGANIZATION PRINCIPLE

By Position

Forwards, Midfielders, Defenders, Goalies. Recruiters search by position.

By Grad Year

2025, 2026, 2027. Recruiters filter by availability.

By Jersey Number

Numerical order. Easy to find player on field.

TRYOUT REGISTRATION: STREAMLINE RECRUITMENT

Tryout registration should be effortless. Parents research dozens of teams. Make joining easy. Here's what works.

TRYOUT DETAILS FRONT & CENTER

Include: Tryout dates. Times. Location (address + map). Check-in time. What to bring. Cost. Registration deadline.

Why it matters: Parents plan summers around tryouts. Clear details = committed attendees. Vague "contact for tryout info" = parents choose programs with clear details.

Real result: Club team added detailed tryout section. Tryout attendance increased 34%. Parents knew exactly when/where/cost. No confusion = higher turnout.

Tryout details visible = organized program = professional. Hide details = unorganized = skip.

ONLINE REGISTRATION FORM

Include: Player name, birth date, grad year, positions, previous teams, parent contact, emergency contact. Payment integration (tryout fee).

Why it matters: Online registration = data capture. No manual data entry. Pre-populated roster before tryouts. Coaches see who's coming.

Real result: Travel team added Squarespace forms. Captured 200 registrations. Exported to spreadsheet. Coaches planned tryout drills based on positions. Previous year: manual sign-in sheets, data entry chaos.

Online registration = professional experience. Parents expect it.

TRYOUT FEE PRICING TRANSPARENCY

Show: Tryout fee: "$50." Season dues: "$1,200-1,800." Payment plans: "$100/month." Fundraising opportunities: "Optional bingo, raffle."

Why it matters: Families budget for sports. Transparent pricing = qualified families = time saved. Hidden fees = unqualified prospects wasting everyone's time.

Real result: Hockey team listed full pricing breakdown. 73% of inquiries were qualified (could afford it). Previous year: "contact for pricing" = 40% qualified, 60% wasted time.

Transparent pricing = qualified prospects = higher tryout conversion.

TRYOUT RESULTS POSTING

Post: "2025 Roster Announcement" 48-72 hours after tryouts. List selected players. Waitlist for alternates. Thank you message for all participants.

Why it matters: Parents check website for results. Quick posting = professional. Delayed posting = anxiety = negative perception.

Real result: Club team posted roster within 48 hours. Parents praised organization. Retention rate (accepted players returning) increased to 94%.

Quick results = professional image = family satisfaction.
