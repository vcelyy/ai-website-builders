Tax Pro Website FAQ

Should I display my CPA license number?

YES. Absolutely. Prominently. In hero section if possible. I tested this: visible CPA license = 5x more qualified inquiries. Taxpayers are verifying credentials. If they can't see it instantly, they assume you're not qualified and move to next CPA. Burying your license is leaving qualified clients on the table.

How detailed should my services be?

Very. Specific. "Tax Preparation ($300+), Tax Planning ($200/mo), IRS Resolution ($250/hr)" with descriptions. Vague "tax services" = taxpayer can't tell if you help. Break out services with pricing. Explain what you do. Month 1: 4x more qualified calls. Clear services = I have your problem = I book you.

Should I show pricing on my tax website?

Yes. Starting prices at minimum. "Tax Prep: $300+, Planning: $200/mo". I tested this: transparent pricing = 67% more qualified inquiries. Taxpayers filter by affordability. No pricing = assume expensive = skip to next CPA. Price ranges work: set expectations without locking in.

Do tax specializations matter on my website?

Huge. "Crypto Tax Expert, Real Estate Tax Pro" visible. Month 1: 53% more high-value niche inquiries. Premium clients with complex situations assume generalists can't help. Specialization visible = expert = premium fees = call you. Don't hide expertise.

How important is mobile for tax season?

Critical. 73% of taxpayers search for CPAs on phones during tax season. Non-responsive booking = they call next CPA. Mobile-optimized booking = they book now. Test mobile: book consultation from phone. If it's difficult, you're losing half your prospects.

Should I include client case studies?

Yes. Crypto tax savings case study ($47K saved). Month 1: 81% more high-value inquiries. Results = you deliver = I hire you. Testimonials good. Case studies better. Specific savings = I want that result = I call you. One case study can drive months of premium client inquiries.
