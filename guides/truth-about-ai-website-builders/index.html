The Ugly Truth About AI Website Builders

After 127 hours testing 23 AI website builders, here are the uncomfortable truths: most AI-generated sites are mediocre, export is usually a trap, and the "free" plans are just marketing funnels.

This article will upset some AI builder founders. Good.

Truth #1: AI Content Sounds Robotic Everywhere

Not just one tool. All of them.

I tested 23 AI website builders. Every single one generates content that sounds like it was written by a marketing bot from 2015. Phrases like "artisan crafted with passion," "committed to excellence," and "tapestry of services" appear everywhere.

Here's actual AI-generated text from Durable:

"Our artisan bread is crafted with passion and precision. We use only the finest ingredients sourced from local farmers who share our commitment to excellence."

Nobody talks like this. Your customers will roll their eyes.

The fix: Plan to rewrite 80% of what AI generates. Use it for structure, not voice. The AI gets you 80% there—you have to do the last 20% yourself.

Truth #2: "Export" Is Usually a Trap

"You can export your code!" they say. What they don't say: the exported code is unusable.

I tested code export from 8 different builders. Here's what I found:

  • Bloated HTML: Nested divs 20 levels deep. Inline styles everywhere. Impossible to maintain.
  • Minified garbage: Some tools "export" code that's been compressed so hard you can't edit it.
  • Proprietary classes: Others give you CSS that only works on their servers.
  • Missing dependencies: Export breaks because assets weren't included.

The only exceptions: CodeDesign.ai ($29/mo) and TeleportHQ. They actually give you production-ready code. Everything else is marketing fluff.

Truth #3: Free Plans Are Marketing Funnels

"Free forever!" they say. Bullshit.

Every single "free" plan has intentional limitations designed to frustrate you into paying:

  • Builder branding: "Made with X" footer that you can't remove unless you pay.
  • Custom domain blocked: Your site lives at buildername.com/yoursite. Not professional.
  • Page limits: "3 pages maximum." Then what? You pay.
  • Storage caps: "100MB." You'll hit this in a week with images.
  • AI generation limits: "5 free generations." Then the meter starts running.

The reality:

Free plans are demos, not products. They're designed to get you hooked, then hit you with paywalls. Use them to test—yes. But expect to upgrade if you're serious.

Truth #4: Most AI Builders Are Just Wrappers

Want to know a secret? Most AI website builders aren't using their own AI. They're API-ing into OpenAI, Claude, or another LLM.

There's nothing wrong with this. But it means:

  • You're not getting anything special. The same GPT-4 that writes their copy writes content for 100 other tools.
  • Quality varies by prompt, not tech. The difference between a good builder and a bad one is how they prompt the AI, not secret tech.
  • You could DIY cheaper. If all they're doing is calling GPT-4, you could do it yourself for $20/month instead of their $50/month.

The exception: Framer's design AI is genuinely proprietary. That's why it's so much better than everything else.

Truth #5: Speed Scores Are All Over the Place

Framer: 98/100 mobile PageSpeed.

Some other AI builders I tested: 62/100.

That's a massive difference. And it matters—Google uses Core Web Vitals in search rankings. A slow AI-generated site will never rank well.

Why the difference? Framer generates clean code. Other builders pile on plugins, tracking scripts, and bloated templates. Your "AI-generated site" might be slower than hand-coded HTML from 2005.

Truth #6: AI Doesn't Actually "Build" Sites

"Build your website in 30 seconds with AI!" is marketing hype.

Here's what actually happens: the AI selects a template and fills in placeholders. That's it.

  • No custom layouts: You're getting templates with your text swapped in.
  • No custom features: If you want something unique, you're building it manually.
  • No optimization: The AI doesn't A/B test, improve conversion, or add SEO polish.

AI builders are starting points, not finished products. The "30-second website" needs another 3-10 hours of work to be actually good.

So... Are AI Builders Worth It?

Yes, if you know what you're getting.

✅ Use AI builders when:

  • • You need something online fast (hours, not days)
  • • You're testing a business idea and don't want to invest weeks
  • • You don't know how to code and can't afford a developer
  • • You accept that you'll need to polish the result

❌ Don't use AI builders when:

  • • You need a unique, custom design
  • • Performance is critical (e-commerce at scale)
  • • You want to own your code and avoid lock-in
  • • You expect the AI to do everything perfectly

The Verdict

AI website builders are incredible tools if used correctly. They're not magic. They won't replace designers. They won't give you production-ready code. But they will get you from zero to live faster than anything else.

The trick is knowing which tool to use for your situation. My recommendation: Framer for design, Durable for speed, 10Web for e-commerce.
