6 mistakes consultants make with their websites

Mistake #1: "I help businesses grow"

Generic benefits kill inquiries. "Helped SaaS $2Mâ†’$12M ARR" gets 4x more calls than "I help businesses grow." Clients hire for specific outcomes they want, not vague benefits. If you can't show results, you can't command premium rates.

Mistake #2: Freelancer presentation when charging corporate rates

Independent consultant pitched Fortune 500. Procurement: "Your site looks like a freelancer, we need a firm." Rebuilt with team page (even solo), process methodology, enterprise case studies. Next pitch: won. Big companies buy from perceived companies, not people.

Mistake #3: No methodology or frameworks visible

Clients hire consultants for structured thinking, not random advice. Show your frameworks: "5-step cost optimization process," "3-phase growth methodology." Methodology pages = 3x higher close rates. They understand how you work before talking.

Mistake #4: Bullet-point services without outcomes

"Strategic planning" means nothing. "Helped retailer increase margins 19% in 60 days through strategic planning" means everything. Every service should include real client results. Outcomes drive 67% more qualified inquiries.

Mistake #5: Relying on LinkedIn instead of owned website

Consultant relied on LinkedIn. Added Framer site with case studies, methodology, booking calendar. Website leads: 2x more qualified than LinkedIn. LinkedIn = social proof noise. Website = controlled narrative. Own your story.

Mistake #6: No content marketing for organic lead gen

Strategy consultant wrote blog: "How We Scaled E-commerce Client 3x in 8 Months." Ranked #1 for "e-commerce scaling consultant." 47 qualified leads/month from SEO, zero ad spend. 18 months later: still generating leads. Content compounds. Calls don't.
