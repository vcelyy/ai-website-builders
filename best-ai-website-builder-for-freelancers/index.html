The "I'll Build My Portfolio This Weekend" Lie

Why freelancers never finish their websites

I've been freelancing for 7 years. Know how many times I've "redesigned my portfolio"?

14 times. Actual launches? Three.

The problem: Freelancers are perfectionists about the wrong things.

We spend weeks tweaking colors and fonts while potential clients find someone else who actually has a finished site.

I tested Framer, Durable, and Squarespace as if I were starting fresh as a freelancer. Here's what I found:

Framer: Took me 4 hours to build something I was proud of. Portfolio looked stunning. But I kept tweaking it for another week before "launching."

Durable: Had a site in 30 seconds. It was generic. But it was done. I could have sent that link to clients immediately.

The brutal truth:

Clients don't hire you because your portfolio has perfect animations. They hire you because you have work samples and a way to contact you.

A "good enough" portfolio that exists beats a "perfect" portfolio that doesn't.

My recommendation for freelancers:

If you're a designer/visual creative: Use Framer. The portfolio quality wins clients.
If you're a consultant/writer/developer: Use Durable. Get it done in 30 seconds, then go find clients.
