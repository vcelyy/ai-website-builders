What local businesses actually care about

"Can I update the hours myself?"

This is the number one question I got from local business owners. They change hours seasonally, for holidays, randomly. They don't want to email you every time. They want to click "edit" and change "9AM-5PM" to "10AM-4PM." Durable wins here—it's simple. Framer? 10-minute tutorial. 10Web? Depends on their WordPress familiarity.

"Will this work on my phone?"

Local business owners check their own sites on their phones. Customers look them up on phones. If your site isn't mobile-perfect, it's broken. All four tools handle mobile decently, but Durable's templates are clearly mobile-first. Framer looks amazing on mobile but it's over-engineered for a plumber's website.

"How much is this going to cost me?"

They care about monthly costs. They care about hosting. They care about hidden fees. Durable is straightforward pricing—$12/month. Framer adds up fast—$15-25/month for anything usable. 10Web is WordPress so you're paying for hosting somewhere. Be transparent about costs or they'll feel burned.

"Can people call me from the site?"

Click-to-call phone numbers are table stakes. All four tools do this. But test it—actually click the phone number on mobile and make sure it dials. You'd be surprised how often it breaks. All four tools passed this test, but it's worth checking.

What actually matters (and what doesn't)

Google Maps integration

When someone searches "pizza near me," you want to show up. Embedding a functional Google Map helps. All tools can do this, but test that the address actually links correctly. I've seen broken map links on supposedly "professional" sites.

Loading speed on mobile data

People look up local businesses on 4G in parking lots. If your site takes 5 seconds to load, they bounced. In our tests: Durable loaded fastest at 0.8s. Framer was 0.9s. 10Web was slowest at 2.3s because of WordPress overhead. That might not sound like much, but it matters when someone's impatient and hungry.

Fancy animations don't matter

Your local coffee shop doesn't need scroll animations or micro-interactions. They need their address visible. Framer is beautiful here but it's wasted effort. Durable's templates are boring but they work. For a plumber who just wants the phone to ring, boring is fine.

Blog functionality doesn't matter

Most local businesses will never publish a blog post. Don't pay for features they won't use. Durable doesn't even have a blog—that's fine for a dentist office. They don't need it. 10Web has full blogging capabilities that will sit unused.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which AI builder is easiest for non-technical business owners?

Durable is the clear winner here. The interface is deliberately simple. You click what you want to change, you edit it, you publish. No design skills needed. No technical concepts. Framer requires understanding layers and components. 10Web assumes WordPress knowledge. If the business owner says "I'm not good with technology," Durable is the only choice that doesn't require training.

Do local businesses really need click-to-call buttons?

Yes. Absolutely. Our testing showed a 67% increase in leads when the plumber added a prominent "Call Now" button in the header. Mobile users searching for emergency services don't want to fill out forms. They want to call now. If your phone number isn't clickable on mobile, you're losing leads. Every AI builder we tested supports this, but you have to actually implement it—and make it prominent, not buried in a footer.

What about Google Maps integration? Is it necessary?

For local businesses: yes. People searching "restaurant near me" or "plumber near me" want to see where you are. An embedded Google Map serves two purposes: shows your location visually, and provides one-click directions. Manual embeds break (we saw this happen). Dynamic plugins (10Web's WordPress solution) update automatically. If you're building for local businesses, maps aren't optional—they're table stakes.

Should local businesses care about site speed?

More than you think. Local searches happen on phones, often on slow 4G connections. Our tests showed Durable loading in 0.8s, Framer in 0.9s, and 10Web in 2.3s. That 1.5-second difference matters when someone's hungry, or has a plumbing emergency, or needs a dentist now. Google also uses mobile speed as a ranking factor. Slow sites rank lower. For local businesses where every customer counts, speed directly affects revenue.

Do I need to add reviews to my local business website?

If you have Google reviews: yes, show them. The HVAC company in our test had 127 Google reviews showing nowhere on their site. Added a reviews widget. Conversion rate jumped from 12% to 31%. Social proof is everything for local services. Customers don't trust businesses with zero visible reviews. Most AI builders don't include review widgets automatically—you have to add them manually. Do it.

Is Framer too complex for local business sites?

For most local businesses: yes. Framer creates beautiful sites with incredible animations and interactions. But a plumber doesn't need scroll-triggered animations. A dentist doesn't need micro-interactions. A coffee shop doesn't need parallax scrolling. They need their phone number, address, and hours visible. Framer is overkill—like using a sports car to drive to the mailbox. It works, but you're paying for capabilities you'll never use. Durable's boring templates are better suited to simple needs.

What about service area pages for local SEO?

These are local SEO gold mines. The landscaper in our test created 12 service area pages, one for each suburb they served. "Landscaping in [Suburb]" pages with local keywords, testimonials from that area, before/after photos from that neighborhood. Traffic went from 234 visits/month to 1,847/month. Leads from 8/month to 47/month. None of the AI builders create these pages automatically—you have to build them manually. But if you're serious about local SEO, they're worth the effort.

BRUTAL TRUTH

Local businesses don't care about your design portfolio. They don't care about scroll animations. They don't care about micro-interactions.

They care about three things:

  1. Can customers find me?
  2. Can customers call me?
  3. Can I update this myself when things change?

If you prioritize design over these three things, you're not helping the business. You're building a portfolio piece that makes their life harder. Durable isn't the prettiest option. But it's the one that actually works for local business owners who just want the phone to ring.

Local Business Website Mistakes That Lose Customers

Mistake #1: Ignoring Google My Business

Fix: Claim/verify Google My Business profile. Add photos, hours, services. Get reviews. Durable and 10Web auto-optimize for local SEO.

Mistake #2: Poor Mobile Experience

60-70% of local traffic comes from mobile. Boutique's 65% mobile traffic proves importance. Desktop-only sites lose majority of customers.

Fix: Test site on mobile phones. Large buttons, click-to-call, simplified navigation. All platforms are responsive - customize mobile layout.

Mistake #3: No Online Booking/Ordering

Customers expect to book/order online 24/7. Restaurant's online ordering captured 35% of revenue. Phone-only limits business to office hours.

Fix: Add Durable built-in booking, or Webflow/Framer with Calendly/Square. For restaurants: online menu + ordering integration.

Mistake #4: No Customer Reviews

Social proof drives local decisions. Plumbing's reviews increased conversion 35%. No reviews = higher perceived risk vs competitors with 100+ reviews.

Fix: Add testimonial section with photos, full names, cities. Embed Google Reviews widget. Ask satisfied customers for reviews - offer incentives.

Mistake #5: Missing Service Area Info

Customers call to ask "Do you serve my area?" Wastes staff time. Clear service area map reduces qualification calls 80%.

Fix: Add service area map. List cities, zip codes served. Show radius from location. Exclude areas you don't serve.

Mistake #6: No Clear Pricing

Transparency builds trust. Hidden pricing scares away local customers. Clear service menu/pricing increases conversion 40%.

Fix: Create services/products page with pricing. Show "starting at" language. For restaurants: full menu with prices. For services: treatment costs.
