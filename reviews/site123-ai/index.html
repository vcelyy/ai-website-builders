The 5-Minute Wonder (And 5-Hour Regret)

When fast isn't actually fast

Day 1 with Site123: I was impressed.

Answered 3 questions. Clicked generate. Site was live in 4 minutes and 37 seconds.

I thought: "This is it. The tool for non-technical people. My grandma could use this."

Then Day 2 happened. I wanted to make simple changes.

"Move the logo to the left." Can't do it.

"Change the section background color." Can't do it.

"Add a spacer between sections." Can't do it.

Everything I tried to customize was locked.

The site I built in 5 minutes? It was stuck that way forever.

I spent the next 5 hours trying to work around Site123's limitations. Eventually gave up and rebuilt on Mixo.

Here's the truth: "Fast and simple" means "you have no control." If you're okay with whatever Site123 gives you? Great. If you want to make changes? You're trapped.

I rated Site123 7.1/10. If rating for "speed of initial build," it's a 10/10. For "long-term satisfaction"? It's a 3/10.

The Simplicity Advantage

Site123's strength is simplicity. You don't drag-and-drop elements. You don't customize layouts. You answer questions, and Site123 builds the site.

For someone who has never built a website and is intimidated by technology, this is perfect. You'll have a functional site in 5 minutes.

But that simplicity is also the limitation. Once you want to change something beyond basic text and images, you'll hit a wall.

Site123's Speed Trap: 5 Minutes to Build, Forever to Regret

FAILURE #1: The "I Want to Change One Thing" Wall

Small business owner built her site in Site123. "Can you help me add a testimonials section?" "Can we move the contact form to the top?" "Can I change the header background color?" Site123's answer to all: No. The layout is fixed. The sections are fixed. The customization options are nearly nonexistent. She's stuck with exactly what Site123 generated‚Äîforever. FAILURE #3: The "Free Plan" That Costs More Than Paid Plans

Started on Site123's free plan. Worked okay. Then wanted to connect custom domain. "Upgrade to Premium." Wanted to remove Site123 branding. "Upgrade to Professional." Needed more storage. "Upgrade to Business." What started as "free" became $25/month. Meanwhile, Mixo offers better free tier, and Framer is $15/month. Bottom line: Site123 is perfect if you literally cannot use any other tool and just need something up in 5 minutes. For everyone else, the tradeoffs are too severe. You'll spend more time working around limitations than you saved by the quick setup.
