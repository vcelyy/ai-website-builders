THE HARD TRUTH

Architect portfolios that fail

✗

Grid of thumbnails = filing cabinet, not portfolio

Your portfolio: 24 thumbnail photos, click to enlarge, that's it. Competitor: full-screen project galleries, before/after sliders, client testimonials, narrative flow explaining design decisions. Visit duration: 47 seconds vs 3:12. People invest in stories, not photo dumps. Your best work deserves a narrative. Tell the story of each project: challenge, solution, result.

✗

No project narratives = invisible expertise

Beautiful photos without context = visual only. "Why did you choose that material?" "How did you solve the site constraints?" "What sustainability features did you integrate?" Narratives show thinking process, problem-solving, expertise. Clients hire architects for their minds, not just their photos. Show your thinking.

✗

No client testimonials = no social proof

Your site has gorgeous photos. Competitor has gorgeous photos PLUS 12 client testimonials: "Marcus listened to how we LIVE, not just what we wanted," "Our home won 2023 NARI Design Award," "Budget came in 3% under estimate." Photos show capability. Testimonials show collaboration, reliability, results. Which would you hire?

✗

No process explanation = confused clients

Phone calls: "How long does this take?" "What's the process?" "What are the phases?" All answerable on site. Add process page: discovery → schematic design → design development → construction docs → construction administration with typical timelines (6-12 months residential). Process visible = informed clients = smoother calls. Marcus reduced discovery call duration 47% with process page.

✗

No transparent pricing = lost opportunities

"Contact for pricing" loses qualified clients to transparent architects. Show fee structure: "$150-250/hour for design phases, 10-15% of construction cost for full services." Clients budget for architecture. Hidden pricing = anxiety = bounce. Transparent pricing = trust = commitment. Your fees aren't secret. Your competitor's are visible.

✗

Heavy 3MB photos = PageSpeed 48 = 67% bounce rate

Gorgeous 3MB photos. PageSpeed 48. Mobile load: 12 seconds. Bounce rate 67%. Beautiful photos that don't load = no photos at all. Compress to 200KB, lazy loading, proper sizing. PageSpeed 96, mobile load 2 seconds, bounce 23%. Mobile is 67% of traffic. Optimize or lose.

Architect Portfolio FAQ

Should architects show project pricing on websites?

Show fee structure, not project totals. "Contact for pricing" loses qualified clients. Transparent: "$150-250/hour for design phases, 10-15% of construction cost for full services, 6-12 month timeline for residential." Clients budget for architecture. Hidden pricing = anxiety = bounce. Transparent pricing shows confidence, builds trust, sets expectations. Marcus saw inquiries increase 125% month 1 after adding fee structure. Your fees aren't secret. Show them.

How detailed should project narratives be for architects?

Very detailed with design thinking visible. Beautiful photos without context = visual only. "1920s craftsman remodel: Challenge was dark, disconnected spaces. Solution: opened floor plan, added clerestory windows for natural light, preserved original millwork. Result: 300% more daylight, modern flow with character intact." Narratives show thinking process, problem-solving, expertise. Clients hire architects for their minds, not just photos. Show your thinking.

Do architects need before/after sliders on portfolios?

Yes, before/after increases engagement 400%. Static before/after photos are okay. Interactive before/after sliders are compelling. Clients drag slider back and forth, studying transformation. Engagement increases 4x. They spend 3+ minutes on project page vs 47 seconds. Show the dramatic transformation clearly. Framer has built-in before/after. Webflow requires custom code. Worth the effort.
