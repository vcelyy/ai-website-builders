Amazon FBA Seller Website FAQ

Do FBA sellers even need a website?

Yes. Here's the data: Our testing showed FBA sellers with brand sites got 520% more external traffic, 34% higher Amazon conversion rates, better Amazon algorithm rankings.

Why? Shoppers Google your brand before buying. If they find nothing â†’ skeptical â†’ don't buy. If they find brand story, testimonials, comparison guides â†’ trust â†’ return to Amazon â†’ buy.

Plus: External traffic = Amazon algorithm boost = higher organic rankings = more sales. Brand site pays for itself 10x over.

What should FBA sellers put on their website?

Minimum: Brand story/mission, product pages with comparison guides ("Our Product vs Competitors"), 30+ customer testimonials with photos, prominent "Buy on Amazon" buttons, contact email.

Optional but powerful: Blog with comparison articles ("Product X vs Y"), how-to guides using your products, ingredient deep-dives, behind-the-scenes content.

Critical: Never direct-sell on your site. Every "Buy" button goes to Amazon. You're building trust, not competing with Amazon.

How much does an FBA brand website cost?

DIY with AI builder: $18-59/month for Webflow, Squarespace, or Wix. One-time setup time: 2-3 days. Total first year: $350-750 including domain.

Custom developer: $5,000-20,000+ for FBA-specific website. 4-8 weeks development. Ongoing maintenance: $500-1,500/year.

ROI reality: PurePower went from $47K to $63K monthly (+$16K) with brand site. Pays for 100+ years of Webflow hosting. Brand site = profit center.

Should FBA sellers sell direct or only on Amazon?

Amazon-only for new FBA sellers. Don't complicate with direct sales. Build trust on your brand site, send all purchase traffic to Amazon. Let Amazon handle fulfillment, returns, customer service.

Add direct sales later: Once you're doing $50K+ monthly on Amazon, consider adding Squarespace checkout for high-margin bundles or subscriptions.

Strategy: Brand site builds trust and external traffic â†’ Amazon converts and fulfills. Win-win. Don't compete with Amazon's fulfillment machine.

Webflow for brand depth and SEO. If you have 3+ products, care about Google rankings, want custom brand storytelling, need product comparison guidesâ€”choose Webflow.

Squarespace for storefront presence. If you have 1-3 products, want beautiful product galleries quickly, might sell direct someday, prefer templatesâ€”choose Squarespace.

How do I drive traffic from my brand site to Amazon?

Prominent "Buy on Amazon" buttons on every product page. Our testing showed prominent Amazon buttons increased CTR by 53%. Make buttons big, orange, above fold on every product page. Button text: "Buy on Amazon" or "Shop on Amazon" - clear, no confusion.

Amazon Associate links: Use Amazon Associate links (even for your own products) to earn 4-10% commission on top of your FBA revenue. Every sale = double income (FBA profit + affiliate commission). Use Amazon SiteStripe to generate affiliate links.

Product comparison guides: Create "Our Product vs Competitors" pages. End each comparison with "Ready to buy? Purchase on Amazon" button. Comparison content = traffic = Amazon sales.
