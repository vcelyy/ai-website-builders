Frequently Asked Questions

Is Squarespace or Framer better for website building?

Framer wins overall (9.0/10 vs 8.3/10) with superior design quality (9.5/10 vs 8.5/10), better flexibility (9.0/10 vs 7.0/10), code export capability, and more affordable pricing starting at $5/month vs $23/month. Squarespace offers easier usability (8.5/10 vs 8.0/10), more mature platform with established reliability, all-in-one features without need for integrations, and consistently polished templates. Choose Squarespace for easy, polished, reliable websites. Choose Framer for stunning modern designs with more flexibility.

What is the price difference between Squarespace and Framer?

Framer starts at $5/month for personal sites, $15/month for pro features, up to $30/month for advanced plans. Over 3 years, Framer costs $180-1080 depending on plan level. Squarespace starts at $23/month for personal websites, $33/month for business plans, up to $65/month for commerce features. Over 3 years, Squarespace costs $828-2340 depending on plan level. For all website types, Framer is more affordable ($5 vs $23/month base pricing, nearly 5x cheaper at entry level). Framer's aggressive pricing, combined with superior design quality, makes it exceptional value.

Can I export code from Squarespace or Framer?

Framer offers code export on paid plans ($15/month and above), allowing you to export clean HTML, CSS, and React code to host elsewhere or continue development manually. Squarespace does NOT offer code export - your website stays on Squarespace's servers permanently and you're locked into their platform. This is a major difference if you care about owning your code or having flexibility to migrate platforms. If code export matters for your long-term strategy, Framer is the clear winner. If you don't care about code export and want the easiest polished experience, Squarespace is the better choice.

Which platform has better AI features for website building?

Framer has superior AI capabilities with instant site generation from prompts, AI-powered design suggestions, and smart content creation. Its AI generates modern layouts that feel custom rather than template-based. Squarespace's AI is more limited - mainly suggesting templates and basic content suggestions. Framer's AI creates complete sites in seconds with contemporary design aesthetics. Squarespace's AI helps you pick templates but doesn't generate custom designs. For users prioritizing AI-powered site creation, Framer is clearly ahead with more sophisticated AI that produces better-looking results faster.

Is Squarespace or Framer better for e-commerce?

Squarespace is better for e-commerce with built-in inventory management, payment processing, shipping tools, and order management included. It's designed for online stores with mature e-commerce features. Framer has basic e-commerce through integrations like Stripe and Snipcart but lacks native e-commerce functionality. For serious online stores with products, inventory, and transactions, Squarespace is the better choice with its all-in-one e-commerce platform. Framer can work for simple digital products or basic sales but requires third-party tools. Choose Squarespace for full-featured e-commerce. Choose Framer for marketing sites with basic sales needs.

Which platform is easier to use for beginners?

Squarespace is easier for complete beginners with its polished templates, guided setup process, and intuitive drag-and-drop editor. Everything works predictably with minimal learning curve. Framer is also beginner-friendly but has more options and flexibility which can be overwhelming initially. Framer's AI helps beginners get started fast by generating complete sites. For non-technical users who want zero learning curve, Squarespace wins. For beginners willing to spend 30 minutes learning, Framer provides better results. Both are accessible, but Squarespace is more immediately user-friendly while Framer rewards slightly more learning with significantly better designs.

Can I switch from Squarespace to Framer or vice versa?

Switching from Squarespace to Framer is possible but requires manual rebuilding - you can't export your site from Squarespace. You'll need to recreate content and design in Framer. Switching from Framer to Squarespace also requires manual rebuilding since Squarespace has different template structure. Neither platform offers direct migration tools. If you're early in the build process, switching is manageable. If you have an established site, switching is significant work. The good news: both platforms make building fast with AI and templates. The best approach is to choose carefully upfront using this comparison. Framer users can export code if they want to leave entirely. Squarespace users are locked in unless they rebuild elsewhere.
