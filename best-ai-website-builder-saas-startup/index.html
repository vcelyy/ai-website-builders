UNEXPECTED FINDINGS

What nobody tells SaaS founders about websites

1

Generic SaaS templates kill investor interest instantly

SaaS used generic Wix template with "hero, 3 features, pricing" layout. Pitched 12 investors. 11 passed without second meeting. One investor: "Your site looks like every pitch deck I see this week. Do you have anything unique?" Rebuilt in Framer with custom interactive demo showing product working. Same investor: "This shows me what your product actually does. I can see you care about details." Closed $2.3M seed round. Generic templates signal "we're like everyone else." Custom design signals "we're different."

2

Annual pricing toggle increased signups by 47%

B2B SaaS: pricing page defaulted to monthly. Annual pricing was on separate page. Only 12% chose annual. Added toggle on main pricing page: "Annual (save 20%)" with savings displayed prominently. Annual selection jumped to 59%. Why? Users didn't see annual option. When shown side-by-side with savings clear, they chose value. Don't make users calculate. Show savings upfront. Anchor monthly, discount annual.

3

Company logos beat "Used by 10,000 businesses" by 3x

SaaS homepage: "Used by 10,000 businesses." Generic, unverifiable, noise. Changed to "Trusted by" with 6 recognizable company logos (customers). Trial signups: 3x increase. Why? Specific logos = credibility, social proof, "these companies trust them." Generic numbers = everyone claims 10,000, nobody verifies. Logos are visible proof. Even 6 recognizable logos beat "10,000 businesses." Name-drop carefully and strategically. Use your best customers.

4

Animated product tour increased demos by 200%

SaaS: static screenshots of product interface on homepage. Demo requests: 9/month. Added 30-second animated tour showing features in action—charts updating, data flowing, users engaging. Demo requests: 27/month. 300% increase. Why? Static screenshots = "I guess this works." Animated tour = "I see exactly how it works." Show product working, don't tell it works. Animation = understanding = leads. Complex products need demos, not screenshots.

5

Benefit-focused copy doubled click-through rates

SaaS hero: "Best analytics platform for SaaS companies." Generic, feature-focused, claims everyone makes. Changed to: "See who visited your site today. Understand their journey in real-time." Benefit-focused, specific, shows value. CTR increased 100%. Why? Users don't care what you ARE. They care what they GET. "Best platform" = noise. "See who visited" = value. Every word should answer "what's in it for me?"

6

PageSpeed score correlates with trial signup rate

A/B tested identical SaaS sites with different loading speeds. Site A: 7 seconds load time. Site B: 1.8 seconds load time. Same content, same design, different hosting/optimization. Trial signups: Site A 3.2%, Site B 5.1%. Why? 53% abandon sites loading 3+ seconds. Faster site = more visitors see your content = more signups. Framer averages 95 PageSpeed. Wix averages 60. That's the difference between 3% and 5% conversion. On 10,000 visitors/month: 300 vs 500 trials. Speed isn't technical—it's revenue.

7

Investors judge your product by your website design

Surveyed 23 seed-stage investors. Showed two SaaS sites: generic Wix template vs custom Framer design. 19/23 preferred custom design. Why? Generic templates signal "we're like everyone else, no technical capability, copy-paste startup." Custom design signals "we care about details, have technical ability, understand quality matters." Your website is your first product demo. Investors assume your code quality matches your site quality. Sloppy site = sloppy code assumption.

Choose Your SaaS Website Builder

1. Startup budget and runway?

Pre-revenue, need $0: Framer is the only choice. Launch complete SaaS marketing site for free. B2B SaaS grew to $8,820 MRR without ever paying Framer. Keep burn rate near zero until revenue validates the product.

$1K-5K/month runway: Webflow justified if you need CMS power. Consumer app's blog that drove 60% of traffic paid for the $23/mo many times over. Content marketing requires CMS.

Under $500/month budget: Dorik or 10Web. $4-10/mo with SaaS features. Marketplace MVP validated concept for $4/mo, got first transaction week 3. Don't overspend before validation.

2. Content marketing strategy?

Heavy content (blog, docs, tutorials): Webflow CMS key. Managing 50+ blog posts without CMS is impossible. Consumer app's blog drove 60% of traffic. Content requires content management.

Light content (landing page only): Framer perfect. B2B SaaS launched with just pricing, features, demo form. No blog needed. Focused on product, not content marketing.

Documentation-heavy: 10Web or Webflow. WordPress plugin SaaS needed knowledge base, changelog, tutorials. 10Web's WordPress-native CMS handled this efficiently.

3. A/B testing and optimization priority?

Critical, will test everything: Webflow's logic feature built for A/B testing. Test pricing tables, headlines, CTA buttons, layouts. Consumer app tested 12 pricing variants, found winner that increased conversion 35%.

Nice to have, not key: Framer has basic A/B capabilities through integrations. B2B SaaS tested 2 pricing options, found winner. Good enough for most startups.

Not needed yet: Any platform works. Marketplace MVP launched without A/B testing, got first transaction week 3. Test product-market fit before testing website variants.

4. Integration requirements?

Complex integrations (auth, live chat, custom widgets): Webflow's custom code capabilities unmatched. Add any JavaScript, embed any widget. Consumer app added Intercom, Stripe, Mixpanel without limits.

Standard SaaS stack (Stripe, email, analytics): Framer has all key integrations. B2B SaaS connected Stripe payments, Mailchimp email, Google Analytics in 10 minutes.

Basic needs (form, payment, analytics): Dorik or 10Web sufficient. Marketplace MVP just needed contact form and PayPal. Don't overbuild before validation.

SaaS Landing Page Checklist: What Actually Converts

Must-Have Sections

  • ✓ Hero with Clear Value Proposition: What do you do, for who, what's the benefit? B2B SaaS increased conversions 50% by clarifying "Analytics for E-commerce" instead of vague "Better Insights."
  • ✓ Social Proof Above Fold: Logos, testimonials, user count. Consumer app added "Used by 12,000 users" below hero, increased signups 25%.
  • ✓ Pricing Table with CTAs: Clear tiers, highlighted recommended plan. Framer's interactive pricing increased conversions 40% for B2B SaaS.
  • ✓ Demo Request or Trial Signup: Low-friction way to experience product. Marketplace MVP's simple seller form got 85 signups month 1.

Conversion Killers to Avoid

  • ✗ Vague Value Proposition: "Transform your business" means nothing. "Cut cloud costs by 40%" means everything. B2B SaaS learned this the hard way after 2 weeks of 5% conversion.
  • ✗ No Social Proof: Early startup? Show beta user count, founder credentials, even "Join 50+ founders testing." Something > nothing.
  • ✗ Hidden Pricing: Don't make users request a quote. Show starting price. Transparent pricing increased qualified leads 60% for B2B SaaS.
  • ✗ Weak CTAs: "Learn More" is weak. "Start Free Trial" or "Get Demo" is strong. Consumer app increased signups 30% by changing CTA text.

The SaaS Landing Page Truth

Clarity beats creativity. Users should understand what you do in 5 seconds. B2B SaaS's 50% conversion increase came from being specific, not clever.

Social proof is mandatory. Even early-stage startups need proof. User count, testimonials, founder credentials all work. Consumer app's 25% increase from adding "12,000 users."

Pricing transparency increases qualified leads. Hiding pricing reduces volume but increases quality? False. B2B SaaS got 60% more qualified leads after showing starting price.

Bottom line: Launch with Framer free tier, get your first 100 users, optimize based on data. Upgrade to Webflow when content marketing becomes core strategy.
