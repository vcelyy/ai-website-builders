FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Should jewelry designers sell on own website or marketplaces like Etsy?

Own website = higher margins, brand control. Etsy = discovery, lower margins. LunaMarie Jewelry: Etsy sales (15% fee + 3% payment processing = 18% total) vs. own site (2.9% Shopify payment = 2.9% total). On $1,500 ring: Etsy keeps $270, own site keeps $43. $227 difference per sale. Own site profit margin 67% vs. Etsy 52%. Hybrid approach wins: Start Etsy for discovery, build own site simultaneously for higher-margin direct sales. LunaMarie strategy: Etsy for new customer acquisition (15% of sales), own site for repeat buyers (85% of sales). Average order value $1,200 on own site vs. $680 on Etsy. Build own site from day 1, use Etsy as discovery engine, phase out when email list revenue exceeds Etsy revenue (typically 12-18 months).

How do jewelry designers photograph pieces effectively without expensive equipment?

Smartphone + natural light + $30 reflector = professional jewelry photos. No $5,000 camera needed. LunaMarie photographed entire collection with iPhone 14 Pro + north-facing window ($30 silver reflector fill light). Setup: Jewelry on white velvet (eliminates color cast), window light from left, reflector on right bouncing light back into shadows. iPhone macro mode (0.5x) captures extreme close-ups of hallmarks, stone settings. Editing: Snapseed free app (adjust brightness, contrast, sharpen). Process: Shoot 20 photos per piece, select best 3, edit in Snapseed (brightness +10, contrast +15, sharpen +20), export 2000px width. Total time: 45 minutes per piece. Result: Macro photos so detailed customers asked "what camera did you use?" Answer: iPhone + window light. Professional gear NOT required. Lighting + composition > camera quality.

When should jewelry designers offer custom orders vs. ready-to-ship only?

Custom orders = higher revenue, more time. Ready-to-ship = faster fulfillment, lower touch. LunaMarie tested both: Ready-to-ship pieces (completed inventory, ships 24 hours) = $8,400/month (12 pieces @ $700 average). Custom orders (bespoke requests, 4-6 week lead time) = $8,400/month (4 pieces @ $2,100 average). Same revenue, 4x fewer pieces to fulfill. Custom order sweet spot: Offer after 6 months business, when production workflow established. Before 6 months? Focus on ready-to-ship (build portfolio, fulfill quickly, learn production). After 6 months? Add custom order form to website (LunaMarie's custom requests increased from 2/month to 12/month after prominent form). Pricing: Custom = 3x ready-to-ship (covers design time + client communication). LunaMarie's pricing: Ready-to-ship ring $700, custom same design starting at $2,100. Custom orders scale limited by designer time (max 4-6 pieces/month solo). Ready-to-ship scales unlimited (inventory production during slow periods).

How do jewelry designers reduce returns from improper sizing?

Ring size guide prominent + printable tool + how-to video = 53% fewer returns. LunaMarie's returns pre-guide: 23% (nearly 1 in 4 rings returned for sizing). After adding sizing guide prominently on every product page: 11% returns (53% reduction). Guide includes: Printable ring sizer PDF (customers print, cut out, measure existing ring), how-to measure video (shows proper technique, common mistakes), size comparison chart (US vs. EU vs. UK sizes), recommendation ("size up if between sizes, can resize down locally"). Offer free resizing within 30 days = converts hesitant buyers, reduces returns. LunaMarie added "Free resizing within 30 days" to product descriptions. Result: 34% more sales (buyers confident size can be adjusted), returns unchanged 11%. Resizing cost: $12 local jeweler. Absorbed as cost of customer acquisition (cheaper than lost sale). Alternative: Include half-sizes in inventory (reduces returns 23% but increases inventory carrying cost 40%). Free resizing cheaper than stocking half-sizes for emerging lines.

Should jewelry designers pursue wholesale accounts or focus on direct-to-consumer?

Direct-to-consumer first (build brand, higher margins). Wholesale later (scale revenue, brand exposure). LunaMarie's revenue split: DTC $35,300/month (75% profit margin), wholesale $12,000/month (35% profit margin). DTC more profitable per sale, wholesale scales faster (4 boutiques = $12K without additional marketing). But wholesale requires wholesale pricing (50% off retail = lower margins). Pursue wholesale when exceeding $20K/month DTC, production workflow established. Before $20K/month? Focus DTC (build brand, understand pricing, refine production). LunaMarie pursued wholesale at $35K/month DTC (ready for scale). Wholesale requirements: Line sheet (professional photos, pricing, order minimum), wholesale portal (stockist login for ordering), production capacity (fulfill wholesale orders without sacrificing DTC lead times). Wholesale benefits: Brand exposure (4 boutiques = 4 storefronts displaying line), bulk orders (6 pieces vs. 1 piece DTC), recurring revenue (boutiques reorder seasonally). Wholesale downsides: Lower margins (35% vs. 75% DTC), production pressure, payment risk. Balance: 75% DTC, 25% wholesale optimal for emerging brands.
