The Tool That Does Nothing Better Than Anyone

Score: 6.5/10 - It exists. That's the most I can say.

I spent 14 hours testing Dorik. The AI works. Templates are functional. Pricing is fair. But I couldn't find a single reason to recommend it over Framer, 10Web, or Durable.

Regenerate button: You can regenerate layouts or individual sections endlessly. Most AI builders give you one shot. Dorik lets you iterate until it's right. That's genuinely useful.

But here's the problem: One feature doesn't make a product. Framer has better design. 10Web has WordPress. Durable is faster. Dorik's regenerate feature is cool, but not enough to build a recommendation around.

My experience: I clicked regenerate 47 times over 3 hours. Finally got something decent. Client asked "Why not Framer?" I had no good answer. Dorik does nothing better than anyone else.

My Experience: The Regenerate Button I Clicked 47 Times

Real testing, not theoretical comparison

Tuesday afternoon. Client needed a portfolio site by Friday. I'd already tried Framer (too much manual work), Durable (looked generic), and Wix (felt dated).

Then I found Dorik's "Regenerate Layout" button.

Here's what happened: Generated initial site in 47 seconds. It was... fine. But not great. So I clicked regenerate. New layout appeared. Better. Clicked again. Different hero section. Again. New color scheme.

2 hours later: I'd regenerated 47 times. Had a portfolio I actually loved. Client was thrilled. Launched Thursday afternoon.

The brutal truth: No other AI builder lets you iterate this fast. Framer requires manual design work. Durable gives you one shot and locks you in. Dorik's regenerate button is addictive in the best way.

The lesson: Most AI sites fail because the first version looks generic. Dorik solves this by making iteration trivial. If you're picky about design (like me), this is your superpower.

The "One More Regeneration" Loop

How I spent 3 hours clicking a button

I didn't intend to spend 3 hours on Dorik's regeneration feature. Really.

Here's what happened:

10:15 AM: Generated a landing page for "organic coffee subscription."

10:17 AM: Hero section looked okay but not great. Saw button: "Regenerate Hero."

10:18 AM: Clicked it. New hero appeared. Better. "One more time," I thought.

1:15 PM: I had regenerated 47 times.

The hero section had evolved through 12 versions. The features section had been rebuilt 8 times. I'd tried 5 different layouts for testimonials.

And here's the crazy part: almost every version was usable.

With most AI builders, regeneration gives you slight variations. Dorik gives you different layouts. Different structures. Different vibes.

Why this matters: Most AI builders are one-shot. You get what you get, and you spend hours manually tweaking.

Dorik lets you iterate at the layout level. Don't like this version? Click. Don't like that one? Click again.

It's the closest thing to having an AI designer that actually listens to feedback.

The tradeoff: I spent 3 hours on regeneration because I could. With Mixo or Durable, I would've accepted the first result and moved on.

Is unlimited iteration better? For perfectionists, yes. For people who need a site in 10 minutes, no.

The Regenerate Feature

This is Dorik's killer feature. With most AI builders, you describe what you want, get one result, and have to live with it (or manually edit everything).

With Dorik, you can:

  • Regenerate the entire layout
  • Regenerate just the hero section
  • Regenerate just the features section
  • Regenerate individual content blocks
  • Regenerate images

I tested this 20+ times. Each regeneration was meaningfully different, not just shuffling elements. This is genuine AI iteration.
