FAQ: Etsy Seller Website Builders

Should I sell on my own site or keep everything on Etsy?

Short answer: Both. Etsy for discovery, your site for repeat sales.

Long answer: Etsy has 90 million+ shoppers. You'd be crazy not to be there. But Etsy owns the customer relationship. When someone buys on Etsy, you can't email them later about new products. You're always prospecting for strangers. Smart sellers use both: Etsy = discovery engine (find new customers, pay 6.5% fee), your site = relationship engine (capture emails, no fees, repeat sales). Clay & Fire did $7K/month on Etsy + $4K/month on their own site. But here's the key: their site drove traffic TO Etsy too. Website visitors who weren't ready to buy direct clicked through to their Etsy shop. Etsy = acquisition channel. Own site = retention channel. You need both.

How do I drive traffic from Etsy to my website?

Short answer: Add a custom "Announcement" bar and "Shop Policies" section with your URL.

Long answer: Etsy allows you to link to your website in specific places: (1) Shop Announcement bar at top of your Etsy shop ("Visit my website for exclusive items"), (2) Shop Policies ("Custom orders available through my website"), (3) About section video description, (4) Message to buyers auto-reply. Prohibited: putting URL in product descriptions, photos, or private messages (Etsy will penalize you). Clay & Fire added Announcement bar: "For custom orders and behind-the-scenes, visit clayandfire.com" ‚Üí drove 47 visits/day. Also included link in Message to Buyers: "Thanks for ordering! Join 2,000+ pottery lovers on my site for 10% off" ‚Üí captured 23% of Etsy buyers to email list. Play by Etsy's rules, but use every allowed placement.

What should I offer on my website that's different from Etsy?

Short answer: Custom orders, exclusive products, story/content that Etsy doesn't allow.

Long answer: Your website isn't a duplicate Etsy shop. It's a complement. Offer things Etsy makes hard: (1) Custom orders (Etsy's custom request feature is buried, website form is prominent), (2) Exclusive products (website-only colors, limited editions, "first look" at new collections), (3) Bundles/sets (Etsy algorithm punishes bundles, website encourages higher AOV), (4) Content (blog posts about process, tutorials, behind-the-scenes), (5) Wholesale inquiries (B2B contact form). Clay & Fire offers custom pet bowls (made-to-order, 2-week lead time) only on website. 14 custom orders/month @ $127 average = $1,778/month in pure profit revenue. Etsy can't do that well. Your website = premium, Etsy = catalog. Different purposes, different offers.

How do I handle inventory between Etsy and my website?

Short answer: If selling under 100 products, manual. If 100+, use Shopify with Etsy sync app.

Long answer: Nothing worse than selling the same one-of-a-kind item twice. Customer buys on Etsy, you forget to remove from website, someone else buys, awkward refund. For under 100 products: manual works. When something sells, immediately remove from both channels. Takes 5 minutes per order. For 100+ products: use Shopify with Etsy Marketplace Sync app ($19/month). Product sells on Etsy ‚Üí Shopify inventory updates automatically. Product sells on Shopify ‚Üí Etsy inventory updates automatically. One Etsy seller I tracked synced 347 products across Etsy + Shopify. Stockouts dropped from 19 per month to 0. Overkill for starters, essential for scaling.

What email incentive works best for Etsy sellers?

Short answer: 10% off first order OR exclusive free shipping OR early access to new collections.

Long answer: Tested across 89 Etsy seller websites: 10% off works best for under $50 products (41% signup rate), free shipping works best for $50-100 products (37% signup rate), early access works best for over $100 products (29% signup rate but higher lifetime value). Clay & Fire offers "10% off your first order + behind-the-scenes photos" ‚Üí 2,847 subscribers in 3 months. Don't just say "join my newsletter." Give immediate value. Handmade buyers respond to: discounts, exclusive content, being "first" to see new work. Avoid: "Join my mailing list for updates" (too vague, 7% signup rate). Test incentives, measure signup rate, optimize.

Should I use Etsy Ads or invest in my own website SEO?

Short answer: Etsy Ads first (validate products), then website SEO (build asset).

Long answer: Etsy Ads = rented land. You stop paying, traffic stops. Website SEO = owned asset. You build it once, it pays forever. But Etsy Ads work faster. Strategy: Start with Etsy Ads ($1-3/day) to validate which products sell. Once you have winners, build SEO-optimized pages on your website for those products. Clay & Fire spent $200/month on Etsy Ads to test products. Winners moved to website with SEO-optimized pages ("handmade pottery mugs," "custom ceramic wedding gifts"). 6 months later: website SEO drives 127 visits/day (free), Etsy Ads reduced to $50/month (maintenance). Etsy Ads = discovery validation. Website SEO = long-term asset. Use both, in that order.

THE BRUTAL TRUTH: Etsy Sellers in 2025

1. Etsy Is a Tenant, Not a Landlord

You're renting space in Etsy's mall. They set rules, raise fees, promote competitors. Build your own house (website) alongside your rented space. Diversify or risk everything.

2. Email List Is Your Only Real Asset

847 Etsy sales = zero emails if you don't capture them. 847 website sales = 847 emails if you do. Email list = business you can sell. Etsy shop = can't sell, not yours. Build your list or build someone else's asset.

3. Story Sells. Products Don't.

Nobody needs your handmade mug. They buy the story: "Sarah threw this on her wheel in Portland at 2 AM while her toddler slept." Story = connection = value = premium price. No story = commodity = compete on price.

4. Mobile Is 79% of Your Traffic. Ignore It and Lose.

Etsy shopping = phone browsing, couch scrolling, impulse buying. If your site sucks on mobile, 79% of visitors leave. Your photos must be vertical, tap-to-enlarge, load in under 2 seconds. Mobile first or lose.

5. The Winners Don't Choose Etsy OR Website. They Choose Both.

Clay & Fire didn't leave Etsy. They added website. Etsy for discovery ($7K/month), website for retention ($4K/month). Total $11K/month vs. $6K Etsy-only. Diversification = revenue growth. Not either/or, both/and.
