COMPARISON

Designer's Dilemma Power vs Templates

Webflow (8.7/10) gives designers total control. Squarespace (8.5/10) gives designers beautiful templates. I built the same portfolio site on both. One made me feel powerful. The other made me feel fast.

Updated: January 2025 - Designer portfolio: 15 projects, blog, contact form

The Portfolio Project

Project: Graphic Designer Portfolio

  • • Homepage with hero animation
  • • 15 project case studies with image galleries
  • • About page with timeline
  • • Services page with pricing tables
  • • Blog section (5 sample posts)
  • • Contact form with validation
  • • Custom smooth-scroll navigation

The Designer's Test: Both platforms target creatives. Webflow promises "total design freedom." Squarespace promises "professional templates." I tested both claims by building the same site.

The Build Experience: What It Felt Like

Webflow: The Frustrating Power Trip

Hour 1-4 (The Hell Phase): I hated it. Nothing made sense. "Classes," "combo classes," "flexbox," "grid"—I felt like I'd never built a website before. Simple things took 30 minutes. I nearly quit twice.

Hour 5-8 (The Click Phase): Something shifted. I started understanding the logic. Built my first complex layout without tutorials. The power became clear. I could break the grid, create custom animations, design interactions.

Hour 9-14 (The Flow Phase): I was in the zone. Built a smooth-scroll navigation. Created hover effects on project cards. Designed a custom contact form with validation. It felt like coding, but visually.

The result: A site that looked custom-coded. Clients thought I'd spent weeks in React. I did it in 14 hours with zero code.

The trade-off: It took 14 hours spread over 5 days. I had to learn a new tool. But I gained a superpower: I can now build anything in Webflow.

Squarespace: The Instant Professional

Minute 1-30: Picked a template. It was already gorgeous. Modern typography, beautiful spacing, professional image treatment. I changed colors, added my logo, uploaded my projects.

Hour 1-3: Customized the template. Moved sections around. Changed image sizes. Added my content. Squarespace's editor is intuitive—click to edit, drag to move.

Hour 4-5: Final polish. Added the blog posts. Set up the contact form. Published. Done.

The result: A beautiful portfolio that looked professional. Not custom—not groundbreaking—but polished and professional.

The trade-off: I hit constraints. Wanted to break the grid? Can't. Wanted custom animations? Limited. Wanted to change the portfolio grid layout? Only 3 options.

But here's the thing: Those constraints are good design. Squarespace doesn't let you make ugly choices.

Design Freedom vs. Smart Constraints

Webflow: Total Freedom

  • ✓ Break the grid, create custom layouts
  • ✓ Pixel-perfect spacing control
  • ✓ Custom animations and interactions
  • ✓ Build components from scratch
  • ✓ Figma-to-Webflow import
  • ✓ CMS for dynamic content
  • ✗ Can make ugly sites if you're not skilled

Squarespace: Smart Constraints

  • ✓ Beautiful templates (can't make ugly)
  • ✓ Consistent design system
  • ✓ Professional image treatment
  • ✓ Built-in blogging platform
  • ✓ E-commerce included
  • ✓ Everything works together
  • ✗ Limited to template structure

The insight: Webflow is like Figma—total freedom, requires skill. Squarespace is like Canva—constrained, but good outcomes guaranteed. 90% of designers don't need Webflow's power. They need Squarespace's beauty.

CMS & Blogging: Power vs Simplicity

Webflow CMS

Power: 10/10

What you can do:

  • • Custom content models (any structure)
  • • Dynamic content binding
  • • Filtering, sorting, search
  • • Multi-item references
  • • Collection pages with pagination

Best for: Content-heavy sites with complex needs (job boards, directories, publications)

Downside: Overkill for simple blogs. Steep learning curve.

Squarespace Blogging

Power: 6/10

What you can do:

  • • Blog posts with categories/tags
  • • Comments (built-in)
  • • RSS feed
  • • Author profiles
  • • Scheduled posts

Best for: Simple blogs, portfolios, content marketing

Downside: Can't create custom content types. Limited filtering.

When Each Platform Wins

Choose Webflow If...

  • ✓ You're a designer who wants total control
  • ✓ You need custom animations and interactions
  • ✓ You're building complex content sites (CMS power)
  • ✓ You want to export your code
  • ✓ You're building client sites (agencies love Webflow)
  • ✓ You're willing to invest 2-3 weeks learning

Choose Squarespace If...

  • ✓ You want a professional site fast (hours, not days)
  • ✓ You love Squarespace templates and don't need to customize heavily
  • ✓ You need e-commerce (included, no plugins)
  • ✓ You want simple blogging
  • ✓ You don't want to learn a complex tool
  • ✓ You value simplicity over power

Final Verdict: The Designer's Choice

After 19 hours of testing (14 Webflow, 5 Squarespace), here's the truth:

Webflow is for professional designers. If you make money building websites, Webflow is a superpower. The learning curve pays off in billable hours. You can charge $5,000+ for sites that take 4 days.

Squarespace is for everyone else. If you're a photographer, consultant, small business, or creative who needs a portfolio—Squarespace delivers professional results in hours, not weeks. The constraints are features, not bugs.

The brutal truth: 90% of people reading this comparison should use Squarespace. It's faster, easier, and the results are beautiful. Webflow is for the 10% who need pixel-perfect control and are willing to earn it.

My recommendation: Start with Squarespace. If you hit its limits (rare), then learn Webflow. Don't start with Webflow unless you know you need its power.
