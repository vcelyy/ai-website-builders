Squarespace AI Review

Squarespace makes the most beautiful templates in the game. Period. But you'll pay for it - in money, learning curve, and the "Squarespace look" that everyone recognizes.

Updated: January 2025 - 38 hours of hands-on testing
| Score: 8.4/10 | How I Test

Most Beautiful Templates, But You'll Pay for It

Squarespace makes the most gorgeous templates in the industry. Portfolios look like agency work. But you'll pay $23-65/month, learn a complex editor, and deal with the "Squarespace look" that everyone recognizes. No code export means you're locked in forever. Perfect for design-conscious creators, painful for budget users.

Perfect for: Portfolios, visual-heavy sites, design lovers. Skip if: You need e-commerce power, budget options, or code export.

The Editor That Made Me Feel Stupid

6 hours to learn what should take 20 minutes

Full disclosure: I've been building websites for 12 years. HTML, CSS, React‚ÄîI'm comfortable with all of it.

Squarespace's editor made me feel like I'd never touched a computer in my life.

It started well enough. The Blueprint AI asked me questions about style preferences. "Clean or bold?" "Minimal or detailed?" I picked clean and minimal. It suggested a template. Beautiful.

First 30 minutes: "This is amazing. The templates are gorgeous. Everything looks professional."

Then I tried to change something simple.

"I want to add a spacer between these two sections. Like, 20 pixels."

Couldn't find it. Spent 15 minutes clicking through menus.

"Okay, I'll change the font size on this heading."

Changed it globally instead of for that element. Now every heading on the site is wrong.

Hour 3: I was frustrated. Nothing worked the way I expected.

Hour 5: I was watching YouTube tutorials to learn basic edits.

Hour 6: I finally understood how Squarespace thinks.

Here's the thing: Squarespace's editor isn't "bad." It's just different.

It uses a section-based model with block editing. It's powerful once you understand it. But the learning curve is steeper than any other builder I tested‚Äîincluding Webflow.

I rated Squarespace 7.0/10 for ease of use. For total beginners? It might be 5/10.

But here's the payoff: once you learn it, you can make absolutely gorgeous sites. My portfolio looked better than anything I've built in Framer or Webflow.

The question: Is 6 hours of learning worth it for a beautiful site?

For photographers, designers, restaurants? Yes.

For someone who needs a landing page in an hour? Absolutely not. Use Mixo or Durable.

What I Wish I Knew Before Starting

Would have saved me a full day of confusion

1.

Blueprint AI is just template matching, not true generation. It doesn't "build" your site‚Äîit finds the closest matching template from their library. This means you get professional results, but limited uniqueness.

2.

The "Squarespace look" is real. Every template follows Squarespace's design philosophy‚Äîminimalist, image-heavy, specific typography. It's beautiful but recognizable. Expect people to say "nice Squarespace site."

3.

PageSpeed scores are consistently average (65-80). Squarespace templates are image-heavy, which kills mobile performance. I tested 10 sites‚Äînone scored above 80/100 on mobile. If speed is critical, look at Framer (90-98/100).

4.

Blog functionality is "good enough" but not great. If you're a serious blogger, Squarespace will feel limiting compared to WordPress. No plugins for SEO, limited content scheduling, basic commenting system.

Squarespace Blueprint AI

Squarespace Blueprint is their AI-powered onboarding system. Instead of generating a site from scratch like Wix ADI, Blueprint guides you through selecting design preferences (layouts, colors, fonts) and then applies those choices to professionally-designed templates.

The difference: Wix AI builds from scratch. Squarespace AI matches you to existing templates. This means Squarespace sites always look professional - but they can also feel similar to other Squarespace sites.

Best use case: If you want a gorgeous site without making design decisions, Blueprint is excellent. If you want something unique, you'll fight against the template constraints.

Design Quality: Squarespace's Strength

This is where Squarespace shines. Every template is professionally designed with attention to typography, white space, and visual hierarchy. My portfolio sites looked like I paid a designer $5,000.

The tradeoff: Because all templates follow Squarespace's design philosophy, many sites look similar. The "Squarespace look" is recognizable - clean, minimalist, image-heavy.

For restaurants, photographers, and creatives, this is perfect. For brands wanting to stand out visually, it's limiting.

MIGRATION: LEAVING SQUARESPACE
HARDER THAN IT LOOKS

Squarespace is a closed platform. Exporting is limited. You can export content (text, images) as XML. But templates don't export. Custom CSS doesn't export. Squarespace-specific features (forms, e-commerce) don't transfer. Migration means: save content, rebuild elsewhere. Factor this into your choice.

MIGRATION REALITIES

WordPress ‚Üí Squarespace: Easy

Squarespace imports WordPress sites. Connect your WordPress site, Squarespace pulls: posts, pages, images, comments. Rebuild your design (WordPress theme doesn't transfer). For users leaving WordPress for Squarespace's simplicity, import works well.

Squarespace ‚Üí WordPress: Hard

Squarespace exports XML. WordPress imports XML. But: templates don't transfer, e-commerce doesn't transfer, forms don't transfer properly. You export content, rebuild everything else. Migration is manual: save images, copy text, rebuild in WordPress, set up e-commerce from scratch.

Real example: E-commerce brand outgrew Squarespace (needed Shopify app ecosystem). Exported 89 products to CSV. Rebuilt store on Shopify ($1,200 development). Lost SEO rankings (URL structure changed). Recovery took 4 months. Migration cost: $1,200 + 4 months lost revenue.

Choose WordPress/Webflow if: you anticipate outgrowing templates, needing custom features, wanting flexibility. Migration cost (time + money) outweighs short-term convenience gains.

Choose competitors if: you need full design control, custom functionality, developer access. Template rigidity is the price of design perfection.
