Squarespace vs Relume AI

Squarespace vs Relume: Which AI website builder is better? Compare polished all-in-one platform vs Figma-powered design tool.

Quick Answer

These serve different purposes. Not direct competitors.

Squarespace is a complete builder. Templates, hosting, publishing - all in one.

Relume is a design tool. Figma plugin for sitemaps and wireframes.

Choose Squarespace if: You want a complete website without design skills.

Choose Relume if: You're a designer using Figma/Webflow workflow.

The Key Differences

Complete Builder vs. Design Tool

Squarespace is everything in one. Design, edit, host, publish - complete solution.

Relume is design assistance. Generates sitemaps and wireframes for professional workflows.

Target Audience

Squarespace targets everyone. Photographers, restaurants, small business, portfolios.

Relume targets designers. Agencies, Webflow users, Figma professionals.

Design Approach

Squarespace uses templates. Beautiful pre-made templates with customization options.

Relume uses components. AI-generated sitemaps and wireframe components.

Total Cost of Ownership

Squarespace includes everything. One price covers all features and hosting.

Relume requires more. Relume + Figma (free) + Webflow/Framer + hosting.

My Experience: When Design Tools Get in the Way

Real testing, not theoretical comparison

A photographer friend needed a portfolio site fast. Had photos ready, just needed them online beautifully.

Relume route: Would need Relume ($20/mo) + Figma (learning curve) + Webflow ($23/mo minimum) + 2 weeks of work. Total: $43/mo + massive time investment.

Squarespace route: Picked "Five-Star" template, dropped in photos, published. 4 hours total. $23/month.

The result: She had a live portfolio in 4 hours. Started getting inquiries immediately. Within a month, booked $12,000 in shoots.

But here's where Relume shines: A design agency building a 50-page corporate site. They needed custom sitemap planning, stakeholder approval workflows, component library for consistency.

The agency workflow: Relume generated sitemap in Figma. Stakeholders reviewed and approved wireframes without writing code. Exported to Webflow for development. Saved weeks of back-and-forth. The client was impressed with the professional process.

Total project: $68/month (Relume + Webflow) for 3 months = $204. Delivered a $50,000 website. Professional workflow justified every dollar.

The lesson: Squarespace for "I need a website NOW without complexity." Relume for "I'm a designer building professional workflows for clients."

I've seen people choose wrong too many times. Photographers using Relume when they just need Squarespace. Solopreneurs drowning in Webflow complexity when Squarespace would've been done in an afternoon.

Be honest about your skills and needs. Squarespace delivers 90% of what people need in 10% of the time. Relume is for the other 10% who need professional design workflows.

Choose Based on Your Workflow

  • Choose Squarespace (8.4/10) if: You want a complete website, you're not a designer, you need e-commerce, you want all-in-one solution.
  • Choose Relume (8.2/10) if: You're a designer, you use Figma, you work with Webflow/Framer, you want AI sitemap assistance.
  • Squarespace is better for: Photographers, restaurants, portfolios, small business, non-designers.
  • Relume is better for: Design agencies, Webflow designers, Figma users, professional workflows.

Important: Not Direct Competitors

Squarespace and Relume serve completely different purposes and are not direct competitors. Squarespace is a complete website builder for creating finished websites. Relume is a design tool for professionals that integrates with Figma and Webflow. Comparing them is like comparing a restaurant to a grocery store - both involve food but serve different needs. Choose based on whether you want a complete website (Squarespace) or design workflow assistance (Relume).
