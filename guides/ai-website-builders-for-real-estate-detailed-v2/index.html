Real Estate Website Tips

Professional photos are non-negotiable

90% of home buyers search online. Poor photos lose buyers before they even inquire. Invest in professional photography - it pays for itself.

Mobile optimization is critical

Buyers browse homes on phones while waiting, commuting, watching TV. If your site doesn't work perfectly on mobile, you're losing 70% of potential leads.

Speed matters enormously

Real estate sites are photo-heavy. Optimize images, use lazy loading, fast hosting. Every second of load time loses potential buyers.

Lead capture should be strategic

Don't gate all content. Offer free value, capture leads when they're ready. Property inquiries, valuation requests, newsletter signup work better than forcing contact.

Showcase your expertise

Market updates, neighborhood guides, buying tips. Position yourself as the local expert, not just another agent.

Video tours are increasingly expected

Buyers want to walk through properties before visiting in person. Video or 3D tours save time for both you and qualified buyers.

Social proof drives conversions

Client testimonials, sold listings, success stories. Trust is everything in real estate. Prove you deliver results.

Multiple contact methods

Phone, email, text, form. Different prospects prefer different methods. Remove all friction from contacting you.

Neighborhood information adds value

Schools, amenities, commute times, market trends. Help buyers understand the lifestyle, not just the house.

Respond quickly to inquiries

Buyers often contact multiple agents. First response wins. Aim for under 5 minutes during business hours.

Real estate websites should showcase properties beautifully, capture leads strategically, and position you as the local expert. Professional photos and mobile optimization are non-negotiable.
