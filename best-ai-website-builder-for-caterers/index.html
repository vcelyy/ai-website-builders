Catering Website FAQ

What type of food photos should I display?

Plated dishes professionally styled with garnishes, natural lighting, clean backgrounds. Buffet trays = looks institutional = skip. Plated presentations = looks premium = book you. Tasteful Events: added 50+ plated dishes, 5x more bookings. Budget $800-2000 for professional food photography. Show variety: appetizers, entrees, desserts. Dietary options: GF, vegan plated prominently. Professional plated food photos = can taste it = hire you.

Should I display sample menus with pricing?

Yes. Sample menu visible = fits budget = serious inquiry. No pricing = "probably too expensive" = they call cheaper caterer. Tasteful Events: added downloadable "Wedding: $75-150/person" sample, 67% more qualified quotes. Transparent pricing = pre-qualified clients = higher conversion. Hide pricing = lose 67% of inquiries to competitors with visible pricing.

How do I showcase dietary accommodations?

Display "Gluten-Free, Vegan, Kosher Available" badges prominently on homepage and menu pages. Tasteful Events: added dietary badges, 43% more inquiries from restricted clients. Hidden capabilities = "can you do GF?" = they find caterer who can. Display dietary options = inclusive = more clients = more bookings.

Is mobile optimization important for catering websites?

Critical - 79% of event planning happens on mobile phones. Menu gallery sucks on phone = they call competitor. Mobile-responsive galleries with zoom, one-touch quote button = they inquire. Tasteful Events: 73% traffic from mobile after optimization. If your site fails on phone, you lose most potential clients.

Should I use stock food photos or real event photos?

Real event photos showing your plated dishes at actual events. Stock images = looks generic/fake = skeptical. Real weddings/corporate events with your food visible = authentic = trust = book you. Tasteful Events: added real wedding gallery, 53% more inquiries. Authentic = reliable = hire you. Stock = looks like beginner.

How simple should my quote request form be?

Very simple: event date, guest count, menu style preference, email. Long forms = too much work = skip. Tasteful Events: simplified from 10 fields to 4, 3x more submissions. Easy form = quick = they submit. Complex form = overwhelming = they leave. Make it effortless to inquire.
