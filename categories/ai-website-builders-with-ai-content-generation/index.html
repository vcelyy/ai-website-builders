Frequently Asked Questions

Is AI-generated content good enough to publish without editing?

No. AI content sounds generic. Uses predictable phrases. Lacks specific details. Visitors recognize AI patterns. "Innovative solutions," "unlock potential," "drive results" = meaningless. Publish as-is, and you blend into noise. AI = 80% done. Last 20% (personality, specifics, voice) = 80% of impact. Always edit. Always add specifics. Always inject your voice.

Which AI builder has the best content generation?

Durable tested best for AI copy quality across 4 formats (landing pages, blog posts, emails, social media). Generated most usable first drafts. Not perfect, but closest to ready-to-use. Mixo fastest for landing pages (60 seconds). Wix has most content options. Framer has basic AI but best designs. Choose based on your priority: speed (Mixo), quality (Durable), variety (Wix), design (Framer).

Does AI content hurt SEO rankings?

Untouched AI content? Yes. Google doesn't penalize AI specifically, but penalizes low-quality, generic content. That's what AI generates without editing. AI + human polish ranks fine because it's actually good. The issue isn't AI origin. It's generic quality. Edit AI content: add specifics, inject personality, optimize SEO. Result ranks well.

How much editing does AI content actually need?

Expect 20-30% editing for usable content. Replace generic phrases ("innovative solutions") with specifics ("reduced churn from 8% to 3.2%"). Add real examples. Inject your voice. Fact-check claims. Optimize for SEO. AI gives you structure and speed. You add authenticity and effectiveness. Without editing: 2% conversion. With editing: 6%+ conversion.

Can AI content replace copywriters?

No. AI generates structure, not substance. Copywriters provide strategy, voice, persuasion, brand alignment. AI provides speed and first drafts. Best combination: AI generates (minutes), copywriter refines (hours). Faster than copywriter from scratch, better than AI alone. AI augments copywriters, doesn't replace them.

What if readers know my content is AI-generated?

They won't know if you edit it. Untouched AI content? They'll recognize patterns: generic phrases, predictable structure, lack of specifics. "This feels fake" = common feedback. But edited AI content (AI + human polish)? Readers can't tell. They just see good content. The secret: don't publish raw AI output. Always edit. Always add specifics. Always inject personality.

Should I fact-check AI content?

Absolutely. AI hallucinates confidently. Invents statistics. Creates fake testimonials. Cites non-existent studies. One error destroys credibility. Reader: "If this claim is wrong, what else is wrong?" Fact-check every number. Verify every example. Confirm every claim. AI generates text, not truth. Truth is your job.
