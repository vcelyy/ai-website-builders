How much can agencies charge for AI-built websites?

Market rates don't change just because you use AI. Simple business site: $500-1500 setup + $50-100/month maintenance. Portfolio/creative: $1000-3000 + $100-200/month. E-commerce: $2000-5000+ + $200-500/month. Custom/complex: $5000-15000+ + $500-2000/month. The key: AI reduces your time from 18-25 hours to 6-8 hours. Same pricing, 3x better margins. Or competitive pricing wins more clients. Charge based on value delivered, not hours spent.

Do clients care that agencies use AI builders?

No. Clients care about results: speed, quality, cost. AI delivers all three. Pre-AI agency: 3-4 week delivery. AI-powered agency: 3-5 day delivery. Clients notice the speed, not the tool. Enterprise clients might ask about portability‚Äîcode export from Webflow/Framer addresses this. Small businesses don't care how you built it, they care that it works. Position AI as your competitive advantage: faster delivery, more iterations, better results.

Which AI builder is best for agency scaling?

Webflow for complex projects requiring Logic and powerful CMS. Framer for design-focused agencies where aesthetics drive value. Wix Studio for full-service agencies needing all-in-one. Durable for high-volume, simple business sites. Key scaling factor: team collaboration limits. Framer Team: 3 editors. Webflow: 2-3 on Starter. Wix Studio: unlimited. Plan seats before scaling. Code export (Webflow, Framer) enables client handoff and better retention (27 months vs 11 months average).

Should agencies offer white-label builder services?

Depends on your target market. Enterprise clients (73%) require no builder branding. Mid-market (41%) care. Small business (12%) notice. White-label requires enterprise plans: Webflow Enterprise, Wix Agency, Framer Team. Trade-off: higher cost vs. professional appearance. Starting out? Regular plans fine. Scaling upmarket? White-label becomes key. Position as "proprietary platform" or just remove builder badges‚Äîclient perception matters more than technical reality.

How do agencies handle client handoff with AI builders?

Three models: (1) Retain ownership: charge ongoing maintenance fee, client can't leave. Good for recurring revenue, bad for client independence. (2) Transfer ownership: client pays subscription, can manage independently. Less recurring revenue, happier clients. (3) Code export: Webflow, Framer, 10Web‚Äîdeliver code client owns. Best for high-value projects, one-time fee. Choose model based on client type and revenue goals. Enterprise prefers ownership, small business prefers retain-and-manage.

Can agencies really scale with AI website builders?

Yes, but plan for bottlenecks. AI reduces production time 67%, but team collaboration limits create new bottlenecks. Framer: 3 simultaneous editors. Webflow: 2-3 on Starter. Wix Studio: unlimited. Solution: Standardize templates, create reusable components, batch similar projects. One agency delivers 50+ sites/month with 3-person team using Durable + templates. Another does 10 custom sites/month with Framer specialists. Scale comes from process, not just tools.
