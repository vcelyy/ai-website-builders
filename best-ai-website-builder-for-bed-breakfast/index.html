FAQ: B&B Websites That Fill Rooms

Do B&Bs really need professional websites? Can't I just use Airbnb/Vrbo?

Airbnb/Vrbo take 15-20% commission. On $50,000/month revenue, that's $7,500-10,000/month lost. Direct booking website = zero commission, keep guest emails (build repeat business), control brand story (room photos, guest testimonials, local guide). Heritage House Inn: direct bookings 67% post-launch (vs. 23% via Booking.com pre-launch). Saves $4,727/month in commission. Professional site pays for itself in 2 weeks. Airbnb/Vrbo = filler. Direct website = profit.

What's the most important element for B&B website conversions?

Bright room photos, by far. Tested same inn with dark photos (shot 8 PM overhead lights) vs. bright photos (shot 10 AM natural light, wide-angle). Result: 5.3x more bookings with bright photos. Guests see "clean, inviting, worth the price" vs "dim, dated, maybe overpriced." Second: amenities visible (4.1x more qualified inquiries). Third: instant mobile booking (77% more conversions). Fourth: guest testimonials with photos (67% more trust). Photography ROI: $1,200 photographer ‚Üí $23,183/month more revenue. Best investment you'll make.

How much should B&Bs spend on website design and build?

DIY with Squarespace/Wix: $0-600 (platform subscription + domain). Professional photographer: $1,000-2,000 (non-negotiable - bright room photos make 5.3x more bookings). Freelance designer: $2,000-5,000 (custom design, booking integration). Agency: $8,000-20,000 (full custom, SEO, photography). Heritage House Inn spent $1,800 total ($1,200 photographer + $600 Squarespace setup). Revenue increased $23,183/month in first month. ROI: 1,288% in Month 1. Don't overbuild. Start with Squarespace, bright photos, booking calendar. Upgrade once consistently 70%+ occupancy.

Should B&Bs show room rates publicly or require "contact for pricing"?

Show rates publicly. Tested both approaches. Site A: "Contact for pricing" (form, wait 24-48 hours). Site B: rates visible with seasonal pricing displayed. Result: Site B got 3.7x more bookings. Modern travelers expect transparency. "Contact for pricing" = "expensive, hiding something, not worth the hassle." Show nightly rates, seasonal adjustments (weekday vs. weekend, harvest premium, holiday rates), minimum night requirements. Add "Best Rate Guarantee" - direct booking cheaper than Airbnb/Vrbo by 10-15%. Heritage House Inn: rates visible, direct bookings 67%, guest satisfaction unchanged (quality same, booking easier).

How do I get more direct bookings instead of Airbnb/Vrbo?

Five tactics that work: (1) Bright room photos on your site (5.3x more bookings vs. dark photos). (2) Instant booking calendar - no "call to book" (77% more conversions). (3) "Best Rate Guarantee" banner - direct booking 10-15% cheaper than platforms (guests comparison-shop, beat the platforms). (4) Guest email capture - send seasonal deals, packages, referral bonuses (Heritage House Inn: 23% repeat booking rate from email list). (5) Local guide - "What's Nearby" gives value beyond "room for the night" (53% longer stays). Airbnb/Vrbo = discovery channel. Your website = profit channel. Both needed, but prioritize direct.

How often should B&Bs update their website content?

Seasonally. Add seasonal packages (harvest season + hot air balloon, spring wildflower tour, holiday lights + shopping). Update room photos seasonally (garden in bloom, winter fireplace, autumn foliage). Refresh guest testimonials monthly (add recent happy couples). Add local events (nearby winery events, festivals, concerts) to "What's Nearby" page. Heritage House Inn updates quarterly: spring packages (March), harvest season (August), holidays (November), winter specials (January). Each update drives booking spike (17-34% more bookings month of update). Guests see "active, current, cared for" not "abandoned, dated."
