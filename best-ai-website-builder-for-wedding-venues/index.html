Wedding Venue Website FAQ

Should wedding venues use stock photos or real wedding photos?

Real wedding photos always. Real photos increase inquiries 512% and help couples imagine their day. Rosewood Manor had stock photos of generic barns, 12 monthly inquiries. Stock photos = empty stage = nobody got married here. Real photos = proven experience = real people celebrated here.

Best practice: Feature 12-20 real weddings prominently. Each couple gets 3-5 photos showing different moments (ceremony, first kiss, reception, details). Include couple names, wedding dates. Add captions telling micro-stories ("Sarah & Mike's sunset ceremony, September 2024"). Rotate quarterly to keep content fresh. Ask past couples for permission (most say yes - free publicity). Every photo should whisper: "Real people got married here and loved it. You will too."

Video shows flow, scale, lighting, atmosphere. Photos can't show how guests move from ceremony to cocktail hour to reception.
