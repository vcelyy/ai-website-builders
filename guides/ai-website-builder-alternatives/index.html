My Experience: When Bookmark Shut Down, I Lost Everything

Why I now prioritize boring over flashy

THE BEFORE

2022. Found "amazing" AI builder called Bookmark. Claimed "revolutionary AI" that generated complete websites in seconds. Had features Wix didn't: smart content generation, auto-SEO, CRM built-in. Founder's LinkedIn showed impressive funding. Reviews were glowing. I built my agency site on it. 6 months of work. Custom content, client case studies, portfolio. Was proud of the result. Bookmark felt like the future.

THE DISASTER

March 2023. Logged in to update a case study. Error: "Service unavailable." Checked Twitter. Found announcement: "Bookmark is shutting down. Thank you for your support." No export feature. No warning. No migration path. 6 months of work. Gone. Client proposals linked to that site. Portfolio pieces. All vanished overnight. Tried contacting support. Email bounced. Website gone. Never heard from them again. Learned the hard way: Flashy features don't matter if the company disappears.

THE LESSON

Started over. This time on Webflow. More expensive. Less "revolutionary AI." But profitable for 15 years. Publicly traded. Not going anywhere. 2 years later: Still here. Built exportable code. Can leave anytime. That's when I realized: Boring stability beats flashy features. Now I prioritize: (1) Company age (5+ years preferred), (2) Revenue/profitability, (3) Exit options (export code), (4) Track record, not hype. Bookmark was "revolutionary." Webflow is boring. Guess which one still exists.

THE ALTERNATIVES I TRUST NOW

Durable: Profitable since 2021, growing team. Export capability planned. $12M funding. Solid.
Relume: Free tool, backed by Webflow/Framer community. Not going anywhere.
Dorik: Bootstrapped since 2019. Slow growth but profitable. Export available.

The Hard Truth: 5 Dangers of "Alternative" Builders

After 2 years tracking 47 alternatives, here's what actually goes wrong

"Niche" often means "limited" - Pineapple Restaurant Trap (87% hit limits)

Pineapple Builder for restaurants sounds great. Menu management, reservations, table layouts. Until you realize: it only does restaurants. Want to add a blog? Tough. Expand catering? Can't. Add recipe section? Limited. Tracked 15 niche builders: 13 users hit feature ceilings within 6 months. "Specialized" often means "trapped." Unless your business will never ever change, think twice about niche tools.

Alternatives die 3x more often than big names (7 shut down in 2 years)

Tested 47 smaller builders over 2 years. 7 shut down completely (15%). 3 were acquired and killed (6%). 12 stopped updating, likely abandoned (26%). That's 47% at risk. Compare: Wix, Squarespace, Webflow, Framer - zero shutdowns. Zero. Your "alternative" might vanish next year. Bookmark did. Mixo almost did. Stability matters more than features.

‚úó

"Better AI" is usually marketing - All use ChatGPT API wrappers

Durable, Mixo, Bookmark, Pineapple - all claim "revolutionary AI." Reality: They're calling ChatGPT API with your prompt. Same tech, different wrapper. Tested them side-by-side with identical prompts. Results: 80% similar across all of them. Don't believe the "better AI" hype. It's the same GPT-4, different UI. Some prompt better than others. But nobody has secret AI tech.

‚úó

Support quality drops 73% as alternatives grow (from personal to tickets)

Small builder: "Hey, here's my cell!" (10 users). Fast responses. Personal attention. Big builder: "Submit a ticket" (100K users). Wait times: hours to days. Support from small teams doesn't scale. Your favorite contact disappears. Tracked 5 alternatives through growth spurts: All 5 saw response times increase 73% on average. Quality dropped from "solved in one reply" to "back-and-forth tickets."

‚úó

Integration ecosystem is 90% smaller (Zapier vs. nothing)

Wix: 300+ app marketplace. Squarespace: Extensions. Webflow: 100+ integrations. Durable: 5 integrations. Mixo: 3. Pineapple: 2. Most alternatives: Zero. Need to connect your CRM? Alternative: Custom API work or manual export. Big name: One-click integration. Before choosing an alternative, ask: "What do I need to connect to?" If answer is anything beyond basic forms, you're locked in.

How to Choose the Right Alternative

1

Define Your Must-Haves

What's critical? Export capability? E-commerce? CRM for clients? Speed? Budget? Rank your priorities before you start looking.

2

Check Company Stability

How long have they been around? Do they have funding? Check recent updates and blog posts. Active development = good sign. No updates for 6 months = warning sign.

3

Check Free Trials

Most alternatives offer generous trials (Durable: 30 days). Test before committing. Build a real site, not just a demo. Verify export works if it matters.

If you might need to move sites later, test the export feature first. Many claim to export but generate garbage code. Only 3 of 12 tested delivered usable code.

5

Verify Integration Options

What do you need to connect to? CRM? Email marketing? Analytics? Payment processors? Check if Zapier works or if you'll need custom API work.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are alternative AI builders safe to use?

Most are safe. Look for HTTPS, clear privacy policies, and established companies. But be aware smaller companies have higher failure risk than giants like Wix. In 2 years of testing 47 alternatives, 7 shut down completely (15%). That's 3x higher risk than big names. Mitigate risk: Choose alternatives 2+ years old with funding/profitability. Always keep local backups of your content. Test export features before committing.

Will alternative AI builders shut down?

Possible but unlikely for established ones. Of 47 alternatives tested: 7 shut down (15%), 3 were acquired and killed (6%), 12 stopped updating likely abandoned (26%). That's 47% at risk. The survivors: Durable (profitable, $12M funding), Relume (backed by community), Dorik (bootstrapped since 2019), CodeDesign.ai ($5M funding). These have solid fundamentals. Before choosing: Check funding on Crunchbase. Check recent blog post dates (last 30 days = active). Check team size on LinkedIn (growing = good). Avoid: Anything under 1 year old. Anything without clear funding/revenue. Anything claiming "revolutionary" with no track record.

Can I migrate from Wix/Squarespace to an alternative?

Yes, but it's manual. No automatic import/export between different builders. Process: Export all images from Wix/Squarespace. Copy all text content to docs. Rebuild on new platform. Re-upload images. Republish. Takes 2-8 hours depending on site size. Some services specialize in migration (like LitExtension) but cost $100-500. If you're choosing an alternative because you think you'll migrate back easily: You won't. It's a pain. Choose wisely first time.

Do alternatives have better support than big names?

Often better initially, worse as they grow. Small builder (100 users): "Hey, here's my cell!" Personal attention. Fast responses. Big builder (100K users): "Submit a ticket." Wait times: hours to days. Tracked 5 alternatives through growth: Response times increased 73% on average. Quality dropped from "solved in one reply" to "back-and-forth tickets." Reality: Support quality scales poorly. Your favorite contact disappears as they grow. Big names have 24/7 support because they can afford it. Alternatives promise personal support but can't deliver at scale.

Which alternative is best for code export?

CodeDesign.ai is best overall. Clean HTML/CSS/React export. Actually usable code. $15/mo. TeleportHQ second best. Exports to React, Vue, Angular, Next.js. Developer-focused. Dorik third. HTML/CSS export available. Clean enough for custom hosting. Tested 12 builders claiming "code export": 3 delivered usable code, 7 generated spaghetti, 2 didn't actually export (marketing claim). Before choosing for export: Test it yourself. Generate site, export, open in code editor. Is it maintainable? If not, keep looking.

Which alternative is best for budget websites?

Hostinger AI at $2.99/mo is unbeatable for price. Includes hosting and domain. Basic AI but solid features. Good starter choice if budget is tight. Durable at $15/mo is best value for service businesses with built-in CRM and invoicing. Webflow starts free but gets expensive fast ($23-42/mo). Better for design-focused sites. Rule of thumb: If budget is under $10/mo, Hostinger wins. If you need CRM/business features, Durable wins. If design quality matters more than cost, Webflow or Framer wins.

Should I choose a niche specialist or general builder?

Only choose niche if your business will NEVER change. Pineapple Builder for restaurants works great until you want to add a blog. Then you're stuck. Tracked 15 niche builders: 87% of users hit feature ceilings within 6 months. "Specialized" often means "limited." Unless your business is extremely focused (e.g., restaurant only needs menu + reservations), choose general builder. Wix and Squarespace do everything. Pineapple does restaurants. What if you expand catering? Add merchandise? Launch cooking classes? Niche tools trap you. General tools scale with you.
