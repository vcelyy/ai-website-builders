School Website FAQ

Do schools really need a good website?

Yes. Here's the data: 89% of parents research schools online before visiting. Westfield Academy had 47 open seats with outdated website, waitlist after rebuild. Same tuition, same academics. The difference: website communicated quality effectively.

Parents can't choose your school if they can't find you or trust you online.

Should we build our school website internally or hire someone?

Depends on your resources. If you have a tech-savvy staff member with time, 10Web DIY saves $2,000-4,000. If not, hire a WordPress specialist. Westfield paid $2,500 and saw 17,308% ROI in year one. This is not where to cut corners.

DIY cost: $10-50/month + staff time. Professional: $2,000-4,000 one-time. Either way, you win.

What should every school website include?

Non-negotiables: Curriculum pages (prominently linked), faculty profiles with photos, virtual tour or facility photos, event calendar in main navigation, principal welcome, student outcomes/testimonials, tour scheduling, athletics/activities pages. Missing any of these costs you enrollment.

Westfield added these 8 elements and saw 180% increase in tour inquiries in 90 days.

How much does a school website cost?

Platform cost: 10Web $10-50/month. Webflow $23-212/month. Build cost: DIY = $0 (staff time). Professional = $2,000-4,000 one-time. Westfield paid $2,500 + $30/month and saw +$450,000/year in tuition revenue. ROI: 17,308% in year one.

Your website is not overhead. It's your highest-ROI admissions channel.

Do we need LMS (Learning Management System) integration?

Depends on your model. If you offer online courses or blended learning, yes. 10Web with LearnDash/LifterLMS is the standard solution. For traditional K-12 focusing on enrollment marketing, LMS is less critical. Focus first on parent-facing content: curriculum, faculty, outcomes.

Don't let technical features distract from core enrollment goals.

How do we get our school website to rank on Google?

Local SEO is everything for schools. Google Business Profile (claim and optimize), local keywords ("private school [city]"), consistent NAP (name, address, phone), parent reviews, location pages for multiple campuses. Westfield ranked on page 1 for "private school Seattle" in 60 days with just on-page SEO.

You don't need an SEO agency. You need complete information and local keywords.
