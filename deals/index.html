Hidden Costs of "Free" Trials

Free trials aren't malicious. But understanding limitations saves headaches.

‚ùå Export Lock-in

The trap: Build entire site on free tier, then can't export without upgrading.

Worst offenders:

  • ‚Ä¢ Wix: No export ever
  • ‚Ä¢ Squarespace: No export ever
  • ‚Ä¢ Framer: 3 free exports max
  • ‚Ä¢ Durable: Export requires Pro ($15/mo)

Safe options: 10Web, Webflow, WordPress-based builders. Export is always available.

‚ùå Branding You Can't Remove

The trap: Free sites show "Made with X" footer. Sometimes impossible to remove even on paid plans.

Check before building:

  • ‚Ä¢ Can you remove branding on free tier?
  • ‚Ä¢ Which plan allows branding removal?
  • ‚Ä¢ Is removal permanent or requires renewal?

My reviews note this for every builder. Check before committing.

‚ùå Storage/Bandwidth Limits

The trap: Site goes offline when you hit invisible limits.

Common limits on free tiers:

  • ‚Ä¢ Storage: 100MB - 1GB (photos fill fast)
  • ‚Ä¢ Bandwidth: 1-5 GB/month (viral = broken site)
  • ‚Ä¢ Pages: 3-10 pages max
  • ‚Ä¢ Forms: Limited submissions/month

Pro tip: Start with content plan. If you need 20+ pages, free tier will break. Plan accordingly.

‚ùå Auto-Renew Traps

The trap: Free trial ends, you're automatically charged annual fee.

How they catch you:

  • ‚Ä¢ Credit card required for "free" trial
  • ‚Ä¢ Auto-enroll in annual billing ($300+ charge)
  • ‚Ä¢ No reminder email before charge
  • ‚Ä¢ Refund takes 4-6 weeks (if at all)

My approach: Use virtual cards for trials (Privacy.com, CIB). Set max charge $5. Trial ends automatically if I forget to cancel.

Can I stack multiple deals?

Usually no. Most builders limit you to one promotion per account. However, you can often combine a free trial with an annual pricing discount for maximum savings.

Free trials let you test before committing. The catch: you'll need to upgrade to publish or access premium features. Some limit storage, custom domains, or remove branding. I note these limitations in each review.

Free Trial Decision Framework

Free trials let you test risk-free. But they also cost time. Here's when to use them vs. jump straight to paid:

‚úì Use Free Trials When:
  • ‚Ä¢ You're undecided between 2-3 platforms. Test them all simultaneously.
  • ‚Ä¢ You need specific features you can't verify without testing (e.g., app integrations, mobile editor).
  • ‚Ä¢ You're learning website building for the first time. Trials let you learn without commitment.
  • ‚Ä¢ Time value is low (your time isn't worth $50-100/hour yet).
  • ‚Ä¢ You want to test support responsiveness before committing.
‚úì Jump Straight to Paid When:
  • ‚Ä¢ You already know which platform you want. Trials waste time you could spend building.
  • ‚Ä¢ Your time is valuable. If you earn $50+/hour, a 5-hour trial costs $250 in opportunity cost.
  • ‚Ä¢ You need to launch THIS WEEK. Trials delay your launch.
  • ‚Ä¢ Most platforms offer 30-day money-back guarantees anyway. That's a risk-free paid trial.
  • ‚Ä¢ You've already tested competitors. This is your final choice.
üí∞ The Time Value Calculation:

If your time is worth $50/hour and a trial takes 5 hours, that trial costs $250.

If the platform costs $20/month, you could pay for a full year ($240) in less time than the trial takes.

Rule of thumb: If you're 90% sure which platform you want, skip the trial. Your time is better spent building.

Trials make sense when: You're truly undecided OR your time has low opportunity cost (learning phase, hobby project).
