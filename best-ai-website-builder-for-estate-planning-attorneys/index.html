6 Website Mistakes That Cost Estate Planning Practices Clients

Mistake #1: Vague "estate planning services"

Site lists "estate planning" without specifics. Family searching "living trust attorney" - does Walsh do that? Unclear = they call competitor. Fix: List services explicitly: "Living Trusts, Last Will & Testament, Power of Attorney, Healthcare Directives, Trust Administration." Specific services = they call you for their need.

Mistake #2: No process explained

Attorney has no visible process. Family: "what happens when I hire you?" Unclear = anxiety = call competitor with clear steps. Fix: Add "Step 1: Free Consultation, Step 2: Custom Plan, Step 3: Draft Documents, Step 4: Signing & Funding" prominently. Process visible = comfort = trust = hire.

Mistake #3: Buried testimonials

Attorney has happy clients but no testimonials visible. Result: families doubt legitimacy, call competitor with social proof. Fix: Add "Protected my family's future - Sarah M., Living Trust Client" prominently. Testimonials visible = others trusted = safe = hire.

Mistake #4: No free consultation offer

Site doesn't mention consultation cost. Family assumes expensive = delays decision = competitor with "free consultation" gets the booking. Fix: Add "Free 30-Minute Consultation - No Obligation" prominently. Risk-free = book now instead of researching forever.

Mistake #5: Hidden credentials

Attorney mentions bar admission in footer. Families miss it, doubt qualifications, call competitor who displays credentials prominently. Fix: Add "18 Years Experience | State Bar Certified | Estate Planning Specialist" near hero. Credentials visible = qualified = safe = hire.

Mistake #6: Difficult mobile booking

Site requires desktop for consultation booking. Family discussion on phone = can't book easily = call competitor. Fix: Mobile-optimized booking form with calendar scheduling. Mobile booking = family books together during discussion = immediate business.
