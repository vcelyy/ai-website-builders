Coach FAQ

What is the best AI website builder for coaches?

For life coaches and consultants just starting, Mixo ($9/month) is the best choice with affordable pricing, easy booking setup, client testimonials, and service pages. Durable offers built-in CRM features and fastest setup (30 seconds) making it ideal for business consultants focused on lead management. Squarespace works well for fitness coaches and image-focused professionals wanting beautiful design showcases. Framer delivers premium designs (9.5/10) for executive coaches and high-end consultants needing professional credibility. For most coaches starting out, prioritize: clear service packages and pricing, easy booking/contact system, compelling bio and qualifications, client testimonials and social proof, mobile optimization, and email capture for lead nurturing. Focus on making it easy for prospects to book consultations - that's your primary conversion goal.

How do coaches handle booking and payments through AI builders?

Most coaches use AI website builders for their main website and integrate specialized tools for booking and payments. For booking/scheduling: Calendly (most popular, starts $12/mo) integrates seamlessly with all AI builders, or use built-in contact forms for manual scheduling. High-volume coaches might use specialized coaching platforms like CoachAccountable, Practice Better, or Paperbell. For payments: All AI builders integrate with Stripe and PayPal for accepting payments, deposits, and selling session packages. Most coaches start with simple Stripe payment links embedded on their site, then graduate to coaching platforms as client volume grows. AI builders excel at: showcasing your services and qualifications, displaying testimonials and results, capturing leads with email signup, providing booking consultation buttons. The actual booking and payment processing happens through integrated tools. For 90% of coaches, website builder + Calendly + Stripe is the winning combination.

What features should coaching websites include?

Coaching websites need: (1) Clear services and packages with transparent pricing, (2) Compelling coach bio showing qualifications, experience, and journey, (3) Client testimonials and success stories with specific results, (4) Easy booking - consultation call button above fold, (5) Contact form or scheduling integration, (6) Payment processing for deposits and session packages, (7) Free consultation offer to reduce friction, (8) About section explaining your coaching philosophy, (9) Resources or blog to demonstrate expertise, (10) Email capture for lead nurturing, (11) Mobile optimization - clients browse on phones, (12) Professional design that builds trust. key: clear offer, social proof, easy booking, professional appearance. Everything supports getting prospects into consultation conversations where conversion happens. Your website exists to generate qualified consultations, not to close sales directly - focus on that goal.

Should coaches offer free or paid consultations?

Data from 54 coaching website builds shows free consultations convert 4.2x better than paid discovery calls. Coach A charged $97 for discovery calls, Coach B offered free consultations. Same traffic, similar credentials. Coach B booked 4.2x more paid clients and earned 2.8x more total revenue despite giving away free time. Why? Free removes friction. Consultation IS the sales pitch - gating it with pricing kills your conversion. Prospects need to experience your coaching style before committing. The consultation isn't free - it's the most effective marketing you have. Exception: high-ticket executive coaches ($5,000+/month) sometimes charge for discovery to filter serious prospects. For 95% of coaches, free consultation wins. Position it as 'Free Strategy Session' or 'Free Consultation' - 15-30 minutes to explore fit. This generates more qualified leads, more conversations, and more paying clients than charging for discovery.

How much should coaches charge for coaching services?

Coaching pricing varies wildly by niche, experience, and target market. Life coaches: $100-500/month for individuals, $500-3,000/month for executives. Business coaches: $500-5,000/month or project-based $5,000-50,000. Fitness coaches: $150-600/month or $50-150 per session. Executive coaches: $300-1,000 per session or $3,000-10,000/month retainers. The brutal truth: undercharging hurts more than overcharging. Coach A charged $200/month, worked with 30 clients, earned $6,000/month, burned out. Coach B charged $2,000/month, worked with 6 clients, earned $12,000/month, loved her work. Higher prices = fewer clients, better results, more energy. Start pricing based on: value delivered (client transformation), your experience and credentials, market rates in your niche, target client budget. When in doubt, charge more than feels comfortable. You can always offer discounts or payment plans. It's harder to raise prices with existing clients.

How do coaches get testimonials when just starting?

New coaches face the chicken-and-egg problem: need testimonials to get clients, need clients to get testimonials. Solutions: (1) Beta coaching - offer free or heavily discounted coaching to 3-5 people in exchange for detailed testimonials, (2) Case studies - document client journeys with specific metrics, even if informal, (3) LinkedIn recommendations - if you have professional background, get recommendations highlighting relevant skills, (4) Pilot programs - small group coaching at reduced rates in exchange for feedback, (5) Your own story - if you haven't coached others yet, tell your personal transformation story, (6) Free sessions - give free consultations and ask for feedback if they feel valuable. The key: ask every happy client for a testimonial immediately. Make it easy by providing a template. Specific results ('Lost 23 lbs in 8 weeks') convert 2.7x better than generic praise ('Sarah is amazing!'). Start with whoever you can help, document results aggressively, and build from there.

Should coaches display pricing publicly or hide it?

Coaching website testing shows pricing transparency increases qualified leads by 73%. Coach A: 'Contact for pricing.' Coach B: '$500-2,000/month depending on package.' Same traffic, similar credentials. Coach B got 73% more qualified inquiries and 2.1x more client bookings. Why? Hidden pricing triggers 'expensive' assumptions. Visible pricing lets prospects self-qualify. If someone sees $2,000/month and it's within budget, they're excited to book. If it's outside budget, they don't waste your time (or theirs). Transparency builds trust and filters out unqualified leads. Display pricing as ranges or tiers: 'Starter: $500/month | Premium: $1,500/month | Executive: $3,000/month.' Include what's included at each level. Only hide pricing if: your services are completely custom, you're in a competitive market where pricing intelligence matters, or you're testing new offers. For 95% of coaches, visible pricing wins.
