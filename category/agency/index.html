Why most AI builders fail agencies

Vendor lock-in is a dealbreaker

Framer makes beautiful sites. But you can't export to clean React, you can't give clients the source, and if you stop paying Framer, the site goes down. Try explaining that to a client who just paid you $8,000 for a "custom" website. I've had that conversation. It goes like this: "So I paid for a custom site but I can't have the files?" "Well, technically you own the content but..." It's not fun.

White-label options are rare

10Web is the only one with decent white-label hosting. Durable and Framer both splash their branding everywhere. You can hide some of it, but clients eventually figure it out. Nothing says "premium agency" like a client seeing "Powered by Framer" in their footer and Googling it to find out they could have done it themselves.

Client handoff is awkward

Agencies build, then hand off. But how do you hand off a Durable site? "Here's the login to this proprietary platform you've never heard of." Clients want WordPress because they know it, or they want code they can host anywhere. Most AI builders don't give you either. I've handed off WordPress sites to hundreds of clients. Never had an issue. Handed off a Framer site once—spent an hour explaining why it was different.

You can't bill appropriately

If a tool builds a site in 30 seconds, what do you charge? Your client knows it didn't take you 40 hours. But they also can't do it themselves. You're selling expertise and curation, not just generation. Most clients don't get that. "So why am I paying you $5,000 when the AI did it?" "Because I knew which prompts to use and I fixed everything it got wrong." That's a hard conversation.

The only agency workflow that makes sense

After testing everything, here's the workflow that actually works:

Relume AI → [Your Builder] → Client

  1. Use Relume AI to generate sitemap and wireframes (20 minutes)
  2. Review with client, get feedback, iterate (1-2 hours)
  3. Export wireframes to Webflow/Framer/React/whatever
  4. Build the actual site with full control
  5. Hand off to client with no platform lock-in

Is it faster than letting AI build the whole thing? No. But can you hand it off to a client without an awkward conversation? Yes. That's worth the extra time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which AI builder gives clients full ownership of their site?

Only 10Web WordPress gives true client ownership. WordPress sites can be exported, moved to different hosting, and handed off with zero platform dependency. Framer sites are locked to Framer hosting—you can't export clean React, you can't give clients the source files. If client ownership matters (and it should), 10Web is the only AI builder that doesn't create lock-in.

Can I white-label Framer for client work?

Sort of, but clients will figure it out. You can remove the visible "Made with Framer" badge from the footer. But the page source still contains Framer references. Savvy clients or their developers will notice. Even worse, if a customer sees the badge and Googles "Framer," they'll find out it's $15/month—and question why they paid your agency $4,000+. Framer is the cleanest option, but it's not truly white-label.

How do I hand off an AI-built site to a client?

WordPress is the only easy handoff. Clients know WordPress. Developers know WordPress. Hosting companies support WordPress. Handing off a Framer site requires explaining why they're stuck with this specific platform, can't export, and have to keep paying Framer forever. I've handed off hundreds of WordPress sites—never an issue. Handed off one Framer site—hour-long awkward conversation about lock-in.

Should agencies use Relume for client work?

Yes, but as a planning tool, not a building tool. Relume AI generates incredible sitemaps and wireframes in 20 minutes. Use it for client presentations, architecture planning, and getting alignment. Then export those wireframes and build the actual site in Webflow, Framer, React, or whatever gives you the control and ownership your clients need. Relume for planning, not for production.

How do I bill clients when AI builds the site in 30 seconds?

Bill for expertise, not time. Your value isn't in clicking "generate"—it's in knowing what to generate, fixing what AI gets wrong, customizing for their brand, and handling revisions. But this is a hard sell when clients see AI marketing claiming "build your site in 30 seconds." Be transparent: "I use AI tools to speed up the process, but you're paying for strategy, curation, and customization." Some clients still won't get it.

What happens when clients want to migrate away from the AI builder?

With most AI builders: painful rebuild. Framer exports to React, but it's messy Framer-specific code—not clean, maintainable React. You're essentially rebuilding from scratch. 10Web WordPress exports cleanly because it's just WordPress. Durable doesn't export at all—rebuild required. If client ownership matters (and it should), avoid platforms that make migration painful or impossible.

Is Durable usable for agency work?

Not really. It's too limited. Durable generates simple sites quickly, but you can't customize much beyond templates. Complex agency work requires custom layouts, advanced interactions, integrations—Durable can't handle it. Even for simple sites, the Durable branding is hard to fully remove, and clients will eventually notice. Durable is great for solopreneurs building their own site. Wrong tool for agency client work.

BRUTAL TRUTH

Your client doesn't care that AI built their site. They care about ownership.

If they can't export it, move it, or hire someone else to work on it—you didn't deliver a website. You delivered a rental.

That rental might be beautiful. It might have loaded fast. It might have been cheap to build. But when they ask "Can I have the files?" and you say "Actually no, you're locked into this platform forever"—you're not an agency delivering value. You're a reseller for someone else's platform. And smart clients will figure that out.

Choose Your Agency Platform

1. Client expectations for ownership?

Clients will ask for source files: Webflow or 10Web only. Full code export means clients own their site. When they ask "can I have the files?" you say yes, not "actually you're locked in." Smart clients figure out platform lock-in - don't be the reseller who can't deliver assets.

Clients just want it to work: Framer or Durable fine. Freelance agency's 52 clients never asked for code - they just want sites that convert. Most small businesses don't care about ownership until they do.

Mixed client base: Start with Framer (free, fast), migrate serious clients to Webflow when they request ownership. Don't overbuild for clients who don't value it yet.

2. Need client self-service or agency-managed?

Clients update content themselves: Webflow's client editor is best. Design agency's 60% client self-service rate means less ongoing work for agency. Clients love making changes without paying hourly fees.

Agency manages everything: Durable's multi-site dashboard perfect. Content agency manages 120 client blogs from one place. Efficient for agency, higher retainers for clients.

Hybrid approach: Webflow gives clients option but you can still manage. Best of both worlds - agency upsells management, clients have freedom if they want it.

3. Project volume and pricing model?

High volume, lower margins: Framer free tier protects margins. Freelance agency delivers 52 projects at $2,800 average with zero platform costs. Scale without platform eating profits.

Lower volume, higher value: Webflow justified at $4,200-6,500 per project. Design agency's 85 sites at $4,200 average absorb $23/mo platform cost easily. Premium clients expect premium tools.

Recurring retainer model: Durable's multi-site blog management enables $1,800/month retainers. Content agency tripled revenue with flat headcount by managing 120 client sites efficiently.

4. E-commerce requirements?

Full e-commerce needed: Webflow or 10Web. WordPress agency's 42 e-commerce client sites at $6,500 average prove demand. Full shopping cart, subscriptions, memberships require platforms built for it.

Simple payment forms: Framer + Stripe enough. Most clients just need to accept payments, not manage inventory. Don't overbuild e-commerce capability clients won't use.

No e-commerce: Any platform works. Choose based on other factors like ownership, margins, client expectations.

AGENCY-SPECIFIC FEATURES What Agencies Need (That Most AI Builders Lack)

👥 CLIENT PORTAL

Secure area for clients to view project updates, proposals, invoices.

Webflow ✓ Native (membership)
Framer ✗ None
Durable/Mixo ✗ None

Workaround: Use ClientHello, Notion, or Slack (extra cost and integration).

📄 PROPOSAL TEMPLATES

Interactive proposals with pricing, scope, terms.

Webflow Via CMS (custom)
Framer Via CMS (custom)
Durable/Mixo ✗ None

Recommend: Use dedicated tools (Proposify, PandaDoc) - AI builders lack this.

📊 PROJECT TRACKING

Timeline, milestones, deliverables for clients to view.

Webflow Via CMS (custom)
Framer ✗ None
Durable/Mixo ✗ None

Recommend: Use Asana, Trello, or Notion - AI builders lack this natively.

⚠️ AGENCY REALITY CHECK

AI builders can handle your agency website (portfolio + services + contact). But they cannot handle agency operations (client portals, proposals, project tracking). You'll need separate tools for those regardless of platform.

Focus on what AI builders do well: Showcase your work beautifully. Use specialized tools for agency operations.
