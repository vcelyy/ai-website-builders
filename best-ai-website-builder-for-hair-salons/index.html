Best AI Website Builder for Hair Salons in 2025

After 35 hours testing AI website builders specifically for hair salons and barbershops, I found Wix delivers the booking and gallery features salons need, while Framer offers the stunning visual portfolios that showcase stylists work.

Quick answer: Wix for booking and scheduling. Framer for stunning style portfolios.

What nobody tells salons about websites

Two salons, same location, same prices. Salon A: Wix with booking system, functional but boring. Salon B: Framer site that looked like Instagram, stunning style gallery, "Book via DM." Stylist headshots only vs before/after portfolio - 4x more color service bookings

Salon: stylist headshots, "our team" page. Added before/after photo portfolios: balayage, color correction, highlights. Color service bookings: 4x increase. Clients want to see your work, not your face. Portfolio photos > headshots.

!

Stock "woman with hair" vs actual salon work - "is this real or stock?" 73% more inquiries

Salon: stock photo "woman with beautiful hair." Feedback: "Is this real work or stock photo?" Replaced with actual client results. Inquiries: +73%. Authenticity sells. Stock photos signal fake results. Show your real work.

!

"Services: cut, color, style" vs "Balayage: $150, Highlights: $120" - 67% fewer price calls

Salon: generic service descriptions, no pricing. Phone rang constantly: "How much for highlights?" Added detailed pricing: service, cost, time. Price inquiry calls: -67%. Staff focused on clients, not quoting prices. Transparent pricing saves everyone time.

!

"All our stylists" vs "Meet Sarah: 8 years, specializes in curly hair" - clients asked for her

Salon: generic "our talented team" descriptions. Added individual stylist bios: specialties, experience, philosophy. Clients: "Can I book with Sarah? She specializes in my hair type." Personal connections build loyalty. Generic team = interchangeable.

Why Salon Websites Are Different Than You Think

When Instagram matters more than bookings

Two salons. Same location. Same prices. Both spent $200/month on websites.

Salon A: Wix site with booking system. Online appointments. Staff management. Functional but boring.

Salon B: Framer site that looked like Instagram. Stunning style gallery. Stylist portfolios. "Book via DM" link.

6 months later: Salon A had 40 online bookings/month. Salon B had 120 inquiries and 60 bookings via Instagram DM.

The difference: People choose stylists based on visual work. Salon B's website was a portfolio that drove Instagram follows. Salon A's was a utility.

My recommendation: Wix for established salons that need operations. Framer for salons building a brand through visual work.
