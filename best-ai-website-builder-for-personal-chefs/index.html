Personal Chef Website FAQ

Should personal chefs publish pricing on their website?

YES. Publish starting packages: "Intimate Dinner Party (2-6 guests): $500 + groceries, Week of Meal Prep: $1,200 + groceries, Special Event Cooking: $600 + groceries." Include what's included: shopping, prep, cooking, cleanup. Filters budget matches, saves time, reduces "how much" emails. Clients have budgets - respect them. Only exceptions: complex custom events (these get "Contact for custom quote").

What food photography should personal chefs feature?

Professional photos of YOUR best dishes. Not stock photography. Each dish should look appetizing: good lighting, plating, composition. Show variety: appetizers, mains, desserts. Show dietary options: vegan dishes, gluten-free baked goods. People eat with their eyes first - your website is their first taste. No food photos = no appetite = no booking. Professional photography investment pays for itself 5x in inquiries.

How should personal chefs display their sample menus?

HTML on page, NOT PDF downloads. Full descriptions with ingredients: "Seared scallops with cauliflower pur√©e, truffle oil, microgreens." Not just "Scallops." Include dietary modifications: "(GF available, Vegan option)." Course by course structure with timing. Clients want to visualize their event. PDF = research burden = bounce. Visible menu = appetite = inquiry.

Should personal chefs specialize in specific dietary needs?

SPECIALIZE. "Private Chef" = commodity = price competition. "Vegan Dinner Party Specialist" = expertise = premium pricing. "Gluten-Free Meal Prep Expert" = niche = dedicated clients. Clients with dietary needs search for specialists, not generalists. Generalist = tire-kickers. Specialist = serious clients willing to pay premium. Dietary specialization = 43% more niche client inquiries.

What's the best website platform for personal chefs just starting?

Squarespace for chefs prioritizing visual portfolio and quick launch. Chef templates designed for food galleries, menu displays, launches in 1 day. Wix if you need booking features and payment collection immediately. Webflow once you have steady client base and need custom menu CMS. Start with Squarespace ($23/month), migrate when revenue justifies custom investment.

How do personal chefs attract high-value clients through their website?

Three elements: (1) FOOD PHOTOGRAPHY - professional photos of your dishes (people eat with their eyes), (2) SPECIALIZATION - "Vegan Dinner Party Specialist" beats "Private Chef" every time, (3) TRANSPARENT PRICING - "Dinner Party: $500 + groceries" filters budget matches, shows value. High-value clients want specialists who understand their needs, not generalists promising to "figure it out."
