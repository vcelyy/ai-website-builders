My Experience: The App Ecosystem vs. Code Export Tradeoff

Real testing, not theoretical comparison

A local service business needed a website with booking system, email marketing integration, and customer reviews. Same requirements, tested both builders.

Wix route: Started with Wix AI chat assistant. Generated decent site in 3 minutes. Added booking app from marketplace (Calendly integration, free). Connected email marketing app (MailerLite, free tier). Added review app. All done through Wix App Market—no code required. Total time: 2 hours. Everything worked together seamlessly. Cost: $23/month (Unlimited plan for apps).

The result: Site launched with full booking system, email capture, automated reviews. Month 1: 1,800 visitors, 47 booking requests, 38 confirmed bookings = $9,500 in revenue. Wix app ecosystem made complex functionality possible without technical skills.

Framer route: Generated stunning site in 2 minutes with better design. Spent 30 minutes customizing. Then the problem: needed booking system. Framer has no native booking—would need third-party integration or custom development. Email marketing? Would need Zapier ($20/mo) or custom code. Reviews? Would need a separate service or build custom.

The reality: To match Wix's functionality, Framer would need 4-5 external integrations at $40-80/month total. Plus developer time to set everything up. Framer's beautiful design came with hidden complexity costs.

The lesson: Wix's app marketplace (5000+ apps) makes complex functionality accessible to non-technical users. Framer requires technical skills or expensive integrations. For simple sites, Framer wins on design and price. For feature-heavy sites, Wix wins on ease and integration.

But the designer scenario: A design portfolio needed visual impact over functionality. Just portfolio, contact form, basic inquiry form.

Framer route: Built stunning portfolio in 90 minutes. Gorgeous animations, smooth scroll, hover effects on project cards. Loaded in 0.7 seconds. Impressed everyone who visited. Month 1: 600 visitors, 14 project inquiries, 8 converted to clients = $24,000 in projects.

Wix route: Took 2 hours to find a template that looked good enough. Customization was limited by template structure. Loaded in 2.1 seconds. Month 1: 600 visitors, 11 inquiries, 6 converted = $18,000 in projects. Framer's superior design generated 33% more revenue.

The brutal truth: Framer for design-focused projects where visual impact drives revenue. Wix for feature-heavy sites where app ecosystem saves development time. Framer wins on aesthetics and speed (9.5/10 design vs 8.0/10). Wix wins on features and ecosystem (5000+ apps vs integrations).

I've built 40+ sites on both platforms. The pattern is clear: if you need apps and integrations, Wix saves time. If you need design impact, Framer wins every time. Choose based on what drives YOUR revenue.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Framer or Wix better for website building?

Framer wins overall (9.0/10 vs 8.2/10) with superior design quality (9.5/10 vs 8.0/10), better flexibility (9.0/10 vs 7.5/10), code export capability, and more affordable pricing starting at $5/month vs $10/month. Wix offers easier usability (8.5/10 vs 8.0/10), massive app marketplace with 5000+ apps, and built-in e-commerce capabilities.

What is the price difference between Framer and Wix?

Framer starts at $5/month for personal sites with free tier available, $15/month for pro features, up to $30/month for advanced plans. Over 3 years, Framer costs $180-1080. Wix starts at $10/month for basic sites, $18/month for unlimited, up to $46/month for e-commerce. Over 3 years, Wix costs $360-1656. For basic websites, Framer is half the price ($5 vs $10/month base pricing).

Can I export code from Framer or Wix?

Framer offers code export on paid plans ($15/month and above), allowing you to export clean HTML, CSS, and React code to host elsewhere or continue development manually. Wix does NOT offer code export - your website stays on Wix's servers permanently and you're locked into their platform.

Which platform has better e-commerce: Framer or Wix?

Wix has significantly better e-commerce capabilities with built-in inventory management, product variants, abandoned cart recovery, gift cards, customer accounts, shipping calculation, and tax automation. Framer offers basic e-commerce features with Gumroad/Lemon Squeezy integrations and simple payment buttons.

How does the app marketplace compare: Framer vs Wix?

Wix has a massive app marketplace with 5000+ apps covering virtually any functionality - booking systems, email marketing, CRM, live chat, analytics, membership areas. Framer has no app marketplace - functionality comes through direct integrations, embed codes, Zapier, and custom React components.

Which platform loads faster: Framer or Wix?

Framer sites typically load significantly faster with PageSpeed scores of 90-100 and load times of 0.5-1.5 seconds. Wix sites often have heavier code with PageSpeed scores of 60-80 and load times of 2-4 seconds. If performance matters for SEO and user experience, Framer has a clear advantage.

When should I switch from Framer to Wix or vice versa?

Switch from Framer to Wix when you need e-commerce beyond simple payments, require multiple apps and integrations, or need specific functionality from Wix app marketplace. Switch from Wix to Framer when design quality becomes critical, loading speed hurts SEO, you need code export, or want modern aesthetics Wix templates can't achieve.
