FAQ FOR DROPSHIPPING SELLERS

Do I need expensive dropshipping apps?

Yes, if you want to scale past 50 orders/day. Starter dropshippers can manually import products and forward orders for $0 extra. But once you hit 50+ orders/day, you're drowning in copy-paste. DSers ($29/month) automates AliExpress orders. Syncee ($49/month) connects to multiple suppliers. Inventory Source ($99/month) syncs stock in real-time. DropShipPro spent $400/month on apps and saved 5+ hours/day. That's $75/hour worth of time. The apps pay for themselves 10x over if you're actually selling. If you're doing less than 20 orders/month, skip the apps, do it manually, upgrade when you hit the ceiling.

How many products should I launch with?

50-100 quality products beats 500 generic ones. Most dropshippers import 1,000 products from AliExpress and wonder why nobody buys. Problem: generic descriptions, supplier photos, no optimization. Better strategy: launch with 50 products, each with custom descriptions, custom photos, keyword-optimized titles, clear shipping times. DropShipPro relaunched with 127 custom products (down from 847) and saw 4.1% conversion rate vs. 1.2% before. Quality over quantity. Once you have the system dialed in, scale to 500, then 1,000 products. But start with 50 killer products, not 500 mediocre ones.

Should I hide shipping times?

No. Hide them and you lose 53% of customers to complaints. Dropshipping newbies think "if I tell them 14-day shipping, nobody will buy." So they hide it. Customer orders, expects 2-day shipping, waits 10 days, emails "where's my order?", you explain, they're pissed, leave bad review, chargeback. DropShipPro used to hide shipping times: 47 complaints/month, 8.3% refund rate. They added "Ships in 7-14 days" everywhere: 8 complaints/month, 2.1% refund rate. Customers don't mind 14-day shipping IF you tell them upfront. They mind 14-day shipping when they expected 2-day shipping. Set expectations, keep customers.

Which is better: AliExpress dropshipping or US suppliers?

AliExpress for margins, US suppliers for speed. AliExpress = 7-14 day shipping, 50-70% margins, 500,000+ products. US suppliers = 2-5 day shipping, 20-40% margins, limited product selection. DropShipPro uses AliExpress for 90% of products (winning product testing, trend-based items) and US suppliers for 10% (premium items, repeat customers). Hybrid approach: test products on AliExpress ($15 cost, sell for $47 = 68% margin), once product proves itself, source US version ($28 cost, sell for $59 = 53% margin, faster shipping = fewer complaints, higher repeat purchase). Start with AliExpress, scale winners to US suppliers.

When should I migrate from Wix to Shopify?

When you hit 500 products or $20K/month revenue. Wix is great for starters: cheaper, easier, good for 50-100 products. But it hits ceilings: app ecosystem smaller, automation limited, template lock-in. Signs you've outgrown Wix: (1) spending 3+ hours/day on operations that should be automated, (2) can't integrate suppliers you need, (3) hitting product limits, (4) revenue plateaus at $15-20K/month. One dropshipper migrated at $18K/month on Wix, hit $51K/month in 90 days on Shopify (automation freed time for marketing). Migrate when the pain of staying outweighs the pain of switching.

How much should I budget for my dropshipping store?

$150-500/month for real dropshipping operation. Minimal viable setup: Shopify ($29/month) + DSers ($29/month) = $58/month. But that's bare bones. Real dropshipping stack: Shopify Basic ($29) + DSers ($29) + Syncee ($49) + Inventory Source ($99) + reviews app ($29) + email marketing ($29) = $264/month. DropShipPro spent $400/month on apps alone. If $400/month sounds expensive, you're not ready for dropshipping. This isn't "build a $50 website and hope." This is "build a $400/month system that prints money." If you're serious, budget $300-500/month. If that breaks the bank, start with Wix ($27/month) and upgrade when you're making $5K+.

Can dropshipping still work in 2025 with saturation?

Yes, but not with 2018 tactics. "Import 1,000 products, set and forget" = guaranteed failure in 2025. Winners in 2025: custom descriptions (not generic supplier copy), custom photography (not supplier photos everyone uses), clear shipping expectations (not hiding delivery times), mobile-first checkout (81% of orders), automation at scale (manual ops cap at 50 orders/day). DropShipPro did $51K/month in 2025 (post-2024 algorithm changes). Their secret wasn't better products. It was better execution. Same AliExpress products as everyone else. Different presentation, different automation, different results. Dropshipping works. Lazy dropshipping doesn't.

DROPSHIPPING SELLERS:
E-COMMERCE ESSENTIALS

Dropshipping has unique website requirements - product catalogs that update frequently, supplier integration, payment processing, and shipping transparency. Your AI builder must support e-commerce, not just brochure sites.

E-COMMERCE PLATFORM CAPABILITIES

PRODUCT CATALOG FEATURES

Bulk Product Upload

CSV import from suppliers. Dropshippers add 100+ products. Manual entry is impossible. Squarespace and Shopify excel here.

Inventory Sync

Automatic stock level updates. Prevent selling out-of-stock items. Requires API integration or manual updates.

Variant Management

Sizes, colors, styles. Dropshipping products often have many variants. Platform must handle this without breaking.

PLATFORM E-COMMERCE RANKING

#1: SHOPIFY (NOT AN AI BUILDER)

Built for dropshipping. AliExpress, Oberlo, supplier integrations native. Use Shopify + AI for content, not AI builder for store.

#2: SQUARESPACE COMMERCE

Solid e-commerce, adequate for small catalogs. Limited dropshipping integrations. Better for branded stores than high-volume dropshipping.

#3: WEBFLOW E-COMMERCE

Powerful but complex. Full design control with e-commerce. Steep learning curve - not for beginners.

CRITICAL DROPSHIPPING CONSIDERATIONS

SHIPPING TRANSPARENCY REQUIREMENT

Clear shipping times and origins - Dropshipping means longer shipping (7-21 days from China). Your site must set expectations. Hiding this causes chargebacks and bad reviews.

SUPPLIER INTEGRATION LIMITATIONS

AI builders don't integrate with suppliers. You'll manually update inventory or use third-party tools. Shopify wins here with native integrations.

REALITY CHECK: For serious dropshipping, use Shopify, not an AI website builder. AI builders (Mixo, Durable, etc.) are great for marketing sites, but Shopify is purpose-built for e-commerce operations. Use AI to generate content for your Shopify store instead.
