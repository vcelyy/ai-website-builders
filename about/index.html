How This Started

January 2024: I needed a website for a side project. I read 20+ "best AI website builder" reviews.

Every single one listed the same 5 tools. All had 4.5+ star ratings. All had affiliate links. All felt... identical.

The problem: I tried the #1 recommended tool.

  • ✗ Export didn't work—trapped my content
  • ✗ AI content hallucinated features that don't exist
  • ✗ "Free plan" was basically unusable

The realization: I tried the second recommendation. Same story. Third, fourth—they all had issues that none of the reviews mentioned.

These weren't reviews. They were SEO-optimized affiliate bait written by people who'd never actually used the tools.

So I started doing my own testing.

Properly. Spending actual time with each tool. Building real sites. Documenting the problems. Sharing screenshots of the bugs. Not getting paid to say nice things.

The Hard Truth About AI Builder Reviews

Most "review" sites are just affiliate bait. Here's how they work:

1

They've never used the tools

Most review sites rewrite the same marketing copy. They haven't built a single site. They're aggregating features, not testing reality.

2

They never mention problems

Every tool is "amazing" and "perfect." No loading issues. No bugs. No limitations. That's not testing—that's marketing.

3

They rank by commission, not quality

The #1 recommended tool is almost always the highest-paying affiliate commission. Not the best tool. The most profitable one.

4

They never update reviews

Tools change rapidly. Most reviews are written once and never updated. Scores from 2023 get presented as current reality in 2026.

This site is different.

I actually use these tools. I document the problems. I update scores when things change (up AND down). Affiliate links don't buy my opinion.

Common Questions

Actual questions I get asked. Actual answers.

Do you make money from this site?

Yes, through affiliate links. But here's the difference: I'll still tell you if a tool sucks. I've given low scores to tools that pay high commissions because they deserve it. Commissions don't buy my opinion—they just fund the testing.

How do you choose which tools to review?

I focus on AI website builders with actual traction—tools that real people are considering. I also review tools that readers request. If there's a tool you want tested, email me and I'll add it to the list.

Why do some tools have such low scores?

Because they're bad. Loading issues, poor design, confusing UX, hidden fees, vendor lock-in—these are real problems that affect your experience. I'm not going to recommend a tool just because it pays well.

How often do you update reviews?

Every 3-6 months I re-test major tools. Between those cycles, I update when tools make significant changes (good or bad). Scores go up AND down based on current reality.

Can I trust your recommendations?

Use them as one data point, not gospel. I'm trying to be honest and thorough, but I have my own biases and preferences. Your mileage may vary. Read the full reviews, look at the screenshots, and decide for yourself.

Who funds this testing?

Affiliate commissions pay for the hosting and the time I spend testing. No tool has paid me for a review. No tool has sponsored content. I buy my own subscriptions.

What's your background?

I'm a freelancer who builds websites for clients. I've used every major website builder (traditional and AI) over the years. I got tired of fake reviews and decided to do real testing.
