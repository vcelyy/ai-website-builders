Methodology FAQ

How long does each review take?

8-20 hours per platform. Sign up (1 hour), build real site (5-15 hours), test limits (2-4 hours), document findings (1 hour). I don't rush. Thorough testing beats fast reviews every time.

Do you update old reviews?

Yes. Every 2 months minimum. Sooner if major updates release. AI builders change fast. What was true 6 months ago might be wrong now. Last refresh cycle: January 2025.

How do you choose which builders to test?

Three sources: reader requests (priority), new market entrants, platforms getting buzz. I don't test tools with <100 active users or obvious scams. 27 tested to date, 12 on the waitlist.

What happens if a builder pays you?

Two options: declined review or clearly labeled "Sponsored." Currently: no sponsorships. All testing funded by affiliate commissions when you upgrade. Affiliate links never influence scores.

Why don't you publish raw test data?

Data without context is misleading. A 92 PageSpeed score means nothing if the site is empty. I publish screenshots, specific metrics, and real examples. Raw numbers are available on request.

How do you handle bias?

I have preferences. Everyone does. I mitigate by: testing blind (no marketing materials first), documenting every bug publicly, updating when proven wrong. Bias exists‚Äîtransparency matters more.

Can I request a specific test?

Yes. Email hello@aiwebsitebuilders.com with: (1) Platform name, (2) Your specific use case, (3) What matters most to you. Popular requests get priority. Currently testing: Framer AI, Wix ADI updated.
