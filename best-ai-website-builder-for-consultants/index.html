CONSULTANT WEBSITE FAQ

Should consultants build a website if fully booked on referrals?

Yes - absolutely. Website = business safety net. James Martinez: fully booked referrals, main client paused projects overnight. 30 hours/week → 3 hours/week. Revenue dropped 90%. Had to build site in crisis. Don't wait. Build while busy = safety net ready when referrals slow. Website takes 30 seconds with Durable, hours with Framer. Small investment now = business insurance later. Referrals are great - until they aren't.

How important is website design for consultant rates?

Design quality caps your rates. Same consultant, identical content. Durable site supported $150/hour rates. Framer redesign enabled $250/hour. 67% rate increase just from design. Clients judge expertise by website quality. Premium design = premium positioning = higher rates. Management consultants: use Framer or Webflow for $200+/hour positioning. Business coaches: Durable adequate for $100-150/hour.

What should consultants include on their homepage?

Bold results prominently. NOT your process, methodology, background. Clients care about outcomes: "$4.2M client savings," "$1.1M revenue increase," "40% cost reduction." Strategy consultant: 17 case studies buried = zero calls for 6 weeks. Added bold "RESULTS" section to homepage → 8 discovery calls in 2 weeks. Outcomes visible = leads. Results on homepage above the fold. Use big numbers. Specific metrics. Client testimonials with real outcomes.

Do consultants need a CRM integrated with their website?

Yes - manual follow-up loses money. Consultant got 23 inquiries in burst, lost track of 11 in email chaos. Rebuilt on Durable for built-in CRM. "System reminded me to follow up after 3 weeks, got $15K project." Forgetting leads = leaving money on table. Durable includes CRM. Or integrate HubSpot, Calendly for scheduling. Auto-capture leads, follow-up reminders, pipeline stages. System = don't forget = more revenue.

How many case studies should consultants show on their website?

3-5 detailed case studies with bold outcomes. NOT 17 impressive stories buried. Quality > visibility. Each case study: client type (industry, size), problem statement, your approach, specific results ($X savings, $Y revenue, Z% improvement), timeline. Feature 2-3 best prominently on homepage. Link to full case study page. James Martinez: 3 detailed case studies ("eCommerce migration: $50K savings," "Cloud optimization: 40% reduction," "Security audit: prevented $200K breach"). Closed 3 projects in first 90 days.

Should consultants offer online booking for discovery calls?

Yes - reduce friction = more calls = more clients. "Email to schedule" = abandonment. Calendly integrated = 73% conversion rate. Easy booking = more discovery calls = more closed deals. Integrate Calendly or HubSpot scheduling. One-click booking from homepage. Show availability. Reduce friction = they book = you close deals. James Martinez: Calendly integrated = tripled discovery call bookings.

How long does it take to build a consultant website?

Durable: 30 seconds. Framer: 2-4 hours. Webflow: 2-3 days. Durable AI generates complete site from description instantly. Just enter: "IT consultant in Austin specializing in cloud migration, cost optimization, security audits" → 30-second site. Framer: requires design decisions, 2-4 hours for quality. Webflow: full customization, 2-3 days with developer. Speed vs. customization tradeoff. Most consultants: start with Durable (fast), upgrade to Framer/Webflow for premium positioning later.
