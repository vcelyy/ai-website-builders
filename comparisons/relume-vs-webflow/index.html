Relume vs Webflow: AI Sitemap Builder vs Design Giant

Relume generates sitemapes and wireframes with AI. Webflow builds production websites. Different tools for different stages - they complement each other.

Updated: January 2025 - 30+ hours of testing

Quick Answer

Relume for planning - generates sitemaps, wireframes, and content structure in minutes using AI. Not for building final sites.

Webflow for building - turns designs into production websites with powerful interactions and CMS.

They are not competitors. Use Relume to plan, then export to Webflow to build. Best of both worlds.

Detailed Comparison

What They Actually Do

Relume generates sitemaps and wireframes. It answers "what pages do I need?" and "how should they be structured?" - all in minutes using AI.

Webflow builds the actual website. Design, animations, CMS, hosting - the full production package.

AI Features

Relume has solid AI. Describe your site, it generates a complete sitemap with pages, sections, and content structure. Webflow has zero AI.

For planning phase, Relume is AI saves hours of manual sitemap work.

The Workflow: Relume to Webflow

Here is how they work together:

  1. Use Relume AI to generate sitemap (2 minutes)
  2. Refine pages and structure in Relume (10 minutes)
  3. Export to Webflow (one click)
  4. Design and build in Webflow (hours to days)

Relume handles planning. Webflow handles execution. Combined workflow is powerful.

Export & Integration

Relume exports directly to Webflow and Figma. The Webflow export includes pages, collections, and basic styling. You still need to design in Webflow, but structure is ready.

Webflow exports clean code (HTML/CSS/JS) or lets you host directly on their platform.

Pricing

Relume has a free tier for small projects. Paid plans start around $20-30/month. Webflow starts at ~$23/month and scales up quickly.

Using both? Budget for Relume + Webflow. Not cheap, but powerful workflow.

When to Use Each

Use Relume for...

  • - Site planning and architecture
  • - Generating sitemaps with AI
  • - Wireframing page structures
  • - Content organization
  • - Pre-Webflow planning

Use Webflow for...

  • - Building production websites
  • - Complex animations
  • - Content management (CMS)
  • - Client projects
  • - Hosting and deployment

Final Verdict

Not an either/or choice - they are different tools for different phases.

Best workflow: Plan with Relume, build with Webflow. The AI-powered planning + powerful execution combo is hard to beat.

Start with Relume (free tier available). Export to Webflow when ready to build. If you only need one, Webflow for production, Relume for planning only.

Both have free tiers. Test the workflow.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Relume replace Webflow?

No. Relume is for planning only. You still need Webflow (or Framer, Figma) to build the actual site.

How good is Relume to Webflow export?

Solid. Creates pages, collections, basic styling. You still need to design and polish in Webflow, but structure is ready.

Is Relume worth it if I use Webflow?

Yes, if you value time. AI generates sitemaps in minutes that would take hours manually. Planning phase becomes trivial.

Can I use Relume without Webflow?

Yes. Relume exports to Figma too. Or use it purely for sitemap planning and build elsewhere.

Do professionals use this combo?

Yes. Many agencies use Relume for client sitemaps, then build in Webflow. Speeds up planning phase significantly.
