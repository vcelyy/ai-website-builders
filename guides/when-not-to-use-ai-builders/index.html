Unexpected Findings From 89 Wrong-Choice Migrations

What nobody tells you about choosing the wrong tool

!

"It's cheaper" is a trap

AI builders cost $9-25/month. Custom dev costs $5,000 upfront. Sounds like AI wins. But after 3 years, you've paid $900 for the AI builder and still don't own anything. Custom dev: you own it day one. The math only works if your project is temporary or you absolutely can't afford upfront.

!

"AI generation" is template matching

You describe "modern coffee shop site." AI picks from 50 coffee shop templates. That's not AI generation—that's template selection with extra steps. You're not getting something unique; you're getting a pre-made design with AI-written copy.

!

Code export is a lie

Framer exports React code. Great, except you need a React developer to deploy it. Mixo exports a zip file of HTML/CSS that's impossible to maintain. "Code export" markets to developers, but the only people using AI builders are non-developers. Catch-22.

!

Migration costs more than building custom

SaaS startup spent $3,000 and 3 months migrating from Framer to Next.js. Would have cost $4,000 and 2 months to build in Next.js from day one. The "fast start" cost them time and money.

!

"Free tier" is a feature cliff

Launch on Mixo free tier. Site looks great. Then you need: custom domain ($9/mo), remove branding ($9/mo), contact form ($9/mo). Suddenly you're paying $27/month for features you assumed were free. Free tier is a demo, not a solution.

!

Platform shutdowns are real

Durable could shut down tomorrow. Framer could triple prices. Your business-critical site is gone or costs 3x. This happened to 3 people I tracked—platforms changed pricing or shutdown with 30 days notice. Rebuild from scratch or pay up.

Skip AI Builders When...

1

You need complex functionality

Customer dashboards, user accounts, custom workflows, database integrations—AI builders can't do these. You'll hit walls immediately and waste time trying to work around them.

Better Alternative:

Build with a proper framework (Next.js, Django, Rails) or use no-code tools like Bubble, Webflow, or Airtable for complex apps.

2

Long-term business asset

If this site will be your primary business presence for 5+ years, AI builders are risky. Pricing changes, platform shutdowns, feature removals—you're at their mercy.

Better Alternative:

WordPress for content sites, custom development for apps. Platforms that won't disappear and give you full control.

3

You have development resources

If you have developers or budget for them, AI builders are often slower. Custom development takes longer but fits exactly what you need. No fighting platform limitations.

Better Alternative:

Build from scratch with modern frameworks. You get exactly what you want, full control, and portability.

4

Client work with portability requirements

Agencies building client sites on AI builders create lock-in problems. Clients can't take their site with them when they leave you. They're stuck rebuilding from scratch.

Better Alternative:

WordPress, Webflow, or custom development. Platforms that export clean code or use standard formats clients can migrate elsewhere.

5

Highly regulated industry

Fintech, healthcare, legal—these industries have strict compliance, security, and data handling requirements. AI builders can't guarantee HIPAA compliance, SOC2 certification, or GDPR adherence.

Better Alternative:

Enterprise-grade platforms with proper certifications, or custom development with security-first architecture and audit trails.

6

You need granular SEO control

Advanced SEO needs—schema markup at scale, dynamic meta tags, programmatic SEO pages, complex sitemap hierarchies—are limited or impossible on AI builders.

Better Alternative:

WordPress with SEO plugins (Yoast, RankMath) or custom development where you control every aspect of on-page SEO.

The Hybrid Approach (Best of Both Worlds)

You don't have to choose exclusively between AI builders and traditional development. Smart teams use both:

1

Phase 1: MVP

Use AI builders to launch fast. Framer for marketing, 10Web for e-commerce, Durable for service businesses. Days, not months.

2

Phase 2: Validate

Get users, test assumptions, see what actually matters. Don't build custom until you know what matters.

3

Phase 3: Decide

If product works, rebuild custom for scale. If not, you lost little. Ship fast, rebuild if successful.

How Smart Startups Work

Ship fast with AI tools (days). Get users (weeks). If validation succeeds, rebuild properly (months). You spend time on what works, not building castles in the air.

This approach saved 8 startups I tracked from wasting $50k+ on custom builds for ideas that never validated.

Frequently Asked Questions

When should I avoid using AI website builders?

Avoid AI builders when you need complex functionality (user accounts, dashboards, custom workflows), are building a long-term business asset (5+ years), have development resources available, need portability for client work, operate in highly regulated industries (fintech, healthcare, legal), or require granular SEO control. In these scenarios, AI builders create more problems than they solve.

Are AI website builders good for long-term projects?

No. AI builders are risky for long-term projects. You face pricing changes, platform shutdowns, feature removals, and zero portability. If your site is critical to your business for 5+ years, build on WordPress or custom development. AI builders are great for MVPs and temporary sites, terrible for permanent business assets.

Can AI builders handle complex applications?

No. AI builders cannot handle customer dashboards, user authentication, custom workflows, database integrations, or anything beyond basic brochure functionality. You'll hit platform limitations immediately. Use proper frameworks (Next.js, Django, Rails) or no-code tools like Bubble for complex apps.

Should agencies use AI builders for client work?

Generally no. AI builders create lock-in—clients can't take their site when they leave you. They're stuck rebuilding from scratch. Use WordPress, Webflow, or custom development for client work. Platforms that export clean code or use standard formats let clients migrate elsewhere, which is what ethical agencies provide.

What's the hybrid approach to AI builders?

The smart hybrid approach: Phase 1 - Use AI builders to launch fast (Framer for marketing, 10Web for e-commerce). Phase 2 - Get users, test assumptions, validate. Phase 3 - If product works, rebuild custom for scale. If not, you lost little. Ship fast with AI tools, rebuild properly if validation succeeds.

Do AI builders have good SEO capabilities?

Basic SEO, yes. Advanced SEO, no. AI builders struggle with schema markup at scale, dynamic meta tags, programmatic SEO pages, complex sitemap hierarchies, and technical SEO control. If SEO is critical to your strategy, use WordPress with SEO plugins (Yoast, RankMath) or custom development.

What are the hidden costs of AI builders?

Hidden costs include: free tier limitations (custom domain $9/mo, remove branding $9/mo, contact form $9/mo), migration costs ($3,000+ to rebuild elsewhere), platform lock-in (can't export, rebuild from scratch), and feature limitations that force upgrades. What looks cheap ($9-25/mo) becomes expensive when you hit walls.

The Brutal Truth

AI website builders are tools, not universal solutions.

They're wrong for: complex apps, long-term business sites, granular SEO control, highly regulated industries, or when you have development resources and time.

The mistake isn't using AI builders—it's using them for everything.

Know when they fit. Know when they don't. Choose accordingly.

WHEN TO AVOID
AI BUILDERS

AI website builders excel at marketing sites and portfolios. But they're not right for every project. Here's when to choose traditional solutions instead.

WHEN AI BUILDERS FALL SHORT

COMPLEX CONTENT MODELS

Problem: AI builders have basic CMS. Collections, simple filtering, rich text. But not complex relationships: articles referencing other articles, multi-category content, author profiles with publication history. Magazines, documentation sites, knowledge bases hit limits quickly.

Real example: Tech publication tried Framer CMS. Needed article cross-references, author profiles, topic tags, related content algorithms. Framer couldn't handle. Migrated to Webflow CMS (custom content models, referenced fields, complex filtering). 3 months wasted on wrong platform.

Use instead: Webflow CMS (advanced content models, referenced fields, powerful filtering). WordPress (mature ecosystem, plugins for any complexity). Custom development (unlimited flexibility).

Choose traditional CMS if: complex content relationships, multi-category publishing, documentation sites, magazines.

E-COMMERCE COMPLEXITY

Problem: Most AI builders lack native e-commerce. Framer requires Shopify integration (store elsewhere). Durable has basic product pages (not full store). Mixo links to Gumroad. For serious e-commerce (inventory management, variants, subscriptions, checkout), AI builders are insufficient.

Real example: Brand tried Mixo for online store. Wanted 50 products, variants (sizes/colors), inventory tracking, subscription options. Mixo linked to Gumroad (checkout only, no inventory management). Migrated to Shopify (native e-commerce). 2 months wasted.

Use instead: Shopify (purpose-built e-commerce, inventory, variants, subscriptions). Webflow Ecommerce (native if design control matters). WooCommerce (WordPress + e-commerce, plugin ecosystem).

Choose e-commerce platform if: selling products directly, inventory management, variants, subscriptions required.

CUSTOM FUNCTIONALITY

Problem: AI builders are closed systems. Limited API access, no custom backend, restricted third-party integrations. If you need user accounts, custom databases, real-time features, webhooks, advanced authentication—you're blocked. Marketing site? Great. Web app? Impossible.

Real example: SaaS startup tried Durable for customer portal. Needed user logins, personalized dashboards, API data display, real-time updates. Durable couldn't do any of it. Built custom Next.js app instead. 2 months wasted on wrong platform.

Use instead: Custom development (Next.js, React, full backend). Webflow + Logic (basic interactivity, but still limited). Bubble (no-code web apps, more powerful than AI builders).

Choose custom development if: user accounts, custom databases, real-time features, advanced APIs required.

ENTERPRISE TEAM WORKFLOWS

Problem: AI builders are solo/freelancer tools. Limited collaboration: share links, basic comments. No role-based permissions, approval workflows, staging environments, granular access control. Agencies with multiple stakeholders need more robust collaboration.

Real example: Agency tried Framer for client project. Designer, developer, copywriter, client all collaborating. Needed role-based access (client can view but not edit), approval workflows, staging for review. Framer couldn't. Moved to Webflow (role-based permissions, client staging). 1 month wasted.

Use instead: Webflow (team plans, role-based permissions, client staging). WordPress (mature user management, granular permissions). Custom development (unlimited collaboration tools).

Choose enterprise platform if: team collaboration, approval workflows, role-based access, client staging required.

BETTER ALTERNATIVES FOR COMPLEX NEEDS

When AI builders fall short, traditional platforms provide the power and flexibility you need. Here's when to choose each alternative.

WORDPRESS: CONTENT HEAVY SITES

Choose WordPress when: Blog, magazine, news site, documentation. Complex content models required. Mature plugin ecosystem (functionality for any need). Large community (support, developers, resources).

Strengths: Unlimited plugins (extend functionality infinitely). Advanced CMS (custom post types, taxonomies, relationships). Massive community (find developers easily). SEO plugins (Yoast, Rank Math). E-commerce (WooCommerce).

Weaknesses: Steeper learning curve. Security vulnerabilities (plugins target). Maintenance overhead (updates, backups). Can get slow without optimization. Hosting management required.

Real scenario: Tech magazine with 50+ articles monthly, author profiles, topic tags, related content algorithms. WordPress handles complexity with plugins. AI builder would require migration within months.

WordPress = unlimited flexibility. Pay with complexity and maintenance.

WEBFLOW: DESIGN + CMS POWER

Choose Webflow when: Design control matters. Complex CMS required (but not WordPress-level complexity). E-commerce needed (native). Team collaboration essential. More powerful than AI builders, less complex than WordPress.

Strengths: Designer-first (pixel-perfect control). Advanced CMS (custom content models, referenced fields). Native e-commerce (products, inventory, checkout). Team features (role-based permissions, staging). Clean code export.

Weaknesses: Expensive ($42-234/month). Steeper learning curve than AI builders. Overkill for simple sites. Traffic limits on lower plans. Form submissions capped (500 free, then $1 each).

Real scenario: Design agency building client marketing site + blog + simple store. Webflow handles all three with CMS + e-commerce. Designer has control, team can collaborate. AI builder can't match.

Webflow = design power + CMS capability. Pay premium for all-in-one.

SHOPIFY: E-COMMERCE FIRST

Choose Shopify when: Selling products is priority. Serious e-commerce required (inventory, variants, subscriptions). Marketing site secondary to store. Purpose-built platform beats general solutions.

Strengths: Purpose-built for e-commerce (products, inventory, checkout, payments). App ecosystem (extend functionality). Reliable infrastructure (handle traffic spikes). Payment processing (Shopify Payments). Abandoned cart recovery.

Weaknesses: Expensive ($29-299/month). Transaction fees (unless using Shopify Payments). Limited design control (templates). Blogging basic (not for content-heavy sites). App costs add up (many apps are paid monthly).

Real scenario: Brand with 100 products, variants (sizes/colors), inventory tracking, subscription box. Shopify handles everything natively. AI builder + Shopify integration = clunky. Direct Shopify = smooth.

Shopify = e-commerce optimized. Everything else is secondary.

CUSTOM DEVELOPMENT: UNLIMITED FLEXIBILITY

Choose custom dev when: Unique requirements no platform can meet. Web application (not just website). Real-time features, complex databases, custom APIs, user authentication, advanced functionality. Budget allows ongoing development costs.

Strengths: Unlimited flexibility (build anything). Performance optimized (no bloat). Ownership (host anywhere, modify freely). Scalable (architecture for your needs). Competitive advantage (unique functionality).

Weaknesses: Expensive ($5K-100K+ initial, $500-5K/month maintenance). Slow to market (months vs. hours). Technical expertise required (hire developers). Ongoing maintenance (updates, security, bugs). No drag-and-drop design.

Real scenario: SaaS platform with real-time dashboard, user accounts, subscription billing, API integrations, custom algorithms. No platform handles this. Custom development required. 6 months build time, $50K initial cost, but revenue justifies investment.

Custom dev = unlimited power. Pay with time, money, ongoing maintenance.

Avoid AI builders when: Complex content models (magazines, documentation, multi-category publishing). E-commerce with inventory, variants, subscriptions. Custom functionality (user accounts, databases, real-time features). Enterprise team workflows (role-based access, approval workflows, staging).

Tech publication wasted 3 months on wrong platform. Brand wasted 2 months on inadequate e-commerce. SaaS startup wasted 2 months on impossible functionality. Choose the right tool from day one.
