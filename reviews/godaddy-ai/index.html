The GoDaddy Upsell Trap

What they don't tell you upfront

Here's my genuine problem with GoDaddy AI: you can't actually use it at the advertised price.

You sign up. Generate a site. Looks okay. Then you hit the walls:

"Want to remove GoDaddy branding?"

Upgrade to Premium plan (+$10/mo)

"Need more than 5 pages?"

Upgrade to Premium plan (+$10/mo)

"Want custom domain email?"

Office 365 integration starts at $6/mo

"Site performance slow?"

Performance optimization requires Pro hosting (+$15/mo)

What starts as "free with domain" quickly becomes $30-40/month if you want actual functionality.

Meanwhile: Framer is $15/month for everything. No upsells. No branding removal fees. All features included.

I don't mind paying for quality. I hate feeling nickel-and-dimed. GoDaddy's entire business model is the latter.

That's my main beef with GoDaddy AI. Not that it's bad (it's fine), but that you're constantly squeezed for more money.

The "Good Enough" Trap

Why mediocrity is dangerous

GoDaddy AI is the definition of "good enough."

It works. It generates sites. It integrates with your GoDaddy domain. Nothing is broken.

But nothing is great either.

After 15 hours of testing, I couldn't recall a single memorable thing about GoDaddy AI.

Not one feature that stood out. Not one moment where I thought "wow, that's clever."

That's the trap.

If you're already paying GoDaddy for hosting, and you need a basic site, and you don't want to learn a new tool... GoDaddy AI is convenient.

You'll get a site that looks like it was built in 2018. Your PageSpeed scores will be mediocre (50-65/100). Your customization options will be limited.

But it'll work.

The problem: "Good enough" sites rarely succeed.

Your website is often the first impression customers get. "Good enough" means "forgettable."

If you care about standing out? Use Framer, Squarespace, or even Wix. If you just need something up? GoDaddy will do.

I rated GoDaddy AI 7.2/10. Not because it's terrible, but because it's aggressively average in a world with better options.
