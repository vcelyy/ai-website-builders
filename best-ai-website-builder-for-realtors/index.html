Realtor Website FAQ

Should I display all my MLS listings on my website?

Yes. Active MLS feed shows you're working, builds credibility with buyers, gives them reason to browse your site. Even if they find a home you didn't list, they'll call you to show it. Inventory = traffic = showings = commissions. More listings visible = more calls.

How many photos should each listing have?

15-25 professional photos. Buyers scroll through photos to eliminate homes. 3 photos = they skip, assume you're hiding something. 20 photos = they tour virtually, fall in love, schedule showing. Professional photography = offers faster = happy sellers = more listings.

Should I include video walkthroughs?

Absolutely. Video walkthroughs increase engagement 73%. Buyers watch 2-minute tour, see flow, envision living there, schedule showing. Video = emotional connection = serious buyer = stronger offer. Add to featured listings for maximum impact.

What should I include on my "Recently Sold" page?

Every home you sold in past 12 months with: address (or neighborhood), sold price, days on market, before/after photos, client testimonial. Sellers research agents before listing. Proof of sales = confidence = hire you. Hide sales = unproven = they list with someone else.

Should I publish market update content?

Yes. Monthly neighborhood market reports ("Austin homes sold 7% above asking in January"), seller guides ("5 Pre-Listing Repairs That Return 10x"), buyer tips ("First-Time Homebuyer Mistakes to Avoid"). Content = SEO = sellers find you = list with you. Consistent publishing = authority = trust = calls.

How do I capture buyer leads on my website?

"Request More Info" form on every property page. "Schedule Showing" button with Calendly integration. "Home Valuation" tool for sellers (enter address, get estimate, you get lead). "Search Homes" that requires email to view full details. Forms = passive lead generation working 24/7.

How fast will I see buyer leads after launching my realtor website?

Depends on IDX integration + property count. If you have 10+ active listings visible on MLS with IDX search, expect first buyer inquiry in 2-4 weeks via search. Zero listings? Expect 3-5 months while you build inventory. The pattern: launch site ‚Üí add IDX ‚Üí buyers search listings ‚Üí inquiries come from specific properties they want to see. Speed = listing count + IDX quality. 50+ searchable listings = inquiries in week 1. 3 listings = inquiries in month 4. Every listing is a lead magnet.
