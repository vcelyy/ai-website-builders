MY EXPERIENCE

The $28/Month Design System Tax

THE BEFORE

"We build everything 2x faster with Relume."

That's what my Webflow designer friend told me. She swore by Relume's component library. "It's our design system. We never start from scratch. Everything is consistent."

She was already paying Webflow $23/month. Adding Relume at $20/month brought her to $43/month total. For comparison, I tested Framer at $15/month.

Math: $43 (Relume + Webflow) - $15 (Framer) = $28/month extra

THE PROBLEM

Three steps vs two steps

Relume workflow: Design in Figma → export to Webflow → customize in Webflow → publish.

Framer workflow: Design in Framer → publish.

Relume adds an entire step. The Figma detour. Every change requires: update Figma → re-export → tweak Webflow. Framer: update and republish.

The friction: More steps = more time = more cost per project

THE TEST

Same site, different tools

Project: 8-page marketing site. Hero, features, pricing, testimonials, FAQ, contact.

Framer: 6 hours from start to publish. Template customization, content swap, publish.

Relume + Webflow: 8 hours. Component selection in Figma, export, Webflow customization, CMS setup, publish.

Result: Framer was 33% faster (6h vs 8h) for the same result

THE REALIZATION

Then I tested with her team

Her perspective changed when I watched her team work. 3 designers, all sharing the same Relume library. One person updates a button component → everyone gets the update. Consistency across 20+ client projects.

That's when it clicked: Relume isn't for individuals. It's for teams at scale.

THE HARD TRUTH

7 myths about Relume vs Framer that waste your money

Myth #1: "Relume makes you faster"

FALSE. Relume adds a step (Figma). For solo freelancers, Framer is 33% faster. Relume only pays off at scale (10+ projects sharing components). The "2x faster" claim assumes a team scenario most users don't have.

Myth #2: "Relume is a website builder"

FALSE. Relume is a component library for Webflow. It cannot build anything without Webflow. The comparison isn't fair because they're different categories. Framer is standalone. Relume is an add-on.

Myth #3: "Framer has bad components"

FALSE. Framer has solid components. They're just not as extensive as Relume's library. For 90% of projects, Framer's components are sufficient. Relume's 1000+ components only matter if you use them.

Myth #4: "You need Figma for good design"

FALSE. Framer's templates are better (9.5/10 vs 8.5/10). You can achieve excellent results without ever touching Figma. Figma is a skill investment. Framer is learnable in an afternoon.

Myth #5: "Relume code export is better"

FALSE. Both export through Webflow. Framer exports React directly (more useful). Relume exports to Webflow, then Webflow exports. Framer is more direct if you want code.

Myth #6: "Component libraries are always better"

FALSE. Component libraries shine when you reuse elements across 10+ projects. For one-off sites, they add overhead. Most freelancers build custom sites anyway. Components become constraints.

Myth #7: "Relume is only for Webflow power users"

FALSE. Relume is for Webflow TEAMS. Solo Webflow users don't benefit enough to justify $20/month. The team consistency feature is Relume's real value. Without a team, you're paying for features you can't use.

Choose Your Scenario

Choose Framer (8.8/10) if:

  • ✓ You're a solo freelancer or independent designer
  • ✓ You want a standalone builder with no dependencies
  • ✓ You care about design quality (best templates: 9.5/10)
  • ✓ You want React code export for development
  • ✓ You build 1-5 projects per month (not high-volume)
  • ✓ You want to minimize monthly costs ($15-35 vs $43+)

Choose Relume (8.2/10) if:

  • ✓ You're already committed to Webflow ($23+/month)
  • ✓ You have a team of 3+ designers who need consistency
  • ✓ You build 10+ projects per year sharing components
  • ✓ You're comfortable in Figma (or willing to learn)
  • ✓ You want a design system across client projects
  • ✓ You don't mind the $20/month extra for team efficiency

BRUTAL TRUTH

Relume is a fantastic product for 20% of users. If you're a Webflow agency with a team of 3+ designers building 10+ projects per year, Relume pays for itself through consistency and speed.

For the other 80%? You're paying $28/month extra for a slower workflow. Framer gives you better designs (9.5/10 vs 8.5/10), faster build times (6h vs 8h), and costs half as much.

The Decision Framework

1. Solo freelancer? Framer (save $28/month)
2. Webflow team of 3+? Relume (consistency wins)
3. Building 10+ projects/year sharing components? Relume
4. Everything else? Framer

Stop paying for features you don't use. Start with Framer. Add Relume only when you have a team that needs it.

Framer for individuals. Relume for teams.
