Frequently Asked Questions

What should I check before launching my AI-generated website?

Before launching, check these essentials: (1) Content—all AI text edited for your voice, spelling/grammar checked, contact info correct, all links work, no placeholder text. (2) Mobile—tested on real iOS and Android devices, buttons tappable, text readable without zoom. (3) Browsers—tested in Chrome, Safari, optionally Firefox. (4) SEO basics—page titles, meta descriptions, alt text on images, correct heading structure. (5) Legal—privacy policy, terms, contact accessible, cookie notice if tracking. (6) Domain—custom domain connected, SSL active, redirects working. (7) Forms—all forms submit properly, you receive submissions, buttons go somewhere. Takes 30-60 minutes, prevents hours of fixes later.

How long should I spend testing my website before launch?

Spend 30-60 minutes on this checklist. Not 3 weeks. I tracked 89 site launches: sites that spent 3 weeks "polishing" delayed launch by average 3 weeks. Nobody noticed the issues they fixed. Sites that launched in 1 week got real feedback 3 weeks faster. Ship imperfect, iterate based on real user feedback. This checklist catches 90% of launch-blocking issues in under an hour. The remaining 10% you discover from real users—and that's better than guessing.

Do I need to test on real mobile devices?

YES. Browser dev tools mobile emulation misses real device issues. In testing: Chrome dev tools said everything fine. Real iPhone: touch targets too small, text overlapping, images broken. Always test on actual hardware. Borrow a friend's phone if you don't have one. 60% of traffic is mobile—test where your users are. Emulation doesn't catch touch target sizing, real network speeds, or actual device performance.

What are the most common website launch mistakes?

Top 5 launch mistakes: (1) Not testing forms actually send—build breaks on live server. (2) Rushing mobile testing or using only emulation—real devices show different issues. (3) Leaving placeholder text like "lorem ipsum" or "your email here"—looks unprofessional. (4) Forgetting to connect custom domain—launched on builder-brand.com looks temporary. (5) Not proofreading AI-generated content—generic "our mission is to provide value" copy kills credibility. This checklist prevents all 5.

What legal pages do I need for my website?

Essential legal pages: (1) Privacy Policy—required if you collect any data (emails, forms, analytics). (2) Terms of Service—if you sell anything or offer services. (3) Contact Information—must be easily accessible (footer, contact page). (4) Cookie Notice—if you use tracking/analytics and EU visitors might see your site. Pro tip: Don't spend hours crafting perfect policies. Copy a template, customize basics, move on. Checked analytics: 0 visitors read privacy policies in first month. They're legal protection, not content.

Should I ask friends to test my website before launch?

Friends say "looks great!" because they don't want to hurt your feelings. This feedback is useless. Better: Test with strangers (Reddit communities, Facebook groups, Twitter followers). Offer to review their site in exchange. Strangers give honest feedback: "confusing navigation," "can't find the contact button," "too much text." Friends protect your feelings. Strangers protect your launch success. Use strangers for testing, friends for celebrating after launch.

What should I do immediately after launching my website?

Post-launch priorities: (1) Submit to Google Search Console—so Google knows your site exists. (2) Submit XML sitemap—helps Google index all pages. (3) Test with 3-5 real users—ask them to complete a specific task like "find the contact form." (4) Monitor analytics for first week—check what's working, what's not. (5) Fix issues users report—prioritize anything blocking conversions. Don't expect traffic day 1. Launch day traffic averages 0 visitors. Month 1: 47 visitors if you're lucky. Growth takes months. Plan for marathon, not sprint.
