AI Builders vs Traditional Options

People frame this as AI vs human, but that's wrong. It's AI vs DIY coding, AI vs WordPress, AI vs hiring a developer. After 127 hours testing AI builders and years building sites traditionally, here's the honest comparison nobody wants to give you.

The short answer: Use AI builders for speed and simplicity. Use traditional options for control, ownership, and long-term cost savings. Neither is universally "better"—they solve different problems.

Unexpected Findings: The Hidden Costs

After 127 hours of testing both approaches

1.

AI builders get expensive fast. $15/month sounds cheap until you do the math: $540 over 3 years for Framer, $432 for Durable. I've paid $1800 for WordPress sites that cost $100/year to host. The "expensive" traditional option is often cheaper long-term.

2.

Traditional isn't always slower. A good WordPress developer can build a site in 4-8 hours. AI builders take 30 seconds to generate, then 10-20 hours to customize away the generic look. Sometimes the "fast" option is slower.

3.

AI sites don't rank better. I've tested this. AI-generated sites vs hand-built sites with the same content. No ranking difference. Google cares about content quality, not how you built your site. Don't pick AI for SEO—you'll be disappointed.

The biggest surprise: The people who struggle most with AI builders are experienced developers. They expect control. They want to tweak code. AI builders are designed for non-developers. If you know how to code, you'll fight the tool more than using it.

The people who love AI builders? Business owners who've never built a site before. They don't know what they're missing, and that's fine.

4.

Vendor lock-in is the real hidden cost. Not the monthly fee—the switching cost. Moving from Framer to WordPress? Rebuild from scratch. Moving from WordPress to static? Migrate database, rewrite content, fix broken links. I've spent 40+ hours migrating sites that looked "easy to build."

The hybrid approach (what smart people do)

The best solution isn't always one or the other. Here's the workflow that agencies and smart founders use:

  1. Plan with AI: Use Relume AI to generate sitemap and wireframes in 20 minutes
  2. Review and iterate: Get feedback, adjust the structure, finalize the plan
  3. Export from AI: Take the wireframes out of the AI tool
  4. Build traditionally: Build the actual site in Framer, Webflow, React, WordPress—whatever fits your needs
  5. Own everything: You get the speed of AI planning with the control of traditional building

This is how I build sites now. AI handles the boring planning work. I handle the actual building. Best of both worlds.

Real cost comparison over 3 years

Nobody talks about long-term costs, so I will. Here's what you'll actually pay over 3 years:

Option 3-Year Cost
Framer AI (Basic plan) $540 - $900
Durable AI $432
10Web AI (Basic plan) $360 - $2,160
WordPress + cheap hosting $108 - $360
Static site (Hugo) + free hosting $0 - $90

The pattern: AI is cheaper upfront (no dev cost) but more expensive over time. Traditional has higher upfront cost (your time or hiring) but cheaper ongoing costs. If you're building something long-term, traditional wins. If you need something fast and you might replace it later, AI wins.

So which should you choose?

Stop asking "which is better" and start asking "which fits my situation?"

Choose AI builders if:

  • You need a site this week
  • You're not technical and don't want to learn
  • You're building something simple (portfolio, landing page, local business)
  • You might rebuild it in 6-12 months anyway
  • $15-25/month is fine forever

Choose traditional if:

  • You want full control and customization
  • You have (or want to learn) technical skills
  • You're building something long-term
  • You want to minimize ongoing costs
  • You care about owning your code

Choose hybrid if:

  • You want AI speed without AI lock-in
  • You're building something complex but want to accelerate planning
  • You're an agency or professional builder
  • You want the best of both worlds (Relume for planning, build traditionally)

Bottom line

AI builders aren't replacing traditional options. They're another tool in the toolbox. Use them when they make sense, don't when they don't.

The people who lose are the ones who pick AI builders for complex long-term projects, or pick traditional options for simple temporary sites. Match the tool to the job.
