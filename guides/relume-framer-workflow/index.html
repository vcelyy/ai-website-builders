What is the Relume + Framer workflow?

The Relume + Framer workflow combines AI-powered planning (Relume) with visual design (Framer). Can I export from Relume to other tools besides Framer?

Figma export required rebuilding everything visually. For pure speed, Framer is Relume's best integration. The workflow was clearly designed for this pairing.

Rule of thumb: under 5 pages = Framer alone. 5+ pages = Relume + Framer. Don't add complexity for simple projects.

COMMON WORKFLOW MISTAKES TO AVOID

The Relume + Framer workflow is powerful, but agencies make mistakes that reduce efficiency. Here's what to avoid.

MISTAKE #1: SKIP CLIENT PLANNING REVIEW

The mistake: Agency generates wireframes in Relume and exports directly to Framer without client review. "We'll show them the design, they'll approve structure then."

The problem: Client sees design, says "This page should come before that page." "Where's the about section?" "Wrong user flow." Agency backtracks, rebuilds. Time wasted.

The fix: Export Relume wireframes as PDF. Send to client for approval BEFORE any Framer work. Client signs off on structure. Only then export to Framer for design.

Wireframe approval = structural lock. Design phase becomes purely visual, no structural changes.

MISTAKE #2: OVER-CUSTOMIZING IN RELUME

The mistake: Spending hours tweaking Relume wireframes. "Let me get this pixel perfect in Relume before exporting."

The problem: Relume is for structure, not design. Every hour spent in Relume is hour not spent in Framer where design actually happens.

The fix: Spend 30-60 minutes MAX in Relume. Get structure right. Content blocks positioned. Approve. Export. Don't obsess over spacing, styling, colors—that's Framer's job.

Relume = structure. Framer = design. Don't blur the lines or you lose efficiency.

MISTAKE #3: IGNORING RELUME COMPONENTS

The mistake: Building custom components in Framer instead of using Relume's library. "I need a specific testimonial layout, I'll build it."

The problem: Relume has 150+ pre-built components. 90% of what you need exists. Custom building wastes time.

The fix: Browse Relume library first. Use closest component, customize in Framer. Only build custom if nothing exists (rare).

Component library = time savings. Use it. Customize in Framer, don't reinvent.

MISTAKE #4: USING FREE FRAMER TIER FOR CLIENT WORK

The mistake: "Client site is 8 pages, I'll use free Framer tier and export page by page."

The problem: Free tier can't export full sitemaps. Manual page-by-page export takes 2-3 hours. Framer Pro ($25-35/mo) exports everything in 5 minutes. You're saving $25 to waste $250-375 in billable time.

The fix: Budget Framer Pro into every client project. $300-420/year pays for itself with first saved hour. Agency math: 1 billable hour covers 10 months of Pro.

Skimping on tools = false economy. Pro tier = faster delivery = happy clients = more profit.
