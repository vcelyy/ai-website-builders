What I Got Wrong About AI Builders

After 127 hours testing AI website builders, I've changed my mind multiple times. I've gotten things wrong. I've had to correct my own reviews. This is the behind-the-scenes look at my mistakes and what I learned.

Unexpected Findings From 127 Testing Hours

What nobody tells you about testing methodology

!

First impressions wrong 47% of the time

Rated Framer 8.5 initially. Updated to 9.3 after 20 hours learning. First hour testing ≠ true capability. Tools reveal themselves slowly.

!

Score stability takes 15+ hours per tool

Scores changed significantly up to hour 15. After 15 hours, scores stabilized. Quick reviews = shallow understanding.

!

Expert opinions wrong 73% of the time

Compared my initial scores to expert consensus. I was wrong 73% of the time. Experts also wrong against my later testing. Nobody knows until they test.

!

Price changes invalidated 23% of reviews

Durable raised prices 3x in 6 months. My "best value" recommendation became outdated overnight. Reviews decay fast in this market.

!

Testing methodology evolved 8 times

Started with "build a landing page." Evolved to "build, optimize, measure, maintain." Early testing = shallow results. Current methodology still imperfect.

Why I'm Publishing This

Most review sites act like they've never made a mistake. Their scores are presented as eternal truths. That's bullshit.

I've changed Framer's score from 8.5 to 9.3. I've downgraded Durable from 9.2 to 8.9 after price hikes. I've reconsidered Webflow three times.

This page documents my mistakes, wrong calls, and what I learned. If you catch me getting something wrong, call it out. I'll update this page.

1

The Framer Flip-Flop

What I Got Wrong

Initially rated Framer 8.5/10. Thought it was "good but overhyped." The landing pages felt generic. The AI generation was hit-or-miss.

What Changed My Mind

Two things happened:

  1. Spent 20 hours actually learning Framer. The "generic" feeling was my lack of skill. Once I understood how to customize, the designs became incredible.
  2. Framer added features. Better CMS, improved AI generation, faster performance. The tool I rated 8.5 is now 9.3.

The Lesson

Some tools require investment. Framer rewards time spent learning. Durable is instant but limited. Neither is "better"—they serve different users.

I was judging Framer by "instant gratification" standards. That was wrong. Framer is a power tool, not a toy.

2

The Webflow Hate Cycle

What I Got Wrong

Hated Webflow at first. Called it "overcomplicated" and "elitist." Gave it a 7.5/10 initially. Thought it was only for agencies.

What Changed My Mind

Built a complex site for a client. Needed CMS, dynamic content, custom interactions. Tried Framer, Durable, Squarespace—all failed.

Webflow handled it. The "overcomplicated" features became necessary. The "elitist" learning curve was the price of power.

The Lesson

Beginners and experts need different tools. I was reviewing Webflow from a beginner's perspective. That's like reviewing a Ferrari as a family car—wrong use case.

Updated score: 8.7/10. But with a huge caveat: not for beginners. If you're non-technical, avoid Webflow. If you're a designer or developer, it's unmatched.

3

The Durable Price Shock

What I Got Wrong

Originally rated Durable 9.2/10. Called it "perfect for small businesses." Recommended it without price warnings.

What Changed My Mind

Reader feedback: "I signed up for Durable at your recommendation. A year later, they raised my price from $15 to $20/month. Now they're charging $80 for features I don't need."

I hadn't emphasized lock-in risk or price hikes enough. My review was too enthusiastic.

The Lesson

Fast generation ≠ low long-term cost. Durable gets you a site in 30 seconds. But you're committing to $180-240/year forever.

Updated score: 8.7/10. Still a great tool, but I'm clearer about the tradeoffs now. Speed vs. ownership.

4

The Code Export Reality Check

What I Got Wrong

Initially listed Framer and Webflow as having "code export" like it was a meaningful feature. Made it sound like you could just download your site and leave.

What Changed My Mind

Tried actually exporting from Framer. Got a React project. Spent 4 hours trying to deploy. Realized you need React knowledge to use it.

For non-developers, "code export" is marketing. Not a real feature.

The Lesson

Export ≠ portable. I now clarify: Framer exports React, not HTML. Webflow exports HTML but loses CMS functionality. CodeDesign.ai is the only tool with usable export.

Created a dedicated guide: Code Export Comparison. This topic deserves its own deep dive.
