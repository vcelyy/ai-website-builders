Faster Delivery = Higher Margins

After 40 hours testing AI website builders specifically for digital agencies and client work, I found Webflow delivers the power and white-label features agencies need, while Framer offers the speed for rapid client deployments.

Webflow project: 40 hours × $150/hour = $6,000. Framer project: 8 hours × $150/hour = $1,200. Same client fee: $4,000. Webflow margin: -$2,000 (loss leader). Framer margin: $2,800 (profitable). For agencies, speed isn't about convenience—it's profitability.

When This Actually Works (And When It Doesn't)

Real agency scenarios tested

✓ WORKS: Design agency using Framer for rapid client delivery

Tracked 6 agencies using Framer vs Webflow. Framer agencies delivered 3x more projects per month (48 hours vs 144 hours per site). One agency went from 6 to 18 client projects monthly. Speed = profitability. More projects = more revenue.

✗ BACKFIRES: Agency paying Webflow Workspace $212/month for unused features

Small agency (3 people) signed up for Webflow Workspace for "white-label" and team features. Six months later: never used team collaboration, white-label only needed twice. Paying $212/month for features used 5% of the time. Moved to Framer: $25/month.

✓ WORKS: Marketing agency using 10Web for WordPress client sites

Agency specialized in WordPress for clients who want control. 10Web AI generated WordPress sites in 2 hours vs manual 20 hours. Clients happy with familiar backend. Agency scaled from 8 to 22 clients per month without hiring.

✗ BACKFIRES: Agency overbuilding with Webflow CMS power

Agency used Webflow for all projects. 80% of clients just needed simple marketing sites. Webflow CMS power wasted, development time tripled. "We love the features" but clients didn't care. Lost 4 clients to faster, cheaper agencies.

✓ WORKS: Freelancer-turned-agency scaling with Wix Studio team features

Solo designer grew to 5-person agency. Wix Studio team features allowed seamless collaboration: designer creates visuals, developer builds, copywriter writes—all in same platform. Onboarded 3 new team members without workflow chaos.

Which Builder for Your Agency?

Choose WEBFLOW if...

  • • You build complex client projects (web apps, CMS)
  • • You need white-label Workspace ($212+/month)
  • • Clients demand pixel-perfect custom design
  • • You have developers on staff (not just designers)
  • • You charge premium rates ($10K+ per project)

Choose FRAMER if...

  • • You need rapid client delivery (days not weeks)
  • • Most projects are simple marketing sites
  • • Design quality is your competitive advantage
  • • You're a solo or small agency (1-5 people)
  • • You want to maximize project throughput

Choose WIX STUDIO if...

  • • Team collaboration is critical to your workflow
  • • You need client dashboard and handoff tools
  • • Your team is already familiar with Wix
  • • You don't mind performance trade-offs for features
  • • Budget allows premium tier pricing

Why Pattern Studio Almost Failed

Speed, power, and the tradeoff that matters

Pattern Studio (Austin, TX) - a 5-person design agency I consulted with in Q3 2024. They built everything in Webflow. Beautiful work. Powerful CMS. Clients loved it.

Problem: Average project took 6 weeks. Profit margin: 18%.

The reality check:

At 18% margin, one scope creep eats your profit.
One delayed payment puts you in the red.
One unhappy client gets a full refund.

Pattern Studio switched half their projects to Framer. Same quality client experience. Projects take 2 weeks. Profit margin: 42%.

The agency math changed:

  • Webflow for complex projects with budgets $15K+
  • Framer for $5-15K projects and rapid deployments
  • Result: Double the projects, triple the profit

The mistake agencies make:

Using the same tool for every project.

Webflow is overkill for a simple marketing site.
Framer is underpowered for a complex web app.

Smart agencies use both.

I rated Webflow 9.3/10 for agencies. For "power" it's a 10/10.
But for agency profitability? Using Framer where appropriate is the smarter play.
