The Quality Gap (Nobody Talks About This)

Speed isn't free. Here's what I found after showing both versions to 15 business owners:

1

Template Detection Rate

Durable: 13/15 people said "this looks like a template"
Framer: 2/15 people said "this looks like a template"

Durable's layouts are locked. You can change colors and images, but the structure screams "AI generated." Framer lets you break the grid, adjust spacing, and make it feel custom.

2

Design Flexibility

Durable: 7 layout options total. Once you pick one, you're stuck.
Framer: Infinite variations. Full grid control, custom breakpoints, animations.

Durable's editor feels like PowerPoint. You drag things around, but they snap into place. Framer feels like Figma. You have pixel-perfect control.

3

Mobile Responsiveness

Durable: Works, but basic. Stacks elements vertically, no optimization.
Framer: Design mobile-specific layouts. Hide elements, reorder content.

Durable mobile sites look like "desktop version shrunk." Framer mobile sites look like they were designed for mobile.

The Build Experience: What It Felt Like

Durable: The 30-Second Experience

Minute 1: I typed "Coffee shop in Austin called Sunrise Brew, artisan coffee, pastries, wifi, open 7am-3pm, located at 123 Main St" into Durable.

Second 30-47: I watched the progress bar. It felt like magic. Text appeared, images populated, sections built themselves.

Second 47: Done. Complete website. Homepage, about, menu, contact, location.

The Good: It's actually usable. Not just a placeholderâ€”real content, real structure, real images. You could publish this tomorrow.

The Bad: Customization is frustrating. I wanted to move the "Contact" button to the top right. Took me 10 minutes to find the setting. Wanted to change the hero section layout. Can't. Only 7 hero layouts exist.

The Ugly: After 30 minutes of tweaking, my site still looked like a Durable template. Just with my words and images.

Framer: The 3-Hour Experience

Minute 1: I typed the same prompt into Framer: "Coffee shop in Austin called Sunrise Brew, artisan coffee, pastries, wifi, open 7am-3pm, located at 123 Main St"

Minute 1-5: Framer generated a site. Better than Durable's. More modern design. But still... generic.

Hour 1-2: I fell into the editing rabbit hole. Changed the hero section spacing (25px instead of 20px). Adjusted the font weights. Swapped the stock photos for higher-quality ones. Added a subtle gradient to the background.

Hour 2-3: Mobile optimization. The hero text was too small on phones. The menu items needed reordering. The contact form didn't look right on tablet. I fixed each breakpoint individually.

The Result: After 3 hours, my site didn't look like a template. It looked like a designer spent a week on it. People asked "who built your site?" instead of "what template is this?"

The Cost: I spent 3 hours on something Durable did in 47 seconds. But those 3 hours transformed "generic AI site" into "professional brand."
