PRICING & CONTACT BEST PRACTICES

PACKAGE PRICING WITH DELIVERABLES

Fixed scope, fixed price

"Logo Package: $3,000. Includes: 3 concepts, 2 revisions, brand guidelines, files for web and print." Packages sell better than hourly (predictable costs). Deliverables prevent scope creep. Clients know exactly what they're getting. Transparency filters price shoppers (saves time).

Real example: Designer switched from hourly ($125/hr) to packages ($3K, $5K, $8K). Revenue up 47%, hours worked down 23%. Clients preferred predictable costs. Packages = profit.

MULTIPLE CONTACT METHODS

Email, phone, calendar—visitor's choice

Contact form only = friction. Offer options: email (direct), phone (urgent), Calendly (schedule). Some prospects prefer forms, others want to talk immediately. Multiple methods = lower barrier. "Response within 24 hours" sets expectations.

Real example: Added Calendly link alongside contact form. Inquiries increased 34% (convenience). 67% of bookings came through Calendly (ease of scheduling). Options = conversion.

PROJECT AVAILABILITY STATUS

Manage expectations upfront

"Currently accepting new projects: Starts February 2025" or "Booked through March, accepting April starts." Prospects respect transparency. "Open for projects" = immediate inquiry. "Booked through [date]" = queue respect. Status reduces ghosting (both directions).

Real example: Added availability status to contact page. Inquiries from qualified prospects increased 28% (serious prospects only). Tire-kickers decreased 41%. Transparency = quality.

PAYMENT PLANS FOR LARGER PROJECTS

Make expensive work accessible

"$8K website package. Payment plan: $2,500 deposit, then $500/month for 11 months." Expensive projects become affordable. Clients who can't pay upfront can still hire you. Most won't default on creative services—they value their brand reputation.

Real example: Offered payment plans for $5K+ projects. Project bookings increased 40% (affordability barrier removed). Zero defaults in 18 months. Payment plans = accessibility.
