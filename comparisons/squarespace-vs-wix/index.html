Squarespace vs Wix AI Website Builder

The two biggest hosted builders compared. Which should you choose?

Quick Verdict

Choose Squarespace if: Design quality and polished templates matter most. Best for portfolios, restaurants, creatives.

Choose Wix if: You want maximum flexibility and features. Best for complex sites with specific needs.

Key Differences Explained

Design: Squarespace is More Polished

Squarespace templates are consistently beautiful and professional-looking. Everything just works together visually. Wix has some great templates but quality varies more widely.

Reality check: For aesthetics, Squarespace wins. No contest.

Flexibility: Wix Lets You Do Anything

Wix's drag-and-drop is completely free-form - place anything anywhere. Squarespace uses structured sections - less flexible but harder to break.

Reality check: Wix for control, Squarespace for consistency.

AI Generation: Different Approaches

Squarespace's Blueprint AI asks questions then builds a complete site. Wix ADI (Artificial Design Intelligence) creates a site but feels more basic.

Reality check: Squarespace's AI feels more sophisticated and produces better results.

App Market: Wix Has One, Squarespace Doesn't

Wix has a massive app store with 500+ apps to add features. Squarespace has built-in features but no app market - if it's not included, you can't add it.

Reality check: Need specific features? Wix is safer.

Price: Wix is Cheaper

Wix starts at $16/month vs Squarespace's $23/month. Both get more expensive for ecommerce, but Wix remains cheaper across all tiers.

Reality check: Wix offers better value for budget-conscious users.

Which Should You Choose?

Choose Squarespace if:

  • Design quality is your top priority
  • You're building a portfolio, restaurant site, or creative business
  • You want templates that just work
  • You don't need lots of extra features

Choose Wix if:

  • You need specific features (booking, forums, etc.)
  • You want maximum design control
  • You're on a budget
  • You want access to an app market

My Experience: The 6-Month Squarespace Site That Became a Wix Site

Real testing, not theoretical comparison

A local gym owner needed a website. Wanted: class schedule, trainer bios, membership pricing, booking system, blog for fitness tips.

Squarespace route: Launched beautiful site in 2 days. Looked stunning - clean, professional, on-brand. But problems started immediately. Needed class booking integration - Squarespace doesn't have a native booking system, had to use Acuity Scheduling (separate subscription). Needed member portal - Squarespace can't do it. Wanted forum for members - Squarespace doesn't support it.

The breaking point: Month 6, wanted to add nutrition tracking with member dashboards. Squarespace: "Not possible." Wix: Has fitness app with exactly this feature in their app marketplace.

The switch to Wix: Rebuilt site in Wix. Took 1 week. Site looked slightly less polished than Squarespace, but now had: booking system built-in, member portal, forum, nutrition tracking, all via Wix App Marketplace. Owner's costs went DOWN (eliminated separate subscriptions) while functionality went UP.

The brutal truth: Squarespace designs are 10/10 beautiful. But if your business needs specific features beyond basics, Squarespace's rigidity becomes a prison. Wix designs are 8/10 good, but you can add ANY feature via apps.

The framework: Choose Squarespace if your needs are simple and design is paramount (portfolio, simple restaurant site, basic services). Choose Wix if you need specific features or anticipate needing to add functionality over time.
