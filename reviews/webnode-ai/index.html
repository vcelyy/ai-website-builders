The 20-Language Problem Solver

When boring features save businesses

I almost skipped testing Webnode's multilingual features. "Who needs 20 languages?" I thought.

Then I remembered my friend's situation:

She runs a tour company in Prague. Needs her site in Czech, English, German, Russian. Four languages minimum.

Her current solution: WordPress + $200/month in translation plugins. Still breaks constantly.

I tested Webnode for her use case.

Created homepage in English. Clicked "Add language." Selected Czech, German, Russian.

5 minutes later: All 4 language versions existed. Language switcher auto-added. Flag icons ready.

Would I recommend Webnode for its design? No. The templates are dated.

Would I recommend Webnode for its AI features? No. They're basic.

But for multilingual European businesses? Webnode solves a $200/month problem for $8/month.

This is the lesson: "Best" doesn't mean "best at everything."

Webnode is mediocre at most things. But it's excellent at multilingual support.

If that's your problem? Webnode is your answer. Period.

I rated Webnode 7.3/10. For a single-language US site? It's a 5/10. For a European business targeting 5+ countries? It's a 9/10.

The Multilingual Advantage

Webnode's killer feature is multilingual support. You can create your site in 20+ languages with built-in translation tools and automatic language switching based on visitor location.

This is rare among AI builders. Wix, Squarespace, and Framer all require manual work or third-party apps for multilingual sites. Webnode builds it in.

If you're a European business targeting multiple markets, this feature alone could make Webnode worth it despite dated designs.

Where Webnode Falls Short

The templates feel dated. Most look like they were designed in 2015 - boxy layouts, generic stock photos, old-school typography.

The AI features are basic. You answer 3 questions and get a template. That's it. No AI writing, no AI image generation, no smart suggestions.

PageSpeed scores are average (60-75). For comparison, Framer scores 85-95, and even Wix beats Webnode here.

Webnode's 20-Language Strength: Multilingual Power, Dated Design

FAILURE #1: The "We Don't Need Multilingual" Regret

US-based consultant needed simple website. "Webnode is affordable, let's try it." Built the site. Looked dated but functional. Two weeks later: "My site looks like it was built 10 years ago. Can we make it look more modern?" Limited customization meant not really. Rebuilt in Framer. Webnode's multilingual strength is amazing, but if you don't need it, you're paying for features you won't use while getting dated design.

Wrong audience = wrong tool

FAILURE #2: The "AI" That's Just Template Matching

Expected AI-powered generation like Durable or Framer. Answered questions about business. Got... a template selector. Webnode's "AI" just matches your answers to existing templates. No content generation, no intelligent layout, no copywriting. Compared to Framer (which generates real content in 30 seconds) or Durable (full CRM integration), Webnode's "AI" feels like false advertising.

FAILURE #3: The Lock-In With Mediocre Results

Small business used Webnode for 2 years. Wanted to modernize their site. "Can we export the code?" No. "Can we migrate to WordPress?" Manual rebuild only. "Can we at least get our content out?" Technically yes, but copy-paste every page manually. They're stuck with dated design because migration cost equals rebuild cost. Webnode's cheap upfront ($4-12/mo) but expensive long-term (complete rebuild).

No exit strategy = eventual rebuild cost

Bottom line: Webnode is excellent if you're a European business needing multilingual support on a budget. For everyone else, especially single-language businesses, there are better options with modern design and real AI.
