**Real example:** My friend (photographer, zero tech skills) needed a portfolio. She picked the "Krysta" template, swapped in her photos, changed the text. Total time: 3 hours. Result? Looked like she paid $5,000 for it.

**What makes templates exceptional:**
- Designed by actual professionals (not crowdsourced)
- Mobile-responsive built-in (no broken layouts)
- Typography is excellent (font pairs already chosen)
- Color palettes work (no clashing colors)
- Animations feel premium (subtle, smooth)

**The template count:** 200+ templates across categories:
- Portfolios (15+ templates)
- Restaurants (12+ templates)
- E-commerce (20+ templates)
- Business (30+ templates)
- Creative (25+ templates)

**Why this matters:** Most people just want a site that looks good. Squarespace delivers that out of the box.

### 2. Ease of Use (9/10) - Actually easy

**Not marketing "easy"—actually easy.**

**Real example:** My mom (62 years old, not tech-savvy) built her bakery website in one Saturday. She called me once: "How do I add this photo?" I explained. She did it. No other calls.

**What makes it easy:**
- Drag-and-drop works as expected (no fighting the interface)
- Changes are instant (no preview mode confusion)
- Undo/redo actually works (saved me multiple times)
- Section-based editing (click, edit, done)
- No "states" or "classes" to understand

**The learning curve:**
- Day 1: "Oh, this makes sense"
- Day 2: "I can actually do this"
- Day 3: "Why do people say building websites is hard?"

**Compare to Webflow:** Webflow takes 2-4 weeks to feel comfortable. Squarespace takes 2-4 hours.

**Real example:** Built a store for a jewelry designer with:

**Time:** 6 hours (including product setup)

## What Squarespace Does Poorly

### 1. Flexibility (4/10) - Templates are prisons

**The problem:** Squarespace templates are beautiful but rigid.

**Real example:** Client wanted to move a button 20px to the left. In Squarespace? Can't do it without custom CSS (which defeats the point). In Webflow? 2 seconds. In Framer? 1 second.

**What you can't easily do:**
- Move elements freely (stuck in template layout)
- Custom breakpoints (mobile/tablet are fixed)
- Complex animations (limited to template presets)
- Custom fonts (limited to Squarespace's library)
- Layout changes (sections have fixed structures)

**The workaround:** Custom CSS
- You CAN add custom CSS
- But if you're using custom CSS, why use Squarespace?
- Feels like fighting the tool

**Who this affects:** Designers, control freaks, anyone with a specific vision.

### 2. The Editor (6/10) - Can be frustrating

**Specific issues:**

**Clicking is imprecise:**
- Want to edit text? Good luck clicking exactly on the text element
- Often select the container instead (then have to navigate to the text)
- Zoom in to 150% just to click accurately

**Sections are confusing:**
- Can't always tell where one section ends and another begins
- Accidentally drag elements into wrong sections
- "Undo" doesn't always undo what you think it did

**Mobile preview is deceptive:**
- Preview looks different than actual mobile
- Have to publish and check on real phone to be sure
- Leads to publish-check-republish loop

**Real example:** Spent 20 minutes trying to select a specific text element. Gave up, added a new text block, deleted the old one. This shouldn't be that hard.

### 3. Price (6/10) - Not cheap, not expensive

**Pricing tiers:**
- Personal: $16/month (1 website, no e-commerce)
- Business: $23/month (2 websites, basic e-commerce)
- Commerce Basic: $27/month (full e-commerce, 2% transaction fee)
- Commerce Advanced: $49/month (no transaction fee, advanced features)

**Hidden costs:**
- Domain registration: $20-50/year (free first year)
- No free plan (only 14-day trial)
- Transaction fees on lower plans (2-3%)
- No email hosting (need separate service)

**Real example:** My client pays $27/month + $20/year domain = $344/year for a simple jewelry store. On WordPress + WooCommerce? $60/year hosting. That's a $284/year difference.

**The value math:**
- If you value your time at $50/hour: Squarespace saves you 10 hours = $500 saved. Worth it.
- If you're willing to learn: WordPress takes 20 hours to learn, then $5/month forever.

### 4. The "Squarespace Look" (5/10) - Templates are recognizable

**The problem:** After you've seen 50 Squarespace sites, they all look... familiar.

**Recognizable elements:**
- Full-width hero images with centered text
- Parallax scrolling effects (every site uses them)
- Same fonts (same 3-4 typefaces across all templates)
- Same color grading (muted, professional, safe)
- Same animations (fade-in on scroll, everywhere)

**Real example:** Went to a restaurant, looked at their menu on my phone, thought "That's a Squarespace site." Checked source code. Yep, squarespace.com. It's that recognizable.

**Why this matters:** If you want your site to stand out, Squarespace might not help. You're using the same templates as thousands of other businesses.

- Don't care about custom design
