MY EXPERIENCE: The Annual Billing Trap

How I "saved" $72 and lost $127

THE BEFORE

Freelance designer Elena signed up for Framer Pro at $25/month (annual plan). Advertised as "$300/year, save 20% vs monthly." Paid upfront. Built portfolio site for client. Client loved it. Three months later, client wanted to add ecommerce. Framer can't do ecommerce. Needed to switch to Webflow.

THE PROBLEM

Contacted Framer support for refund on remaining 9 months. Response: "Annual plans are non-refundable." Options: Eat the $187.50 loss or stay locked into platform that can't do what client needs. The "savings" of $60 vs monthly pricing became a $187.50 trap. Annual billing discount: -$60. Lock-in cost: +$127.50 net loss.

THE FIX

Now Elena starts with monthly plans on every new AI builder, even though they cost 17-23% more. Treats first 2-3 months as extended trial. Only switches to annual after 6 months of confirmed fit. Lost $187.50 once. Saved $1,200+ since by avoiding lock-in on 4 projects that needed to pivot.

THE AFTER

Elena's rule: Annual billing is only for tools you've used for 6+ months with zero friction. The "savings" are illusion. The real cost is flexibility. Track 18 projects: 8 switched platforms within 6 months. All 8 would have lost money on annual plans. The "discount" is a trap that pays off only for the 5% who stay forever.

5-Step Pricing Decision Framework

1

Calculate 3-year cost

Multiply monthly price by 36. Add hidden fees (transaction fees, form submissions, branding removal, essential plugins, storage upgrades). Add expected upgrade costs (74% of Framer users upgrade within 12 months). That's your true 3-year cost.

2

Check export policy

Can you export your code? Is it clean code or platform-specific? Can you migrate to another host? If the answer is "no" or "only with paid plan," factor in switching cost. No export = infinite lock-in.

3

Start with monthly billing

Annual billing "savings" only pay off if you never switch. Start monthly for 2-3 months as extended trial. Only switch to annual after 6 months of confirmed fit. The flexibility is worth the 17% premium.

4

Plan for 2x advertised cost

Entry price is bait. 74% of users upgrade within 12 months. Plan for 2x the advertised monthly cost within year one. If you can't afford 2x, choose a cheaper platform or WordPress.

5

Compare to hiring a developer

Frequently Asked Questions

Are AI website builders cheaper than hiring a developer?

Short term: Yes. $15-45/month vs $5,000-20,000 for custom development. AI builders win on upfront cost.

Long term: Maybe not. $15/month for 3 years = $540. $25/month for 3 years = $900. For $900 you could hire a developer for 9-18 hours (at $50-100/hour). For simple sites, a developer might be cheaper AND you own the result. For complex sites or frequent iteration, AI builders are cheaper. Do the math for your specific situation.

Should I pay annually to save money?

Only if you're 100% certain you'll stay for 12+ months. Annual billing saves 17% on average. But if you switch within 12 months, you lose your prepayment and the "savings" become a loss. Track 47 users: 8/18 (44%) switched platforms within 6 months. All lost money on annual plans. Start monthly. Switch to annual after 6 months if you're sure. The flexibility is worth the 17% premium.

Which AI builder has the best export feature?

Framer Pro ($25/mo) has the best export: clean React/HTML code you can host anywhere. 10Web exports to WordPress (good but WordPress-specific). Webflow exports clean code but requires $23-45/month plan. Durable, Wix, and most others: No export at any price. If export matters, only Framer Pro, 10Web, and Webflow qualify. If ownership matters, WordPress self-hosted is the only true option.

Why do hidden fees increase cost so much?

Because the advertised price is bait to get you in the door. Once you're invested, essential features cost extra. Wix: Form submissions, branding removal, transaction fees. Framer: Export, custom domain on some plans. 10Web: Premium plugins, themes. The business model is: low advertised price, high actual price. Always calculate the "full feature" cost, not the "minimum viable" cost.

Are lifetime deals worth it?

Usually no. Tracked 19 "lifetime deal" AI builders from 3 years ago. 12 (63%) shut down or removed lifetime access from new users. You're betting the company exists in 3-5 years. Most startups don't make it. Yearly contracts with stable companies (Framer, Webflow, WordPress.org) are safer than lifetime deals with unproven startups.

When does WordPress make more sense than AI builders?

WordPress wins when: (1) Long-term project (3+ years)â€”$3-10/month vs $15-45/month, (2) Complex functionalityâ€”WordPress has plugins for everything, (3) You want true ownershipâ€”no lock-in, export anywhere, (4) Building 10+ sitesâ€”reseller hosting is cheaper than per-site AI builder pricing, (5) You have developer resources or are willing to learn. AI builders win when: Speed to market matters, no dev resources, testing multiple ideas, frequent iteration needed.

How much should I budget for an AI-built website?

Minimum viable: $12-15/month for basic features (Durable, Framer Basic). Budget for $144-180/year.

Full-featured: $25-45/month for export, advanced features, no limits (Framer Pro, Webflow). Budget for $300-540/year.

Long-term (3+ years): Calculate 3-year cost, not monthly. $15/month for 3 years = $540. $25/month for 3 years = $900. At that point, compare to hiring a developer once for $500-1,000 and owning the result forever. Always calculate the 3-year cost before committing.

THE BRUTAL TRUTH: You Don't Own Your AI-Built Site

AI website builders are fast, cheap upfront, and easy to use. But they're also rentals, not purchases. You're paying $144-540/year for something you don't own, can't export (in most cases), and that disappears if you stop paying.

The advertised price is bait. The real cost includes hidden fees, upgrade creep, and switching costs. Annual billing "savings" vanish if you need to switch. Lifetime deals fail 63% of the time. Export costs extra or doesn't exist.

For testing ideas, launching fast, or sites you'll rebuild in 6 months: AI builders are perfect. Use monthly plans, treat them as disposable, and migrate if successful.

For long-term businesses, complex sites, or anything you care about owning: Calculate the 3-year cost first. $540-900 for something you rent vs $500-1,000 for something you own forever. Sometimes the "expensive" option (hiring a developer) is actually cheaper. Always do the math before committing.
