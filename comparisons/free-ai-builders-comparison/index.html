My Experience: The "Free" Trap That Wasted 3 Weeks

Real testing, not theoretical comparison

Three years ago, a client insisted on starting with a "free" AI builder. Budget was zero—bootstrapping mode. I should have pushed back harder.

The free route: Signed up for Mixo free plan. Generated landing page in 52 seconds. Looked okay. Published to mixo-builder.com/sitename subdomain. Client thrilled—didn't cost a cent.

Two weeks in: Client wanted custom domain. "That'll be $9/month to upgrade." Passed—still using free subdomain.

Three weeks in: Needed more than 3 pages. "That's $19/month for the premium plan." Client declined.

Month 2: Site getting traffic. "Made with Mixo" badge looking unprofessional. "Remove branding requires paid plan." Client still refused.

The breaking point: Month 3, client landed big opportunity. Potential partner visited site, saw "Made with Mixo" badge on subdomain URL. Said "looks amateurish, call us when you have a real site." Deal worth $12,000/year vanished. Client called me in tears.

Upgraded to Mixo premium ($19/month). Bought custom domain ($12/year). Spent 3 hours rebranding the site. Total damage: $12,000 opportunity cost + $240/year going forward to save $19/month initially.

The lesson: "Free" plans are trials. They're for testing, not production. If you're serious about business, pay from day one. The subdomain, builder branding, and feature limitations scream "side project" not "real business."

The exception that worked: A hobby blogger testing different niches. Used Relume free tier to plan 4 different site structures. Never published—just planning. Found the right niche after 3 weeks, then paid for Framer to build properly.

Free tier done right: Relume free tier was perfect—genuine value without limitations that block progress. But Relume's a planning tool, not hosting. Different use case.

The brutal truth: If you're building a real business, "free" costs more than paid. Subdomain hurts SEO. Builder branding kills credibility. Feature limitations waste time when you outgrow them. Pay $15/month from day one or don't bother.

The Catches

1. Custom Domain Costs Money

All "free" plans put your site on a subdomain (yoursite.builder.com). To use your own domain, you must pay.

2. Builder Branding

Free plans often show "Made with X" badges. You can't remove these without paying.

3. Limited Features

Free tiers limit pages, storage, bandwidth, and features. You'll hit a ceiling quickly.

4. No Code Export

Almost none let you export on free plans. If you leave, you lose everything.

Best Free Option

Relume AI - Truly Free Planning

Relume is the only one that's actually useful for free. You get sitemap generation, wireframing, and component libraries. It's a planning tool, not hosting - but it's genuinely free forever.

Use case: Plan your site in Relume, then build in Framer or Webflow.

My Recommendation

Don't expect a production-ready site for free. These are trials. The "free" tier is to test the builder before committing.

Best approach: Use free trials to test 2-3 builders. Pick the one you like best. Then pay for a plan - it's worth it for a real business.

Budget tip: Framer ($15/mo) and Durable ($15/mo) are the most affordable paid plans that still give you useful features.
