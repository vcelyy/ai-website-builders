Frequently Asked Questions

Is Squarespace worth the extra cost?

If design drives your revenue: Yes. Wedding photographer made $211,500 in year one. Generic Durable site would've cost bookings. Squarespace's $23-65/month is cheap when design generates hundreds of thousands in revenue.

If design doesn't drive revenue: No. Pest control company made $11,560 from Durable's speed. Spending 4-8 hours on Squarespace design would've lost emergency calls. Know whether design is investment or procrastination for your business.

Can I switch from Durable to Squarespace later?

You can migrate content, but it's manual work. Neither platform offers code export. You'll need to copy text, download images, rebuild forms on the new platform. Expect 2-4 hours of migration work.

Better strategy: Start with Durable if you need speed immediately. If the business works, rebuild on Squarespace with better design. Treat Durable as extended trial, not permanent home. The 30-second launch validates demand. The Squarespace rebuild maximizes conversion once you know it's worth it.

Does Durable's built-in CRM justify choosing it over Squarespace?

For lead-heavy businesses: Yes. Pest control company captured 247 leads in 3 months with automatic follow-ups. Squarespace would've sent form submissions to emailâ€”no lead pipeline, no automation, no reminders. They'd need separate CRM (HubSpot, Mailchimp) at additional cost.

For low-lead businesses: No. Wedding photographer gets 3-5 inquiries monthly. Manual email follow-up is easy. Built-in CRM adds little value. Blogging, portfolio aesthetics, and e-commerce matter more. Choose based on your lead volume.

Which is better for e-commerce?

Squarespace wins by a mile. Full e-commerce platform: product variants (sizes, colors), inventory tracking, shipping zones, tax calculation, abandoned cart recovery, discount codes. Real online store.

Durable: Basic product pages + Stripe integration. Fine for selling 3-5 service packages or simple products. No variants, no inventory, no shipping zones. Not viable for 50+ products with options. Jewelry boutique with 120 products? Squarespace. Handyman selling 3 service packages? Durable works.

Which is better for blogging?

Squarespace wins easily. Full blogging platform: RSS feeds (critical for 2024), categories, tags, author profiles, scheduled posts, comments, rich media embedding. Clean, powerful editor.

Durable: Basic posts only. No RSS feeds, limited categorization, weak comment system. Fine for occasional updates (company news, announcements). Not viable for content marketing, SEO blogging, or thought leadership. If blogging drives your strategy, Squarespace is the only choice.

How fast can I really get online with each platform?

Durable: 30 seconds AI generation + 10-15 minutes content edits = 15 minutes to publish. Pest control company published same day, first emergency call next day.

Squarespace: 30 minutes template selection + 2-4 hours content addition + 1-2 hours customization + 1 hour forms/pages = 4-8 hours minimum. Can publish same day if you work fast, but not instantaneous.

Speed difference: Durable is 16-32x faster. For emergency services or time-sensitive launches, that matters. For evergreen businesses, it doesn't.

Can I make Durable look as good as Squarespace?

No. The limitation is structural. Durable lets you change text, images, colors. But layout structure? Fixed. Hero section arrangement? Fixed. Section ordering? Limited. You're customizing within AI-generated framework.

Squarespace: Customize everything. Layouts, spacing, fonts, colors, sections. Wedding photographer spent 3 weeks making every detail intentional. On Durable, she'd be stuck with generic layout that couldn't reflect her brand. If design uniqueness matters, Durable's limitations will frustrate you.
