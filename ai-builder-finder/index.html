      Not sure which AI website builder is right for you? Answer these questions to get a personalized recommendation based on your specific needs.

      QUESTION 1: WHAT'S YOUR PRIMARY GOAL?

      MARKETING SITE OR LANDING PAGE

      You need: Promote business, capture leads, showcase portfolio, sell services. Single page or few pages. Design quality matters. Speed to launch important.

      Best AI builders: Framer (best design), Durable (fastest), Mixo (simplest). All excel at marketing sites. Choose based on: design priority (Framer), speed (Durable), simplicity (Mixo).

      Real example: Coach needed marketing site fast. Used Durable: generated in 1 minute, live in 30 minutes. Captured 23 leads first week. For marketing sites, AI builders are perfect.

      Marketing site = Framer/Durable/Mixo. Can't go wrong with any.

      E-COMMERCE OR ONLINE STORE

      You need: Sell products directly. Inventory management, checkout, payment processing. AI builders can't do this natively. Need e-commerce platform.

      Best options: Shopify (purpose-built for e-commerce), Webflow Ecommerce (design + store), WooCommerce (WordPress + store). AI builders link to external stores but aren't stores themselves.

      Real example: Brand tried Mixo for online store. Needed inventory, variants, checkout. Mixo linked to Gumroad (clunky). Migrated to Shopify (native e-commerce). 2 months wasted.

      E-commerce = Shopify/Webflow/WooCommerce. AI builders link to stores, not replace them.

      PORTFOLIO OR CREATIVE SHOWCASE

      You need: Showcase work visually. Design quality is priority. Image galleries, project case studies, visual storytelling. Portfolio needs to look stunning.

      Best AI builders: Framer (best design quality, stunning animations), Squarespace (portfolio templates), Webflow (pixel-perfect control if you're designer). For creatives, Framer wins on design.

      Real example: Photographer compared Durable vs. Framer. Durable: functional but generic. Framer: stunning, magazine-quality. Clients hired photographer based on portfolio design. Framer paid for itself in one project.

      Portfolio = Framer for design quality. Your work deserves stunning presentation.

      BLOG OR CONTENT-HEAVY SITE

      You need: Publish content regularly. Blog, magazine, news, documentation. Complex content models: categories, tags, author profiles, related content. Advanced CMS required.

      Best options: WordPress (unlimited CMS power), Webflow CMS (advanced but manageable), Ghost (purpose-built for blogs). AI builders have basic CMS but hit limits quickly.

      Real example: Writer started with Mixo blog. Added 50 posts, needed: author profile, topic tags, related posts. Mixo couldn't. Migrated to WordPress. 3 months wasted. Should have started with WordPress.

      Content-heavy = WordPress or Webflow CMS. AI builders too basic.

      GOAL-BASED RECOMMENDATIONS

      Marketing/Landing

      Framer, Durable, Mixo. All excellent choices.

      E-Commerce

      Shopify, Webflow, WooCommerce.

      Content-Heavy

      WordPress, Webflow CMS. AI builders too basic.

      Start with goal. Platform choice follows naturally.

      Limitations: Platform subdomain (yoursite.builder.com), builder branding, limited features, can't use custom domain. Look amateur to serious visitors.

      Best for: Serious small businesses, freelancers, solopreneurs. Professional site, custom domain, sufficient features. 90% of users belong here.

      You get: Business plans, team access, advanced features. Framer Business ($30) adds team collaboration. Webflow CMS ($42) adds advanced content management. More power, higher cost.

      Best for: Teams, agencies, growing businesses needing collaboration or advanced CMS. If you're solo, probably overbuying. Pay for features you'll actually use.

      When to upgrade: You hit free/pro plan limits. Need team access. CMS can't handle your content complexity. Site generates enough revenue to justify premium. Otherwise, stay on pro plans.

      Real scenario: Agency used Framer Pro ($15). As team grew, needed: multiple editors, client staging, role-based access. Upgraded to Framer Business ($30). Paid for itself in team efficiency.

      $30-75/mo = teams and advanced features. Upgrade when you hit limits, not before.

      BUDGET: $75-250/MONTH

      You get: Enterprise platforms (Webflow Enterprise, custom development). Unlimited everything. White-label options. Advanced security. Dedicated support. Only for large organizations.

      Best for: Large companies, enterprise needs, agencies with multiple clients. If you're reading this guide, you probably don't need enterprise tier. Most businesses overbuy.

      Reality check: 90% of businesses succeed on $15-30/month plans. Enterprise tier is for: massive traffic, complex compliance, white-label requirements, dedicated support. Don't overpay for prestige.
