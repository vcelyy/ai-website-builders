What should orthodontist website include?

Smile transformation gallery (200+ before/after photos), transparent pricing ("Treatment starts at $199/month"), clear treatment options (braces, Invisalign, clear aligners), treatment timeline (18-24 months), consultation request form, virtual consultation option, patient testimonials with photos, FAQ for parents. How many smile photos should I display?

Minimum 200. 5-10 photos signals weak portfolio. 200+ before/after photos communicates experience and beautiful results. Organize by treatment type (braces, Invisalign). More photos = more credibility = more consultations.

Should I display treatment pricing?

Absolutely. Parents search "braces cost" before calling. Display "Treatment starts at $199/month," "Braces: $4,500-6,000, Invisalign: $5,500-7,000" prominently. Price transparency = parents can plan = inquiry = treatment.

Do I need before/after smile galleries?

Essential. Before/after photos provide visual proof of smile transformations. Parents need to see possible results. Smile gallery = visual proof = confidence = consultation. 6x more consultations with smile galleries.

Should I offer virtual consultations?

Yes, absolutely. Busy parents can't always visit first. Virtual consultation option = they inquire immediately = convert to in-person treatment. 43% more inquiries with virtual consult option.

How important is treatment timeline explanation?
