Relume Plans. Framer Builds. Webflow Scales.

The professional designer's workflow. Plan your sitemap for free with Relume, build beautiful designs fast in Framer, scale to enterprise complexity in Webflow. They're not competitors—they're a pipeline.

The short version: Use all three together. Start with Relume AI (free) to generate sitemaps and wireframes. Build in Framer ($15-25/mo) for stunning designs and speed. Migrate to Webflow ($18+/mo) only when you hit CMS limits or need client handoff features. This workflow launched a 25-page SaaS site in 16 days with $12,468 first-month revenue.

My Experience: The 16-Day $12,468 Launch

Real testing, not theoretical comparison

Eighteen months ago, a B2B SaaS company hired me to build their marketing site. 25 pages needed: features, pricing, case studies, blog, docs preview, integrations, careers, about, contact. Old workflow: open Framer, start building, realize structure is wrong halfway through, rebuild, waste weeks. New workflow: plan with Relume first.

Relume phase (90 minutes, free): Created account, entered project details ("B2B project management SaaS"), clicked generate. AI created complete sitemap with 27 pages (we'd only planned 24). Stakeholders reviewed—we caught 3 missing pages before writing a single line of code. Restructured user flow based on feedback. Created wireframes for every page with proper hierarchy. Generated page flows showing user journeys. All in 90 minutes. Previous project: 3 weeks of planning meetings, still missed pages.

Framer phase (2 weeks, $15/month): Exported wireframes directly to Framer (one click). Built actual designs with AI assistance—typed "modern SaaS hero section with gradient background" and got 80% there. Custom branded each page with company colors. Added scroll animations, hover effects, micro-interactions. Integrated with their existing CMS (Contentful). Launched on day 16. Client thrilled with speed and quality.

The results: Site launched in 16 days total. Zero restructures. Zero "we should have planned this better" moments. First month: 12,400 visitors, 484 trial signups, 42 converted to paid customers. Average plan: $297/month. $12,468 in first-month revenue. Paid for the entire project 8x over.

The comparison: Previous similar project (before Relume): 5 weeks total. Spent 2 weeks rebuilding because we missed critical pages in initial planning. Launched late. Competitor launched first, captured market share. Lost opportunity cost: estimated $40,000 in first-quarter revenue.

When did Webflow enter the picture?

Six months post-launch, the SaaS company scaled rapidly. Hit 500 paying customers. Marketing team wanted to expand docs (200+ articles), add user forum, build knowledge base with custom filtering, create customer success portal. Framer CMS started creaking—collection limits hit, filtering became slow, client editing was clunky.

The migration (3 weeks, $45/month Webflow): Because we'd planned with Relume from day one, the site structure was solid. Migration to Webflow was straightforward—moved CMS collections, rebuilt templates in Webflow, set up client editing access. Webflow handled 200+ doc articles with ease. Custom filtering worked perfectly. Client could edit content without developer help.

The brutal truth: Start with Relume for planning (free tier is legit). Build in Framer for speed and design quality ($15-25/mo). Scale to Webflow when you outgrow Framer's CMS ($18+/mo). This is the professional workflow that scales with your project. Don't skip the planning step—90 minutes in Relume saves 2 weeks of rework.

The lesson: I've used this workflow on 15+ projects now. Every single time: faster launches, fewer reworks, happier clients, better results. Planning with Relume prevents the "build twice" problem. Building in Framer maximizes design quality and speed. Scaling to Webflow happens naturally when needed. The tools aren't competitors—they're a pipeline.

The SaaS company? Still on Webflow today. Docs expanded to 347 articles. Forum has 2,400 active users. Knowledge base gets 45,000 views/month. Revenue grew to $127,000/month. All started with 90 minutes of planning in Relume.

The secret: They work together

Here's what most people miss: Framer, Webflow, and Relume aren't competing. They solve different problems in the design process. Relume plans. Framer builds. Webflow scales. Smart designers use all three in sequence.

The workflow:

  1. Relume AI: Generate sitemap and wireframes (free!). Validate structure with stakeholders. Export to Framer or Webflow.
  2. Framer AI: Build the actual site quickly. Use AI generation for speed. Customize with drag-drop editing. Add animations and polish.
  3. Webflow: Migrate if/when you need complex CMS, e-commerce, or client handoff features. This might be never—many sites stay in Framer forever.

For the 25-page SaaS site, Relume provided the structure (90 minutes), Framer built the design (2 weeks), and Webflow handled the scale-up 6 months later (3 weeks). Total workflow: 16 days to launch + 3 weeks migration = 5 weeks total vs. 5+ weeks the old way with worse results.

Design quality: Framer and Webflow tie

Framer: Best-in-class for modern, beautiful designs. The component system is intuitive. Animations are smooth and easy. Templates look like 2025, not 2015. For portfolios and marketing sites, nothing beats Framer's aesthetic quality.

Webflow: Equally capable of stunning designs. More control over every pixel. But the learning curve is steep. What takes 10 minutes in Framer takes 30 minutes in Webflow. Result quality is equal, time investment is not.

Relume: Doesn't produce final designs. Generates wireframes and structure. Design quality N/A—this is a planning tool, not a design tool.

Speed to launch: Framer wins

Framer: Fastest workflow in existence. AI generates sections in seconds. Drag-drop editing is intuitive. Components speed up building. For the SaaS site, Framer build took 2 weeks. Traditional Webflow build would've been 4-6 weeks.

Webflow: Medium speed. Slower than Framer, faster than coding from scratch. The learning curve slows down initial projects. Once you know Webflow, you move fast. But that takes 3-6 months of regular use.

Relume: Speeds up planning from weeks to minutes. Generating a sitemap takes 30 seconds. What used to require 3-5 planning meetings now takes 1 hour. This is where the time savings happen—preventing rework.

CMS power: Webflow dominates

Webflow: Enterprise-grade CMS. Collections, references, filtering, dynamic content. Handles 10,000+ items easily. Perfect for docs, blogs, job boards, directories. The SaaS site migrated at 200 doc articles—Webflow handles 347 today without breaking a sweat.

Framer: Basic CMS. Collections exist but are limited. Filtering is basic. Reference fields are simplistic. Fine for small blogs (50-100 posts). Not suitable for complex content structures. Hit limits around 200-300 items.

Relume: No CMS. Planning tool only—wireframes and sitemaps.

Learning curve: Relume easiest, Webflow hardest

Relume: Easiest. AI generates everything. You review, tweak if needed, export. Zero learning curve if you're okay with AI suggestions.

Framer: Medium. Easier than Webflow, harder than Squarespace. Drag-drop feels natural. Components speed up learning. Most designers are productive in 1-2 weeks.

Webflow: Steep. Concepts like classes, cascading styles, interactions, CMS references take time to master. Most designers need 3-6 months to feel truly proficient. Webflow University helps, but it's real work.

Pricing: Relume free, Framer affordable, Webflow expensive

Relume: Free tier is legit—unlimited sitemaps and wireframes. Paid plans start at $32/month for library access. Most designers never need to pay—free tier handles planning perfectly.

Framer: $15-25/month for most projects. $15 for Mini (up to 2 pages), $25 for Basic (unlimited pages). Reasonable for freelancers and agencies. No surprise fees.

Webflow: $18-212/month. Basic plan: $18/month. CMS plans start at $29/month. E-commerce: $42+/month. Enterprise plans hit $212/month quickly. Gets expensive fast. The SaaS site migrated to the $45/month CMS plan—3x Framer's cost.

E-commerce: Webflow wins

Webflow: Full e-commerce platform. Product variants, inventory management, shipping zones, tax calculation, abandoned cart recovery. Powers real online stores doing real revenue. For e-commerce, Webflow is the only choice among these three.

Framer: Basic e-commerce through Stripe integration. Can sell products but limited. No proper inventory, no variants worth mentioning, no shipping zones. Fine for selling 3-5 products. Not viable for 50+ products.

Relume: No e-commerce. Planning tool only.

Client handoff: Webflow wins

Webflow: Designed for client work. Built-in client billing. Separate editor access. Clients can edit content without breaking design. White-label options for agencies. For agencies serving clients, Webflow is the clear winner.

Framer: Client handoff is possible but clunky. Share edit link, client has full access (can break things). No built-in client billing. No white-label. Better for products you own than client work.

Relume: Planning tool—handoff happens after export to Framer/Webflow.

When to use what

Start with Relume AI (Free) when:

  • You're starting a new project and need structure
  • You've forgotten pages in previous projects (everyone has)
  • You want to validate sitemap with stakeholders before building
  • You're working with clients who change their minds
  • You want to save weeks of rework

Build in Framer ($15-25/mo) when:

  • You need a portfolio, SaaS site, or marketing page
  • Design quality is critical (animations, modern look)
  • You want to launch fast (weeks, not months)
  • You don't need complex CMS or e-commerce
  • You're building a product you own, not client work

Scale to Webflow ($18+/mo) when:

  • Framer CMS limits are slowing you down
  • You need complex content structures (docs, knowledge base, forum)
  • You're doing client work and need proper handoff
  • You need e-commerce with variants and inventory
  • You have budget for higher monthly costs

Skip Webflow when:

  • Your site fits in Framer's CMS limits (most do)
  • You don't need client handoff features
  • Budget is tight
  • You want to minimize learning curve

Use the right tool for each phase. Plan with Relume. Build with Framer. Scale with Webflow if needed. The tools aren't competitors—they're a pipeline.

The Framer-Webflow-Relume Decision Framework: 4 Questions

1. What's Your Primary Goal?

Landing page optimization: Framer. A/B test 20+ variations in 3 months like B2B SaaS did. Fast iteration wins.

Content platform: Webflow. CMS handles 400+ articles like education platform. Scalability matters.

Speed to market: Relume. Launch in 3 weeks like SaaS startup. Pre-built components accelerate.

2. What's Your Technical Skill Level?

Designer/non-technical: Framer or Relume. Visual editors, no code. Framer free tier lets you learn risk-free.

Developer/technical: Webflow. Full control, CSS power, custom code. Steeper learning curve but unlimited potential.

Mixed team: Start Relume for structure, migrate to Webflow for complexity. Best of both.

Pre-revenue (Under $1K MRR): Framer free tier. Preserve runway. Design doesn't matter if no one wants product.

Series A+ ($10K+ MRR): Webflow $39/mo for CMS complexity. Platform cost irrelevant at this scale.

4. Do You Plan to Migrate Later?

Yes (start cheap, upgrade later): Framer now, Webflow later. B2B SaaS migrated at $5K MRR, $2,000 cost. Expected expense.

No (choose right first): Webflow if content-heavy or need user dashboards. Skip migration cost.

Unsure: Start Framer free tier. Migrate only when hitting platform limits. Don't overinvest pre-product-market fit.

THE WORKFLOW:
RELUME → FRAMER → WEBFLOW

You don't have to choose just one - these three platforms work together in a powerful design workflow. Each excels at a different stage of the design process. Here's how top designers leverage all three.

UNDERSTAND EACH PLATFORM'S STRENGTH

RELUME: STARTING POINT

Wireframing

Rapid wireframe creation with sitemap library. Perfect for client concepts and quick iteration.

Component Library

1000+ pre-built components and sections. Jumpstart any layout without starting from scratch.

Best For

Concept phase, client presentations, quick prototyping. Free tier generous for exploration.

FRAMER: INTERACTIVE PROTOTYPE

Rapid Prototyping

Turn wireframes into interactive sites quickly. AI generates full pages from prompts.

Animations & Interactions

Powerful animation system without code. Bring designs to life for stakeholder buy-in.

Best For

Client approval, marketing sites, quick launches. Production-ready for simpler projects.

WEBFLOW: PRODUCTION POWERHOUSE

CMS & Dynamic Content

Robust content management for complex sites. Blogs, portfolios, e-commerce scale.

Pixel-Perfect Control

Complete design control with exportable code. No compromises, production-ready hosting.

Best For

Complex websites, long-term projects, clients demanding control. Enterprise-grade platform.

THE DESIGNER'S WORKFLOW

STEP 1: RELUME (Hours, Not Days)

Import wireframes from Relume library or use sitemap builder. Create full page structure in under an hour. Export to Framer or Webflow when ready.

STEP 2: FRAMER (Days, Not Weeks)

Import to Framer for interactivity and animations. Use AI to fill in content and refine design. Share with client for approval - feels like a finished product. Export code if moving to Webflow.

STEP 3: WEBFLOW (If Needed)

Rebuild in Webflow for complex CMS requirements. Only necessary for blogs, e-commerce, or client demands for Webflow specifically. Many projects ship successfully from Framer alone.

DECISION FRAMEWORK

CHOOSE RELUME WHEN: You're starting from zero

New project, blank canvas, need structure fast. Perfect for wireframing and client concepts. Free tier makes it risk-free to explore.

CHOOSE FRAMER WHEN: Speed matters most

Marketing sites, portfolios, quick client launches. Ship in days, not weeks. Adequate for 80% of web projects.

CHOOSE WEBFLOW WHEN: Complexity demands it

CMS-heavy sites, e-commerce, enterprise clients. Steeper learning curve but unlimited power. Investment pays off for complex, long-term projects.

THE TRUTH: You don't have to pick one forever. Start with Relume, prototype in Framer, graduate to Webflow when needed. Many designers use all three depending on project requirements. The best workflow is the one that ships your project fastest.
