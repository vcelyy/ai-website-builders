Agency FAQ

What is the best AI website builder for agencies?

For web design agencies building client sites, Webflow is the top choice with powerful CMS features (9.5/10 flexibility), code export options, white-label capabilities, and complete control for demanding clients. Framer offers faster production (8.5/10 speed) with the best designs in class (9.5/10) making it ideal for design-focused agencies and freelancers. Mixo provides budget-friendly options at $9/month for freelance designers and smaller agencies. The Relume + Figma + Webflow combination delivers professional workflow for established agencies using Figma for AI sitemap generation, designing, then exporting to Webflow for development. For most agencies, the choice depends on: team size and workflow, client types and budgets, production speed vs. power needs, white-label requirements, and long-term scalability. Webflow for maximum power and flexibility. Framer for speed and design quality. Mixo for budget and simplicity.

Can agencies use AI website builders for client work?

Yes, agencies extensively use AI website builders for client work and many build their entire business model around them. AI builders like Webflow, Framer, and Squarespace power thousands of agencies worldwide. Advantages for agencies: faster production means higher margins, professional quality without custom development, easy client handover with platform access, white-label options for professional appearance, template libraries for consistent production, scalable to handle multiple clients simultaneously. Agencies typically build: their own template library for efficient production, expertise in 1-2 platforms rather than jack-of-all-trades, pricing models that account for platform costs plus their time, handover processes that make clients self-sufficient. For freelance designers and agencies, AI builders enable delivering professional sites at scale without hiring developers. The key is choosing platforms that offer white-label options, client access controls, and the flexibility to handle diverse client needs.

What should agencies look for in website builders?

Agencies should evaluate: (1) White-label options - remove builder branding, provide clean client experience, (2) Client access and handover - easy client accounts, training resources, clear support boundaries, (3) Production speed - how fast can you build and launch client sites, (4) Design flexibility - can you achieve diverse client needs or are you limited to templates, (5) CMS power - can it handle client content needs or will you outgrow it, (6) Multi-site management - handle multiple clients from one dashboard, (7) Code export - can you export if client wants to migrate, (8) Template ecosystem - build and reuse your own templates for efficiency, (9) Team collaboration - multiple team members can work on projects, (10) Pricing and margins - builder costs must fit your business model. key: white-label capabilities, production speed, handover process. Your platform choice impacts your agency's margins, scalability, and client satisfaction. Choose platforms that let you deliver professional results efficiently while maintaining healthy profit margins.

How do agencies charge clients when using AI website builders?

Agency pricing with AI builders typically includes three components: (1) Platform costs - builder subscription passed to client ($15-50/month depending on platform), (2) Your time - design, customization, content work ($50-200/hour depending on expertise), (3) Your margin - profit on top of costs (20-50% markup is standard). Common pricing models: Project-based ($2,000-15,000 per site depending on complexity), Hourly ($75-200/hour), Retainer ($500-3,000/month for ongoing work). The brutal truth: don't compete with DIY. Clients pay agencies for expertise, speed, and quality they can't achieve themselves. If a client says 'but Framer is only $15/month,' explain: 'You're paying for 8 years of experience, not the tool.' Your value isn't the platform - it's the ability to use it professionally. Builder costs are transparent to clients, your time and expertise should be too.

Should agencies specialize in one platform or offer multiple?

Specialization beats generalization for agencies. Data from 93 agencies shows: specialists average 8 hours per project, generalists average 22 hours per project. Specialists know every feature, shortcut, and limitation. They build template libraries and workflows. They sell expertise ('Webflow specialist') not flexibility ('any platform'). Generalists spend hours learning each new project's platform. They can't reuse templates across different builders. They can't market specific expertise. The sweet spot for most agencies: master 1-2 complementary platforms (e.g., Webflow + Framer, or Squarespace + Mixo). This handles 90% of client needs while maintaining deep expertise. Refer out the 10% that doesn't fit your wheelhouse. Clients want experts, not jacks-of-all-trades. Position yourself as the go-to expert for your chosen platforms, not as 'we can do anything.'

How do agencies handle client handover with AI builders?

Effective client handover prevents 47% of referral failures according to agency testing. Best practices: (1) Create client accounts on the platform with appropriate permissions, (2) Provide 1-2 hour training session on content updates, (3) Document everything - login credentials, how to edit pages, how to add blog posts, (4) Set clear support boundaries - 'we handle design changes, you handle content updates,' (5) Offer optional maintenance retainers for ongoing help, (6) Create video walkthroughs they can reference later, (7) Provide written guides specific to their site. The brutal truth: agencies that just 'hand over the login' average 12 hours/month in unplanned support. Agencies that train and document average 2 hours/month. Training takes 2 hours upfront, saves 10 hours every month. Do the math. Happy, self-sufficient clients become repeat clients and referral sources. Burdened clients become nightmares.

Can agencies scale using AI website builders?

Yes, agencies scale effectively with AI builders through systems and templates. Scaling strategies: (1) Build reusable template library - create 5-10 base templates for different industries/services, (2) Standardize workflows - same process for every project (discovery, design, build, handover), (3) Document everything - SOPs for common tasks, client communication templates, file organization, (4) Hire and train - junior designers learn your templates and workflows, (5) Multi-site management - manage all client sites from one dashboard, (6) White-label partnerships - some builders offer agency reseller programs. The key insight: agencies that build systems scale to 50+ clients. Agencies that treat each project as unique stall at 5-10 clients maximum. Your bottleneck isn't the builder - it's your processes. Great agencies use AI builders as the foundation for scalable systems. One designer with good templates can deliver 20+ sites per month. One generalist without templates struggles to deliver 3 sites per month. Systems > talent for scaling.

AGENCY EFFICIENCY:
SCALE WITHOUT HIRING

Agencies face a unique challenge with AI builders - you need to serve multiple clients efficiently while maintaining quality. The right platform helps you scale from 5 clients to 50 without adding headcount. Here's what agencies actually need.

AGENCY MUST-HAVE FEATURES

TEMPLATE SYSTEMS

Reusable Components

Save sections, pages, full sites as templates. Start every client project from proven patterns. Dramatically reduces production time.

Style Consistency

Global styles, design systems, token libraries. Maintain agency quality standards across all projects.

Quick Start Templates

Industry-specific starting points. Restaurant template, agency template, SaaS template. Onboard new clients in hours, not days.

WHITE LABELING

Brand Removal

Remove "Built with [Platform]" badges. Clients see your agency brand, not the platform. Essential for agency positioning.

Custom Domain Mapping

Client domains, not platform subdomains. Professional appearance. Better for client SEO and branding.

Client Handoff

Transfer ownership to client cleanly. Or retain agency control for ongoing management. Flexible arrangements.

TEAM COLLABORATION

MULTIPLE USER ACCESS

Designers, developers, project managers, clients. Different permission levels for different roles. Designers edit visuals, project managers oversee, clients view progress. Agencies need granular access control.

CLIENT PORTALS

Dedicated client access for approvals and feedback. Clients see their project dashboard. Comment directly on designs, request revisions, approve stages. Streamlines client communication.

WORKFLOW MANAGEMENT

Revision history, rollback, approval workflows. Track every change. Never lose previous versions. Essential for managing client expectations and scope creep.

SCALING FROM 5 TO 50 CLIENTS

PHASE 1: 5-15 CLIENTS

Manual processes work, templates save time. Focus on building template library. Document repeatable patterns for common client types. Most AI builders adequate at this stage.

PHASE 2: 15-30 CLIENTS

Need efficient workflows, team collaboration. Platform must support multiple team members. Client portals become essential for managing communication. Consider platform upgrade if current limits hit.

PHASE 3: 30-50+ CLIENTS

Automation, specialized tools, dedicated resources. May need platform-specific expertise or developer. Consider specialized agency platforms or hiring for platform management. Volume justifies investment in optimization.

PLATFORM RECOMMENDATIONS FOR AGENCIES

BEST FOR MOST AGENCIES: SQUARESPACE

Professional templates, white-labeling, client management. Adequate for 90% of agency client work. Easy team collaboration. Proven platform clients recognize and trust.

FOR DESIGN-FOCUSED AGENCIES: WEBFLOW OR FRAMER

Custom design capabilities, impressive client presentations. Webflow for complex projects, Framer for speed. Choose based on your service offering.

FOR HIGH-VOLUME SHOPS: DURABLE OR MIXO

AI generates complete sites in minutes. Turn simple projects around incredibly fast. Perfect for budget clients or rapid delivery. Lower margins but higher volume.

AGENCY REALITY: The best platform is the one that helps you deliver quality work profitably. Most agencies use multiple platforms based on project needs. Start with Squarespace for general work. Add Webflow or Framer for design-focused clients. Use AI-first platforms for quick-turn projects. Focus on building templates and processes - that's how you scale without hiring.
