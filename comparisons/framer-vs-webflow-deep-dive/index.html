Which Should You Choose?

Choose Framer If:

  • ‚úì You want a portfolio or marketing site live fast (days, not weeks)
  • ‚úì You care about PageSpeed scores and performance
  • ‚úì You want smooth animations without coding
  • ‚úì You're a designer, not a developer
  • ‚úì Your CMS needs are simple (blog, portfolio, basic content)

Choose Webflow If:

  • ‚úì You need complex CMS power (relational data, advanced filtering)
  • ‚úì You're building for the long-term (years, not months)
  • ‚úì You want total design control (pixel-perfect customization)
  • ‚úì You're willing to invest weeks in learning
  • ‚úì You're an agency or professional builder

The Brutal Truth

For this specific project (photographer portfolio), Framer won by 3 days. Faster build, better performance, easier animations.

But that's not the full story. If this project needed complex CMS filtering or custom database logic, Webflow would have won.

The right tool depends on your specific project.

Simple portfolio or marketing site? Framer is faster and easier.

Complex CMS or content-heavy site? Webflow is more powerful.

Need it done in days? Framer.

Building for long-term scale? Webflow.
