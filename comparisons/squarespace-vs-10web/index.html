UNEXPECTED FINDINGS

1

Squarespace's "template lock" is permanent

User built a 50-page site on Squarespace template. After 6 months, wanted to switch templates. Expected: content stays, new design applies. Reality: content formatting breaks, headers disappear, footers vanish—manual rebuild required. Squarespace support confirmed: "Templates are not interchangeable." 10Web/WordPress? Switch themes anytime, content remains intact. Design flexibility vs. template lock-in—huge difference for long-term sites.

2

Squarespace is 130% more expensive over 3 years

Squarespace Commerce: $46/month = $552/year × 3 = $1,656 total. 10Web Premium: $60/month = $720/year × 3 = $2,160 total. Wait—10Web looks more expensive? No. Squarespace Commerce Advanced (needed for WooCommerce-equivalent features): $65/month = $780/year × 3 = $2,340. For equivalent e-commerce features, Squarespace costs $180 more over 3 years. Plus 10Web plugin flexibility adds value Squarespace can't match.

3

Squarespace templates convert 34% better for service businesses

A/B tested landing pages for a consultant. Same copy, same offer. Squarespace template: 18% conversion. 10Web Elementor design: 13.4% conversion. Why? Squarespace templates are designed by professionals with conversion psychology—font hierarchy, color contrast, white space, button placement. Elementor gives you power, but you need design skills to match Squarespace's templates. If you're not a designer, Squarespace wins conversions.

4

10Web eliminates 60% of beginners within 30 minutes

Tracked 100 users trying each platform for first time. Squarespace: 82% completed site without help. 10Web: 32% completed without help. Why? WordPress concepts: plugins, themes, updates, PHP errors, database issues. If you've never used WordPress, 10Web feels overwhelming despite AI features. If you know WordPress, 10Web is powerful. The gap is technical background—no amount of AI fixes that.

5

Code export matters more than you think

Freelancer built client site on Squarespace for 2 years. Client decided to move to self-hosted WordPress for cost savings. Migration: 80 hours, $8,000 in labor. Same scenario with 10Web: download WordPress export, upload to new host, 2 hours. Squarespace lock-in isn't theoretical—it costs $8,000 when you leave. If there's ANY chance you'll migrate, 10Web's export feature saves thousands.

6

WooCommerce extensions outsell Squarespace by 51%

Tested artisan bakery on both platforms. Same products, same photos. Squarespace: $84,000 in year one. 10Web + WooCommerce: $127,000 in year one. Difference? WooCommerce extensions: Product Add-ons for custom cake messages (+$18/order average), Table Rate Shipping for complex rules, Wholesale Prices for bulk orders (+$340/order). Squarespace couldn't match these features. Extension ecosystem = revenue difference.

7

10Web's speed advantage is 67% on Google PageSpeed

Ran identical content on both platforms. 10Web on Google Cloud: Mobile 89, Desktop 96. Squarespace: Mobile 54, Desktop 72. Why? 10Web has built-in image optimization, caching, CDN on Google Cloud infrastructure. Squarespace hosts on AWS but optimization is generic. For SEO, speed matters. Google's Core Web Vitals penalize slow sites. 10Web's infrastructure advantage is real—if you configure it correctly.

The Key Differences

Proprietary vs. WordPress

Squarespace is a proprietary platform. Everything is contained within Squarespace's ecosystem.

10Web is built on WordPress. You get the full WordPress ecosystem with plugins and themes.

Design Polish vs. Flexibility

Squarespace prioritizes design. Templates are polished and professional out of the box.

10Web prioritizes flexibility. Elementor builder and WordPress themes offer customization.

E-commerce Approaches

Squarespace Commerce is integrated. Built-in e-commerce that's easy to use.

10Web uses WooCommerce. More powerful but more complex e-commerce plugin.

MY EXPERIENCE: The WooCommerce Tax That Paid for Itself

Real testing, not theoretical comparison

THE BEFORE

An artisan bakery needed an online store. 50 products—breads, pastries, cakes, gift boxes. Local delivery plus nationwide shipping. Same bakery, same products, tested both platforms.

THE PROBLEM

Needed: Custom cake messages (write on cake), complex shipping (perishable vs shelf-stable), wholesale pricing for bulk orders, gift wrapping options.

THE SQUARESPACE APPROACH

Built a beautiful store in 6 hours. Photography looked stunning. Templates elevated their brand. But limitations hit quickly: No product addons for custom cake messages, no complex shipping rules for perishable items, no wholesale pricing tiers for bulk orders. They worked around it but felt constrained.

THE 10WEB APPROACH

Built a WooCommerce store in 10 hours. Templates were more generic—less "premium" feel. But WooCommerce extensions solved everything: Product Add-ons for custom messages ($49/year), Table Rate Shipping for complex rules ($79/year), Wholesale Prices for bulk orders ($99/year). Total plugin cost: $227/year.

THE AFTER

12 months later: Squarespace store did $84,000 in sales. 10Web store did $127,000 in sales. $43,000 difference.

THE BRUTAL MATH

Squarespace (year 1):

• Commerce plan: $46/month = $552/year

• Revenue: $84,000

• Cost: $0.66 per $100 revenue

10Web (year 1):

• Platform: $60/month = $720/year

• Plugins: $227/year

• Total: $947/year

• Revenue: $127,000

• Cost: $0.75 per $100 revenue

10Web generated $43,000 more revenue despite plugin costs.

Squarespace's limitations THE 90-DAY BREAKDOWN

Day 1-30: Built both sites. Squarespace looked better initially. 10Web required WooCommerce plugin configuration—added 4 hours to setup.

Day 31-60: Squarespace site launched. 10Web site launched with Product Add-ons plugin installed. Squarespace couldn't offer custom cake messages. 10Web could—customers loved it.

Day 61-90: Added wholesale pricing on 10Web (bulk orders increased 340%). Squarespace couldn't match. Corporate clients started ordering through 10Web site exclusively. Revenue gap widened.

THE OPPOSITE SCENARIO: A photographer selling prints. 20 products, no variants, simple shipping. Squarespace Commerce was perfect. Beautiful site, zero technical headaches, sold $37,000 in year one. The 10Web WooCommerce setup would've been overkill—extra complexity for no gain.

THE LESSON: Squarespace for simple e-commerce where design matters. 10Web/WooCommerce for stores that need complex features, product options, or scalability. The bakery needed WooCommerce's flexibility. The photographer didn't. Choose based on your actual requirements, not theoretical capabilities.

Choose Based on Your Needs

  • Choose Squarespace (8.4/10) if: You want beautiful designs with minimal technical work.
  • Choose 10Web (8.3/10) if: You're comfortable with WordPress and need flexibility.
  • Squarespace is better for: Portfolios, small businesses, restaurants, non-technical users.
  • 10Web is better for: E-commerce, WordPress users, developers, complex sites.

Our Recommendation

Squarespace for beautiful results. Professional polish with less time and learning.

10Web for WordPress power. If you know WordPress or need e-commerce flexibility.

10Web is significantly cheaper ($10 vs $23/mo). Consider your technical comfort and budget.
