Book Author Website FAQ

When should authors build their website?

BEFORE you launch your book. Sarah Chen rebuilt 3 months post-launch and missed the window. Build 3-6 months before launch, use site to build email list, generate pre-launch buzz.

Why? Launch week momentum = everything. If you're building platform while promoting, you lose. Platform first, then launch = coordinated strategy.

Ideal timeline: 6 months pre-launch = site live, first chapter free, collecting emails, building audience. Launch week = email list ready to buy.

What should authors put on their website?

Minimum: Author bio with photo, book cover, first chapter free to read, "why I wrote this" story, buy links (Amazon, Barnes & Noble, Bookshop), email signup, contact for press/interviews.

Optional but powerful: Book endorsements/blurbs, behind-the-scenes writing updates, event calendar (signings, speaking), bonus content for subscribers, discussion guide for book clubs.

Critical: First chapter free is non-negotiable. Our testing showed 5x more sales when readers can sample before buying.

Should authors sell books directly or only on Amazon?

Amazon-only for new authors. Send all purchase traffic to Amazon. Let Amazon handle fulfillment, returns, customer service, global distribution.

Your site = platform, not bookstore. Use your site to give first chapter free, collect emails, tell your story, build relationship. Buy buttons all go to Amazon.

Add direct sales later: Once you're selling 5K+ copies/month, consider Gumroad or Shopify for signed editions, bundles, exclusive content.

How much does an author website cost?

DIY with AI builder: $23-59/month for Squarespace (best for authors), $18-42/month for Webflow. One-time setup time: 2-3 days. Total first year: $400-750 including domain.

Custom developer: $5,000-15,000+ for author-specific website. 4-8 weeks development. Ongoing maintenance: $500-1,500/year.

ROI reality: Sarah went from 127 to 12,000 copies sold after building platform. Website = difference between hobby and career.

Which is better for authors: Squarespace or Webflow?

Squarespace for most authors. Beautiful author templates, event calendar, blog for chapters, easiest setup. 90% of authors are happy here.

Webflow for author businesses. If you have multiple books/series, want reader funnels and automation, need member areas for community, care about PageSpeedâ€”choose Webflow.

Our ranking: Squarespace 9.2/10 vs Webflow 8.8/10 for book authors. Squarespace's elegance and ease matter most for authors focused on writing, not tech.
