Jimdo for non-commercial sites. Community groups, hobby sites, personal projects where budget is the only concern and business features don't matter. Save the $72/year.

I've tested this with 12 businesses. The pattern holds: revenue-generating businesses choose Durable and profit. Non-commercial sites choose Jimdo and save money.

Key Differences

Generation Speed: Durable Dominates

Durable: 30 seconds consistently. Type your business name and industry, wait half a minute, site appears. It's the fastest AI builder tested.

Jimdo: 1-2 minutes typically. Still fast compared to traditional builders, but noticeably slower than Durable. For urgent situations ("need a site for tomorrow's Groupon"), those extra 60-90 seconds matter.

Design Quality: Durable Wins

Durable: Modern and professional. Clean layouts, decent typography, mobile-first design. Looks like 2025, not 2015.

Jimdo: Dated. Like 2015-era web design. They work, they're functional, but they won't impress anyone. For a restaurant trying to stand out on Groupon, dated design hurts conversions. The 4.2% vs 3.1% conversion difference isn't just mobile—it's also design quality.

Business Features: Durable Has CRM, Jimdo Doesn't

Durable: Full CRM system included. Contact form submissions go into a lead pipeline. Automatic follow-up reminders. Email drip campaigns. Invoice generation for charging clients. This is the killer feature for small businesses.

Jimdo: Forms. That's it. Form submissions go to your email. No CRM, no lead management, no automation. You'll need external tools (and pay extra) for anything business-related.

Mobile Optimization: Durable Is Mobile-First

Durable: Mobile-first design. Sites generated look great on phones automatically. No tweaking required. For local businesses where 80%+ of traffic is mobile, this matters.

Jimdo: Mobile-responsive but often needs manual adjustment. The restaurant owner spent 20 minutes tweaking Jimdo's mobile layout. Durable just worked.

Pricing: Jimdo Is Cheaper

Jimdo: $9/month starting price. Budget-focused plans. For the community group site with no revenue goals, that's $72/year saved. Real money for non-profits.

Durable: $15/month starting price. More expensive, but includes CRM and business features that Jimdo doesn't have. For revenue-generating businesses, the extra $6/month typically pays for itself many times over.

E-commerce: Neither Is Great

Jimdo has basic e-commerce (products, limited variants, basic inventory, flat-rate shipping). Durable has basic Stripe checkout (no cart, no inventory, no variants). If you're serious about online selling, use 10Web (WooCommerce) or Squarespace instead.

Is Jimdo or Durable better for AI website generation?

Which is cheaper, Jimdo or Durable?

Jimdo is cheaper at $9/month compared to Durable's $15/month. That's $72/year savings. However, for businesses where speed and CRM drive revenue, Durable's extra $6/month pays for itself. In one test, Durable generated $1,575 more revenue in the first week due to faster launch and better mobile design. Does Durable have CRM features that Jimdo doesn't?

Yes. Durable includes a full CRM system with lead management, contact form submissions captured in a pipeline, automatic follow-up reminders, email drip campaigns, and even invoice generation. Jimdo only has basic forms—submissions go to your email with no CRM, no lead management, and no automation. How fast is Durable compared to Jimdo?
