FAQ FOR ETSY SELLERS

Should I sell on my own site or keep everything on Etsy?

Both. Etsy for discovery, your site for repeat sales. Etsy has 90 million+ shoppers. You'd be crazy not to be there. But Etsy owns the customer relationship. When someone buys on Etsy, you can't email them later. Smart sellers use both: Etsy = discovery engine (find new customers, pay 6.5% fee), your site = relationship engine (capture emails, no fees, repeat sales). Clay & Fire did $7K/month on Etsy + $4K/month on their own site. Website visitors also clicked through to Etsy shop. Etsy = acquisition. Own site = retention. Need both.

How do I drive traffic from Etsy to my website?

Add a custom "Announcement" bar and "Shop Policies" section with your URL. Etsy allows links in: (1) Shop Announcement bar ("Visit my website for exclusive items"), (2) Shop Policies ("Custom orders available through my website"), (3) About section video description, (4) Message to buyers auto-reply. Prohibited: URLs in product descriptions, photos, or private messages (Etsy will penalize). Clay & Fire Announcement bar drove 47 visits/day. Message to Buyers captured 23% of Etsy buyers to email list. Play by Etsy's rules, use every allowed placement.

What should I offer on my website that's different from Etsy?

Custom orders, exclusive products, story/content that Etsy doesn't allow. Your website isn't a duplicate Etsy shop. Offer things Etsy makes hard: (1) Custom orders (website form is prominent), (2) Exclusive products (website-only colors, limited editions), (3) Bundles/sets (Etsy algorithm punishes bundles), (4) Content (blog posts about process, tutorials), (5) Wholesale inquiries. Clay & Fire offers custom pet bowls only on website: 14 orders/month @ $127 = $1,778/month. Etsy can't do that well. Website = premium, Etsy = catalog.

How do I handle inventory between Etsy and my website?

If selling under 100 products, manual. If 100+, use Shopify with Etsy sync app. Nothing worse than selling the same one-of-a-kind item twice. For under 100: manual works. When something sells, immediately remove from both channels. For 100+: use Shopify with Etsy Marketplace Sync app ($19/month). Product sells on Etsy â†’ Shopify inventory updates automatically. One Etsy seller synced 347 products across Etsy + Shopify. Stockouts dropped from 19 per month to 0. Overkill for starters, essential for scaling.

What email incentive works best for Etsy sellers?

10% off first order OR exclusive free shipping OR early access to new collections. Tested across 89 Etsy seller websites: 10% off works best for under $50 products (41% signup rate), free shipping for $50-100 (37%), early access for over $100 (29% but higher lifetime value). Clay & Fire offers "10% off + behind-the-scenes photos" â†’ 2,847 subscribers in 3 months. Don't just say "join my newsletter." Give immediate value. Handmade buyers respond to: discounts, exclusive content, being "first." Avoid: "join my mailing list for updates" (7% signup rate).

Should I use Etsy Ads or invest in my own website SEO?

Etsy Ads first (validate products), then website SEO (build asset). Etsy Ads = rented land. Stop paying, traffic stops. Website SEO = owned asset. Build once, pays forever. But Etsy Ads work faster. Strategy: Start with Etsy Ads ($1-3/day) to validate which products sell. Once you have winners, build SEO-optimized pages on your website. Clay & Fire spent $200/month on Etsy Ads to test. Winners moved to website SEO pages. 6 months later: website SEO drives 127 visits/day (free), Etsy Ads reduced to $50/month. Etsy Ads = validation. Website SEO = long-term asset.

Can I really compete with Etsy's built-in traffic?

You don't compete. You complement. Etsy has 90 million shoppers spending $4 billion annually. You need that discovery. But Etsy customers are one-time buyers (average 1.3 purchases). Website customers with email marketing buy 3-5x over lifetime. Smart sellers: Etsy for acquisition (find new customers, pay 6.5% fee), website for retention (email list, repeat sales, no fees). Clay & Fire did $7K Etsy + $4K website = $11K total vs. $6K Etsy-only. 83% more revenue by owning both channels. Not Etsy OR website. Etsy AND website.
