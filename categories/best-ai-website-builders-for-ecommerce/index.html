The Hard Truth

6 Ecommerce Myths That Cost You Money

"AI checkout works just fine"

Wrong. Durable, Framer, Mixo checkout is front-end only. No backend order processing. No inventory management. Test purchase succeeds? No, it fails silently. Customers see error, leave, never return. 847 visitors, 0 sales. Real ecommerce platforms process payments. Fake platforms lose sales.

"I'll never hit 1,000 products"

You will faster than expected. T-shirt in 5 sizes, 3 colors = 15 products. 50 t-shirts = 750 products. Add hoodies, hats, accessories = 1,200 products. Squarespace basic: 1,000 products. Month 3: upgrade or delete products. Plan for growth or pay to rebuild later.

"3% transaction fee is no big deal"

$10,000/month revenue = $300/month in fees = $3,600/year. $50,000/month = $1,500/month = $18,000/year. That's pure profit lost to platform fees. WooCommerce: 0% transaction fees. Pay $3,600/year for convenience or $120/year for power. You choose.

"I don't need abandoned cart emails"

63% of carts are abandoned. Without recovery emails, that's lost revenue. With automated emails: recover 15-20% of abandoned carts. $10,000 in abandoned carts = $1,500-$2,000 recovered per month. That's free money. AI builders don't have this. Real platforms do.

"Wix apps are cheap"

Reviews app: $10/month. Abandoned cart: $10/month. Subscriptions: $15/month. Advanced shipping: $8/month. That's $43/month in apps. On top of $27/month Wix plan. Total: $70/month. WooCommerce: all features free via plugins. $10/month hosting. Wix looks cheaper. Adds up fast.

"I'll switch platforms when I grow"

Rebuilding ecommerce store: 2-3 months. Downtime. Lost sales. Broken SEO. Migrating products, orders, customers is nightmare. Start on platform that scales. WooCommerce: 1 product or 100,000 products, same platform. Build once, scale forever.

What actually matters for ecommerce

Real payment processing, not fake checkout

Durable, Framer have checkout forms that look real. But they don't connect to payment processors properly. No order management. No inventory tracking. No shipping calculations. It's front-end theater, not backend commerce. For real sales, you need: Stripe/PayPal integration, order management system, inventory tracking, shipping calculations, tax automation. WooCommerce, Squarespace Commerce, Webflow Ecommerce have this. AI site builders don't.

Unlimited products, not surprise limits

Squarespace basic: 1,000 products. Wix basic: 100 products. You'll hit these faster than expected. Each variant counts as a product. T-shirt in 5 sizes, 3 colors = 15 products. 50 t-shirts = 750 products. 100 t-shirts = 1,500 products. You're over limit before you know it. WooCommerce: unlimited products. 10,000 SKUs? No problem. Plan for growth or rebuild later.

Zero transaction fees save thousands at scale

Some builders charge 2-3% on every sale. 3% of $10,000 = $300/month. $3,600/year. That's pure profit lost to platform fees. WooCommerce: 0% transaction fees. You pay payment processor fees (Stripe: 2.9% + $0.30). That's it. No platform tax on top. At $50k/month revenue, that's $1,500/month saved. Scale without penalty.

Ecommerce platform reality

10Web + WooCommerce: most powerful, steepest learning curve

WooCommerce powers 30% of all online stores. Unlimited products, 100+ payment gateways, 50,000+ extensions. Need subscriptions? Plugin. Need bookings? Plugin. Need international shipping? Plugin. Anything ecommerce can do, WooCommerce does. But: WordPress learning curve. Plugin management. Security updates. Not for non-technical users. Best for serious stores prioritizing power over simplicity.

Squarespace Commerce: easiest, most expensive at scale

Beautiful templates. Built-in ecommerce. No plugins needed. Inventory, shipping, taxes included. Easy to set up, easy to manage. But: product limits, transaction fees on lower plans, expensive scaling. Basic plan: 1,000 products, $23/month. Advanced: $46/month. Transaction fees: 3% on basic, 0% on advanced. Perfect for 10-500 products. Expensive beyond that.

Webflow Ecommerce: designer-focused, premium pricing

Beautiful checkout flows. Custom product pages. Full design control. Results are stunning. But: $212/month minimum. Complex setup. Not for beginners. If design is your brand and budget is unlimited, Webflow delivers. For everyone else, overkill.

FAQ

Can I use Durable for ecommerce?

No. Durable creates product catalogs but doesn't have real ecommerce functionality. No payment processing, no inventory management, no order tracking. It's a marketing site builder, not an ecommerce platform. For real online stores, use WooCommerce, Squarespace Commerce, or Webflow Ecommerce.

Which is cheapest for ecommerce?

WooCommerce. $10/month hosting, 0% transaction fees. Total cost: ~$120/year. Squarespace: $276-$552/year plus 3% transaction fees on basic plan. At $10k/month revenue, Squarespace costs $3,600/year in fees alone. WooCommerce is cheapest at any scale.

What's the product limit on Squarespace?

Basic plan: 1,000 products. Advanced plan: unlimited. But at $46/month vs $23/month. Most stores hit 1,000 products faster than expected (each variant counts separately). Plan for growth or face forced upgrades.

Do I need abandoned cart emails?

Yes. 63% of carts are abandoned. Without recovery emails, that's lost revenue. With automated emails: recover 15-20% of abandoned carts. $10k in abandoned carts = $1,500-$2,000 recovered per month. This is free money.

Is Webflow worth $212/month?

Only if design is your brand. If you're selling commoditized products, no. If you're a luxury brand where design = trust, maybe. For 99% of stores, WooCommerce or Squarespace delivers better ROI. Webflow is overkill unless design is your competitive advantage.

How hard is WooCommerce to learn?

3-4 weeks for basics. WordPress + WooCommerce has learning curve. Plugin management, security updates, hosting setup. But one-time learning, lifetime benefits. At $50k/month revenue, WooCommerce saves $1,500/month in transaction fees vs Squarespace. That's $18,000/year saved for 3 weeks of learning.

Can I migrate platforms later?

Technically yes, practically nightmare. Rebuilding ecommerce store: 2-3 months. Downtime. Lost sales. Broken SEO. Migrating products, orders, customers is complex. Start on platform that scales. WooCommerce: 1 product or 100,000 products, same platform. Build once, scale forever.
