Frequently Asked Questions

Which AI builder creates the most unique designs?

Webflow, if you have design skills. Webflow's blank canvas + custom CSS = truly unique sites. No two Webflow sites need look alike.

For non-designers: Framer creates the most unique AI-generated designs. Templates feel custom, not cookie-cutter.

Avoid: Wix, 10Web, Hostinger. Heavy template reuse creates generic look.

Can I make AI-generated sites look custom?

Partially. Depends on the platform:

  • Webflow: Yes, completely. AI is just a starting point.
  • Framer: Mostly yes. Can customize 80% of design elements.
  • Durable: Somewhat. Can change colors, fonts, content within template.
  • Wix, Hostinger: Barely. Locked into template structure.

Reality: If you need truly custom design, start with Webflow or hire a designer. AI can only customize so much.

Why do Framer templates look better than Wix?

Design philosophy + target audience.

Framer: Built for designers, agencies, startups. Templates use modern trends (brutalism, minimalism, bold typography). Design system prioritizes aesthetics.

Wix: Built for everyone. Templates prioritize mass appeal over cutting-edge design. Result: Generic, safe, dated.

Analogy: Framer = boutique design agency. Wix = stock photo marketplace. Both serve markets, but aesthetics differ wildly.

Is Webflow worth the learning curve for design?

Yes, if design quality is your top priority.

Learning curve: 20-40 hours to get proficient. 100+ hours for mastery.

Premium you get:

  • Pixel-perfect control (break templates completely)
  • Custom CSS (write your own styles)
  • Advanced animations (parallax, micro-interactions)
  • Unique layouts (no two sites alike)

Tradeoff: Time vs quality. Framer = 3 hours, great design. Webflow = 30 hours, award-winning design. Choose based on priorities.

How do I avoid my site looking "AI-generated"?

Five strategies:

  1. Choose unique templates: Avoid the most popular Framer/Webflow templates
  2. Customize heavily: Change colors, fonts, imagery, layout
  3. Add custom content: Personal photography, unique copy
  4. Break patterns: Modify grid, add unexpected elements
  5. Start with Webflow: If uniqueness is critical, blank canvas beats templates

Reality check: Some template reuse is OK. Users don't notice templates like designers do. Focus on content quality, not template uniqueness.
