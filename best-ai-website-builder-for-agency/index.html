FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Should agencies use their website as a portfolio showcase or lead generation tool?

Lead generation tool first, portfolio second. Agency site exists to generate new business, not show off. Forge Digital's old site: Portfolio showcase (pretty project images, no results). New site: Lead generation (pricing transparent, results visible, testimonials with metrics). Result? Inquiries 175% increase, qualified leads 300% increase. Agencies mistake: "Our site = our portfolio." Reality: Your site = lead gen engine. Prospects don't hire because portfolio pretty. Prospects hire because results visible ("+340% leads"), pricing transparent ("Web Design $20-40K" = fits budget), testimonials prove ("Forge delivered 6 weeks early"). Portfolio supports claims, not primary purpose. Agency site structure: Homepage = lead generation (services visible, pricing transparent, CTA prominent), Portfolio = results proof (every project with metrics), About = trust (team bios, testimonials, process). Lead gen first, portfolio second.

How do agencies break "our own site is lowest priority" paralysis?

Treat agency site as client project with hard deadline. Allocate 1 week, ship, iterate. Forge Digital's approach: 18 months "our site next month" paralysis. Breakthrough: Treat agency site as paying client with deadline. Allocate: Monday-Tuesday content (copy, images), Wednesday build (Framer), Thursday edits, Friday launch. No perfection, no "just one more animation." Ship Friday, improve next month. Result: Site launched in 1 week (vs. 18 months custom paralysis). Revenue started month 1. Agency site pitfall: Perfectionism = never launches. Solution: Timebox 1 week, ship good enough, iterate based on data. Month 2: Add testimonials. Month 3: Add case studies. Month 4: Optimize conversion. Perfect never launches. Good enough generates revenue immediately, improves over time.

Should agencies show pricing or keep it hidden?

Show pricing transparently. Pricing visible = 73% fewer unqualified inquiries + 300% more qualified leads. Forge Digital tested: "Contact for quote" vs. "Branding $30-50K, Web $20-40K, Strategy $15-25K." Results with pricing visible: Unqualified inquiries decreased 73% (agencies with $5K budgets self-select out), qualified inquiries INCREASED 300% (serious clients see budget fits, inquire confidently), close rate increased 67% (pricing transparency = trust = faster decisions). Pricing visible = saves agency time (no more $5K discovery calls), attracts qualified only (serious clients know budget fits), builds trust (transparency = confidence). Hide pricing only if: 100% custom work (every project unique pricing), strategy-first services (value-based pricing, not scope-based). Most agencies: Show pricing ranges ("Web Design $20-40K") sufficient for self-selection.

Which AI builder is best for agencies building client sites (not agency site)?

Framer for design-forward client sites. 10Web for WordPress-demanding clients. Durable for emergency client sites. Client site decision = client's needs, not agency preference. Framer client sites: Design-forward brands (startups, tech, lifestyle), portfolios and marketing sites, clients valuing modern aesthetics over CMS familiarity. 10Web client sites: WordPress-demanding clients (80% of websites WP = client expectation), e-commerce businesses (WooCommerce included), clients wanting freedom to hire other developers later. Durable client sites: Emergency rush ("need live by tomorrow"), simple lead gen (local businesses), non-technical clients (edit themselves). Agency role: Recommend based on client needs, not agency preference. Client freedom = happy clients = referrals = agency growth.

Testimonials = social proof = shortest path to trust. Collect from every project. Build library over time. 6-12 testimonials sufficient.

How often should agencies update their portfolio with new work?
