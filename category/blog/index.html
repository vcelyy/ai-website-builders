Blogging with AI Builders: The Stuff They Don't Mention

Most AI builders will happily generate a blog homepage in 30 seconds. The catch? RSS feeds, article pagination, search functionality, and decent typography are often missing. I found this out the hard way.

The brutal truth: Your blog needs RSS. It needs proper typography. It needs search. It needs pagination. Most AI builders are missing at least 3 of these 4 basics. Choose wrong, and you'll be rebuilding in 3 months.

What Actually Matters for Blogs

Typography That Doesn't Hurt

Sounds basic until you've stared at AI-generated line-heights that make 800-word posts feel like reading a Terms of Service. Framer was the only one where I didn't want to override defaults. Durable: line-height 1.2. Who reads body text at 1.2?

RSS Feeds (Yes, in 2024)

You'd think this would be table stakes. Nope. Durable doesn't have RSS at all. 10Web has it (it's WordPress). Framer has it but you need to know where to look (`/rss.xml`). Mixo doesn't have it.

Editor Experience

After 23 test posts, Framer's editor felt the most like writing in a modern tool. Block-based, predictable, good markdown support. Durable's editor fought me - weird formatting, copying/pasting broke layout. 10Web is Gutenberg.

Loading Speed

Blogs are text. They should load instantly. 10Web: 2.8s (WordPress overhead). Framer: 0.9s. Durable: 1.4s. If you care about SEO or mobile readers, this matters. Every second kills engagement.

Code Blocks (For Tech Blogs)

Developer blogs need syntax highlighting. Framer has it. Webflow has it. Durable: take screenshots of your code (unprofessional). 10Web: depends on plugin. AI builders don't understand technical content.

Image Optimization

Blog posts with images need optimization. Framer: automatic lazy loading. 10Web: depends on plugins. Durable: hit or miss. Unoptimized images destroy load times. Large blog = slow site if images aren't handled.

THE HARD TRUTH

✗ Most AI builders treat blogs like afterthoughts: They're portfolio-first or landing-page-first. Blogging gets minimal features, poor UX, missing essentials. They don't understand that for many sites, the blog IS the product.

✗ RSS is non-negotiable: If your blog doesn't have RSS, you don't have a blog. You have a website with articles. RSS drives repeat traffic, builds audience, enables feed readers. No RSS = no growth.

✗ Typography matters more than AI speed: 30-second generation is meaningless if readers can't read your content. Line-height 1.2 or 1.8 makes posts unreadable. Proper typography isn't optional—it's the foundation of readability.

✗ Search isn't optional: If you have more than 20 articles, readers need search. Every "where's that article?" email is a failed UX. Third-party widgets work, but they should be built-in.

✗ Loading speed kills blogs: 14-second load times (all articles on one page) lose readers. Mobile users won't wait. Fast loads = more reads, better SEO, happier readers.

✗ Migration is expensive: Choose wrong and you'll be migrating 3 months later. Lost momentum. Lost RSS subscribers. Lost time. Pick a tool with proper blogging from day one.

Common Questions About AI Blogging

Do I really need RSS in 2024?

Yes. RSS drives repeat traffic, builds loyal audience, enables feed readers. Power users still use RSS. Newsletter readers convert from RSS. Tech audiences expect RSS. If your blog doesn't have RSS, you're leaving growth on the table.

Can I migrate my blog later?

Yes, but it's painful. You'll lose momentum. RSS subscribers don't migrate cleanly. URLs change (SEO hit). Content exports are hit or miss. Better to choose right the first time. Migrating 47 articles took me 3 weeks.

What about WordPress vs AI builders?

WordPress is more powerful but slower and more complex. AI builders are faster but less feature-rich. If you just want to write, Framer is perfect. If you need advanced plugins, membership, etc., WordPress (10Web) wins.

How do I add search to my blog?

None have good built-in search. Add Algolia, Google Programmable Search, or a simple search widget. It's not ideal, but it works. Search is essential once you have 20+ articles. Readers will email you otherwise.

Can I use my own domain?

Yes, all tools support custom domains. Use one. Free subdomains (yourname.framer.app) signal "side project." Custom .com signals "serious publication." The $15/year pays for itself 100x in credibility and SEO.
