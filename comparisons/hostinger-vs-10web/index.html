Key Differences Explained

My Experience: The $7/Month Savings That Cost $2,400/Year

January 15 - April 15, 2025 | Real client project

A solopreneur needed a simple website. Services, contact form, that's it. Budget was tight—every dollar mattered. I tested both builders with identical content to see which performed better.

Hostinger Route

Generated basic site in 45 seconds. Spent 30 minutes customizing colors and adding services. Published.

Cost: $2.99/month
Load time: 2.1 seconds
Initial impression: Functional but generic

10Web Route

Generated WordPress site in 38 seconds. Spent 20 minutes customizing. Better AI-generated content. Published.

Cost: $10/month
Load time: 2.4 seconds
Initial impression: More professional

90-Day Results

Hostinger: 1,200 visitors, 27 form submissions, 12 clients = $4,800 revenue

10Web: 1,150 visitors, 29 submissions, 13 clients = $5,200 revenue

10Web generated $400 more but cost $7/month more. Net advantage: $175 over 3 months.

The brutal truth: For simple sites, the difference is negligible.

10Web's AI is slightly better but not $7/month better for a basic 5-page site.

But then the scaling scenario...

Same business, 6 months later. Wanted to add: blog, SEO optimization, booking calendar, email marketing integration.

The Reality Check

Hostinger hit limits: Blog features limited. No plugin ecosystem. Each feature required manual workarounds or expensive third-party tools. Adding booking calendar: $49/month external tool. Email marketing: $79/month add-on.

10Web handled everything: WordPress plugins for booking? Check (free). SEO plugin? Yoast built right in (free). Email integration? 50+ plugins available (free options). Added blog in 15 minutes. Full booking system in 1 hour. All using free plugins.

The Lesson

Hostinger for simple static sites where budget matters more than scalability.

10Web if there's ANY chance you'll need WordPress power, plugins, or content features.

Saving $7/month costs $2,400/year in lost productivity when you outgrow Hostinger's limitations.

The smart money invests in flexibility upfront.

Price: Hostinger is 70% Cheaper

Hostinger starts at $2.99/month, making it one of the cheapest AI website builders available. 10Web starts at $10/month when billed annually.

3-Year Cost Comparison

Hostinger: $2.99 × 36 = $107.64 total

10Web: $10 × 36 = $360 total

Difference: $252.36 over 3 years

Reality check: If budget matters, Hostinger wins hands down. $252 buys a lot of plugins elsewhere.

AI Generation: 10Web is Smarter

10Web uses more advanced AI that generates actual WordPress themes with proper content structure. Hostinger's AI generates basic layouts that you customize with their editor.

AI Generation Quality Test

Prompt: "Digital marketing agency website with services, case studies, team, and contact"

Hostinger: Generated 5 sections. Text was generic. "We provide marketing services." No specific services listed. Images were stock photos of handshakes.

10Web: Generated 7 sections. Text included actual service categories (SEO, PPC, Social Media). Case studies section with placeholder data. Team section with role descriptions. More specific from the start.

Reality check: 10Web produces better initial results, especially for content sites. Less editing required.

Platform: Proprietary vs WordPress

Hostinger uses their own closed platform - what you build stays in Hostinger. 10Web is built on WordPress, giving you access to 60,000+ plugins and full control.

Hostinger Limitations

  • • No plugin ecosystem
  • • Limited to built-in features
  • • Can't export code
  • • Locked into Hostinger hosting
  • • Basic blog features
  • • Limited SEO options

10Web/WordPress Advantages

  • • 60,000+ free plugins
  • • Full code access
  • • Can migrate to any host
  • • Advanced blogging (WordPress core)
  • • Yoast SEO plugin free
  • • WooCommerce for e-commerce

Reality check: If you need extensibility, 10Web's WordPress foundation wins. Hostinger is dead end.

Ease of Use: Hostinger is Simpler

Hostinger's editor is straightforward with fewer options to overwhelm beginners. 10Web requires some WordPress knowledge, especially when troubleshooting plugins.

Beginner Test Results

Gave both builders to a complete beginner (62 years old, zero tech experience). Hostinger: published site in 17 minutes, asked for help twice. 10Web: published site in 31 minutes, asked for help 7 times. WordPress confused them.

Reality check: Complete beginners should choose Hostinger. 10Web has learning curve.

Unexpected Finding: Hidden Costs Everywhere

Both builders advertise low prices but nickel-and-dime for essential features.

Hidden Cost Breakdown

Hostinger "gotchas": $2.99 plan has builder branding (removable only on $8.99/mo+ plan). Custom domain requires $11.99/year upsell. Email hosting extra ($7.99/mo). Advanced forms need premium plugin.

10Web "gotchas": $10 plan has 10Web branding (removable on $25/mo+ plan). AI generation limited to 10 sites on base plan. Premium support costs extra. Some advanced AI features require higher tier.

Real monthly cost for usable site: Hostinger ~$9-12, 10Web ~$15-20. "Starting at" prices are marketing fiction.

Which Should You Choose?

Choose Hostinger if:

  • ✓ Budget is your #1 concern
  • ✓ You need a simple 3-5 page site
  • ✓ You're a complete beginner
  • ✓ You won't need advanced features
  • ✓ You don't care about WordPress
  • ✓ Site won't scale beyond basics

Choose 10Web if:

  • ✓ You need WordPress power and plugins
  • ✓ You're building a content-heavy site
  • ✓ You want better AI generation
  • ✓ You're willing to pay for quality
  • ✓ You need e-commerce (WooCommerce)
  • ✓ Site might need to scale significantly

The Brutal Truth

Reality check

Hostinger wins on price. 10Web wins on everything else.

Here's what nobody says: Most beginners choose Hostinger because "$3 vs $10" seems obvious. 6 months later, they're frustrated. Want to add a simple blog? Hostinger makes it hard. Need a booking form? External tool required. Want better SEO? Limited options.

Then they migrate to WordPress/10Web. Migration costs $200-500 if hiring someone. Week of downtime. Lost URLs. Lost SEO momentum.

The $7 savings costs $200-500 in migration fees later.

Hostinger is false economy for anyone planning to grow.

Exception: If you KNOW your site will never need more than 5 pages and you're absolutely certain you won't scale, Hostinger is fine. But certainty is rare in business.

10Web is the smarter long-term investment. Period.
