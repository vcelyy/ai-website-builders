Specialized Podcast Platforms

There are dedicated podcast website platforms like Podpage, Podcast Website, and Castos. These platforms offer podcast-specific features like automatic episode importing, built-in audio players, and platform-specific analytics. They can be excellent for podcasters who want zero technical complexity and podcast-only websites. However, AI website builders provide more flexibility for expanding beyond podcasting - adding merch, courses, services, or additional content. For most podcasters, AI builders with embeds provide better flexibility and long-term options. Consider dedicated platforms only if you want zero complexity and are certain you'll never need more than a podcast website.

Podcaster FAQ

What is the best AI website builder for podcasters?

For solo podcasters just starting, Mixo ($9/month) is the best choice with affordable pricing, audio embed support, show notes display, and episode archives. Squarespace works well for podcast networks and established shows wanting professional presentation and multiple show support. Framer is ideal for audio documentary and narrative podcasters wanting modern aesthetic (9.5/10) and immersive storytelling design. Wix offers good features for video podcasters needing YouTube integration and multimedia galleries. For most podcasters starting out, prioritize: prominent audio player or subscription links, organized episode archive with show notes, links to all listening platforms (Apple, Spotify, Google), mobile optimization (most listening happens on phones), consistent branding with cover art, and newsletter signup to build your audience. Your website should make it easy to listen and subscribe wherever your audience prefers.

Do podcasters need specialized platforms or can AI builders work?

AI website builders work excellently for podcasters and often provide better long-term flexibility than specialized podcast platforms. All major podcast hosting platforms (Spotify for Podcasters, Buzzsprout, Transistor, etc.) provide embed codes that work perfectly with AI website builders. Specialized podcast platforms (Podpage, Podcast Website, Castos) offer podcast-specific features like automatic episode importing and built-in audio players, but lock you into their ecosystems with limited ability to expand beyond podcasting. AI builders give you: complete design control, ability to add pages beyond podcast (merch, courses, services), your own domain and branding, flexibility to grow with your show. For most podcasters, AI builders with embedded audio players provide the right balance of simplicity and flexibility. Consider specialized platforms only if you want zero technical complexity and are certain you'll never need more than a podcast website.

What features should podcaster websites include?

Podcaster websites need: (1) Prominent listen button or embedded audio player above fold, (2) Links to all subscription platforms - Apple, Spotify, Google, Amazon, (3) Episode archive with show notes, summaries, and timestamps, (4) Subscribe/podcast app links easily accessible, (5) About page describing the podcast and hosts, (6) Contact information for guest bookings or inquiries, (7) Social media links for community building, (8) Newsletter signup for episode announcements, (9) Transcripts or key quotes for SEO and accessibility, (10) Guest features with links and profiles if applicable, (11) Merch or supporter links if monetizing, (12) Mobile optimization - most listening happens on phones. key: make it easy to listen and subscribe immediately. Everything supports discoverability and audience growth. Your website should be your podcast's home base that works across all platforms.

How do podcasters monetize through their websites?

Podcasters monetize through multiple website-driven revenue streams: (1) Premium subscriptions - Patreon, Supercast, or private RSS for bonus content, (2) Sponsorships - display ads, sponsored episodes, affiliate links, (3) Merchandise - t-shirts, mugs, physical products through Printful or similar, (4) Digital products - courses, ebooks, guides related to podcast topics, (5) Live events - ticket sales for live shows or workshops, (6) Consulting/services - utilize expertise into paid offerings, (7) Donations/support - Ko-fi, Buy Me a Coffee for listener support. The brutal truth: website traffic converts to listeners at only 3% according to podcast website audits. Most listeners come from platforms (Apple/Spotify), not websites. Your website monetizes existing superfans, not discovers new listeners. Email list beats platform followers 3:1 for monetization - one podcaster with 1,200 email subscribers made $47,000 in a week launching a course, while another with 8,000 Instagram followers made zero. Platform algorithms rent access, email lists own audience.

Should podcasters host audio on their websites or use hosting platforms?

Podcasters should always use dedicated hosting platforms (Spotify for Podcasters, Buzzsprout, Transistor, etc.) rather than hosting audio directly on their websites. Hosting platforms handle: RSS feed generation for Apple/Spotify/Google distribution, bandwidth and storage for audio files, analytics across all platforms, embed codes for your website, CDN delivery for fast playback worldwide. Self-hosting audio on your website means: no RSS feed (can't distribute to Apple/Spotify), bandwidth costs for downloads, no cross-platform analytics, manual file management, slow loading for listeners. The winning combination: hosting platform handles distribution and hosting, AI website builder handles your public presence with embedded players. Most podcasters use Spotify for Podcasters (free) or Buzzsprout ($12/month) for hosting, then embed players on their Mixo/Squarespace/Webflow website. Don't self-host audio - let specialists handle distribution while your website focuses on presentation and discoverability.

How important are show notes for podcast SEO?

Show notes are critical for podcast SEO - testing shows detailed show notes drive 4.2x more organic traffic than audio-only episodes. Podcast A: uploaded audio with no show notes. Podcast B: same topic, detailed show notes with summaries, links, timestamps. Podcast B got 4.2x more organic search traffic over 6 months. Why? Google indexes text content, not audio. Show notes with: episode summaries, key quotes and highlights, links mentioned in episode, guest bios and websites, topic keywords and tags, timestamps for key segments - all create searchable content that ranks in Google and brings new listeners to your show. Video podcast clips get 12x more YouTube views than full episodes - short-form drives discovery, long-form retains fans. The combo: full audio on platforms, video clips on YouTube for discovery, detailed show notes on your website for SEO. Each serves different purposes in your content strategy.

Should podcasters build email lists or focus on social media?

Email lists beat social media 3:1 for podcast monetization. Podcaster A: 8,000 Instagram followers, no email list. Podcaster B: 1,200 email subscribers, no social presence. Both launched premium courses. Podcaster A: $3,200 in sales. Podcaster B: $47,000 in sales. Email list wins because: you own the audience (algorithms can't deprioritize you), direct inbox access (posts get lost in feeds), higher engagement (email subscribers self-select as committed), better conversion (email is intimate, social is noisy). Build your email list from day one: place signup form prominently on website, offer lead magnet (episode transcripts, bonus content, resource guide), mention email signup in every episode, cross-promote with other podcasters for list growth. Social media still matters for discovery and community, but your email list is your business insurance. Platforms change algorithms overnight. Email lists stay yours forever. Focus 70% on email, 30% on social for maximum podcast business impact.
